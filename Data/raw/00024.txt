<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">List of <i>The Little Prince</i> adaptations</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><br/>
This <b>list of <i>The Little Prince</i> adaptations</b> is based on the <a href="/wiki/Novella" title="Novella">novella</a> <a href="/wiki/The_Little_Prince" title="The Little Prince">of the same name</a> (original title: <i><a class="extiw" href="//fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Le_Petit_Prince" title="fr:Le Petit Prince">Le Petit Prince</a></i>) by the French writer, poet and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aviator" title="Aviator">aviator</a> <a href="/wiki/Antoine_de_Saint-Exup%C3%A9ry" title="Antoine de Saint-Exupéry">Antoine de Saint-Exupéry</a>.</p>
<p>The illustrated book was first published in 1943. The novella is both the most read and most translated book in the French language, and was voted the best book of the 20th century in France. Translated into more than 250 languages and dialects (including braille),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NY_Times-2005.04.03_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NY_Times-2005.04.03-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Peak-2011.03_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Peak-2011.03-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> selling close to two million copies per year with sales totalling more than 140 million copies worldwide, it has become <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_books" title="List of best-selling books">one of the best-selling books</a> ever published.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TimesTribune-2012.05.03_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-TimesTribune-2012.05.03-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Due to the story's wide appeal, the novella has been adapted into various media over the decades, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Audio_recording" title="Audio recording">audio recordings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Graphic_novel" title="Graphic novel">graphic novel</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Movie_musical" title="Movie musical">movie musicals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Movie_theater" title="Movie theater">movie screen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Animated_series" title="Animated series">animated series</a>, <a href="/wiki/Theatre" title="Theatre">live stage theatre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ballet" title="Ballet">ballet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Statesman-2010.01.14_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Statesman-2010.01.14-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Audio_adaptations">Audio adaptations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_The_Little_Prince_adaptations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Audio adaptations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vinyl_record">Vinyl record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_The_Little_Prince_adaptations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Vinyl record">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Richard_Burton_narrating_%27The_Little_Prince%27,_short_45_RPM_demo_excerpt.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="517" data-file-width="550" height="207" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Richard_Burton_narrating_%27The_Little_Prince%27%2C_short_45_RPM_demo_excerpt.jpg/220px-Richard_Burton_narrating_%27The_Little_Prince%27%2C_short_45_RPM_demo_excerpt.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Richard_Burton_narrating_%27The_Little_Prince%27%2C_short_45_RPM_demo_excerpt.jpg/330px-Richard_Burton_narrating_%27The_Little_Prince%27%2C_short_45_RPM_demo_excerpt.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Richard_Burton_narrating_%27The_Little_Prince%27%2C_short_45_RPM_demo_excerpt.jpg/440px-Richard_Burton_narrating_%27The_Little_Prince%27%2C_short_45_RPM_demo_excerpt.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<ul>
<li><b>1954</b>: An audio adaptation, as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vinyl_record" title="Vinyl record">vinyl record</a>, was made with the French actor <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A9rard_Philipe" title="Gérard Philipe">Gérard Philipe</a> in the role of The Narrator, <a href="/wiki/Georges_Poujouly" title="Georges Poujouly">Georges Poujouly</a> as The Prince, <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Larquey" title="Pierre Larquey">Pierre Larquey</a> in the role of The Lamplighter, <a href="/wiki/Michel_Roux_(actor)" title="Michel Roux (actor)">Michel Roux</a> in the role of The Serpent, <a class="extiw" href="//fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacques_Grello" title="fr:Jacques Grello">Jacques Grello</a> in the role of The Fox, and <a class="extiw" href="//fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sylvie_Pelayo" title="fr:Sylvie Pelayo">Sylvie Pelayo</a> in the role of The Rose.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deji_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Deji-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><b>1956</b>: A German adaption on a Philips label double LP record album, narrated by <a class="extiw" href="//de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hardy_Kr%C3%BCger" title="de:Hardy Krüger">Hardy Krüger</a></li>
<li><b>1972</b>: An adaptation with <a href="/wiki/Jean-Louis_Trintignant" title="Jean-Louis Trintignant">Jean-Louis Trintignant</a> in the role of narrator and <a class="extiw" href="//fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_Damain" title="fr:Eric Damain">Eric Damain</a> in the role of The Prince</li>
<li><b>1973</b>: An adaptation with <a href="/wiki/Marcel_Mouloudji" title="Marcel Mouloudji">Marcel Mouloudji</a> in the role of narrator and <a class="extiw" href="//fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_R%C3%A9my" title="fr:Eric Rémy">Eric Rémy</a> in the role of The Prince.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mouloudji_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mouloudji-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><b>1975</b>: Actor <a href="/wiki/Richard_Burton" title="Richard Burton">Richard Burton</a> won the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Album_for_Children" title="Grammy Award for Best Album for Children">Grammy Award for Best Album for Children</a> for his narration of <i>The Little Prince</i>, featuring <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Winters" title="Jonathan Winters">Jonathan Winters</a> and Billy Simpson. It was produced by <a href="/wiki/Buddy_Kaye" title="Buddy Kaye">Buddy Kaye</a> with music by <a href="/wiki/Mort_Garson" title="Mort Garson">Mort Garson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gammy_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gammy-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><b>1978</b>: A phono adaptation with <a href="/wiki/Jean-Claude_Pascal" title="Jean-Claude Pascal">Jean-Claude Pascal</a> in the role of narrator was released.</li>
<li><b>1983</b>: songs from the German version, <i>Der kleine Prinz</i>, by Kurt Demmler, and later produced in 1985 as a double LP on the Amiga label</li>
<li><b>1990</b>: A phono adaptation with <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Arditi" title="Pierre Arditi">Pierre Arditi</a> in the role of narrator and <a class="extiw" href="//fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_Pascal" title="fr:Benjamin Pascal">Benjamin Pascal</a> in the role of The Prince was released.</li>
<li><b>1996</b>: A Digital Quebec production with a musical score by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Normandeau" title="Robert Normandeau">Robert Normandeau</a> was released. Actor Michel Dumont (as the Narrator–Aviator) is surrounded by an array of characters, including a young <a href="/wiki/Martin_Pensa" title="Martin Pensa">Martin Pensa</a> who embodies the title role.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio_broadcasts">Radio broadcasts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_The_Little_Prince_adaptations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Radio broadcasts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><b>1956</b>: <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Burr" title="Raymond Burr">Raymond Burr</a> starred in a radio adaptation of <i>The Little Prince</i> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_CBS_Radio_Workshop" title="The CBS Radio Workshop">The CBS Radio Workshop</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBS_Radio-1956_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBS_Radio-1956-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><b>2000</b>: <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_4" title="BBC Radio 4">BBC Radio 4</a> broadcast, on 1 January 2000, a <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">dramatization</a> by <a href="/wiki/Bonnie_Greer" title="Bonnie Greer">Bonnie Greer</a> of a new translation into English of <i>The Little Prince</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC.b_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC.b-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Statesman-2010.01.14_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Statesman-2010.01.14-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> It starred <a href="/wiki/Robert_Powell" title="Robert Powell">Robert Powell</a> as the aviator and narrator, Garrett Moore as The Little Prince, and <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Cribbins" title="Bernard Cribbins">Bernard Cribbins</a> as he The King, The Drunkard, and The Lamplighter. An audio cassette recording is available in the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Collection" title="BBC Radio Collection">BBC Radio Collection</a> series.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cassette_tape_and_CD">Cassette tape and CD</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_The_Little_Prince_adaptations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cassette tape and CD">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><b>1959</b>: An audio cassette adaptation in German, with Will Quadflieg in the role of narrator.</li>
<li><b>1994</b>: Adapted to a CD, by Matthew Mancini and others, with music by <a class="extiw" href="//it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fabio_Concato" title="it:Fabio Concato">Fabio Concato</a>, directed by Marco Carniti, on the EMI label from <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, Italy in 1994</li>
<li><b>1996</b>: Marc André Coallier narrated <i>Le Petit Prince</i>, supported by <a href="/wiki/Marc-Andr%C3%A9_Grondin" title="Marc-André Grondin">Marc-André Grondin</a>, Sophie Stanké, Paul Buissoneau, Ghislain Tremblay, Gaston Lepage, Jean-Pierre Gonthier and Gilbert Lachance. The accompanying music was performed by Alexandre Stanké.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deji.b_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Deji.b-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><b>1998</b>: A CD adaptation is directed by Romain Victo-Pujebet, with rumors of <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Leroy_(actor)" title="Philippe Leroy (actor)">Philippe Leroy</a>, <a class="extiw" href="//it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lella_Costa" title="it:Lella Costa">Lella Costa</a> with original music by Olivier Priszlak, released in Paris by Gallimard and in Milan by Pontaccio.</li>
<li><b>1999</b>: An <a href="/wiki/Audiobook" title="Audiobook">audiobook</a> adaptation on the Patmos label, read by <a class="extiw" href="//de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ulrich_M%C3%BChe" title="de:Ulrich Mühe">Ulrich Mühe</a>, wins a <i><a href="/wiki/Preis_der_deutschen_Schallplattenkritik" title="Preis der deutschen Schallplattenkritik">Preis der deutschen Schallplattenkritik</a></i> award in 2000 (<a class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn" href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/3491240581">ISBN 3-491-24058-1</a>).</li>
<li><b>2009</b>: <i>Hörbuch von Rausch</i> (Ecstasy Audiobook) adaptation of <i>Der kleine Prinz</i>, with a new translation narrated by <a class="extiw" href="//de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jan_Josef_Liefers" title="de:Jan Josef Liefers">Jan Josef Liefers</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_television">Film and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_The_Little_Prince_adaptations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Film and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>

<ul>
<li><b>1964</b>: Russian <a href="/wiki/List_of_major_opera_composers" title="List of major opera composers">operatic composer</a> <a href="/wiki/Lev_Knipper" title="Lev Knipper">Lev Knipper</a> wrote a 3-part symphony in 1962,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SFGate-2008.04.27_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-SFGate-2008.04.27-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> his <i>skazka</i> (tale) entitled <i>Malen′kiy Prints</i> (<i>The Little Prince</i>), which was first performed in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> in 1978.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dvoskina_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dvoskina-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><b>1981</b>: A <a href="/wiki/Musical_theatre" title="Musical theatre">musical theatre</a> adaptation entitled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Little_Prince_and_the_Aviator" title="The Little Prince and the Aviator">The Little Prince and the Aviator</a></i>, co-produced by lawyer A. Joseph Tandet who held the rights to <i>The Little Prince</i>, with music composed by <a href="/wiki/John_Barry_(composer)" title="John Barry (composer)">John Barry</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Adler" title="Jerry Adler">Jerry Adler</a>. Previews began on December 26, 1981 at the <a href="/wiki/Neil_Simon_Theatre" title="Neil Simon Theatre">Alvin Theatre</a>. Choreographed by Billy Wilson, it starred <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michael_York_(actor)" title="Michael York (actor)">Michael York</a> as The Aviator and <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Rapp" title="Anthony Rapp">Anthony Rapp</a> as The Prince, with <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Greene" title="Ellen Greene">Ellen Greene</a> in a supporting role. The musical deviates from Saint-Exupéry's original story in that the aviator, Toni, whose plane crashes in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sahara_Desert" title="Sahara Desert">Sahara Desert</a>, is explicitly in real-life the author (Saint-Exupéry), and the plot alternates flashbacks to actual events in his life with his interaction with the fictional Little Prince, a refugee from <a class="extiw" href="//fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/B_612_(Le_Petit_Prince)" title="fr:B 612 (Le Petit Prince)">Asteroid B-612</a>. After 16 preview shows the stage production closed on January 17, 1982, prior to its <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> debut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibdb.com_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-ibdb.com-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mandelbaum_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mandelbaum-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></li>

<ul>
<li><b>1970s</b>: Actor <a href="/wiki/James_Dean" title="James Dean">James Dean</a>'s fondness for the work extended to his memorizing most of its passages. The nickname of his 1955 Porsche 550 Spyder, "The Little Bastard", is a play on words of his favorite book. A stylized sculpture in memorial to Dean was built in <a href="/wiki/Cholame,_California" title="Cholame, California">Cholame, California</a> during the late 1970s. It carries a plaque quoting the <i>Little Prince</i> that reads "What is essential is invisible to the eye." He reportedly quoted the phrase often.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Globe_.26_Mail-2004.04.08_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-Globe_.26_Mail-2004.04.08-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><b>2012</b>: A non-profit, founded in California by Ellianne Kadence, is named StarSlight Yoga, derived from <i>The Little Prince</i> passage: “All men have stars, but they are not the same things for different people. For some, who are travelers, the stars are guides. For others they are no more than little lights in the sky. For others, who are scholars, they are problems... But all these stars are silent. You, —you alone will have stars as no one else has them... In one of the stars I shall be living. In one of them I shall be laughing. And so it will be as if all the stars will be laughing when you look at the sky at night..You, only you, will have stars that can laugh! And when your sorrow is comforted you will be content that you have known me... You will always be my friend. You will want to laugh with me. And you will sometimes open your window, so, for that pleasure... It will be as if, in place of the stars, I had given you a great number of little bells that knew how to laugh".</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Games">Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_The_Little_Prince_adaptations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><b>2003</b>: The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boardgame" title="Boardgame">boardgame</a>, <i>Der Kleine Prinz</i>, designed by Kai Haferkamp, and published by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kosmos_(game_publisher)" title="Kosmos (game publisher)">Kosmos</a>, 2003, is a semi-cooperative game where the players try to help the little prince "tame the fox" by performing activities and guessing games, somewhat like <a href="/wiki/Cranium_(board_game)" title="Cranium (board game)">Cranium</a>.</li>
<li><b>2011</b>: The video game <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_Galaxy" title="Super Mario Galaxy">Super Mario Galaxy</a></i> has many similarities to the descriptions and pictures from the original book.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBeaumont2011_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBeaumont2011-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><b>2013</b>: Another tabletop game, <i>The Little Prince: Make Me A Planet</i>, designed by Antoine Bauza (author of famous <a href="/wiki/7_Wonders_(board_game)" title="7 Wonders (board game)">7 Wonders</a>) and Bruno Cathala, is a competitive game having players collect sets of themed pieces to build their own planet and then score it according to their characters by number of sheep, roses, lamp posts etc. they managed to collect.</li>
</ul>
