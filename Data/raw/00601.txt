<p><b>Agnetha Fältskog</b> (<small>Swedish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Swedish" title="Help:IPA for Swedish">[aŋˈneː.ˈta ˈfɛlt.ˈskuːɡ]</a></span>, born 5 April 1950) is a Swedish recording artist. She achieved success in Sweden after the release of her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog_(album)" title="Agnetha Fältskog (album)">Agnetha Fältskog</a></i> in 1968, and reached international stardom as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> group <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a>, which has sold over 370 million albums and singles worldwide,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> making them one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_music_artists" title="List of best-selling music artists">best-selling music artists in history</a> and the seventh best-selling band in history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Since the break-up of ABBA, Fältskog has continued to have success as a solo artist, albeit interspersed by periods of relative recording inactivity.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_.281950.E2.80.931966.29">Early life (1950–1966)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life (1950–1966)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Agnetha Fältskog (known as Anna in some countries<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup>) was born in <a href="/wiki/J%C3%B6nk%C3%B6ping" title="Jönköping">Jönköping</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sm%C3%A5land" title="Småland">Småland</a>, Sweden on 5 April 1950.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daily_Mail_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Daily_Mail-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> She was the first of two daughters of department store manager Knut Ingvar Fältskog (1922–1995) and his wife Birgit Margareta Johansson (1923–1994).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Ingvar showed much interest in music and showbusiness,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> whereas Birgit was a calm and careful woman who devoted herself to her children and household.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AF_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-AF-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Fältskog wrote her first song at the age of six, entitled "Två små troll" ("Two Little Trolls").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-se_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-se-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1958, she began taking piano lessons, and also sang in a local church choir.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> In early 1960, Fältskog formed a musical trio, the Cambers, with her friends Lena Johansson and Elisabeth Strub. They performed locally in minor venues and soon dissolved due to a lack of engagements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> At age 15, Fältskog decided to leave school and pursue a career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Fältskog cites <a href="/wiki/Connie_Francis" title="Connie Francis">Connie Francis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marianne_Faithfull" title="Marianne Faithfull">Marianne Faithfull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aretha_Franklin" title="Aretha Franklin">Aretha Franklin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lesley_Gore" title="Lesley Gore">Lesley Gore</a> as her strongest musical influences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-5"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_development_in_Sweden_.281966.E2.80.931971.29">Career development in Sweden (1966–1971)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career development in Sweden (1966–1971)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fältskog worked as a telephonist for a car firm while performing with a local <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a> band, headed by Bernt Enghardt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-6"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> The band soon became so popular that she had to make a choice between her job and her musical career. She continued singing with the Bernt Enghardt band for two years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-7"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> During that time, Fältskog broke up with her boyfriend Björn Lilja; this event inspired her to write a song, "Jag var så kär" ("I Was So in Love"), that would soon raise her to media prominence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-8"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AF_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-AF-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> At that time, Karl Gerhard Lundkvist, a relative of one of the band's members, retired from his successful <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">rock and roll</a> career and began working as a record producer at Cupol Records. Enghardt sent him a demo recording of the band, but Lundkvist only showed interest in Fältskog and her song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AF_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-AF-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> She was worried because he was not interested in the band and they were not to be included on the record. However, she decided to accept the offer, and signed a <a href="/wiki/Recording_contract" title="Recording contract">recording contract</a> with <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">CBS Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-9"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Her self-penned début single "Jag var så kär" was released through Cupol Records in 1967, and topped the Swedish Chart on 28 January 1968 and sold more than 80,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-10"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> She also submitted the song "Försonade" ("Reconciled") to <a href="/wiki/Melodifestivalen" title="Melodifestivalen">Melodifestivalen</a>, the Swedish preliminary for the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a>, but it was not selected for the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AF_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-AF-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Fältskog developed a career as one of Sweden's most popular pop music artists, participating in a television special about pilots in 1968.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nu_Ska_Vi_Opp.2C_Opp.2C_Opp_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nu_Ska_Vi_Opp.2C_Opp.2C_Opp-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> The same year, she released the single "Zigenarvän" ("Gypsy Friend") about a young girl attending a <a href="/wiki/Romani_people" title="Romani people">Gypsy</a> wedding and falling in love with the bride's brother. Its release coincided with a heated debate about Gypsies in the Swedish media, and Fältskog was accused of deliberately trying to make money out of the situation by writing the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-se_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-se-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Fältskog's success continued throughout the late 1960s. She met German songwriter/producer Dieter Zimmerman, to whom she became engaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-11"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Her albums thus reached German charts, and Zimmerman promised her she would achieve great success in Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-12"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> However when she went there and met with record producers, the venture was not productive; Fältskog refused to meet the demands of the producers, describing their chosen material as "horrible".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-13"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> She soon ended her engagement to Zimmerman and returned to Sweden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-14"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1970, she released "Om tårar vore guld" ("If Tears Were Gold"), which was perhaps her most successful song in Sweden before <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> This was in spite of a claim from a Danish composer that she had used 22 bars from his composition "Tema" ("Theme"), even though it had been written in the 1950s and had never been recorded. The case dragged on until 1977, when a settlement was reached and Fältskog paid the Dane <a href="/wiki/Swedish_krona" title="Swedish krona">SEK</a>5,000.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1972, Fältskog portrayed <a href="/wiki/Mary_Magdalene" title="Mary Magdalene">Mary Magdalene</a> in the Swedish production of the international musical hit <i><a href="/wiki/Jesus_Christ_Superstar" title="Jesus Christ Superstar">Jesus Christ Superstar</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AF_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-AF-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_marriage_and_years_with_ABBA_.281971.E2.80.931982.29">First marriage and years with ABBA (1971–1982)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: First marriage and years with ABBA (1971–1982)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fältskog met <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_Ulvaeus" title="Björn Ulvaeus">Björn Ulvaeus</a>, a member of the <a href="/wiki/Hootenanny_Singers" title="Hootenanny Singers">Hootenanny Singers</a>, for the first time in 1968, and then again in 1969.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daily_Mail_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Daily_Mail-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AF_9-5"><a href="#cite_note-AF-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Her relationship with Ulvaeus, as well as her friendship with <a href="/wiki/Anni-Frid_Lyngstad" title="Anni-Frid Lyngstad">Anni-Frid Lyngstad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benny_Andersson" title="Benny Andersson">Benny Andersson</a>, with whom Ulvaeus had already written songs, eventually led to the formation of <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a>. Fältskog and Ulvaeus married on 6 July 1971 in the village of Verum, with Andersson playing the organ at their wedding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-15"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Their first child, <a href="/wiki/Linda_Ulvaeus" title="Linda Ulvaeus">Linda Elin Ulvaeus</a>, was born on 23 February 1973, and their son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_Ulvaeus" title="Christian Ulvaeus">Peter Christian Ulvaeus</a> on 4 December 1977.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daily_Mail_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Daily_Mail-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> The couple decided to separate in late 1978, and Fältskog moved out of their home on Christmas night, 25 December 1978.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In January 1979 the couple filed for divorce, which was finalised in June 1980. Both Fältskog and Ulvaeus agreed not to let their failed marriage interfere with their responsibilities with ABBA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-16"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daily_Mail_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-Daily_Mail-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AF_9-6"><a href="#cite_note-AF-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> The failure of their marriage inspired Ulvaeus to write "<a href="/wiki/The_Winner_Takes_It_All" title="The Winner Takes It All">The Winner Takes It All</a>", one of ABBA's greatest hits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnetha_6-17"><a href="#cite_note-Agnetha-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daily_Mail_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-Daily_Mail-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AF_9-7"><a href="#cite_note-AF-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>In 1975, during the same period as her bandmate <a href="/wiki/Anni-Frid_Lyngstad" title="Anni-Frid Lyngstad">Anni-Frid Lyngstad</a> recorded her Swedish number one album <i><a href="/wiki/Frida_ensam" title="Frida ensam">Frida ensam</a></i>, Fältskog recorded and produced her solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Elva_kvinnor_i_ett_hus" title="Elva kvinnor i ett hus">Elva kvinnor i ett hus</a></i>. These albums were both recorded between sessions and promotion for the ABBA albums <i><a href="/wiki/Waterloo_(album)" title="Waterloo (album)">Waterloo</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/ABBA_(album)" title="ABBA (album)">ABBA</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Fältskog's album spent 53 weeks on the Swedish album chart, longer than any of ABBA's albums, though it only peaked at number 11. It contained three further <a href="/wiki/Svensktoppen" title="Svensktoppen">Svensktoppen</a> entries for Fältskog: her Swedish language version of ABBA's "SOS" (also number 4 on the single sales chart); "Tack För En Underbar Vanlig Dag"; and "Doktorn!". Except for the version of "SOS", all the songs had lyrics by Bosse Carlgren and music by Fältskog herself. The creation of the album had been underway since 1972, when Fältskog started writing the songs, but it was delayed because of the work with ABBA and her pregnancy.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1974 Fältskog and Carlgren had agreed on a concept for the album; it should consist of 12 songs, each sung by 12 different female characters living in the same apartment building, however in the end only 11 songs were put onto the album, and the concept wasn't fully developed.</p>

<p>Between the years 1968 and 1980, Fältskog had a total of 18 entries on the Svensktoppen radio chart, starting with the debut single "Jag Var Så Kär" in January 1968 (peak position number 1) and ending with "När Du Tar Mig I Din Famn" ("When You Take Me in Your Arms") from the compilation <i><a href="/wiki/Tio_%C3%A5r_med_Agnetha" title="Tio år med Agnetha">Tio år med Agnetha</a></i> in January 1980 (peak position number 1). The 18 entries, most of which were composed or co-written by Fältskog herself, spent a total of 139 weeks on the chart during this time, with the biggest hit being 1970s "<a href="/wiki/Om_t%C3%A5rar_vore_guld" title="Om tårar vore guld">Om tårar vore guld</a>" (number 1, 15 weeks).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Fältskog also recorded the Swedish Christmas album <i><a href="/wiki/Nu_t%C3%A4ndas_tusen_juleljus_(Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog_%26_Linda_Ulvaeus_album)" title="Nu tändas tusen juleljus (Agnetha Fältskog &amp; Linda Ulvaeus album)">Nu tändas tusen juleljus</a></i> with daughter <a href="/wiki/Linda_Ulvaeus" title="Linda Ulvaeus">Linda Ulvaeus</a> which peaked at number 6 on the Swedish album sales chart in December 1981. Chartwise Fältskog was the most successful solo artist of the four ABBA members, both before and during the band's international career,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> though both Björn Ulvaeus and Benny Andersson achieved a comparably high profile within the Hootenanny Singers and Hep Stars, their respective non-ABBA bands.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Fältskog participated in <a href="/wiki/Melodifestivalen" title="Melodifestivalen">Melodifestivalen</a> again, albeit only as a composer. In 1981, she wrote the ballad "Men Natten Är Vår" ("But the Night Is Ours") with lyrics by <a href="/wiki/Ingela_Forsman" title="Ingela Forsman">Ingela Forsman</a>, but instead of performing the song in the contest herself, she chose new talent Kicki Moberg. The song placed 9th out of 10. The single, which Fältskog produced in the Polar Studios with the same musicians as on contemporary ABBA recordings, was backed with the Swedish version of "I'm Still Alive", entitled "Här Är Mitt Liv" ("Here Is My Life"), a song which she herself had performed in its English version (lyrics by ex-husband Björn Ulvaeus) during ABBA's 1979 world tour.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Moberg's recording of the song remains the only version to have been officially released to date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Fältskog sang solo parts in the following ABBA songs: <a href="/wiki/Disillusion_(ABBA_song)" title="Disillusion (ABBA song)">"Disillusion"</a> (music written by her, lyrics by Björn), "I Am Just A Girl", "<a href="/wiki/Hasta_Ma%C3%B1ana" title="Hasta Mañana">Hasta Mañana</a>", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dance_(While_The_Music_Still_Goes_On)" title="Dance (While The Music Still Goes On)">Dance (While The Music Still Goes On)</a>", "<a href="/wiki/SOS_(ABBA_song)" title="SOS (ABBA song)">SOS</a>", <a href="/wiki/I%27ve_Been_Waiting_for_You_(ABBA_song)" title="I've Been Waiting for You (ABBA song)">"I've Been Waiting for You"</a>, "<a href="/wiki/When_I_Kissed_the_Teacher" title="When I Kissed the Teacher">When I Kissed the Teacher</a>", "<a href="/wiki/My_Love,_My_Life_(song)" title="My Love, My Life (song)">My Love, My Life</a>", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Take_A_Chance_on_Me" title="Take A Chance on Me">Take A Chance on Me</a>", "<a href="/wiki/The_Name_of_the_Game_(ABBA_song)" title="The Name of the Game (ABBA song)">The Name of the Game</a>", <a href="/wiki/Move_On_(ABBA_song)" title="Move On (ABBA song)">"Move On"</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Thank_You_for_the_Music" title="Thank You for the Music">Thank You for the Music</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Chiquitita" title="Chiquitita">Chiquitita</a>" ,"Lovelight", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/As_Good_As_New" title="As Good As New">As Good As New</a>", "Kisses of Fire", "Dream World", "<a href="/wiki/Gimme!_Gimme!_Gimme!_(A_Man_After_Midnight)" title="Gimme! Gimme! Gimme! (A Man After Midnight)">Gimme! Gimme! Gimme! (A Man After Midnight)</a>" "<a href="/wiki/The_Winner_Takes_It_All" title="The Winner Takes It All">The Winner Takes It All</a>", <a href="/wiki/Happy_New_Year_(song)" title="Happy New Year (song)">Happy New Year</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Lay_All_Your_Love_on_Me" title="Lay All Your Love on Me">Lay All Your Love on Me</a>", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Way_Old_Friends_Do" title="The Way Old Friends Do">The Way Old Friends Do</a>", <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Head_Over_Heels_(ABBA_song)" title="Head Over Heels (ABBA song)">"Head Over Heels"</a>, <a href="/wiki/One_of_Us_(ABBA_song)" title="One of Us (ABBA song)">"One of Us</a>, "Soldiers", "<a href="/wiki/Slipping_Through_My_Fingers" title="Slipping Through My Fingers">Slipping Through My Fingers</a>", <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Just_Like_That_(song)" title="Just Like That (song)">"Just Like That</a>, "I am the City", "<a href="/wiki/Under_Attack" title="Under Attack">Under Attack</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/The_Day_Before_You_Came" title="The Day Before You Came">The Day Before You Came</a>", and her voice was of course clearly audible in most other ABBA tracks as well. "The Name of the Game", "If it wasn't for the nights", "The Way Old Friends Do", "Cassandra" and "I am the City" are the only officially released ABBA tracks that feature solo parts from both Agnetha and Anni-Frid. Anni-Frid sings the lead vocal in "Cassandra" but Agnetha sings solo parts in the chorus ('But on the darkest of nights ...').</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career_development_.281982.E2.80.931989.29">Solo career development (1982–1989)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Solo career development (1982–1989)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although no official announcement was made, ABBA effectively disbanded in late 1982 to early 1983. At the end of 1982 Fältskog duetted with Swedish singer (and former ABBA backing vocalist) <a href="/wiki/Tomas_Ledin" title="Tomas Ledin">Tomas Ledin</a> on a song called "<a href="/wiki/Never_Again_(Agnetha_song)" title="Never Again (Agnetha song)">Never Again</a>", which became a Top Five hit in Sweden, Norway, Belgium, and South America.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The song was also released in a Spanish-language version, entitled "Ya Nunca Más". In the summer of the same year, Fältskog received positive reviews for her leading role in the hit Swedish movie <i>Raskenstam</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In May 1983, Fältskog released her first post-ABBA solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Wrap_Your_Arms_Around_Me" title="Wrap Your Arms Around Me">Wrap Your Arms Around Me</a></i>. The album became a moderate hit in North America and Australia, and reached the higher regions of the charts across Europe, including No. 1 in Sweden, Norway, Finland, Belgium, and Denmark (where it became the biggest-selling album of the year), and No. 18 in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> The album achieved sales of 1,2 million copies in the first year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Two singles from the album became hits in continental Europe: "<a href="/wiki/The_Heat_Is_On_(Agnetha_song)" title="The Heat Is On (Agnetha song)">The Heat Is On</a>" became a No. 1 hit in Sweden, Denmark, Norway, Finland, Belgium, and the Netherlands, and just scraped into the UK Top 40,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> and the title track also peaked at No. 1 in Belgium as well as the in Top Five in the Netherlands and got just into Top 30 in west Germany and Top 50 in the UK and France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-home.zipworld.com.au_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-home.zipworld.com.au-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> In North America, the album track "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Shake_Loose" title="Can't Shake Loose">Can't Shake Loose</a>" was released as the lead-off single, reaching No. 29 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard.com_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-billboard.com-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> and No. 23 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RPM" title="RPM">RPM</a> Top 50 singles chart in Canada.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Fältskog was voted by the readers of <a href="/wiki/Aftonbladet" title="Aftonbladet">Aftonbladet</a> as Best Female Artist of the Year in the <a href="/wiki/Rockbj%C3%B6rnen" title="Rockbjörnen">Rockbjörnen</a> music awards.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Fältskog's next studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Eyes_of_a_Woman" title="Eyes of a Woman">Eyes of a Woman</a></i>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Eric_Stewart" title="Eric Stewart">Eric Stewart</a> of <a href="/wiki/10cc" title="10cc">10cc</a>, was released in March 1985. "She is quite content to grace the works of various other lesser mortals with her immaculate, sugar-sweet voice," wrote Barry McIlheney in <i><a href="/wiki/Melody_Maker" title="Melody Maker">Melody Maker</a></i>. The album sold well in parts of Europe, peaking at No. 2 in Sweden and in the Top 20 in Norway and Belgium, but scraped into the UK Top 40 just for one week<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> and failed to match the success of <i><a href="/wiki/Wrap_Your_Arms_Around_Me" title="Wrap Your Arms Around Me">Wrap Your Arms Around Me</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> All in all, the album sold up to 800,000 copies. However, the self-penned lead single "<a href="/wiki/I_Won%27t_Let_You_Go" title="I Won't Let You Go">I Won't Let You Go</a>" enjoyed a moderate chart success in both continental Europe and in Scandinavia: No. 6 in Sweden, No. 18 in the Netherlands and No. 24 in west Germany. On the contrary, the single was a flop in the UK – No. 84.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-home.zipworld.com.au_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-home.zipworld.com.au-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1986, Fältskog recorded another duet, "<a href="/wiki/The_Way_You_Are_(F%C3%A4ltskog_and_H%C3%A5kansson_song)" title="The Way You Are (Fältskog and Håkansson song)">The Way You Are</a>", with Swedish singer <a href="/wiki/Ola_H%C3%A5kansson" title="Ola Håkansson">Ola Håkansson</a>, which became another No. 1 hit in Sweden and Norway.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In early 1987, Agnetha recorded an album "Kom följ med I vår karusell" ('Come ride with me on my carousell') with her son Christian. The album contained songs for children and was sung in Swedish. For the album Agnetha recorded duets with her son and with a choir of children. She also recorded a few solo songs. The production was modern and fresh. The single 'På Söndag' was much played at the radio and even made the Swedish top 10, unique for a song made for kids to enjoy.</p>
<p>In the summer of 1987, Fältskog travelled to <a href="/wiki/Malibu,_California" title="Malibu, California">Malibu, California</a>, to record her fourth solo album (after ABBA), <i><a href="/wiki/I_Stand_Alone_(Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog_album)" title="I Stand Alone (Agnetha Fältskog album)">I Stand Alone</a></i>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Cetera" title="Peter Cetera">Peter Cetera</a> (formerly of the band <a href="/wiki/Chicago_(band)" title="Chicago (band)">Chicago</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Gaitsch" title="Bruce Gaitsch">Bruce Gaitsch</a>, who had collaborated on <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/La_Isla_Bonita" title="La Isla Bonita">La Isla Bonita</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Released in November of that year, <i>I Stand Alone</i> was a minor hit in Europe, except in Sweden where it spent eight weeks at No. 1 and became the best-selling album of 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> More than 200,000 copies were sold throughout Scandinavia. According to Hans Englund, the Swedish head of WEA, more than 500,000 copies were sold globally.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> The results outside Scandinavia were less impressive, the album got into Top 30 in the Netherlands, Top 50 in west Germany and became No. 72 in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-home.zipworld.com.au_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-home.zipworld.com.au-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> The single from the album, "<a href="/wiki/I_Wasn%27t_the_One_(Who_Said_Goodbye)" title="I Wasn't the One (Who Said Goodbye)">I Wasn't the One (Who Said Goodbye)</a>", on which Fältskog duetted with Peter Cetera, was released primarily in North America, and became her second solo single to chart on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, peaking at No. 93.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard.com_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-billboard.com-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> It was also a Top 20 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_Adult_Contemporary" title="Billboard Adult Contemporary"><i>Billboard</i> Adult Contemporary</a> hit. Two tracks were also recorded in Spanish for the Latin American market; "La Ultima Véz" ("The Last Time") and "Yo No Fui Quién Dijo Adiós" ("I Wasn't the One (Who Said Goodbye)". Agnetha refused to promote the album in major TV shows in West Germany, but she made a promotional visit to London in February 1988 – her appearance in Terry Wogan Show should have become her last public singing performance till November 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hiatus_from_recording_.281989.E2.80.932004.29">Hiatus from recording (1989–2004)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Hiatus from recording (1989–2004)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the release of <i>I Stand Alone</i> in mid-1988, Fältskog went on hiatus from her musical career and completely withdrew from public life.</p>
<p>In 1990, Fältskog married Swedish surgeon Tomas Sonnenfeld. They divorced in 1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> She also had a two-year (unconfirmed) "friendship" with Dutchman Gert van der Graaf at the end of the 1990s. After Fältskog decided to terminate the friendship, he stalked her at her mansion, resulting in a court issuing Van der Graaf with a restraining order. He was arrested in 2003 and banned from entry into Sweden. In 2005, the deportation order from Sweden ran out, and within months van der Graaf was again sighted near Fältskog's estate in <a href="/wiki/Eker%C3%B6" title="Ekerö">Ekerö</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1996, her autobiography <i>Som jag är</i> was published in Swedish (and in English the following year titled <i>As I Am</i>), followed by several compilation CDs of her Swedish and English recordings, including one called <i>My Love, My Life</i> for which Fältskog picked out the music herself. Fans welcomed the autobiography, but critics panned it.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="section (September 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The book was updated in 1998 and released worldwide via Virgin Publishing, selling over 50,000 books.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_music_and_selected_public_appearances_.282004.E2.80.93present.29">Return to music and selected public appearances (2004–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Return to music and selected public appearances (2004–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Faltskog.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="499" data-file-width="346" height="317" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Faltskog.png/220px-Faltskog.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Faltskog.png/330px-Faltskog.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5b/Faltskog.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Faltskog.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

</div>
</div>
<p>In April 2004, Fältskog released a new single, "<a href="/wiki/If_I_Thought_You%27d_Ever_Change_Your_Mind" title="If I Thought You'd Ever Change Your Mind">If I Thought You'd Ever Change Your Mind</a>" (a cover of the song originally recorded by <a href="/wiki/Cilla_Black" title="Cilla Black">Cilla Black</a>). It peaked at number 2 in Sweden, number 11 in the UK, and became a minor hit in the Netherlands and Denmark when the single got into Top 20.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> "It is exciting to hear her voice, utterly undimmed, delivering a tellingly-titled song," commented London's <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Week" title="Music Week">Music Week</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> One week later, the album <i><a href="/wiki/My_Colouring_Book" title="My Colouring Book">My Colouring Book</a></i>, a collection of Fältskog's covers of 1960s classic <a href="/wiki/Oldies" title="Oldies">oldies</a>, was released, topping the chart in Sweden, hitting the Top Five in Finland and in Denmark, number 6 in Germany, and peaking at number 12 in the United Kingdom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> The title song, "My Colouring Book", is a cover of the song originally recorded by <a href="/wiki/Dusty_Springfield" title="Dusty Springfield">Dusty Springfield</a>. "I love this record," enthused Pete Clark in London's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evening_Standard" title="Evening Standard">Evening Standard</a></i>, while the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mail" title="Daily Mail">Daily Mail</a></i> pointed out that "it reveals a genuine affection for the era's forgotten pop tunes."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sunday_Times" title="The Sunday Times">The Sunday Times</a></i> reviewer Mark Edwards noted that "her voice is still an impressive pop instrument,"<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Observer" title="The Observer">The Observer</a></i> shared the same sentiment suggesting that "time hasn't diminished her perfect voice."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> Reviewing the release in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>, Caroline Sullivan wrote: "Agnetha Fältskog has a vulnerability that gets under the skin of a song. She may be cheating a trifle by including no original material on this collection of 1960s covers, but if anyone can do justice to the likes of "<a href="/wiki/Sealed_with_a_Kiss" title="Sealed with a Kiss">Sealed with a Kiss</a>", it's her. The soaring sentimentality evokes <a href="/wiki/Cilla_Black" title="Cilla Black">Cilla Black</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sandie_Shaw" title="Sandie Shaw">Sandie Shaw</a> in their mini-skirted pomp, and I don't say that lightly."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup> The release attracted media attention across Europe, but Fältskog staunchly refused to be involved in any extensive promotion of the album (including personal appearances), and thus limited her public exposure to several short newspaper interviews, a few videos, and a Swedish-language low-key TV special.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Yet, the album managed to sell around 500,000 copies worldwide (according to Warner Music), 50,000 of those in the United Kingdom alone. The album became the 10th most selling album of 2004 in Sweden (incredible 64,000 copies were sold only in the first week). A second single release from the album, "<a href="/wiki/When_You_Walk_in_the_Room" title="When You Walk in the Room">When You Walk in the Room</a>", peaked at number 11 in Sweden and also entered the UK Top 40 as No. 34 (which was still higher than any of her UK singles in the 1980s).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Sometimes_When_I%27m_Dreaming" title="Sometimes When I'm Dreaming">Sometimes When I'm Dreaming</a>", originally recorded by <a href="/wiki/Art_Garfunkel" title="Art Garfunkel">Art Garfunkel</a>, failed to be released as the third single from the album. The album sold nearly one million albums worldwide as was confirmed by producer Jorgen Elofson, at the eve of the release of 'A' (Agnetha's album released in 2013).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Shortly after this release, for the 2004 semi-final of the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a>, staged in Istanbul 30 years after ABBA had won the contest in Brighton, Fältskog appeared briefly in a special comedy video made for the interval act, entitled "Our Last Video."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> Each of the four members of the group appeared briefly in cameo roles, as did others such as <a href="/wiki/Cher" title="Cher">Cher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rik_Mayall" title="Rik Mayall">Rik Mayall</a>. It was billed as the first time the four had worked together since the group split. In fact, while Björn and Benny filmed their more extensive parts together, Frida and Agnetha were filmed for a very short appearance separately and edited to appear together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2004, Fältskog was nominated for <i>Best Nordic Artist</i> at the Nordic Music Awards, and at Christmas of that year she gave an extensive interview (first after many years) which was filmed by Swedish TV.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> Around the same time, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Music" title="Sony Music">Sony Music</a> released a lavishly produced 6-CD <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boxed_set" title="Boxed set">boxed set</a> comprising Fältskog's Swedish solo career, mostly before ABBA, with five original solo albums: 1968, 1969, 1970, 1971, and her 1975 album recorded and released during her time with ABBA) – plus an additional compilation disc with bonus tracks.</p>
<p>In January 2007, Fältskog appeared at the final performance of <i>Mamma Mia!</i> in Stockholm (as she had at its opening in 2005). Together with ex-husband and former colleague Björn Ulvaeus, she appeared on stage at the after show party held at Stockholm's Grand Hotel.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> She also sang a duet, "True Love", with <a href="/wiki/Tommy_K%C3%B6rberg" title="Tommy Körberg">Tommy Körberg</a> of <i>Chess</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In October 2008, a new compilation album, <i><a href="/wiki/My_Very_Best" title="My Very Best">My Very Best</a></i>, was released in Sweden. The double CD contains both Swedish (CD 1) and English-language hits (CD 2) from her whole solo career, from 1967 to 2004. It successfully entered as number 4 on the Swedish albums chart and was certified gold within the first week of its release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On 4 July 2008, Fältskog joined former colleagues Anni-Frid Lyngstad, Björn Ulvaeus, and Benny Andersson at the Swedish premiere of the film version of <i><a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia!_(film)" title="Mamma Mia! (film)">Mamma Mia!</a></i>, held at the Rival Theatre (owned by Andersson) in <a href="/wiki/Mariatorget" title="Mariatorget">Mariatorget</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>. Fältskog arrived with Lyngstad and movie star <a href="/wiki/Meryl_Streep" title="Meryl Streep">Meryl Streep</a>, the three dancing in front of thousands of fans before joining the film's other stars and Andersson and Ulvaeus on the hotel balcony for the first photograph of all four ABBA members together in 22 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2009, Fältskog appeared onstage together with Lyngstad at the Swedish <a href="/wiki/Rockbj%C3%B6rnen" title="Rockbjörnen">Rockbjörnen</a> Awards to receive a lifetime honorary award for ABBA, and even giving a short, lightweight interview.</p>
<p>In February 2010, ABBA World, an extensive multi-million-pound (in monetary value) exhibition, debuted at London's Earls Court and included an extensive interview with Fältskog filmed in Sweden the previous summer.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> For the exhibition's Melbourne launch, she recorded a light-hearted opening film together with former ABBA colleague Benny Andersson, shot in Stockholm in June 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2010, Fältskog was at the opening of <i>Mamma Mia!</i> (the musical), in Denmark, with former husband Ulvaeus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> In December of the same year Agnetha gave a long interview to 'M' Magazine in Sweden. This interview also included a new official photo session, the first since 'My Colouring Book' in 2004/5.</p>
<p>On 10 May 2013 (13 May in the UK; 14 May in North America), Fältskog released a new album called "<a href="/wiki/A_(Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog_album)" title="A (Agnetha Fältskog album)">A</a>", produced by Jörgen Elofsson and Peter Nordahl. In the UK, the first single "When You Really Loved Someone" had previously been released for download on 11 March, with the accompanying video also released on the same day. In Germany, the first single was "The One Who Loves You Now" and it was also released for download on 11 March 2013. The album includes a duet with <a href="/wiki/Gary_Barlow" title="Gary Barlow">Gary Barlow</a> of <a href="/wiki/Take_That" title="Take That">Take That</a>, called "I Should've Followed You Home". In June 2013, officials from Universal Records in The Netherlands revealed that 600,000 copies of 'A' were sold in the first two months after the release. A TV special made by the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> helped the album reach number six in the UK Charts. The album made the Top 10 in Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Germany, Switzerland, the Netherlands, the UK, Belgium, Austria, and Australia and Top 20 in Finland and Ireland. The first single from the "A" album to be released in the US, <a href="/wiki/Dance_Your_Pain_Away" title="Dance Your Pain Away">Dance Your Pain Away</a>, was released on 28 May 2013. The digital single included two new mixes by Smash Mode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup> "Dance Your Pain Away" was released internationally as a single on 15 July 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On 17 May 2013, Fältskog was awarded the SKAP 2013 Kai Gullmar Memorial Award at the Stockholm release party for "A".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 November 2013 Fältskog sang live on stage for the first time in 25 years at the BBC <a href="/wiki/Children_in_Need_Rocks_2013" title="Children in Need Rocks 2013">Children in Need Rocks 2013</a> concert in London; she sang a duet with <a href="/wiki/Gary_Barlow" title="Gary Barlow">Gary Barlow</a>, the organiser of the event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On 18 November 2013 in order to promote the song, a video for "I Should've Followed You Home" was shot the same day as the release of the single itself. Although the single was released in most countries of the world in November 2013, its release in the United Kingdom was held over.</p>
<p>She also won Best Female Album – 6th Annual Scandipop 2014, and was nominated for the German 2014 ECHO award in the Artist Rock/Pop International category.</p>
