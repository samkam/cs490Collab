<p><br/>
</p><p><i><b>The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe</b></i>, often shortened to <b>LWW</b>, was written by <a href="/wiki/C._S._Lewis" title="C. S. Lewis">C. S. Lewis</a> and published in 1950. It records the adventure of four ordinary English children - <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pevensie" title="Peter Pevensie">Peter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Susan_Pevensie" title="Susan Pevensie">Susan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Pevensie" title="Edmund Pevensie">Edmund</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Pevensie" title="Lucy Pevensie">Lucy</a> Pevensie - who found their way into the magical land of <a href="/wiki/Narnia" title="Narnia">Narnia</a> by way of a <a href="/wiki/Wardrobe" title="Wardrobe">wardrobe</a> that they stumbled across in an old house.
</p><p><i>The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe</i> was the first of the Chronicles of Narnia to be written and published, but it comes second in the chronological history of Narnia.
</p><p><br/>
</p>

</script><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="About_Narnia">About Narnia</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Time">Time </span></h3>
<p>The events of <i>The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe</i> occured between 1000 and 1015 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narnian-years" title="Narnian-years">NY</a>, and in 1940 AD on <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>. After their reign of more than a decade in Narnia, the children returned to Earth at the exact moment they left (see <a href="/wiki/Narnian_Time" title="Narnian Time">Narnian Time</a> for more information).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atmosphere"> Atmosphere </span></h3>
<p>In Narnia, the atmosphere felt different than Earth's- as if the air was sweeter - and it stirred in the children--and all other visitors from our world--feelings of bravery and maturity.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events_Summary"> Events Summary </span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Inside_the_Wardrobe">Inside the Wardrobe </span></h3>
<p>Because of the dangers of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> , the four Pevensie children were sent away from London to the home of Professor <a href="/wiki/Digory_Kirke" title="Digory Kirke">Digory Kirke</a>, who lived in a large old house with many rooms useful for hiding and exploration.
</p><p>While exploring the house with her siblings, Lucy decided to investigate an old <a href="/wiki/Wardrobe" title="Wardrobe">wardrobe</a> she found in one of the remote rooms. Upon entering the wardrobe, Lucy found herself walking through trees, rather than the expected coats. She pressed on and found herself standing in the middle of a snowy wood next to a <a href="/wiki/Lamp-post" title="Lamp-post">lamp-post</a>, where she met <a href="/wiki/Tumnus" title="Tumnus">Tumnus</a> the <a href="/wiki/Faun" title="Faun">Faun</a>, who invited her to his home for tea. He later told her that he had intended to hand her over to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_Witch" title="White Witch">White Witch</a>, the usurping Queen of Narnia. The faun, feeling guilty for what he had planned to do, helped Lucy find her way back to the wardrobe, despite the risk that the White Witch would find him out and punish him.
</p><p>Later, Lucy tried to tell her siblings about the strange land she had found, but they did not believe her. During a game of hide-and-seek, Lucy returned to the country in the wardrobe, and Edmund followed her so he could go on teasing her about her "imaginary" country. Although he denied it when the older children asked him, soon all four of them had gone into the wardrobe and seen Narnia. Susan, the most sensible of them, wanted to return home, but Lucy convinced Peter that they needed to help her new friend, Mr. Tumnus, who was arrested.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Prophecy"><a href="/wiki/Golden_Age_Prophecy" title="Golden Age Prophecy">The Prophecy</a> </span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source: The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe (book)">"<i>...down at Cair Paravel there are four thrones and it's a saying in Narnia time out of mind that when two Sons of Adam and two Daughters of Eve sit on those four thrones, then it will be the end only of the White Witch's reign but of her life...</i>"</span>
</dd><dd>―<a href="/wiki/Mr._Beaver" title="Mr. Beaver">Mr. Beaver</a><sup class="noprint"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Lion,_the_Witch,_and_the_Wardrobe_(book)" title="The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe (book)">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl></div>

<p>The four children were soon found by <a href="/wiki/Mr._Beaver" title="Mr. Beaver">Mr. Beaver</a>, who took them home, introduced them to his wife Mrs. Beaver, and they had dinner. He told them of Aslan the Great Lion, and a Prophecy that said when two Sons of Adam and two Daughters of Eve sat upon the four thrones at Cair Paravel, all Narnia would be put to rights.
</p><p>While they were talking they found out that Edmund had slipped away and they decided to flee, after realizing that he would tell where they were to the White Witch.
</p><p>On their journey, they met Father Christmas, who gave Lucy a cordial made from the juice of fireflowers that would heal others with a single drop, and a dagger, and gave Susan bow and arrows, and a horn which could bring help whenever it was blown; and Peter a sword and a shield.
</p>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source: The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe (book)">"<i>Come out, Mrs Beaver. Come out, Sons and Daughters of Adam. It's all right! It isn't </i>Her<i>!</i>"</span>
</dd><dd>―<a href="/wiki/Mr._Beaver" title="Mr. Beaver">Mr. Beaver</a><sup class="noprint"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Lion,_the_Witch,_and_the_Wardrobe_(book)" title="The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe (book)">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl></div>

<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source: The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe (book)">"<i>This was bad grammar of course, but that is how beavers talk when they are excited; I mean in Narnia - in our world they usually don't talk at all</i>"</span>
</dd><dd>―C.S Lewis<sup class="noprint"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Lion,_the_Witch,_and_the_Wardrobe_(book)" title="The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe (book)">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl></div>

<p>Later, after they arrived at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aslan%27s_Camp" title="Aslan's Camp">Aslan's Camp</a>, while Susan and Lucy were having fun near the woods, two <a href="/wiki/Wolf" title="Wolf">wolves</a> attacked them. Susan called for help with her horn and Peter and Aslan came to their rescue. Peter killed <a href="/wiki/Maugrim" title="Maugrim">Maugrim</a>, one of the wolves, in his first fight, and Aslan let the other wolf go, followed by centaurs and eagles from Aslan's army. That wolf led them right to Edmund, whom they rescued.
</p><p>Later, the Witch requested an audience with Aslan. There, she claimed that since Edmund was a traitor, according to the Deep Magic, his blood belonged to her. Eventually, she and Aslan went inside the tent, where they reached an agreement that she would kill him later that night, but renounce her claim on Edmund. Aslan eventually went, though without telling anyone. However Lucy and Susan saw him leaving, and followed. There, they saw Aslan being killed, after which the Witch's army left.
</p><p>In the morning, just as they were about to leave, they heard the Stone Table crack. When they turned around to look, they saw Aslan standing before them, who revealed to them the Deeper Magic from before the Dawn of Time stated that when "a willing victim is killed in a traitor's stead, the Table would crack and even death would start working backwards."
</p><p>Afterwards, they went to the Witch's castle, and revived all the statues. Lucy found Mr. Tumnus, begged Aslan to revive him, and soon afterward she and the revived Faun were dancing happily. They then joined the battle between Aslan's army, and the Witch's, which had started earlier that morning.
</p><p>When it was all over, after Aslan had killed the Witch, the children were crowned High King Peter the Magnificent, Queen Susan the Gentle, King Edmund the Just, and Queen Lucy the Valiant.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Back_to_London">Back to London</span></h3>
<p>The four of them ruled over Narnia for many years, and grew into adults. The land prospered under them, and the time of their rule became known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Age" title="Golden Age">Golden Age</a> of Narnia. But one day, as they were hunting a <a href="/wiki/White_Stag" title="White Stag">White Stag</a> in the woods, they came across a lamppost, and soon they were tumbling back out of the wardrobe in the back room of the Professor's house.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_Scenes">Behind the Scenes</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dedication">Dedication</span></h3>
<p>The book is dedicated to C.S. Lewis's goddaughter, <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Barfield" title="Lucy Barfield">Lucy Barfield</a>, a remarkable young girl who Lewis both based and named the fictional Lucy after.
</p><p>In May 1949, Lewis sent her the completed manuscript of <i>The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe</i>, with a letter.
</p><p>In October 1950, when the book was published, this letter became its dedication: -
</p>
<dl><dd><i>MY DEAR LUCY,</i>
</dd></dl>
<dl><dd><i>I wrote this story for you, but when I began it I had not realized that girls grow quicker than books. As a result you are already too old for fairy tales, and by the time it is printed and bound you will be older still. But some day you will be old enough to start reading fairy tales again. You can then take it down from some upper shelf, dust it, and tell me what you think of it. I shall probably be too deaf to hear, and too old to understand, a word you say, but I shall still be</i>
</dd></dl>
<dl><dd><dl><dd><dl><dd><dl><dd><dl><dd><i>your affectionate Godfather,</i>
</dd></dl>
</dd></dl>
</dd></dl>
</dd></dl>
