<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Alice in Wonderland (song)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><i><b>Alice in Wonderland</b></i> is the theme song composed by <a href="/wiki/Sammy_Fain" title="Sammy Fain">Sammy Fain</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney" title="Walt Disney">Walt Disney</a> 1951 animated film <i><a href="/wiki/Alice_in_Wonderland_(1951_film)" title="Alice in Wonderland (1951 film)">Alice in Wonderland</a></i>. The lyrics were written by <a href="/wiki/Bob_Hilliard" title="Bob Hilliard">Bob Hilliard</a> and it was arranged by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Simeone" title="Harry Simeone">Harry Simeone</a> for treble voices and performed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Jud_Conlon_Chorus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Jud Conlon Chorus (page does not exist)">The Jud Conlon Chorus</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Mellomen" title="The Mellomen">The Mellomen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Office1952_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Office1952-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Muljadi_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Muljadi-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The song plays during the opening and end credits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HischakRobinson2009_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-HischakRobinson2009-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Izumi_Yukimura" title="Izumi Yukimura">Izumi Yukimura</a> sang her own theme song for the Japanese release of the film. The "dreamy"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HischakRobinson2009_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-HischakRobinson2009-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> song has become a <a href="/wiki/Jazz_standard" title="Jazz standard">jazz standard</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RawlinsBahha2005_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-RawlinsBahha2005-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> that has been performed by <a href="/wiki/Bill_Evans" title="Bill Evans">Bill Evans</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ake2002_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ake2002-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Peterson" title="Oscar Peterson">Oscar Peterson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Brubeck" title="Dave Brubeck">Dave Brubeck</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BogdanovWoodstra2002_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-BogdanovWoodstra2002-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> and others. In his book <i>The History of Jazz</i>, <a href="/wiki/Ted_Gioia" title="Ted Gioia">Ted Gioia</a> cites "Alice in Wonderland" as one of Evans's most beautiful performances, likening its "pristine beauty" to his "<a href="/wiki/Waltz_for_Debby_(song)" title="Waltz for Debby (song)">Waltz for Debby</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gioia2011_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gioia2011-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Evans recorded it at the <a href="/wiki/Village_Vanguard" title="Village Vanguard">Village Vanguard</a> which featured on his 1961 album <i><a href="/wiki/Sunday_at_the_Village_Vanguard" title="Sunday at the Village Vanguard">Sunday at the Village Vanguard</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Levine2011_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Levine2011-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rosemary_Clooney" title="Rosemary Clooney">Rosemary Clooney</a> recorded the ballad with "The Unbirthday Song" which also appeared on the soundtrack to the movie, and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Feinstein" title="Michael Feinstein">Michael Feinstein</a> has also recorded it along with the other songs from the movie in a medley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HischakRobinson2009_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-HischakRobinson2009-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The original recording for the film was in the key of <a href="/wiki/G_major" title="G major">G major</a>, but the jazz standard is usually played in <a href="/wiki/C_major" title="C major">C major</a> as it was by both Evans and Peterson.</p>
