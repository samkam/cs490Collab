<p><i><b>The Avengers</b></i> (also known as <i><b>Avengers Assemble</b></i> in the UK and Ireland) is a 2012 superhero film and the sixth film in the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Cinematic_Universe" title="Marvel Cinematic Universe">Marvel Cinematic Universe</a>, based on the superhero team the <a href="/wiki/The_Avengers_(team)" title="The Avengers (team)">Avengers</a> created by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby. The film is a crossover of all the films independently produced by <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Studios" title="Marvel Studios">Marvel Studios</a> set within the Marvel Cinematic Universe, namely <i><a href="/wiki/Iron_Man_(film)" title="Iron Man (film)">Iron Man</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Incredible_Hulk_(film)" title="The Incredible Hulk (film)">The Incredible Hulk</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/Iron_Man_2" title="Iron Man 2">Iron Man 2</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Thor_(film)" title="Thor (film)">Thor</a></i> (2011) and <i><a href="/wiki/Captain_America:_The_First_Avenger" title="Captain America: The First Avenger">Captain America: The First Avenger</a></i> (2011). In the film, <a href="/wiki/S.H.I.E.L.D." title="S.H.I.E.L.D.">S.H.I.E.L.D.</a> director <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nick_Fury_(Marvel_Cinematic_Universe)" title="Nick Fury (Marvel Cinematic Universe)">Nick Fury</a> assembles <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Stark" title="Anthony Stark">Iron Man</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steve_Rogers" title="Steve Rogers">Captain America</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hulk_(Marvel_Cinematic_Universe)" title="Hulk (Marvel Cinematic Universe)">Hulk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thor_Odinson" title="Thor Odinson">Thor</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natasha_Romanoff" title="Natasha Romanoff">Black Widow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clint_Barton" title="Clint Barton">Hawkeye</a> to battle Thor's adoptive brother <a href="/wiki/Loki_Laufeyson" title="Loki Laufeyson">Loki</a>, who attempts to subjugate humanity by leading an invasion by the extraterrestrial race known as the <a href="/wiki/Chitauri" title="Chitauri">Chitauri</a>.
</p><p><i>The Avengers</i> is the first major crossover in the Marvel Cinematic Universe and the first to be released by <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Pictures" title="Walt Disney Pictures">Walt Disney Pictures</a>; the previous films were released by <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a> (apart from <i>The Incredible Hulk</i> which was distributed by Universal Studios) and as part of the deal of transfering the distribution rights from Paramount to Disney, the Paramount logo appears on the film, its promotion and its merchandise, as well as Paramount earning 8% of the films income. The Walt Disney Company is credited at the end of the film, however.
</p><p><i>The Avengers</i> was directed by <a href="/wiki/Joss_Whedon" title="Joss Whedon">Joss Whedon</a> and stars <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Downey,_Jr." title="Robert Downey, Jr.">Robert Downey, Jr.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Evans" title="Chris Evans">Chris Evans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Ruffalo" title="Mark Ruffalo">Mark Ruffalo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Hemsworth" title="Chris Hemsworth">Chris Hemsworth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scarlett_Johansson" title="Scarlett Johansson">Scarlett Johansson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Renner" title="Jeremy Renner">Jeremy Renner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hiddleston" title="Tom Hiddleston">Tom Hiddleston</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samuel_L._Jackson" title="Samuel L. Jackson">Samuel L. Jackson</a>. The film was successful financially and critically and broke box-office records as the biggest weekend opening for a film and the fastest film to gross $1 billion worldwide. The film is statistically the most successful film released by Walt Disney to date and it regarded by many as one of the greatest superhero films of all time. The film has been nominated for the Academy Award for Best Visual Effects.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nick_Fury" title="Nick Fury">Nick Fury</a>, the director of the espionage agency known as <a href="/wiki/S.H.I.E.L.D." title="S.H.I.E.L.D.">S.H.I.E.L.D.</a> arrives at <a href="/wiki/Project_P.E.G.A.S.U.S." title="Project P.E.G.A.S.U.S.">Project P.E.G.A.S.U.S.</a>, a remote research facility, during an evacuation. His second-in-command, <a href="/wiki/Maria_Hill" title="Maria Hill">Maria Hill</a>, explains that the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosmic_Cube" title="Cosmic Cube">Tesseract</a>, an self-sustaining energy source of unknown potential, has activated and opened a portal through space, from which the exiled Asgardian prince <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loki" title="Loki">Loki</a>, steps through. Loki takes the Tesseract, and uses a sceptre to control the minds of several S.H.I.E.L.D. personnel including Agent <a href="/wiki/Clint_Barton" title="Clint Barton">Clint Barton</a>, and physicist consultant <a href="/wiki/Erik_Selvig" title="Erik Selvig">Dr. Erik Selvig</a> in order to aid in his getaway.
</p><p>In response, Fury reactivates the <a href="/wiki/Avengers_Initiative" title="Avengers Initiative">Avengers Initiative</a>. Agent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natasha_Romanoff" title="Natasha Romanoff">Natasha Romanoff</a> recruits Dr. <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Banner" title="Bruce Banner">Bruce Banner</a> in India, while Agent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phil_Coulson" title="Phil Coulson">Phil Coulson</a> visits <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Stark" title="Anthony Stark">Tony Stark</a> and requests that he reviews Selvig's research. Fury himself approaches <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steve_Rogers" title="Steve Rogers">Steve Rogers</a> with an assignment to retrieve the Tesseract from Loki; Rogers is familiar with the Tesseract as it played a vital role in his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Captain_America:The_First_Avenger" title="Captain America:The First Avenger">World War II exploits</a>.
</p><p>Romanoff, Coulson, Rogers, and Banner meet on the <a href="/wiki/S.H.I.E.L.D._Helicarrier" title="S.H.I.E.L.D. Helicarrier">S.H.I.E.L.D. Helicarrier</a> before it goes airborne. Dr. Banner is escorted to his lab after a brief meeting with Fury; Banner has been consulted to trace the gamma signature that the Tesseract radiates. After spending some time tracking the whereabouts of the Tesseract on the Helicarrier, a man of Loki's description is located in Europe.
</p><p>Iron Man, Captain America, and Romanoff travel to Stuttgart, Germany to apprehend Loki, who, with the aid of a mind-controlled Barton, is stealing iridium needed to stabilize the Tesseract and demanding worship. After a battle with the heroes, Loki surrenders and is being returned to the Helicarrier in the Quinjet. <a href="/wiki/Thor_Odinson" title="Thor Odinson">Thor</a>, Loki's adoptive brother and the Asgardian god of thunder, attempts to free Loki and reason with him. After a confrontation with Iron Man and Captain America, Thor accompanies them to imprison Loki on the Helicarrier.
</p><p>After Loki is put into the cell originally intended for the <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Banner" title="Bruce Banner">Hulk</a>, Thor reveals to the team that Loki is allied with the <a href="/wiki/Chitauri" title="Chitauri">Chitauri</a>, a powerful and technologically-advanced extraterrestrial race unknown to both Earth and Asgard. In return for winning him the Earth, Loki has pomised them the Tesseract. Stark has <a href="/wiki/J.A.R.V.I.S." title="J.A.R.V.I.S.">J.A.R.V.I.S.</a> hack into the S.H.I.E.L.D. database while he works with Dr. Banner. Rogers is less than thrilled at Stark's behavior and tells the two scientists to resume their work and investigates Phase Two, S.H.I.E.L.D.'s top secret weapons program. Romanoff speaks one-on-one with Loki, and eventually discovers that he plans on using the Hulk as a means of dismembering the team.
</p><p>After all these things come to light, the Avengers are divided over how to deal with Loki and the revelation that S.H.I.E.L.D. plans to harness the Tesseract’s power to develop weapons. Fury admits that the events in <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a> a year prior made S.H.I.E.L.D. aware of other extraterrestrial races that may threaten Earth. Weapons developed from the Tesseract would form a means of deterrence. As the group argues, Barton and Loki’s possessed agents attack the Helicarrier, disabling its engines. As Iron Man and Captain America attempt to restart the damaged engines, Banner transforms into the Hulk, despite Romanoff's efforts to calm him, and runs amok inside the ship, soon battling Thor. 
</p><p>During a fight with Barton, Romanoff discovers that knocking him unconscious breaks Loki's mind control. Loki escapes his cell with the help of a possessed agent and traps Thor in the cell. Coulson confronts Loki in an attempt to rescue Thor, and Loki then stabs Coulson through his back with his staff before ejecting Thor from the ship. Fury confronts the expiring Coulson, who hopes his death can be used to motivate the Avengers into working as a team.
</p><p>Stark and Rogers work out that Loki will orchestrate the alien invasion at Stark's New York-based home, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stark_Tower" title="Stark Tower">Stark Tower</a>, and that defeating them will not be enough for Loki; he needs to overpower them in a public way so as to validate himself as ruler of Earth. Using a Tesseract-powered interdimensional generator Selvig has built, Loki opens a portal to the Chitauri fleet over Manhattan, summoning an invasion. The Avengers rally in defense of New York, but quickly conclude they will be overwhelmed as wave after wave of Chitauri and <a href="/wiki/Leviathan" title="Leviathan">Leviathans</a> descend. The Avengers keep their ground to hold off the invasion and evacuate civilians while the Hulk beats Loki into submission. Romanoff makes her way to Selvig's device, where Selvig, freed of Loki's control, reveals that Loki's staff can be used to close the portal.
</p><p>Meanwhile, Fury's superiors attempt to end the invasion by launching a nuclear missile at Manhattan. Despite Agent Hill and Fury's orders to not fire, a rogue jet launches the missile at Manhattan. Iron Man intercepts it and takes it through the portal toward the Chitauri fleet before running out of power and plummeting back to Earth. The Hulk catches him as he falls. The Avengers then apprehend Loki, who is still weak from his encounter with the Hulk. News channels confirm the legitimacy of the extraterrestrial attack and some Americans regard the Avengers with praise and thanks while others call for their arrests. Thor escorts Loki and the Tesseract back to Asgard to take responsibility for his crimes. Fury notes that the Avengers will go their separate ways until such time as a new world-threatening menace emerges.
</p><p>In the first post-credits scene, <a href="/wiki/The_Other" title="The Other">The Other</a> tells his master that humans are not the "cowering wretches" they were promised and that attacking Earth again "would be to court Death". His master, <a href="/wiki/Thanos" title="Thanos">Thanos</a> turns and smiles.
</p><p>In a second post-credits scene, the Avengers — gathered at a shawarma restaurant and looking a bit worse for the wear — eat in silence.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Title">Title</span></h2>
<p>The film's official title internationally is <i>Marvel's The Avengers</i>. In the United Kingdom and the Republic of Ireland, Walt Disney Pictures made the decision to reclassify the film as <i>Avengers Assemble</i> to avoid confusion with the popular British 1960s spy television series of the same title. "Avengers Assemble" is a battle cry used in the comic books for decades, first spoken by Thor. The British Board of Film Classification (BBFC) officially classifies the film as <i>Marvel Avengers Assemble </i>while the website and DVD release refer to the film as <i>Marvel's Avengers Assemble</i>. Products in the United Kingdom related to the film, such as action figures and comic book tie-ins, remain titled <i>The Avengers</i>. On the British title change, Kevin Feige assured that the decision wasn't made lightly and was done for legal reasons, also joking that the extra word "<i>Assemble</i>" was added as something special for the film's U.K. fans.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span></h2>
<p><i>The Avengers</i> set the all-time industry record at the box office, grossing $207.4 million in America and $641.8 million globally, twelve days after its release. It opened internationally on April 25, 2012 and by May 8, 2012, it had already made over $700 million globally. It was also the fastest movie to make $200 million domestically, after only three days. It opened May 3, in Russia, with $17.9 million, made $17.4 million in China, and The Avengers was the Biggest opening weekend of all time in Mexico, Brazil, Argentina, Ecuador, Peru, Central America, Bolivia, Hong Kong, Malaysia, New Zealand, and Philippines. The film took Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest's record as the highest-grossing Disney film of all time. It also beat Spider-Man 3's record as the highest-grossing Marvel film and beat The Dark Knight's record as the highest-grossing superhero film of all time. The film recived high critical acclaim. It scored a 93% approval rating on Rotton Tomatos. The film was well received for its writing, acting, action, visuals, and music. The film is considered one of the best superhero films ever.
</p>
