class="entry-content clearfix"><p><a href="http://www.ravenoak.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/watershipdown.jpg?66bae5"><img alt="Watership Down" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-939" height="300" pagespeed_url_hash="2062540153" src="http://www.ravenoak.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/208x300xwatershipdown-208x300.jpg,q66bae5.pagespeed.ic.vqssGcC-Us.jpg" width="208"/></a><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0743277708/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0743277708&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ravoak-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0743277708/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0743277708&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ravoak-20', 'Watership Down']);">Watership Down</a><img alt="" border="0" class="xdznrqpxyzexieagwiyk xoytztwfwdulfcmfvspx" height="1" pagespeed_url_hash="1346716161" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=ravoak-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0743277708" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/> by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Adams" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Adams', 'Richard Adams']);" target="_blank" title="Richard Adams">Richard Adams</a> was the first adult novel I read as a child where the main character wasn’t a human. When much younger, I’d enjoyed children’s chapter books such as <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B009FNBQS8/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B009FNBQS8&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ravoak-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B009FNBQS8/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B009FNBQS8&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ravoak-20', 'Bunnicula']);">Bunnicula</a><img alt="" border="0" class="xdznrqpxyzexieagwiyk xoytztwfwdulfcmfvspx" height="1" pagespeed_url_hash="537777977" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=ravoak-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B009FNBQS8" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/> by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Howe" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Howe', 'James &amp; Deborah Howe']);" target="_blank" title="James Howe">James &amp; Deborah Howe</a> and <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0064400557/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0064400557&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ravoak-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0064400557/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0064400557&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ravoak-20', 'Charlotte’s Web']);">Charlotte’s Web</a><img alt="" border="0" class="xdznrqpxyzexieagwiyk xoytztwfwdulfcmfvspx" height="1" pagespeed_url_hash="3212996215" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=ravoak-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0064400557" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/> by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E.B._White" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E.B._White', 'E. B. White']);" target="_blank" title="E.B. White">E. B. White</a>, but <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0743277708/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0743277708&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ravoak-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0743277708/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0743277708&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ravoak-20', 'Watership Down']);">Watership Down</a> was my first adventure of real length involving animals.</p><p>My copy of it, much like the photo to the left, was a used paperback, yellowed with age and bearing some water damage in the bottom-right corner. The spine was partially broken, but I didn’t care. The book cost me a quarter at my neighborhood <a href="http://www.hpb.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.hpb.com/', 'Half-Price Books']);" target="_blank" title="Half-Price Books">Half-Price Books</a>.</p><p>I’ve always loved animals. For a time, I’d played with the idea of being a veterinarian and a writer. In middle school, my father bought me a microscope kit that came complete with slides, blades to take cross-sections of things, petri dishes, etc. He told me that if I wanted to be a vet, I needed to understand how animals work. I was the child who brought home baby birds that had fallen out of the nest or critters with broken wings / limbs. Being broke meant that the vet was for “rich folk,” so I tried to nurse them back to health.</p><p><a href="http://www.ravenoak.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Great_Grey_Slug.jpg?66bae5"><img alt="Great Grey Slug" class="alignleft wp-image-940" height="150" pagespeed_url_hash="1119780737" src="http://www.ravenoak.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/199x150xGreat_Grey_Slug-300x225.jpg,q66bae5.pagespeed.ic.8BPij0FgHu.jpg" width="199"/></a>When I inevitably failed and the animal died, I cried for weeks. I mourned the loss of life as if they were family. Imagine my pain when I realized that cutting up a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slug" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slug', 'slug']);" target="_blank" title="slug">slug</a> to view it under a microscope meant the creature died. (<em>Somehow I thought that like an iguana’s tail or some worms, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slug" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slug', 'slug']);" target="_blank" title="slug">slug</a> would just split in two and keep living.</em>)</p><p>To cheer me up, my father demonstrated <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EhMq_NUwbUY" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EhMq_NUwbUY', 'what happens when one pours salt on a slug']);" target="_blank" title="Salt on a Slug">what happens when one pours salt on a slug</a>. (<em>He never did understand my sensitivity towards animals, though in his defense, he thought more experiments would ease the pain.</em>) Several weeks later, a good friend lost her dog to cancer. My hamster escaped and starved to death in my bedroom (<em>we found him weeks later</em>). I couldn’t be a vet. Not if it meant being so powerless to stop the suffering of others.</p><p>I loved reading <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0743277708/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0743277708&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ravoak-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0743277708/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0743277708&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ravoak-20', 'Watership Down']);">Watership Down</a> until I reached the ending. When I realized what El-ahrairah was a symbol for, I mourned the loss of my pet all over again. It was certainly not the first time I’d been exposed to death in a children’s book (<em>cough, cough, <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0064400557/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0064400557&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ravoak-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0064400557/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0064400557&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ravoak-20', 'Charlotte’s Web']);">Charlotte’s Web</a></em>), but it meant that the grand new world of sci-fi and fantasy I’d found, held similar topics. It meant that <strong>real life was intruding on my escapism</strong>.</p><p>It was a harsh lesson to learn, but I feel lit gave me my sense of compassion. To this day I go out of my way to stop in the middle of traffic and rescue the turtle trying to cross the street. The high value I place on life is something I try in impart in my novels as well. When I kill a character, it’s for a reason. It serves a purpose.</p><p>We (<em>fans of sci-fi/fantasy</em>) don’t like to admit it, but the characters who have died in books and film have usually served their purpose as well. That is, if the author is doing it right. <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" pagespeed_url_hash="1725369152" src="http://www.ravenoak.net/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif?66bae5"/> <em>They’re
