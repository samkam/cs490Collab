<p><b>Anni-Frid Synni Princess Reuss of Plauen</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Anni-Frid Synni Prinzessin Reuss von Plauen</i></span>; born <b>Anni-Frid Synni Lyngstad</b>, <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Swedish" title="Help:IPA for Swedish">[ˈlʏŋstɑːd]</a></span>, 15 November 1945), widely known as <b>Frida Lyngstad</b> or by just the mononym <b>Frida</b>, is a Norwegian-born Swedish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pop_(music)" title="Pop (music)">pop</a> and jazz singer. Born in Norway to a Norwegian mother and a German father, she grew up in Sweden and was a member of the Swedish <a href="/wiki/Musical_ensemble" title="Musical ensemble">group</a> <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-r1-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> between 1972 and 1982. After the break-up of ABBA, she continued an international solo singing career with mixed success. In 1997, Frida recorded her final album before 'retiring' from music. She now focuses on environmental issues and intends never to return to the music business.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1963, she married 17-year-old Ragnar Fredriksson. Immediately after their divorce in 1970, Lyngstad courted eventual ABBA band member <a href="/wiki/Benny_Andersson" title="Benny Andersson">Benny Andersson</a>, co-habiting together until they officially married in 1978. The couple divorced in 1981. In 1992, Lyngstad married <a href="/wiki/Prince_Heinrich_Ruzzo_Reuss_of_Plauen" title="Prince Heinrich Ruzzo Reuss of Plauen">Heinrich Ruzzo Prince Reuss of Plauen</a>, who was a <a href="/wiki/German_Prince" title="German Prince">German Prince</a> of the former sovereign <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Reuss" title="House of Reuss">House of Reuss</a>. The prince died of <a href="/wiki/Lymphoma" title="Lymphoma">lymphoma</a> in October 1999. Lyngstad currently lives in <a href="/wiki/Zermatt" title="Zermatt">Zermatt</a>, Switzerland, sharing a home with her <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">British</a> boyfriend, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Smith,_5th_Viscount_Hambleden" title="Henry Smith, 5th Viscount Hambleden">Henry Smith, 5th Viscount Hambleden</a>, since 2008.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anni-Frid_Lyngstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Anni-Frid Synni "Frida" Lyngstad was born in Bjørkåsen, a small village in <a href="/wiki/Ballangen" title="Ballangen">Ballangen</a> near <a href="/wiki/Narvik" title="Narvik">Narvik</a>, in northern Norway, to a Norwegian mother, Synni Lyngstad (19 June 1926 – 28 September 1947), and a German father, Nazi officer <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Haase_(officer)" title="Alfred Haase (officer)">Alfred Haase</a> (9 September 1898 – 24 February 2000), just after the end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Occupation_of_Norway_by_Nazi_Germany" title="Occupation of Norway by Nazi Germany">German occupation of Norway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Lyngstad's father returned to Germany when his troops were evacuated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In early 1947, Lyngstad, her mother, and her maternal grandmother, Arntine Lyngstad ("Agny"), left her birthplace, fearing reprisals against those who had dealings with the Germans during the occupation. This could entail not just insults and threats, but also forced separation of infants from their parents and relatives (see <a href="/wiki/War_children" title="War children">War children</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Lyngstad was taken by her grandmother to Sweden, where they settled in the region of <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A4rjedalen" title="Härjedalen">Härjedalen</a> and her grandmother took any available job. Her mother remained in Norway and worked for a period in the south of the country. She soon joined her mother and daughter in Sweden, and the three moved to <a href="/wiki/Malmk%C3%B6ping" title="Malmköping">Malmköping</a> (72 km from Stockholm). Her mother soon died of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kidney_failure" title="Kidney failure">kidney failure</a>, aged 21. Lyngstad was raised solely by her grandmother. In June 1949, they both relocated to <a href="/wiki/Torsh%C3%A4lla" title="Torshälla">Torshälla</a> (just outside of <a href="/wiki/Eskilstuna" title="Eskilstuna">Eskilstuna</a>), where Agny Lyngstad worked as a seamstress. Frida Lyngstad grew up in Torshälla and began attending school there in August 1952. Close contact with her family in Norway (notably her uncle and four aunts) continued, and Lyngstad recalls summer holidays spent with them at her birthplace. She was especially close to her Aunt Olive, who once stated that she saw how lonely and subdued Frida was and, as a result, always did her best to make her feel loved and welcomed during visits.</p>
<p>Lyngstad believed that her father, Alfred Haase, had died during the war on his way back to Germany as his ship was reported to have sunk. However, in 1977, the <a href="/wiki/Bravo_(magazine)" title="Bravo (magazine)">German teen magazine <i>Bravo</i></a> published a poster and a complete biography with details of Lyngstad's background, including the names of her father and mother. It was seen by Lyngstad's half-brother, Peter Haase, who went to his father and asked him if he had been in Ballangen during the war. A few months later, Lyngstad met Haase in Stockholm for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-r2-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anni-Frid_Lyngstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1958.E2.80.931969:_Early_work">1958–1969: Early work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anni-Frid_Lyngstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1958–1969: Early work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lyngstad stated in several interviews that her grandmother frequently sang songs to her (notably old Norwegian songs), which resulted in her love for music. She soon showed musical talent at a very early age, beginning in her first years at school. On Fridays, she was often asked by her teacher to sing in front of the class and soon became known in school and in the neighbourhood for her beautiful voice. Although her grandmother encouraged her to sing (according to Lyngstad herself), she never attended any of her performances. Her grandmother died shortly before ABBA formed and therefore never experienced the success of the group.</p>
<p>At the age of 13, she got her first job as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dance_band_(disambiguation)" title="Dance band (disambiguation)">dance band</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schlager" title="Schlager">schlager</a> singer in 1958, with the Evald Eks Orchestra. Evald Ek himself remembers: "It was hard to believe, such a young person could sing that well. She was so easy to rehearse with and she was never shy onstage. The only thing I taught her was to sing out. In those days, she had a tendency of holding back her voice a little." With the Evald Eks Orchestra, the 13-year-old Lyngstad, performed every weekend in front of a dancing audience. The sets often lasted up to five hours. The songs she liked most to sing were the evergreens; <i>"All of Me"</i>, <i>"Night and Day"</i> and <i>"Begin the Beguine"</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> To advance and develop, she also started to take singing lessons. Later, she teamed up with a 15-piece 'big band', who performed a <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> repertoire covering <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Miller" title="Glenn Miller">Glenn Miller</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duke_Ellington" title="Duke Ellington">Duke Ellington</a> and <a href="/wiki/Count_Basie" title="Count Basie">Count Basie</a>; her vocal idols being <a href="/wiki/Ella_Fitzgerald" title="Ella Fitzgerald">Ella Fitzgerald</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peggy_Lee" title="Peggy Lee">Peggy Lee</a>. In 1963, she formed her own band, the Anni-Frid Four.</p>
<p>On 3 September 1967, Frida won the Swedish national talent competition, "New Faces", arranged by record company <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a> and held at <a href="/wiki/Skansen" title="Skansen">Skansen</a>, Stockholm. The song she chose to sing was "En Ledig Dag" ("A Day Off"). The first prize in this contest was a recording contract with <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a> Sweden. Unbeknownst to Lyngstad, the winner of the contest was also expected to appear the same evening in the country's most popular TV show at that time, <i>Hylands Hörna</i>. This happened on the same day <a href="/wiki/Dagen_H" title="Dagen H">Sweden switched</a> from driving on the left side of the road to the right side. Driving on that day was discouraged, so most of the nation was watching TV that night. Frida performed her winning song live. (The performance can be seen on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frida_-_The_DVD" title="Frida - The DVD">Frida - The DVD</a></i>)</p>
<p>This first exposure to a wider television audience caused a sensation, and many record companies and producers contacted Frida immediately. <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a> executives, fearing they might lose their new singer, took the precaution of driving from Stockholm to Frida's home in Eskilstuna the next morning with a recording contract for her to sign. EMI producer Olle Bergman remembers: "<i>We got so interested and fond of her and I thought she had everything a person needs to become something.</i>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On 11 September 1967, Frida recorded the vocals for "En Ledig Dag", which was to become her first single for EMI Sweden. Professional and self-assured on this first day in the studio, she recorded the vocals in just one take.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> The early songs she recorded for EMI Sweden are included in the EMI compilation <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frida_1967-1972" title="Frida 1967-1972">Frida 1967-1972</a></i>, digitally remastered and released by EMI Sweden in 1997.</p>
<p>On 29 January 1968, she performed this song on national TV, and at this occasion briefly met future <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a> member <a href="/wiki/Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog" title="Agnetha Fältskog">Agnetha Fältskog</a>, who also performed her first single in the same programme. Lyngstad toured Sweden in 1968, and recorded several singles for EMI. She decided to move to <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> to start working full-time as a singer.</p>
<p>In 1969, she participated in <a href="/wiki/Melodifestivalen" title="Melodifestivalen">Melodifestivalen</a> – the Swedish heats for the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a> – with the song "Härlig är vår jord" ("Our Earth Is Wonderful"), and finished fourth.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1970.E2.80.931971:_Pre-ABBA">1970–1971: Pre-ABBA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anni-Frid_Lyngstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1970–1971: Pre-ABBA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Her first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Frida_(album)" title="Frida (album)">Frida</a></i>, produced by her then-fiancé Andersson, was released in 1971. The album received unanimously generous praise from the critics and the press, who especially noted the precision and versatility of Lyngstad's voice. For example Sweden's biggest morning paper <i>Dagens Nyheter</i> (Daily News) wrote: "Professional, sure and certain LP-debut ... low-key but self-assured personality with sprinkles of temperament, humor and tenderness. And she sings in such a way that you understand that she's got something between her ears – she sings, in other words, in a very intelligent way". She now scored her first Swedish No.1 hit with "Min Egen Stad" ("My Own Town"). All four future members of ABBA sang back-up vocals on this song. The album is now included in the <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a> compilation <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frida_1967-1972" title="Frida 1967-1972">Frida 1967-1972</a></i>.</p>
<p>Frida continued to play in cabarets, and tour and regularly perform on TV and radio. Subsequently, her relationship with Andersson, and friendship with <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_Ulvaeus" title="Björn Ulvaeus">Björn Ulvaeus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog" title="Agnetha Fältskog">Agnetha Fältskog</a> led to the formation of ABBA. In 1972, after five years at EMI Sweden, Lyngstad changed record companies and moved to the <a href="/wiki/Polar_Music" title="Polar Music">Polar Music</a> label. She recorded the single "Man vill ju leva lite dessemellan" ("One wants to live a little from time to time"), which became her second No. 1 hit on the Swedish charts.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1972.E2.80.931982:_The_ABBA_years">1972–1982: The ABBA years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anni-Frid_Lyngstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1972–1982: The ABBA years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a></div>
<p>At first, Lyngstad was hesitant to perform with her boyfriend <a href="/wiki/Benny_Andersson" title="Benny Andersson">Benny Andersson</a>, his best friend <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_Ulvaeus" title="Björn Ulvaeus">Björn Ulvaeus</a> and his wife, <a href="/wiki/Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog" title="Agnetha Fältskog">Agnetha Fältskog</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Their first project together was the cabaret act <i>Festfolk</i>, which flopped in the winter of 1970–1971. The following year, 'Frida' toured on her own while the other three future ABBA members started performing together on a regular basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> Eventually, she rejoined them. Andersson and Ulvaeus were busy producing other artists, but soon discovered the qualities of Lyngstad's and Fältskog's voices combined: ABBA came to life.</p>
<p>Frida sang solo parts in the following ABBA songs: "<a href="/wiki/Andante,_Andante" title="Andante, Andante">Andante, Andante</a>", "Cassandra", "<a href="/wiki/Fernando_(song)" title="Fernando (song)">Fernando</a>", "Gonna Sing You My Lovesong", "<a href="/wiki/I_Have_a_Dream_(song)" title="I Have a Dream (song)">I Have a Dream</a>", "<a href="/wiki/I_Let_The_Music_Speak" title="I Let The Music Speak">I Let The Music Speak</a>", "I Wonder (Departure)", "<a href="/wiki/The_King_Has_Lost_His_Crown" title="The King Has Lost His Crown">The King Has Lost His Crown</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Knowing_Me,_Knowing_You" title="Knowing Me, Knowing You">Knowing Me, Knowing You</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Like_an_Angel_Passing_Through_My_Room" title="Like an Angel Passing Through My Room">Like an Angel Passing Through My Room</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Lovers_(Live_a_Little_Longer)" title="Lovers (Live a Little Longer)">Lovers (Live a Little Longer)</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Me_and_Bobby_and_Bobby%27s_Brother" title="Me and Bobby and Bobby's Brother">Me and Bobby and Bobby's Brother</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Me_and_I" title="Me and I">Me and I</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Money,_Money,_Money" title="Money, Money, Money">Money, Money, Money</a>", "<a href="/wiki/The_Name_of_the_Game_(ABBA_song)" title="The Name of the Game (ABBA song)">The Name Of The Game</a>", "<a href="/wiki/One_Man,_One_Woman" title="One Man, One Woman">One Man, One Woman</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Our_Last_Summer" title="Our Last Summer">Our Last Summer</a>", "Put On Your White Sombrero", "<a href="/wiki/Should_I_Laugh_or_Cry" title="Should I Laugh or Cry">Should I Laugh or Cry</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Super_Trouper_(song)" title="Super Trouper (song)">Super Trouper</a>", "Tropical Loveland", "<a href="/wiki/The_Visitors_(song)" title="The Visitors (song)">The Visitors</a>", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Way_Old_Friends_Do" title="The Way Old Friends Do">The Way Old Friends Do</a>" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/When_All_Is_Said_And_Done" title="When All Is Said And Done">When All Is Said And Done</a>", and her voice was of course clearly audible on most other ABBA tracks as well.</p>
<p>Lyngstad clearly enjoyed the spotlight more than the other three members of ABBA. She truly liked to tour and to meet audience members one-to-one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> She took an active part in co-designing the famed ABBA costumes for their tours and TV performances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Since the members of ABBA went their separate ways, Frida has been the only one who openly regrets there has never been a reunion to date.</p>

<p>Her next solo album, in Swedish, was <i><a href="/wiki/Frida_ensam" title="Frida ensam">Frida ensam</a>,</i> (<i>Frida Alone</i>), released in 1975 during the ABBA years, and produced by <a href="/wiki/Benny_Andersson" title="Benny Andersson">Benny Andersson</a>. This album includes her successful Swedish version of "<a href="/wiki/Fernando_(song)" title="Fernando (song)">Fernando</a>", which stayed at the no. 1 spot in the Svensktoppen radio charts for 9 weeks, but was never released as a single. The album was recorded between sessions of the ABBA albums "<a href="/wiki/Waterloo_(album)" title="Waterloo (album)">Waterloo</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/ABBA_(album)" title="ABBA (album)">ABBA</a>". Due to the rising popularity of the group, the album took 18 months to record. It became an enormous commercial and critical success in Sweden, topping the Swedish album charts for six weeks and remaining in the charts for 38. The album was mostly a collection of covers of songs by artists like the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beach_Boys" title="Beach Boys">Beach Boys</a>, <a href="/wiki/10cc" title="10cc">10cc</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>, receiving positive reviews from <i><a href="/wiki/Melody_Maker" title="Melody Maker">Melody Maker</a></i>: "The album portrays Frida as a very strong and emotive singer and shows the true value of the music, that if sung properly and with enough feeling it transcends all language barriers". This album was such a big success, it eventually went platinum.</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1982.E2.80.931984:_International_solo_career">1982–1984: International solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anni-Frid_Lyngstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1982–1984: International solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Frida_Lyngstad_1982b.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1956" data-file-width="1404" height="251" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Frida_Lyngstad_1982b.jpg/180px-Frida_Lyngstad_1982b.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Frida_Lyngstad_1982b.jpg/270px-Frida_Lyngstad_1982b.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Frida_Lyngstad_1982b.jpg/360px-Frida_Lyngstad_1982b.jpg 2x" width="180"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Frida_Lyngstad_1982b.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Lyngstad in Amsterdam, The Netherlands, October 1982</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1982, during ABBA's last year as a working band, Frida recorded and released her first post-Abba solo album. This was also her first solo album in English. The <a href="/wiki/Phil_Collins" title="Phil Collins">Phil Collins</a>-produced album was called <i><a href="/wiki/Something%27s_Going_On" title="Something's Going On">Something's Going On</a></i>, and became a big success for Frida worldwide. A much rockier sound was found on many of the songs and Phil Collins' drum sound contributed a lot, especially on the lead single. The album sold 1.5 million copies<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r2_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-r2-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> and spawned the successful single "<a href="/wiki/I_Know_There%27s_Something_Going_On" title="I Know There's Something Going On">I Know There's Something Going On</a>", which topped the charts in Switzerland, Belgium, Costa Rica and France, where it stayed No 1 for five weeks. The song also reached the top five in Germany, Austria, the Netherlands, Norway and Australia amongst others. In the United States, the single reached #13 in March 1983 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and #9 on <a href="/wiki/Radio_%26_Records" title="Radio &amp; Records">Radio &amp; Records</a>, and was the 20th biggest selling single in the US that year.</p>
<p>The song and its video were heavily promoted and played on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>. The album itself received good reviews, with <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_magazine" title="Billboard magazine">Billboard</a></i> writing: "ABBA's auburn-haired songstress makes a bold solo project a stunning success", while Mark Coleman described the album in the third edition of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone_Album_Guide" title="Rolling Stone Album Guide">Rolling Stone Album Guide</a></i> as "<i>a sharp, rock-oriented, delightfully eclectic album</i>". William Cooper had a similar opinion in <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a>: "Frida escapes the creative limitations of being a member of one of the world's most popular groups on this solid and often riveting album". Swedish Television <a href="/wiki/Sveriges_Television" title="Sveriges Television">SVT</a> documented this historical event by filming the whole recording process. The result became a one-hour TV documentary, including interviews with Frida and Phil, Björn and Benny, as well as all the musicians on the album. Due to the success of this album and its lead single, Frida was voted "Best Female Artist Of The Year" 1982, by the readers of Sweden's biggest evening paper <i><a href="/wiki/Aftonbladet" title="Aftonbladet">Aftonbladet</a></i>, receiving the Swedish Music Award Price <a href="/wiki/Rockbj%C3%B6rnen" title="Rockbjörnen">Rockbjörnen</a>.</p>
<p>In 1983, Lyngstad assisted with <i><a href="/wiki/Abbacadabra" title="Abbacadabra">Abbacadabra</a></i>, and recorded one of the tracks with two different male vocalists in different languages, including Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Balavoine" title="Daniel Balavoine">Daniel Balavoine</a> on the track "Belle" and on the English version "Time" with <a href="/wiki/B._A._Robertson" title="B. A. Robertson">B. A. Robertson</a>. This track was a cover of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arrival_(song)" title="Arrival (song)">Arrival</a>", an instrumental track from the ABBA album of the same name.</p>
<p>Lyngstad's next album was the experimental <i><a href="/wiki/Shine_(Frida_album)" title="Shine (Frida album)">Shine</a></i> (1984). This album was recorded at Studios De La Grande Armée in Paris, France and produced by <a href="/wiki/Steve_Lillywhite" title="Steve Lillywhite">Steve Lillywhite</a>, known for his work with artists like <a href="/wiki/Peter_Gabriel" title="Peter Gabriel">Peter Gabriel</a>, <a href="/wiki/U2" title="U2">U2</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rolling_Stones" title="Rolling Stones">Rolling Stones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Morrissey" title="Morrissey">Morrissey</a> amongst others. The young producer Lillywhite was only 25 when this album was recorded and he gave Frida a very experimental sound and managed to create a relaxed atmosphere in the studio. The album had much less success than hoped, though it reached the Top 20 in many European countries, #6 in Sweden being its highest position.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1985.E2.80.932004:_Later_career">1985–2004: Later career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anni-Frid_Lyngstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1985–2004: Later career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1986 Frida was in the choir for the recording of her former husband Benny Andersson's song "Klinga Mina Klockor". Also in 1987, Lyngstad recorded the single "Så Länge Vi Har Varann" ("As Long As We Have Each Other") with the Swedish pop group Ratata, one of Lyngstad's favourites. She was contacted by singer <a href="/wiki/Mauro_Scocco" title="Mauro Scocco">Mauro Scocco</a>, who mentioned that he had a song suitable for a duet. After hearing it, Lyngstad accepted immediately.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> The song achieved great success in Sweden, and was also recorded in English under the title of "As long as I have you". An English-language video of the song was produced, although an Australian release of this song on Festival Records in January 1998 was eventually shelved.</p>
<p>In 1990, Lyngstad became a member of the committee of the Swedish environmental organization <i>Det Naturliga Steget</i> (<i>The Natural Step</i>). The organization wanted a "famous face" to help them reach the public, and in 1991 she became chairwoman for the organization <i>Artister För Miljö</i> (<i>Artists For The Environment</i>). In 1992, Lyngstad performed live at the <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> Water Festival at the Kings Castle and released the environmental charity single with her cover of <a href="/wiki/Julian_Lennon" title="Julian Lennon">Julian Lennon</a>'s song "Saltwater". All the money from this single went to charity. In 1993, on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Silvia" title="Queen Silvia">Queen Silvia</a>'s 50th birthday, Frida was asked to perform "<a href="/wiki/Dancing_Queen" title="Dancing Queen">Dancing Queen</a>" on stage, as performed by ABBA when the king and queen got married. Frida contacted <a href="/wiki/The_Real_Group" title="The Real Group">The Real Group</a> and together they performed the song at the Stockholm Opera House in front of the king and queen. The Swedish prime minister at the time, Ingvar Carlsson, also present that night, said it was an ingenious step to do "Dancing Queen" a cappella. This performance was filmed by Swedish TV and can be seen in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frida_-_The_DVD" title="Frida - The DVD">Frida - The DVD</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1996, Lyngstad recorded her Swedish language album <i><a href="/wiki/Djupa_andetag" title="Djupa andetag">Djupa andetag</a></i> (<i>Deep Breaths</i>). It was a long-awaited album as 12 years had passed since <i><a href="/wiki/Shine_(Frida_album)" title="Shine (Frida album)">Shine</a></i> was released. The album attracted overall relatively positive reviews and was a success in Sweden where it became #1 on the album chart. Frida did many TV appearances in Sweden, Norway, Denmark and Finland to promote the album. <i>Djupa andetag</i> was one of the first Swedish albums to be released as a combined audio-video <a href="/wiki/CD-ROM" title="CD-ROM">CD-ROM</a>, including interviews with Lyngstad, footage from the making of the album, as well as promotional videos. Despite the fact that <i><a href="/wiki/Djupa_andetag" title="Djupa andetag">Djupa andetag</a></i> was officially only released in Scandinavia and the songs were entirely sung in Swedish, a remix album of the single tracks "<a href="/wiki/%C3%84ven_en_blomma" title="Även en blomma">Även en blomma</a>", "Alla Mina Bästa År" (a duet with <a href="/wiki/Roxette" title="Roxette">Roxette</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Marie_Fredriksson" title="Marie Fredriksson">Marie Fredriksson</a>) and "Ögonen" was released in Germany in 1998, entitled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frida_-_The_Mixes" title="Frida - The Mixes">Frida - The Mixes</a></i>. Despite the success of the album, none of these singles made a big impression on the Swedish charts, leaving Frida very disappointed and she would never to record an album again. A one-hour documentary about the making of this album, both in the studio and from Frida's home in Mallorca, Spain, can be seen in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frida_-_The_DVD" title="Frida - The DVD">Frida - The DVD</a></i>. A follow-up album with producer <a href="/wiki/Anders_Glenmark" title="Anders Glenmark">Anders Glenmark</a> was reportedly in the works, but was shelved due to the death of Frida's daughter in 1998. Lyngstad dedicated the song "Chemistry Tonight" (co-written by <a href="/wiki/Kirsty_MacColl" title="Kirsty MacColl">Kirsty MacColl</a>,) to the songwriter after her death in 2000.</p>
<p>A few low key and one-off recordings followed, including a 2002 duet with opera singer <a href="/wiki/Filippa_Giordano" title="Filippa Giordano">Filippa Giordano</a> of the "<a href="/wiki/Barcarolle" title="Barcarolle">Barcarolle</a>" from <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Offenbach" title="Jacques Offenbach">Jacques Offenbach</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Les_contes_d%27Hoffman" title="Les contes d'Hoffman">Les contes d'Hoffman</a></i> as well as the song "The Sun Will Shine Again", written by former <a href="/wiki/Deep_Purple" title="Deep Purple">Deep Purple</a> member <a href="/wiki/Jon_Lord" title="Jon Lord">Jon Lord</a>, and recorded in 2004. "Barcarolle" is only available on the Japanese edition of Giordano's album <i>Rosso Amore</i> and "The Sun Will Shine Again" can be found on Jon Lord's album <i>Beyond The Notes</i> (although a limited-promotional single had been made available). Lord and Lyngstad made several TV appearances in Germany performing the song, on shows like <i>The Sunday Night Classics</i> and <i>The Golden Henne Gala</i>. Lyngstad also joined Lord on stage singing the song during his European autumn tour in 2004. During this tour, she also performed "In the Deep Midwinter", a performance of which can be found on social media.</p>
<p>For the 2004 semi-final of the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a>, staged in Istanbul thirty years after <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a> had won the contest in Brighton, Lyngstad appeared in a special comedy video made for the interval act, entitled <i>Our Last Video</i>. Each of the four members of the group appeared briefly in cameo roles, as did, amongst others, <a href="/wiki/Cher" title="Cher">Cher</a> and British comedian <a href="/wiki/Rik_Mayall" title="Rik Mayall">Rik Mayall</a>. The video was not included in the official DVD release of the Eurovision Contest, but was issued as a separate DVD release on the Universal Music label. It was billed as the first time the four had worked together since the group split in 1982 although they each filmed their appearances separately. Also in 2004, Lyngstad appeared with former band mates Benny Andersson and Björn Ulvaeus at London's fifth anniversary performance of <i><a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia!" title="Mamma Mia!">Mamma Mia!</a></i>, the musical based on ABBA songs. In 2005, she joined all three of her former ABBA colleagues at the Swedish premiere of <i>Mamma Mia!</i> at the arena Cirkus in Stockholm.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005.E2.80.93Present:_after_music">2005–Present: after music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anni-Frid_Lyngstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2005–Present: after music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 15 November 2005, to celebrate Lyngstad's 60th birthday, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Records" title="Universal Records">Universal Records</a> released the box set <i>Frida</i>, consisting of all the solo albums she recorded for <a href="/wiki/Polar_Music" title="Polar Music">Polar Music</a>, all digitally remastered and including a set of bonus tracks. Also included was <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frida_-_The_DVD" title="Frida - The DVD">Frida - The DVD</a></i>, a 3½ hour documentary where Lyngstad talks about her entire career in the music business. Filmed in the Swiss Alps, she talks about her singing technique and about her career both before and after Abba and explains how songs were performed and recorded. In collaboration with Swedish TV, <a href="/wiki/Sveriges_Television" title="Sveriges Television">SVT</a>, the film included many rare TV clips from her early performances, like her first TV performance with "En Ledig Dag", ("A Day Off"). Also included are TV-documentaries about the making and recordings of <i><a href="/wiki/Something%27s_Going_On" title="Something's Going On">Something's Going On</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Djupa_andetag" title="Djupa andetag">Djupa andetag</a></i> (<i>Deep Breaths</i>).</p>
<p>In September 2010, a new album by musician <a href="/wiki/Georg_Wadenius" title="Georg Wadenius">Georg Wadenius</a> titled "Reconnection" was released. Frida and George had discussed working together for many years, as they had long been good friends. The album opens with her rendition of the traditional tune <a href="/wiki/Morning_Has_Broken" title="Morning Has Broken">Morning Has Broken</a> popularized by <a href="/wiki/Cat_Stevens" title="Cat Stevens">Cat Stevens</a>. This song by Cat Stevens, is a favourite for Frida and the song was also on the playlist in the church for Frida and Prince Ruzzo's wedding on August 26, 1992.</p>
<p>On 16 February 2011 BBC Radio 4 broadcast a 45-minute play featuring Frida and the play's writer, long term fan and performer Christopher Green. The play, <i>Like An Angel Passing Through My Room</i>, was billed as 'a story about love. The unconditional love of a devoted fan ... about a real and an imagined intimacy.' It was a project several years in the making; what started as an upbeat reflection on fame and the notion of being a fan, developed into a meditation on the communication between two people and coping with the blows life deals. In an interview with Frida she and Green talked about her long recovery from the death of her husband in 1999. The play is reflective but with a comic sensibility. Frida stated in 2004 that she never intended to return to the music industry although this was short lived and future studio albums were discussed in 2010. Nothing ever came of them and Frida has never returned to recording music since. After the death of her friend <a href="/wiki/Jon_Lord" title="Jon Lord">Jon Lord</a>, in 2013, Frida has almost become almost a recluse, rarely seen in the public eye.</p>
<p>In 2013, Frida helped organise the opening of the <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a> Museum in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> stated she wanted to 'let ABBA rest'. The Long awaited <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a> reunion was expected to happen in 2014 to celebrate their win at the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a> in 1974, some 40 years prior, but this never happened, disappointing fans.</p>
<p>Frida met with fellow <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a> member, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agnetha_Faltskog" title="Agnetha Faltskog">Agnetha Faltskog</a> in <a href="/wiki/Majorca" title="Majorca">Majorca</a>, where the pair talked about singing together but no information regarding this has been released.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anni-Frid_Lyngstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_to_Ragnar_Fredriksson_.281963.E2.80.931970.29">Marriage to Ragnar Fredriksson (1963–1970)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anni-Frid_Lyngstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Marriage to Ragnar Fredriksson (1963–1970)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 3 April 1963, at age 17, Frida married salesperson and fellow musician Ragnar Fredriksson. They had two children: Hans Ragnar (born 26 January 1963) and Ann Lise-Lotte (25 February 1967 – 13 January 1998). They separated early in 1969 and were officially divorced on 19 May 1970. On the very same day, Lyngstad's grandmother, Arntine, died, aged 71.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_.281969.E2.80.931978.29_and_marriage_to_Benny_Andersson_.281978.E2.80.931981.29">Relationship (1969–1978) and marriage to Benny Andersson (1978–1981)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anni-Frid_Lyngstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Relationship (1969–1978) and marriage to Benny Andersson (1978–1981)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1969, Lyngstad met <a href="/wiki/Benny_Andersson" title="Benny Andersson">Benny Andersson</a>. By 1971, they were living together, but did not marry until 6 October 1978, during the height of ABBA's success.</p>
<p>However, after less than three years of marriage, they separated in February 1981, and were divorced in November the same year.</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_to_Prince_Reuss_.281992.E2.80.931999.29">Marriage to Prince Reuss (1992–1999)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anni-Frid_Lyngstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Marriage to Prince Reuss (1992–1999)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1982, Lyngstad left Sweden and moved to London. In 1986, she relocated to Switzerland, and lived with her boyfriend, architect <a href="/wiki/Prince_Heinrich_Ruzzo_Reuss_of_Plauen" title="Prince Heinrich Ruzzo Reuss of Plauen">Heinrich Ruzzo Prince Reuss of Plauen</a> (1950–1999) in his family castle in <a href="/wiki/Fribourg" title="Fribourg">Fribourg</a>.</p>
<p>In 1988, Lyngstad became a grandmother when her daughter, Ann Lise-Lotte, gave birth to a son named Jonathan.</p>
<p>On 26 August 1992, Lyngstad married Prince Reuss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-r1-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> By this marriage, she has two stepdaughters, the twins <a href="/wiki/Princess_Henriette_Reuss_of_Plauen" title="Princess Henriette Reuss of Plauen">Henriette Princess Reuss</a> and Pauline Princess Reuss (both born 2 June 1977, Oslo, Norway).</p>
<p>Through her marriage to Prince Reuss, who had been a student at the same <a href="/wiki/Boarding_school" title="Boarding school">boarding school</a> as <a href="/wiki/Carl_XVI_Gustaf_of_Sweden" title="Carl XVI Gustaf of Sweden">Crown Prince Carl Gustaf</a> – who later became the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_Sweden" title="King of Sweden">King of Sweden</a> – Lyngstad became acquainted with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swedish_royal_family" title="Swedish royal family">Swedish royal family</a> and eventually became close friends with Sweden's <a href="/wiki/Queen_Silvia_of_Sweden" title="Queen Silvia of Sweden">Queen Silvia</a>.</p>
<p>On 13 January 1998, Lyngstad's daughter, Ann Lise-Lotte Casper (<i>née</i> Fredriksson), died of injuries sustained in a car accident in <a href="/wiki/Livonia,_New_York" title="Livonia, New York">Livonia, New York</a> – a town 20 miles south of <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_New_York" title="Rochester, New York">Rochester, New York</a>.</p>
<p>The prince died of <a href="/wiki/Lymphoma" title="Lymphoma">lymphoma</a> in October 1999.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_relations_and_interests">Other relations and interests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anni-Frid_Lyngstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Other relations and interests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lyngstad remains involved in charity work, and stated in a 2005 interview that she had no interest in ever returning to a music career.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>She currently lives in <a href="/wiki/Zermatt" title="Zermatt">Zermatt</a>, Switzerland, sharing a home with her <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">British</a> boyfriend, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Smith,_5th_Viscount_Hambleden" title="Henry Smith, 5th Viscount Hambleden">Henry Smith, 5th Viscount Hambleden</a>, since 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> Frida also has an interest in racing pigeons and is actively involved in the UK. She is the Honorary President of the Hardwick Homing Society in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stockton_On_Tees" title="Stockton On Tees">Stockton On Tees</a>. She also competes as a partner in a team in the same club "Cunningham, Nixon, Lyngstad". Lyngtad also owns several homes in the UK; one in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newcastle_Upon_Tyne" title="Newcastle Upon Tyne">Newcastle Upon Tyne</a>, another in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and another, shared home in the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Dales" title="Yorkshire Dales">Yorkshire Dales</a>.</p>
