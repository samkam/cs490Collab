<p><b>Roger Meddows Taylor</b> (born 26 July 1949) is an English musician, multi-instrumentalist, singer and songwriter. He is best known as the drummer of the rock band <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>, but has also served as occasional songwriter and lead vocalist. As a drummer, he is renowned for his unique sound<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rs-149_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-rs-149-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> and is considered one of the most influential rock drummers of the 1970s and 1980s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>As a songwriter, Taylor contributed songs to the band's albums from the very beginning, composing at least one track on every album, and often singing lead vocals on his own compositions. He wrote or co-wrote three UK number 1s ("<a href="/wiki/These_Are_the_Days_of_Our_Lives" title="These Are the Days of Our Lives">These Are the Days of Our Lives</a>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Innuendo_(song)" title="Innuendo (song)">Innuendo</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Under_Pressure" title="Under Pressure">Under Pressure</a>") and contributed a further five major hits ("<a href="/wiki/Radio_Ga_Ga" title="Radio Ga Ga">Radio Ga Ga</a>", "<a href="/wiki/A_Kind_of_Magic_(song)" title="A Kind of Magic (song)">A Kind of Magic</a>","<a href="/wiki/Heaven_for_Everyone" title="Heaven for Everyone">Heaven for Everyone</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Breakthru_(song)" title="Breakthru (song)">Breakthru</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/The_Invisible_Man_(Queen_song)" title="The Invisible Man (Queen song)">The Invisible Man</a>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>). He is also commonly known to have been the main writer on the international Top Ten hit '<a href="/wiki/One_Vision" title="One Vision">One Vision</a>', although the track is credited to the whole band. He has collaborated with such artists as <a href="/wiki/Eric_Clapton" title="Eric Clapton">Eric Clapton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Waters" title="Roger Waters">Roger Waters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Daltrey" title="Roger Daltrey">Roger Daltrey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Plant" title="Robert Plant">Robert Plant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phil_Collins" title="Phil Collins">Phil Collins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Genesis_(band)" title="Genesis (band)">Genesis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Nail" title="Jimmy Nail">Jimmy Nail</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gary_Numan" title="Gary Numan">Gary Numan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shakin%27_Stevens" title="Shakin' Stevens">Shakin' Stevens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foo_Fighters" title="Foo Fighters">Foo Fighters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Al_Stewart" title="Al Stewart">Al Stewart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Vai" title="Steve Vai">Steve Vai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yoshiki_(musician)" title="Yoshiki (musician)">Yoshiki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyndi_Almouzni" title="Cyndi Almouzni">Cyndi Almouzni</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bon_Jovi" title="Bon Jovi">Bon Jovi</a>. As a producer, he has produced albums by <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Wolf" title="Virginia Wolf">Virginia Wolf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Nail" title="Jimmy Nail">Jimmy Nail</a> and <a href="/wiki/Magnum_(band)" title="Magnum (band)">Magnum</a>. He currently resides in <a href="/wiki/Guildford" title="Guildford">Guildford</a>, Surrey, but also has a home in <a href="/wiki/Helford" title="Helford">Helford</a>, Cornwall.</p>
<p>In addition to his drum work, Taylor routinely played keyboards, guitars and bass on his own songs. During the 1980s, in addition to his work with Queen, he formed a parallel band known as the Cross, in which he was the lead vocalist and the rhythm guitarist. During the early 1980s, Taylor was also a panelist on the popular UK quiz show <i><a href="/wiki/Pop_Quiz" title="Pop Quiz">Pop Quiz</a></i>, hosted by <a href="/wiki/Mike_Read" title="Mike Read">Mike Read</a>.</p>
<p>In 2005, he was voted by UK fans as the eighth greatest drummer in classic rock music history, in a poll conducted by Planet Rock Radio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> According to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Sunday_Times_(UK)" title="The Sunday Times (UK)">The Sunday Times</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Sunday_Times_Rich_List" title="Sunday Times Rich List">Rich List</a> he was worth £80 million or around $127 million in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2014, he appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_of_Rock_with_Brian_Pern" title="The Life of Rock with Brian Pern">The Life of Rock with Brian Pern</a></i> as himself.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Taylor_(Queen_drummer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Roger Taylor was born in <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Lynn" title="King's Lynn">King's Lynn</a>, Norfolk, on 26 July 1949. Taylor moved to <a href="/wiki/Truro" title="Truro">Truro</a>, Cornwall, south west England, with his mother Winifred, father Michael and younger sister Clare. When he was seven years old, he and some friends formed his first band, the Bubblingover Boys, in which he played the ukulele. He briefly attended <a href="/wiki/Truro_Cathedral_School" title="Truro Cathedral School">Truro Cathedral School</a>; then, at the age of 13, he joined <a href="/wiki/Truro_School" title="Truro School">Truro School</a> as a day boy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> At the age of 15, Taylor became a member of the Reaction, a very busy semi-pro rock band formed mainly of boys from Truro School. In 1967, he went to London to study dentistry at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_Hospital_Medical_College" title="London Hospital Medical College">London Hospital Medical College</a>, but he soon became bored with dentistry and, after changing to Biology, obtained a BSc<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1968.E2.80.9370:_Smile">1968–70: Smile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Taylor_(Queen_drummer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1968–70: Smile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Smile_(band)" title="Smile (band)">Smile (band)</a></div>
<p>Taylor met <a href="/wiki/Brian_May" title="Brian May">Brian May</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tim_Staffell" title="Tim Staffell">Tim Staffell</a> in 1968 after seeing an advert on a noticeboard at <a href="/wiki/Imperial_College_London" title="Imperial College London">Imperial College</a> for a drummer. <a href="/wiki/Smile_(band)" title="Smile (band)">Smile</a> included May on lead guitar, Staffell on vocals and bass, and later Taylor on drums. The band only lasted for two years before Staffell departed to join <a href="/wiki/Humpy_Bong" title="Humpy Bong">Humpy Bong</a>, leaving the band with only a catalogue of nine songs:</p>
<ul>
<li>"Earth" (Staffell)</li>
<li>"Step On Me" (Staffell/May) originally from May and Staffell's band 1984.</li>
<li>"Doin' Alright" (Staffell/May) (later remade for Queen's <a href="/wiki/Queen_(Queen_album)" title="Queen (Queen album)">debut album</a>)</li>
<li>"Blag" (Taylor)</li>
<li>"Polar Bear" (May)</li>
<li>"Silver Salmon" (Staffell)</li>
<li>"See What A Fool I've Been" (May, based on the song "That's How I Feel" by <a href="/wiki/Sonny_Terry" title="Sonny Terry">Sonny Terry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brownie_McGhee" title="Brownie McGhee">Brownie McGhee</a>)</li>
<li>"<a href="/wiki/If_I_Were_a_Carpenter_(song)" title="If I Were a Carpenter (song)">If I Were a Carpenter</a>" (<a href="/wiki/Tim_Hardin" title="Tim Hardin">Tim Hardin</a>) a <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover</a> regularly featuring in their live set.</li>
<li>"April Lady" (Lucas) a song presented to the band by Mercury Records during their second studio session.</li>
</ul>
<p>Smile reunited for several songs on 22 December 1992. Taylor's band <a href="/wiki/The_Cross_(band)" title="The Cross (band)">The Cross</a> were headliners and he brought May and Staffell on to play "Earth" and "If I Were a Carpenter".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1970s.E2.80.93present:_Queen">1970s–present: Queen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Taylor_(Queen_drummer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1970s–present: Queen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen (band)</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>In 1969, Taylor was working with <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Mercury" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a> at the Kensington Market (they were living in an apartment around the same time). Mercury, then known as Freddie Bulsara, was an avid fan of Smile. After the band split up in 1970, Bulsara convinced the remaining two members to continue, and he eventually joined the band. In 1971, they found bass guitarist <a href="/wiki/John_Deacon" title="John Deacon">John Deacon</a> and brought him into the band, before going on to release their <a href="/wiki/Queen_(Queen_album)" title="Queen (Queen album)">debut album</a> in 1973. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1977.E2.80.93present:_Solo_career">1977–present: Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Taylor_(Queen_drummer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1977–present: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taylor has had a productive solo career, releasing five albums. His first single was his 1977 "I Wanna Testify". He recorded it during Queen's sessions for the <i><a href="/wiki/News_of_the_World_(album)" title="News of the World (album)">News of the World</a></i> album. The A-side, although a cover of The Parliaments song of the same name, was completely different from the original. The B-side was a self-penned song "Turn on the TV".</p>
<p>His first solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Fun_in_Space" title="Fun in Space">Fun in Space</a></i>, was released in 1981. Taylor performed all vocals and played all instruments aside of about half of the keyboards, which were done by engineer <a href="/wiki/David_Richards_(record_producer)" title="David Richards (record producer)">David Richards</a>. With Queen still touring heavily and recording at the time of release, Taylor was unable to promote the album to its fullest extent, so he appeared on some European TV shows to promote the single, "Future Management", including <i><a href="/wiki/Top_of_the_Pops" title="Top of the Pops">Top of the Pops</a></i>. The only other single to come from the album was "My Country". The only US single released from the album was "Let's Get Crazy".</p>
<p>His next venture, <i><a href="/wiki/Strange_Frontier" title="Strange Frontier">Strange Frontier</a></i>, came in July 1984. The three singles from the album were the title track, "Beautiful Dreams" (in Portugal only) and "Man on Fire", the latter becoming a live favourite for him in later years. No attempts to promote the singles were made since Queen toured to promote <i><a href="/wiki/The_Works_(Queen_album)" title="The Works (Queen album)">The Works</a></i>, and Taylor did not perform on any TV shows. <i>Strange Frontier</i> included guest appearances by bandmates Freddie Mercury and John Deacon. Mercury sang backing vocals on "Killing Time", Deacon remixed the B-side "I Cry For You" and <a href="/wiki/Rick_Parfitt" title="Rick Parfitt">Rick Parfitt</a> co-wrote and played on "It's An Illusion". David Richards, Queen's engineer and producer at the time, also co-wrote two of the tracks. The album includes covers of <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Racing_in_the_Street" title="Racing in the Street">Racing in the Street</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Bob_Dylan" title="Bob Dylan">Bob Dylan</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Masters_of_War" title="Masters of War">Masters of War</a>".</p>
<p>After Queen finished their 1986 <a href="/wiki/A_Kind_of_Magic" title="A Kind of Magic">Magic Tour</a>, Taylor started a new band, <a href="/wiki/The_Cross_(band)" title="The Cross (band)">The Cross</a>, which released three albums over their six years of existence. In 1993 the band split up, after performing one final gig at the Gosport Festival.</p>
<p>In 1994 Taylor worked with <a href="/wiki/Yoshiki_(musician)" title="Yoshiki (musician)">Yoshiki</a>, drummer and pianist of <a href="/wiki/X_Japan" title="X Japan">X Japan</a> and released the song "Foreign Sand" and a reworking of The Cross's "Final Destination". The album <i>Happiness?</i> was "Dedicated to the tasmanian tiger – thylacinus cynocephalus, but most especially... for Freddie". "Nazis 1994" from this album became Taylor's first hit single in England and was followed by two other top 40 UK hits, "Happiness" and "Foreign Sand."</p>
<p>In 1998 he released his fourth solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Electric_Fire" title="Electric Fire">Electric Fire</a></i>. He supported it with a small tour in the spring of 1999, on which Brian May joined him at the gig in Wolverhampton. Taylor also performed one of the first Internet-gigs – for which he got a mention in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guinness_Book_of_World_Records" title="Guinness Book of World Records">Guinness Book of World Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, after nearly twelve years between solo albums, Taylor planned to release a new studio album titled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Unblinking_Eye_(Everything_Is_Broken)" title="The Unblinking Eye (Everything Is Broken)">The Unblinking Eye</a></i>. The first single, of the same name, was released on 23 November 2009 as a digital download, but due to popular demand it was released as a limited edition single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> Originally leaked to the official Queen fan club's website for members only, it still managed to be on YouTube within hours of the early bird release. It was announced in late 2012 that fans would be able to vote on which songs appeared on the new album, with the album itself slated for release that year. The album is called, <a href="/wiki/Fun_on_Earth" title="Fun on Earth">Fun on Earth</a>',<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> and was released on 11 November 2013.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Cross">The Cross</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Taylor_(Queen_drummer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Cross">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Cross_(band)" title="The Cross (band)">The Cross (band)</a></div>
<p>The Cross were a side project of Taylor's that existed from 1987 to 1993 and released three albums. While still the drummer for Queen, Taylor fronted The Cross as rhythm guitarist and lead vocalist. On its debut release, <i>The Cross</i> incorporated dance influences which they dropped on their remaining two albums. The band never enjoyed much commercial success, except in Germany.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shove_It"><i>Shove It</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Taylor_(Queen_drummer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Shove It">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_Cross_1990_Neu_Isenburg_1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="409" data-file-width="714" height="126" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/The_Cross_1990_Neu_Isenburg_1.jpg/220px-The_Cross_1990_Neu_Isenburg_1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/The_Cross_1990_Neu_Isenburg_1.jpg/330px-The_Cross_1990_Neu_Isenburg_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/The_Cross_1990_Neu_Isenburg_1.jpg/440px-The_Cross_1990_Neu_Isenburg_1.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:The_Cross_1990_Neu_Isenburg_1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Taylor performing with <a href="/wiki/The_Cross_(band)" title="The Cross (band)">The Cross</a> in 1990</div>
</div>

<p>After Queen's 1986 Magic Tour, the band members went their separate ways to do various solo work. Taylor decided to form a new band with whom he could tour. He had already written and recorded the album himself before finding a band to play the songs with. He eventually placed an ad for band members in a national newspaper, hinting he was a famous rock musician. The position of keyboard player was duly offered to <a href="/wiki/Spike_Edney" title="Spike Edney">Spike Edney</a> after two successful Queen tours in which Edney had played. When the auditions were over, the line-up was completed by Peter Noone on bass, Clayton Moss on guitar and <a href="/wiki/Josh_Macrae" title="Josh Macrae">Josh Macrae</a> on drums. Taylor himself would take the responsibility on lead vocals and rhythm guitar.</p>
<p>The first album, <i>Shove It</i>, was released in 1988. In Europe, <a href="/wiki/Heaven_for_Everyone" title="Heaven for Everyone">Heaven for Everyone</a> (later a Queen song) contained Freddie Mercury on lead vocals and Taylor on backing vocals. However, on the single version and American album version the roles were switched. The European CD contained an extra track (compared to cassette and LP) in The 2nd Shelf Mix, the US version having "Feel The Force" as its extra track. The band promoted hard (especially in Germany), with many TV performances of singles including an appearance at the Montreux Golden Rose festival in 1988. The tour took in dates in the UK and Germany. Three singles were released from the album: "Cowboys and Indians", "Heaven for Everyone" and "Shove It". Another single, "Manipulator," was released in 1988, but it was not included on any album. It was also the only song from the time that had joint writing credits, Taylor sharing them with Edney and Steve Strange.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mad.2C_Bad_and_Dangerous_to_Know"><i>Mad, Bad and Dangerous to Know</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Taylor_(Queen_drummer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mad, Bad and Dangerous to Know">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_Cross_1990_Neu_Isenburg_2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="360" data-file-width="241" height="329" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/The_Cross_1990_Neu_Isenburg_2.jpg/220px-The_Cross_1990_Neu_Isenburg_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3d/The_Cross_1990_Neu_Isenburg_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3d/The_Cross_1990_Neu_Isenburg_2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:The_Cross_1990_Neu_Isenburg_2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Taylor performing with The Cross in 1990.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>After finishing Queen's 1989 album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Miracle_(album)" title="The Miracle (album)">The Miracle</a></i>, Taylor went into the studio with the rest of <i>The Cross</i> for the first time to record <i><a href="/wiki/Mad,_Bad_and_Dangerous_to_Know_(The_Cross_album)" title="Mad, Bad and Dangerous to Know (The Cross album)">Mad, Bad and Dangerous to Know</a></i>. The band composed the opening track "On Top of the World Ma" with a riff bearing a resemblance to the <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a> track <a href="/wiki/Whole_Lotta_Love" title="Whole Lotta Love">Whole Lotta Love</a>. The rest of the album consisted mainly of individually written songs, except for "Power To Love" which was the result of the combined effort of Macrae, Noone and Moss. Clayton Moss sang lead vocals on his own track "Better Things", and Spike Edney played <a href="/wiki/Mandolin" title="Mandolin">mandolin</a> on "Final Destination", which was written by Taylor. "Final Destination" was released as a single, as were "Liar" and "Power To Love", the latter being the last single to be released in the UK by the band. "Final Destination" came with a live rendition of Taylor's song "Man on Fire" as a B-side, and "Liar" (Noone) had a brand new track, "In Charge of My Heart", which was also penned by Taylor. The 12" single and CD of "Liar" also included extended remixes of both "Liar" and "In Charge of My Heart". The instrumental section at the beginning of "In Charge of My Heart" was used as the opening to concerts on the accompanying tour. "Closer To You" (Edney) had been planned to be released in America, but never was. The group having seemingly given up on the UK market, the accompanying tour only included dates in Germany, Austria, Switzerland and Ibiza. Unusual for such tours, every song from the new album was played live.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Blue_Rock"><i>Blue Rock</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Taylor_(Queen_drummer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Blue Rock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Made at a time when Taylor's efforts were concentrated on Queen and Mercury himself, <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Rock_(album)" title="Blue Rock (album)">Blue Rock</a></i> gave the other members of the band a chance to take control of the upcoming album. It was in fact mostly written by Edney, who contributed three of his own tracks and contributed to four more. Once again the opening track was penned by the entire band, "Bad Attitude" was written (although not complete) by the Christmas fan club party of 1990. The album itself contained a lot of vocals not from Taylor, but mainly of Edney and Noone. Examples of this can be heard in "Put It All Down To Love" (Edney), "Life Changes" (Moss, Noone, Edney and Macrae) and "Heartland" (The B-side to Life Changes, penned by Noone). Blue Rock itself was only released in Germany (although promo copies were released in Italy and Japan), so copies are somewhat rare. "New Dark Ages" (Taylor) was released in Germany with another live version of "Man on Fire", whilst "Life Changes" was released with the B-side "Heartland". It was however immediately withdrawn due to Freddie Mercury's passing. The tour was in support of <a href="/wiki/Magnum_(band)" title="Magnum (band)">Magnum</a>, so the concert lengths were short (45 mins.) and very few bootlegs have survived. The tour pace was rapid, covering 20 dates in one month.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Break-up_of_the_Cross">Break-up of the Cross</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Taylor_(Queen_drummer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Break-up of the Cross">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band broke up in 1993 after performing a final show. Taylor continued working with Queen and produced solo albums. Drummer Macrae accompanied Taylor on his solo tours and also played percussion at the Freddie Mercury Tribute Concert. Both Taylor and keyboardist Edney participated in the <a href="/wiki/Queen_%2B_Paul_Rodgers" title="Queen + Paul Rodgers">Queen + Paul Rodgers</a> tours, while Macrae worked behind the scenes as co-producer, sound engineer and Pro Tools engineer for them. In the late 1990s, Edney formed the SAS Band ("Spike's All Stars"), a group with an ever-changing line-up of popular '80s musicians.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Taylor_(Queen_drummer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 21st century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Taylor has appeared along with May for various other events and promotions, including Queen's 2001 induction into the <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> and the "<a href="/wiki/Party_at_the_Palace" title="Party at the Palace">Party at the Palace</a>" in 2002, celebrating the golden jubilee of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_II" title="Queen Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> Taylor and May, performing as Queen, also appeared three times on the American singing contest television show <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i>, once on 11 April 2006, during which week contestants were required to sing a Queen song, (which included "<a href="/wiki/Bohemian_Rhapsody" title="Bohemian Rhapsody">Bohemian Rhapsody</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Fat_Bottomed_Girls" title="Fat Bottomed Girls">Fat Bottomed Girls</a>", "<a href="/wiki/The_Show_Must_Go_On_(Queen_song)" title="The Show Must Go On (Queen song)">The Show Must Go On</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Who_Wants_to_Live_Forever" title="Who Wants to Live Forever">Who Wants to Live Forever</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Innuendo_(song)" title="Innuendo (song)">Innuendo</a>"), a second time on the show's <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_8)" title="American Idol (season 8)">season 8</a> finale in May 2009, performing "We Are the Champions" with finalists <a href="/wiki/Adam_Lambert" title="Adam Lambert">Adam Lambert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kris_Allen" title="Kris Allen">Kris Allen</a>, and a third time on the <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_11)" title="American Idol (season 11)">eleventh season</a> on 25 and 26 April 2012, performing a Queen medley with the six finalists on the first show, and the following day performed "<a href="/wiki/Somebody_to_Love_(Queen_song)" title="Somebody to Love (Queen song)">Somebody to Love</a>" with the 'Queen Extravaganza' band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Live_Earth" title="Live Earth">Live Earth</a> in 2007, Taylor opened the concert with <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Hawkins" title="Taylor Hawkins">Taylor Hawkins</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Foo_Fighters" title="Foo Fighters">Foo Fighters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chad_Smith" title="Chad Smith">Chad Smith</a> of <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a>.</p>
<p>In November 2009, Taylor appeared on the popular reality TV show <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (UK TV series)">The X Factor</a></i> with May as Queen mentoring the contestants, then later performing "<a href="/wiki/Bohemian_Rhapsody" title="Bohemian Rhapsody">Bohemian Rhapsody</a>". Also in that month, Taylor confirmed he is planning to tour with Taylor Hawkins, which Taylor described as a "quick tour".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/2011_MTV_Europe_Music_Awards" title="2011 MTV Europe Music Awards">2011 MTV Europe Music Awards</a> on 6 November, Queen received the <a href="/wiki/MTV_Europe_Music_Award_for_Global_Icon" title="MTV Europe Music Award for Global Icon">Global Icon Award</a>, and Taylor and Brian May closed the awards ceremony, with <a href="/wiki/Adam_Lambert" title="Adam Lambert">Adam Lambert</a> on vocals, performing "The Show Must Go On", "We Will Rock You" and "We Are the Champions".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Taylor, along with <a href="/wiki/Steven_Tyler" title="Steven Tyler">2</a> <a href="/wiki/Roger_Daltrey" title="Roger Daltrey">other</a> music legends, joined the advisory board of <a class="external text" href="http://www.edgemusicnetwork.com/about" rel="nofollow">Edge Music Network</a>. The company is innovative in their approach to connecting fans and their favorite bands. Taylor performed in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics_closing_ceremony" title="2012 Summer Olympics closing ceremony">2012 Summer Olympics closing ceremony</a> in London on 12 August.</p>
<p>In 2013 &amp; 2014, Taylor served as an Executive Producer of the movie "Solitary", directed by Sasha Krane. In addition to those duties, he provided original music including the song When We Were Young and three instrumental songs which serve as incidental/background music in the film and during closing credits. Taylor also appeared as special guest for Welsh Rock artist <a href="/wiki/Jayce_Lewis" title="Jayce Lewis">Jayce Lewis</a> and his project Protafield, with Taylor providing drums on the track 'Wrath'<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 November 2014, Taylor joined the charity group <a href="/wiki/Band_Aid_30" title="Band Aid 30">Band Aid 30</a>, playing drums alongside current British and Irish pop acts on the latest version of the track "<a href="/wiki/Do_They_Know_It%27s_Christmas%3F" title="Do They Know It's Christmas?">Do They Know It's Christmas?</a>" at <a href="/wiki/Sarm_West_Studios" title="Sarm West Studios">Sarm West Studios</a> in <a href="/wiki/Notting_Hill" title="Notting Hill">Notting Hill</a>, London, to raise money for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014_Ebola_outbreaks" title="2014 Ebola outbreaks">2014 Ebola crisis</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Africa" title="Western Africa">Western Africa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Queen_%2B_Paul_Rodgers" title="Queen + Paul Rodgers">Queen + Paul Rodgers</a></div>
<p>Since his fourth solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Electric_Fire" title="Electric Fire">Electric Fire</a></i> in 1998 Taylor has been performing as a solo artist, as part of ensembles, and infrequently as Queen with May.</p>
<p>At the end of 2004, May and Taylor announced that they would reunite and return to touring in 2005, with <a href="/wiki/Paul_Rodgers" title="Paul Rodgers">Paul Rodgers</a> (founder and former vocalist of <a href="/wiki/Free_(band)" title="Free (band)">Free</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bad_Company" title="Bad Company">Bad Company</a>). May's website also stated that Rodgers would be 'featured with' Queen as <a href="/wiki/Queen_%2B_Paul_Rodgers" title="Queen + Paul Rodgers">Queen + Paul Rodgers</a>, not replacing the late Freddie Mercury. The retired John Deacon would not be participating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bmcom_jdr_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-bmcom_jdr-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Between 2005 and 2006, Queen + Paul Rodgers embarked on a world tour, the first leg being Europe, the second Japan and the US in 2006. On 15 August 2006, May confirmed through his website and fan club that Queen + Paul Rodgers would begin producing their first studio album beginning in October, to be recorded at a "secret location".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> The album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cosmos_Rocks" title="The Cosmos Rocks">The Cosmos Rocks</a></i>, was released in Europe on 12 September 2008 and in the US on 28 October 2008. Following the album release the band embarked on a tour through Europe and South America, opening on Kharkov's freedom square in front of 350,000 Ukrainian fans. This performance was shown in cinemas on 6 November 2008 for one airing only, and later released on DVD.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Taylor_(Queen_drummer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taylor has stated that his main influence as a rock drummer is the late <a href="/wiki/John_Bonham" title="John Bonham">John Bonham</a> of <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a>. Speaking of Bonham, Taylor said, "The greatest rock and roll drummer of all time was John Bonham, who did things that nobody had ever even thought possible before with the drum kit. And also the greatest sound out of his drums – they sounded enormous, and just one bass drum. So fast on it that he did more with one bass drum than most people could do with three, if they could manage them. And he had technique to burn and fantastic power and tremendous feel for rock and roll".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Taylor_(Queen_drummer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By the time <i><a href="/wiki/News_of_the_World_(album)" title="News of the World (album)">News of the World</a></i> was released in 1977, Taylor had met his future girlfriend, Dominique Beyrand. She was working for <a href="/wiki/Richard_Branson" title="Richard Branson">Richard Branson</a> at the time, who was at the helm of Queen's free concert at Hyde Park. They lived together from 1980 to 1987, raising their two children: Felix Luther and Rory Eleanor. They decided to get married for reasons relating to his estate, to protect his children's interests in the future. At the time, Taylor was seeing another girl, Debbie Leng (also seen in the "<a href="/wiki/Breakthru_(song)" title="Breakthru (song)">Breakthru</a>" video); he moved in with her a month after his <a href="/wiki/Marriage_of_convenience" title="Marriage of convenience">marriage of convenience</a> to Dominique. Before Freddie Mercury's death, Taylor and Leng had their first child, Rufus Tiger, who was born in March 1991. He ended up having two more children with Leng: Tiger Lily (1994) and Lola Daisy May (2000). In late 2002, they decided to break up. Taylor remarried on 26 August 2010, to Sarina Potgieter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> Taylor is also a <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea F.C.</a> supporter<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> but also supported Manchester United fans who campaigned against media mogul Rupert Murdoch buying a stake into the club.</p>

<p>Until 2008, Taylor exclusively used <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ludwig-Musser" title="Ludwig-Musser">Ludwig</a> or <a href="/wiki/Sleishman" title="Sleishman">Sleishman</a> drum kits, or a combination of the two in the 2005 <i>Return of the Champions Tour</i>. In the recent Queen + Paul Rodgers 2008 <i>The Cosmos Rocks</i> tour he opted for a <a href="/wiki/Drum_Workshop" title="Drum Workshop">Drum Workshop</a> kit.</p>
<p><b>DW Natural satin Maple Kit with Black Chrome Hardware</b> Main kit on tour</p>
<ul>
<li>Toms – 10"x8", 12"x 9" &amp; 14"x11"</li>
<li>Floor Toms – 16"x 16" &amp; 18"x16"</li>
<li>Bass Drum – 26"x 18"</li>
<li>Snare Drum – 14"x 6.5" (Ludwig Classic Maple)</li>
<li>Roto-Toms – 12" &amp; 14" Remo</li>
</ul>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zildjian" title="Zildjian">Zildjian</a> Cymbals</p>
<ul>
<li>Hi-Hats – 15" Avedis Zildjian New Beat Hats</li>
<li>Crash: 17" Dark Crash Medium Thin, 18" Dark Crash Medium Thin, 19" Dark Crash Thin</li>
<li>Ride: 20" Avedis Ping Ride</li>
<li>China: 20" Oriental China Trash</li>
</ul>
<p>In Queen's formative years and up until circa 1984, Taylor used a mixture of <a href="/wiki/Paiste" title="Paiste">Paiste</a> and Zildjian cymbals, before permanently settling on Zildjian, which he has played exclusively ever since.</p>
<p>Drum Pedal – DW 9002 (On all live performances with Queen, Taylor's pedal of choice was the Ludwig Speed King)</p>
<p><b>DW Black Mirra with 24 Karat Gold Hardware</b> Front of stage kit<br/>
Used for drum solo and <a href="/wiki/I%27m_in_Love_with_My_Car" title="I'm in Love with My Car">I'm in Love with My Car</a></p>
<ul>
<li>Toms – 10"x8", 12"x10" &amp; 14"x12"</li>
<li>Floor Toms – 16"x16" &amp; 18"x16"</li>
<li>Bass Drum – 24"x18"</li>
<li>Sub-Bass Drum – 24" x 8"</li>
<li>Snare Drum – 14"x6.5"</li>
<li>Roto-Toms – 12" &amp; 14" Remo</li>
</ul>
<p>Taylor favours the <a href="/wiki/Vic_Firth" title="Vic Firth">Vic Firth</a> X5B American Classic Extreme 5B (length : 16 1/2" | Diameter : 0.595") as his current choice of stick.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Remo" title="Remo">Remo</a> drumheads</p>
<ul>
<li>Remo Ambassador Clear heads on all toms.</li>
<li>Remo Powerstroke 3 Clear on Bass Drums.</li>
<li>Remo Powerstroke 3 Coated on Snare Drum.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stamps">Stamps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Taylor_(Queen_drummer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Stamps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1999 Taylor became the first living person, other than members of the British Royal Family, to appear on a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Mail" title="Royal Mail">Royal Mail</a> <a href="/wiki/Postage_stamp" title="Postage stamp">stamp</a>, being seen behind Freddie Mercury as part of a "Great Britons" issue. This caused controversy as it was an understood rule that the only living people allowed to appear on British stamps could be members of the Royal Family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2002, Taylor appeared on the "Twelve Drummers Drumming" Christmas card in the "<a href="/wiki/Twelve_Days_of_Christmas" title="Twelve Days of Christmas">Twelve Days of Christmas</a>" set sold at <a href="/wiki/Woolworths_Group" title="Woolworths Group">Woolworths</a> to raise money for the <a href="/wiki/National_Society_for_the_Prevention_of_Cruelty_to_Children" title="National Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Children">NSPCC</a> – alongside <a href="/wiki/Roger_Taylor_(Duran_Duran_drummer)" title="Roger Taylor (Duran Duran drummer)">the "other" Roger Taylor</a>, the drummer for <a href="/wiki/Duran_Duran" title="Duran Duran">Duran Duran</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
