<p><i><b>Finding Nemo</b></i> is a 2003 American <a href="/wiki/Computer_animation" title="Computer animation">computer-animated</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comedy_drama_film" title="Comedy drama film">comedy-drama</a> <a href="/wiki/Adventure_film" title="Adventure film">adventure film</a> written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Stanton" title="Andrew Stanton">Andrew Stanton</a>, released by <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Pictures" title="Walt Disney Pictures">Walt Disney Pictures</a>, and the fifth film produced by <a href="/wiki/Pixar" title="Pixar">Pixar Animation Studios</a>. It tells the story of the overprotective <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphiprion_ocellaris" title="Amphiprion ocellaris">clownfish</a> named Marlin (<a href="/wiki/Albert_Brooks" title="Albert Brooks">Albert Brooks</a>) who, along with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paracanthurus_hepatus" title="Paracanthurus hepatus">regal tang</a> named Dory (<a href="/wiki/Ellen_DeGeneres" title="Ellen DeGeneres">Ellen DeGeneres</a>), searches for his abducted son Nemo (<a href="/wiki/Alexander_Gould" title="Alexander Gould">Alexander Gould</a>) all the way to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sydney_Harbour" title="Sydney Harbour">Sydney Harbour</a>. Along the way, Marlin learns to take risks and let Nemo take care of himself.</p>
<p>It is Pixar's first film to be released in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Movie_theatre" title="Movie theatre">cinemas</a> in the northern hemisphere summer. The film was re-released for the first time in <a href="/wiki/3D_film" title="3D film">3D</a> on September 14, 2012, and it was released on <a href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray</a> on December 4, 2012. The film received widespread critical acclaim, won the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Animated_Feature" title="Academy Award for Best Animated Feature">Academy Award for Best Animated Feature</a>, and was nominated in three more categories including <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Screenplay" title="Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay">Best Original Screenplay</a>. It was the <a href="/wiki/2003_in_film" title="2003 in film">second highest-grossing film of 2003</a>, earning a total of $936 million worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-BOM-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Finding Nemo</i> is the best-selling DVD of all time, with over 40 million copies sold as of 2006<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Finding_Nemo&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> and was the highest-grossing G-rated film of all time before Pixar's own <i><a href="/wiki/Toy_Story_3" title="Toy Story 3">Toy Story 3</a></i> overtook it. It is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_highest-grossing_films" title="List of highest-grossing films">27th highest-grossing film of all time</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_highest-grossing_animated_films" title="List of highest-grossing animated films">5th highest-grossing animated film</a>. In 2008, the <a href="/wiki/American_Film_Institute" title="American Film Institute">American Film Institute</a> named it the 10th greatest animated film ever made as part of their <a href="/wiki/AFI%27s_10_Top_10" title="AFI's 10 Top 10">10 Top 10</a> lists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFITop10_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-AFITop10-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> A <a href="#Sequel">sequel</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Dory" title="Finding Dory">Finding Dory</a></i>, is in production, set to be released on June 17, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slashfilm.com_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-slashfilm.com-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Finding_Nemo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Two <a href="/wiki/Ocellaris_clownfish" title="Ocellaris clownfish">ocellaris clownfish</a>, Marlin and his wife Coral, admire their new home in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Barrier_Reef" title="Great Barrier Reef">Great Barrier Reef</a> and their clutch of eggs when a <a href="/wiki/Barracuda" title="Barracuda">barracuda</a> attacks, knocking Marlin unconscious. He wakes up to find Coral and all but one of the eggs have disappeared. Marlin names this last egg Nemo, a name that Coral liked.</p>
<p>Nemo develops a smaller right fin as a result of damage to his egg during the attack, which limits his ability as a swimmer. After Marlin embarrasses Nemo during a school field trip, he sneaks away from the reef and is captured by scuba divers. As the boat departs, a diver accidentally knocks his <a href="/wiki/Diving_mask" title="Diving mask">diving mask</a> overboard. While attempting to save Nemo, Marlin meets Dory, a good-hearted and optimistic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paracanthurus_hepatus" title="Paracanthurus hepatus">regal blue tang</a> with <a href="/wiki/Anterograde_amnesia" title="Anterograde amnesia">short-term memory loss</a>. Marlin and Dory meet three <a href="/wiki/Shark" title="Shark">sharks</a> – Bruce, Anchor and Chum – in an old minefield. Marlin discovers the diver's mask and notices an address written on it. When he argues with Dory and accidentally gives her a nosebleed, the blood scent causes Bruce to enter a <a href="/wiki/Feeding_frenzy" title="Feeding frenzy">feeding frenzy</a>. The pair escape from Bruce but the mask falls into a trench in the <a href="/wiki/Deep_sea" title="Deep sea">deep sea</a>.</p>
<p>During a hazardous struggle with an <a href="/wiki/Anglerfish" title="Anglerfish">anglerfish</a> in the trench, Dory sees the diving mask and reads the address located in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. After receiving directions to Sydney from a <a href="/wiki/Shoaling_and_schooling" title="Shoaling and schooling">school</a> of <a href="/wiki/Silver_moony" title="Silver moony">moonfish</a>, Marlin and Dory encounter a bloom of <a href="/wiki/Chrysaora_quinquecirrha" title="Chrysaora quinquecirrha">jellyfish</a> that nearly kills them. Marlin loses consciousness and wakes up to a <a href="/wiki/Green_sea_turtle" title="Green sea turtle">sea turtle</a> named Crush, who takes Marlin and Dory on the <a href="/wiki/East_Australian_Current" title="East Australian Current">East Australian Current</a>. Marlin tell the details of his long journey with a group of sea turtles, and his story is spread across the ocean. He also sees how Crush gets on well with his son Squirt.</p>
<p>Meanwhile Nemo is placed in a fish tank in the office of a Dentist named Phillip Sherman on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sydney_Harbour" title="Sydney Harbour">Sydney Harbour</a>. He meets aquarium fish called the Tank Gang, led by a <a href="/wiki/Moorish_idol" title="Moorish idol">moorish idol</a> named Gill, who has a broken fin. The Tank Gang includes Bloat, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diodon_holocanthus" title="Diodon holocanthus">puffer fish</a>; Bubbles, a <a href="/wiki/Yellow_tang" title="Yellow tang">yellow tang</a>; Peach, an <a href="/wiki/Pisaster_ochraceus" title="Pisaster ochraceus">ochre starfish</a>; Gurgle, a <a href="/wiki/Royal_gramma" title="Royal gramma">royal gramma</a>; Jacques, a <a href="/wiki/Lysmata_amboinensis" title="Lysmata amboinensis">pacific cleaner shrimp</a>; and Deb, a <a href="/wiki/Dascyllus_melanurus" title="Dascyllus melanurus">blacktailed humbug</a>.</p>
<p>The fish learn that Nemo is to be given to Sherman's niece, Darla, who killed a fish by constantly shaking its bag. Gill then reveals his plan to escape, jamming the tank's <a href="/wiki/Filter_(aquarium)" title="Filter (aquarium)">filter</a>, forcing the dentist to remove the fish to clean it. The fish would be placed in plastic bags, and then they would roll out the window and into the harbor. After an attempt at the escape goes wrong, a <a href="/wiki/Brown_pelican" title="Brown pelican">brown pelican</a>, Nigel, brings news of Marlin's adventure. Inspired by his father's determination, Nemo successfully jams the filter, but the dentist installs a new high-tech filter before they can escape.</p>
<p>After leaving the East Australian Current, Marlin and Dory are engulfed by a <a href="/wiki/Blue_whale" title="Blue whale">whale</a>. Inside the whale's mouth, Dory communicates with the whale, which carries them to <a href="/wiki/Port_Jackson" title="Port Jackson">Port Jackson</a> and expels them through his blowhole. They meet Nigel, who recognizes Marlin from the stories he has heard, and he takes them to Sherman's office. Darla has just arrived and the dentist is handing Nemo to her. Nemo plays dead to save himself as Nigel arrives. Marlin sees Nemo and believes he is dead. In despair, Marlin leaves Dory and begins to swim home. Gill then helps Nemo escape into a drain that leads to the ocean. Dory loses her memory and becomes confused, and meets Nemo, who reached the ocean. Eventually, Dory's memory is restored after reading the word "Sydney" on a drainpipe. She directs Nemo to Marlin and they reunite, but then Dory is caught in a fishing net with a school of <a href="/wiki/Grouper" title="Grouper">grouper</a>. Nemo enters the net and orders the group to swim downward to break the net, enabling them to escape. After returning home, Nemo leaves for school, with Crush's son Squirt, and Marlin, no longer overprotective, proudly watches Nemo swim away with Dory at his side.</p>
<p>At the dentist's office, the high-tech filter breaks down and the Tank Gang escapes into the harbor, belatedly realizing they are still confined in plastic bags.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Finding_Nemo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The inspiration for Nemo sprang from multiple experiences, going back to when director <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Stanton" title="Andrew Stanton">Andrew Stanton</a> was a child, when he loved going to the dentist to see the fish tank, assuming that the fish were from the ocean and wanted to go home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-commentary_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-commentary-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1992, shortly after his son was born, he and his family took a trip to <a href="/wiki/Six_Flags_Discovery_Kingdom" title="Six Flags Discovery Kingdom">Six Flags Discovery Kingdom</a> (which was called Marine World at the time). There, after seeing the shark tube and various exhibits, he felt that the underwater world could be done beautifully in computer animation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Later, in 1997, he took his son for a walk in the park, but realized that he was over protecting him and lost an opportunity to have a father-son experience that day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-commentary_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-commentary-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In an interview with <a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_(magazine)" title="National Geographic (magazine)"><i>National Geographic</i> magazine</a>, he said that the idea for the characters of Marlin and Nemo came from a photograph of two clownfish peeking out of an <a href="/wiki/Sea_anemone" title="Sea anemone">anemone</a>:</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;"><i>It was so arresting. I had no idea what kind of fish they were, but I couldn't take my eyes off them. And as an entertainer, the fact that they were called clownfish—it was perfect. There's almost nothing more appealing than these little fish that want to play peekaboo with you</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>In addition, clownfish are colourful, but do not tend to come out of an anemone often. For a character who has to go on a dangerous journey, Stanton felt a clownfish was the perfect type of fish for the character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-commentary_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-commentary-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Pre-production of the film began in early 1997. Stanton began writing the screenplay during the post-production of <i><a href="/wiki/A_Bug%27s_Life" title="A Bug's Life">A Bug's Life</a></i>. As a result, <i>Finding Nemo</i> began production with a complete screenplay, something that co-director <a href="/wiki/Lee_Unkrich" title="Lee Unkrich">Lee Unkrich</a> called "very unusual for an animated film".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-commentary_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-commentary-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The artists took <a href="/wiki/Scuba_diving" title="Scuba diving">scuba diving</a> lessons to study the coral reef.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-commentary_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-commentary-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pixar_team_66%C3%A8me_Festival_de_Venise_(Mostra)_2_cropped.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="356" data-file-width="272" height="223" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Pixar_team_66%C3%A8me_Festival_de_Venise_%28Mostra%29_2_cropped.jpg/170px-Pixar_team_66%C3%A8me_Festival_de_Venise_%28Mostra%29_2_cropped.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Pixar_team_66%C3%A8me_Festival_de_Venise_%28Mostra%29_2_cropped.jpg/255px-Pixar_team_66%C3%A8me_Festival_de_Venise_%28Mostra%29_2_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/4a/Pixar_team_66%C3%A8me_Festival_de_Venise_%28Mostra%29_2_cropped.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>

<p>The idea for the initiation sequence came from a story conference between Andrew Stanton and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bob_Peterson_(animator)" title="Bob Peterson (animator)">Bob Peterson</a> while they were driving to record the actors. <a href="/wiki/Ellen_DeGeneres" title="Ellen DeGeneres">Ellen DeGeneres</a> was cast after Stanton watched <i><a href="/wiki/Ellen_(TV_series)" title="Ellen (TV series)">Ellen</a></i> with his wife and saw Ellen "change the subject five times before finishing one sentence".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-commentary_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-commentary-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The pelican character named Gerald (who in the final film ends up swallowing and choking on Marlin and Dory) was originally a friend of Nigel. They were going to play against each other with Nigel being neat and <a class="extiw" href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/fastidious" title="wikt:fastidious">fastidious</a> and Gerald being scruffy and sloppy. The filmmakers could not find an appropriate scene for them that did not slow the pace of the picture, so Gerald's character was minimized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-commentary_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-commentary-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Stanton himself provided the voice of Crush the sea turtle. He originally did the voice for the film's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animatics" title="Animatics">story reel</a>, and assumed they would find an actor later. When Stanton's performance became popular in test screenings, he decided to keep his performance in the film. He recorded all his dialogue while lying on a sofa in co-director Lee Unkrich's office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-commentary_5-7"><a href="#cite_note-commentary-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Crush's son Squirt was voiced by Nicholas Bird, the young son of fellow Pixar director <a href="/wiki/Brad_Bird" title="Brad Bird">Brad Bird</a>. According to Stanton, the elder Bird was playing a tape recording of his young son around the Pixar studios one day. Stanton felt the voice was "this generation's <a href="/wiki/Thumper_(Bambi)" title="Thumper (Bambi)">Thumper</a>" and immediately cast Nicholas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-commentary_5-8"><a href="#cite_note-commentary-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Megan_Mullally" title="Megan Mullally">Megan Mullally</a> was originally going to provide a voice in the film. According to Mullally, the producers were dissatisfied to learn that the voice of her character <a href="/wiki/Karen_Walker_(Will_%26_Grace)" title="Karen Walker (Will &amp; Grace)">Karen Walker</a> on the television show <i><a href="/wiki/Will_%26_Grace" title="Will &amp; Grace">Will &amp; Grace</a></i> was not her natural speaking voice. The producers hired her anyway, and then strongly encouraged her to use her Karen Walker voice for the role. When Mullally refused, she was dismissed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> To ensure that the movements of the fish in the film were believable, the animators took a crash course in fish biology and oceanography. They visited aquariums, went diving in Hawaii and received in-house lectures from an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ichthyologist" title="Ichthyologist">ichthyologist</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> As a result, Pixar's animator for Dory, <a href="/wiki/Gini_Cruz_Santos" title="Gini Cruz Santos">Gini Cruz Santos</a>, integrated "the fish movement, human movement, and facial expressions to make them look and feel like real characters."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Annieawards_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Annieawards-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yahoo_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The film was dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Glenn_McQueen" title="Glenn McQueen">Glenn McQueen</a>, a Pixar animator who died of <a href="/wiki/Melanoma" title="Melanoma">melanoma</a> in October 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Remembering_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Remembering-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Finding Nemo</i> shares many plot elements with <i><a href="/wiki/Pierrot_the_Clownfish" title="Pierrot the Clownfish">Pierrot the Clownfish</a></i>, a children's book published in 2002, but conceived in 1995. The author, Franck Le Calvez, sued Disney for infringement of his intellectual rights. The judge ruled against him, citing the color differences between Pierrot and Nemo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_game">Video game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Finding_Nemo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Video game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Finding_Nemo_(video_game)" title="Finding Nemo (video game)">Finding Nemo (video game)</a></div>
<p>A video game based on the film was released in 2003, for <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">PC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xbox" title="Xbox">Xbox</a>, <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PS2</a>, <a href="/wiki/GameCube" title="GameCube">GameCube</a> and <a href="/wiki/Game_Boy_Advance" title="Game Boy Advance">Game Boy Advance</a>. The goal of the game is to complete different levels under the roles of film protagonists Nemo, Marlin or Dory. It includes cutscenes from the movie, and each clip is based on a level. It was also the last Disney/Pixar game developed by <a href="/wiki/Traveller%27s_Tales" title="Traveller's Tales">Traveller's Tales</a>. Upon release, the game received mixed reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> A Game Boy Advance sequel, titled <i>Finding Nemo: The Continuing Adventures</i>, was released in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Finding_Nemo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Finding_Nemo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">review aggregator</a> website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> reported a 99% approval rating with an average rating of 8.6/10 based on 238 reviews. The site's consensus reads: "Breathtaking animation, talented vocal work, and a well-written screenplay add up to another Pixar success."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> Another review aggregation website <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 top reviews from mainstream critics, calculated a score of 90 out of 100 based on 38 reviews, indicating "universal acclaim."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> gave the film four out of four stars, calling it "one of those rare movies where I wanted to sit in the front row and let the images wash out to the edges of my field of vision".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> Broadway star <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Lane" title="Nathan Lane">Nathan Lane</a>, who was the voice of Timon the meerkat in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King" title="The Lion King">The Lion King</a></i>, said <i>Finding Nemo</i> was his favorite animated film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> Ed Park of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Village_Voice" title="The Village Voice">The Village Voice</a></i> gave the film a positive review, saying "It's an ocean of eye candy that tastes fresh even in this ADD-addled era of <i>SpongeBob SquarePants</i>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> Mark Caro of the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Tribune" title="Chicago Tribune">Chicago Tribune</a></i> gave the film four out of four stars, saying "You connect to these sea creatures as you rarely do with humans in big-screen adventures. The result: a true sunken treasure."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> Hazel-Dawn Dumpert of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L.A._Weekly" title="L.A. Weekly">L.A. Weekly</a></i> gave the film a positive review, saying "As gorgeous a film as Disney's ever put out, with astonishing qualities of light, movement, surface and color at the service of the best professional imaginations money can buy."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rottentomatoes1_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-rottentomatoes1-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> Jeff Strickler of the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Tribune" title="Star Tribune">Star Tribune</a></i> gave the film a positive review, saying "Proves that even when Pixar is not at the top of its game, it still produces better animation than some of its competitors on their best days."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rottentomatoes1_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-rottentomatoes1-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> Gene Seymour of <i><a href="/wiki/Newsday" title="Newsday">Newsday</a></i> gave the film three and a half stars out of four, saying "The underwater backdrops take your breath away. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Finding_Nemo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Finding Nemo</i> earned $380,843,261 in North America, and $555,900,000 in other countries, for a worldwide total of $936,743,261.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-BOM-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> It is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_highest-grossing_films" title="List of highest-grossing films">twenty-seventh highest-grossing film</a> and the second highest-grossing film of 2003, behind <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Return_of_the_King" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King">The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> Worldwide, it was the highest-grossing Pixar film, up until 2010 when <i><a href="/wiki/Toy_Story_3" title="Toy Story 3">Toy Story 3</a></i> surpassed it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In North America, <i>Finding Nemo</i> set an opening weekend record for an animated feature, making $70,251,710 (first surpassed by <i><a href="/wiki/Shrek_2" title="Shrek 2">Shrek 2</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> It became the highest-grossing animated film in North America ($339.7 million), outside North America ($528.2 million) and worldwide ($867.9 million), in all three occasions out-grossing <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King" title="The Lion King">The Lion King</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup> In North America, it was surpassed by both <i>Shrek 2</i> in 2004, and <i>Toy Story 3</i> in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> After the re-release of <i>The Lion King</i> in 2011 and after <i><a href="/wiki/Despicable_Me_2" title="Despicable Me 2">Despicable Me 2</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Frozen_(2013_film)" title="Frozen (2013 film)">Frozen</a></i> passed it in 2014, it stands as the fifth highest-grossing animated film in these regions. Outside North America, it stands as the fifth highest-grossing animated film. Worldwide, it now ranks fourth among animated films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The film had impressive box office runs in many international markets. In Japan, its highest-grossing market after North America, it grossed ¥11.2 billion ($102.4 million), becoming the highest-grossing foreign animated film in local currency (yen).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-onebill_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-onebill-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> It has only been surpassed by <i><a href="/wiki/Frozen_(2013_film)" title="Frozen (2013 film)">Frozen</a></i> (¥12.1 billion).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup> Following in biggest grosses are the U.K., Ireland and Malta, where it grossed £37.2 million ($67.1 million), France and the Maghreb region ($64.8 million), Germany ($53.9 million) and Spain ($29.5 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="3D_re-release">3D re-release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Finding_Nemo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 3D re-release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After the success of the 3D re-release of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King" title="The Lion King">The Lion King</a></i>, Disney and Pixar re-released <i>Finding Nemo</i> in 3D on September 14, 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> with a conversion cost estimated to be below $5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hollywood3D_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hollywood3D-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup> For the opening weekend of its 3D re-release in North America, <i>Finding Nemo</i> grossed $16.7 million, debuting at the No. 2 spot behind <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil:_Retribution" title="Resident Evil: Retribution">Resident Evil: Retribution</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup> In total, it earned $41.1 million in the United States, and $31.0 million outside the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_concerns_and_consequences">Environmental concerns and consequences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Finding_Nemo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Environmental concerns and consequences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film's use of clownfish prompted mass purchase of the fish breed as pets in the United States, even though the story portrayed the use of fish as pets negatively and suggested that saltwater aquariums are notably tricky and expensive to maintain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58"><span>[</span>58<span>]</span></a></sup> The demand for clownfish was supplied by large-scale harvesting of tropical fish in regions like <a href="/wiki/Vanuatu" title="Vanuatu">Vanuatu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59"><span>[</span>59<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Tourism_Australia" title="Tourism Australia">Australian Tourism Commission</a> (ATC) launched several marketing campaigns in China and the United States to improve tourism in Australia, many of them utilizing <i>Finding Nemo</i> clips.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60"><span>[</span>60<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a> used <i>Finding Nemo</i> to draw tourists to promote its state for vacationers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup> According to <i>National Geographic</i>, "Ironically, Finding Nemo, a movie about the anguish of a captured clownfish, caused home-aquarium demand for them to triple."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-natgeowild_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-natgeowild-63"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The reaction to the film by the general public has led to environmental devastation for the clownfish, and has provoked an outcry from several environmental protection agencies, including the Marine Aquarium Council, Australia. The demand for tropical fish skyrocketed after the film's release, causing reef species decimation in Vanuatu and many other reef areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64"><span>[</span>64<span>]</span></a></sup> After seeing the film, some aquarium owners released their pet fish into the ocean, but failed to release them into the correct oceanic habitat, which <a href="/wiki/Invasive_species" title="Invasive species">introduced species that are harmful</a> to the indigenous environment, a practice that is harming reefs worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65"><span>[</span>65<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66"><span>[</span>66<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Finding_Nemo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Finding Nemo</i> was released on DVD and VHS on November 4, 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67"><span>[</span>67<span>]</span></a></sup> The DVD release included an original short film, <i><a href="/wiki/Exploring_the_Reef" title="Exploring the Reef">Exploring the Reef</a></i>, and a 1989 short animated film, <i><a href="/wiki/Knick_Knack" title="Knick Knack">Knick Knack</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68"><span>[</span>68<span>]</span></a></sup> The film had a home video release on both Blu-ray and Blu-ray 3D on December 4, 2012, with both a 3-disc and a 5-disc set.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69"><span>[</span>69<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtrack">Soundtrack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Finding_Nemo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Soundtrack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><b>Finding Nemo</b></i>, the original soundtrack album,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70"><span>[</span>70<span>]</span></a></sup> was the first Pixar film not to be scored by <a href="/wiki/Randy_Newman" title="Randy Newman">Randy Newman</a>. The album was scored by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Newman" title="Thomas Newman">Thomas Newman</a> and released on May 20, 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71"><span>[</span>71<span>]</span></a></sup> The album was nominated for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Original_Music_Score" title="Academy Award for Original Music Score">Academy Award for Original Music Score</a>, losing to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Return_of_the_King" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King">The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72"><span>[</span>72<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Theme_park_attractions">Theme park attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Finding_Nemo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Theme park attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Finding Nemo</i> has inspired numerous attractions and properties at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney_Parks" title="Disney Parks">Disney Parks</a> around the world, including: <a href="/wiki/Turtle_Talk_with_Crush" title="Turtle Talk with Crush">Turtle Talk with Crush</a> which opened in 2004 at <a href="/wiki/Epcot" title="Epcot">Epcot</a>, 2005 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney_California_Adventure_Park" title="Disney California Adventure Park">Disney California Adventure Park</a>, 2008 in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Disneyland" title="Hong Kong Disneyland">Hong Kong Disneyland</a>, and 2009 in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_DisneySea" title="Tokyo DisneySea">Tokyo DisneySea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finding_Nemo_Submarine_Voyage" title="Finding Nemo Submarine Voyage">Finding Nemo Submarine Voyage</a> which opened in 2007 in <a href="/wiki/Disneyland" title="Disneyland">Disneyland Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Seas_with_Nemo_%26_Friends" title="The Seas with Nemo &amp; Friends">The Seas with Nemo &amp; Friends</a> which opened in 2007 at Epcot, <i>Finding Nemo – The Musical</i> which opened in 2007 in <a href="/wiki/Disney%27s_Animal_Kingdom" title="Disney's Animal Kingdom">Disney's Animal Kingdom</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Crush%27s_Coaster" title="Crush's Coaster">Crush's Coaster</a> which opened in 2007 at <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Studios_Park" title="Walt Disney Studios Park">Walt Disney Studios Park</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73"><span>[</span>73<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74"><span>[</span>74<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75"><span>[</span>75<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Finding_Nemo.C2.A0.E2.80.93_The_Musical"><i>Finding Nemo – The Musical</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Finding_Nemo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Finding Nemo – The Musical">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Finding_Nemo_The_Musical_at_Animal_Kingdom.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2320" data-file-width="3088" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Finding_Nemo_The_Musical_at_Animal_Kingdom.JPG/220px-Finding_Nemo_The_Musical_at_Animal_Kingdom.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Finding_Nemo_The_Musical_at_Animal_Kingdom.JPG/330px-Finding_Nemo_The_Musical_at_Animal_Kingdom.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Finding_Nemo_The_Musical_at_Animal_Kingdom.JPG/440px-Finding_Nemo_The_Musical_at_Animal_Kingdom.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Finding_Nemo_The_Musical_at_Animal_Kingdom.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The "Theater In The Wild", home to <i>Finding Nemo – The Musical</i></div>

<p><br/>
The stage musical <i>Tarzan Rocks!</i> occupied the Theater in the Wild at <a href="/wiki/Disney%27s_Animal_Kingdom" title="Disney's Animal Kingdom">Disney's Animal Kingdom</a> in <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando, Florida</a> from 1999 to 2006. When the musical closed in January 2006, it was rumored that a musical adaptation of <i>Finding Nemo</i> would replace it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-magic_76-0"><a href="#cite_note-magic-76"><span>[</span>76<span>]</span></a></sup> This was confirmed in April 2006, when Disney announced that the musical adaptation of <i>Finding Nemo</i>, with new songs written by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a>-winning <i><a href="/wiki/Avenue_Q" title="Avenue Q">Avenue Q</a></i> composer <a href="/wiki/Robert_Lopez" title="Robert Lopez">Robert Lopez</a> and his wife, <a href="/wiki/Kristen_Anderson-Lopez" title="Kristen Anderson-Lopez">Kristen Anderson-Lopez</a>, would "combine puppets, dancers, acrobats and animated backdrops" and open in late 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77"><span>[</span>77<span>]</span></a></sup> Years later, Anderson-Lopez explained that she had written a compact 15-minute <i><a href="/wiki/A_cappella" title="A cappella">a cappella</a></i> version of the story of <a href="/wiki/Oedipus" title="Oedipus">Oedipus</a>; someone at Disney read it and recognized her talent for condensing material, and offered her the opportunity to make a pitch for the <i>Finding Nemo</i> project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78"><span>[</span>78<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Tony Award-winning director Peter Brosius signed on to direct the show, with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Curry_(puppet_designer)" title="Michael Curry (puppet designer)">Michael Curry</a>, who designed puppets for Disney's successful stage version of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King_(musical)" title="The Lion King (musical)">The Lion King</a></i>, serving as leading puppet and production designer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wired1_79-0"><a href="#cite_note-wired1-79"><span>[</span>79<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Anderson-Lopez said that the couple agreed to write the adaptation of "one of their favorite movies of all time" after considering "the idea of people coming in [to see the musical] at 4, 5 or 6 and saying, 'I want to do that'....So we want to take it as seriously as we would a Broadway show".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orlando_80-0"><a href="#cite_note-orlando-80"><span>[</span>80<span>]</span></a></sup> To condense the feature-length film to 30 minutes, she and Lopez focused on a single theme from the movie, the idea that "the world's dangerous and beautiful".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orlando_80-1"><a href="#cite_note-orlando-80"><span>[</span>80<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The 40-minute show (which is performed six times daily) opened on January 2, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wired1_79-1"><a href="#cite_note-wired1-79"><span>[</span>79<span>]</span></a></sup> Several musical numbers took direct inspiration from lines in the film, including "(In The) Big Blue World", "Fish Are Friends, Not Food", "Just Keep Swimming", and "Go With the Flow".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wired1_79-2"><a href="#cite_note-wired1-79"><span>[</span>79<span>]</span></a></sup> In January 2007, a New York studio recording of the show was released on <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes</a>, with Lopez and Anderson-Lopez providing the voices for Marlin and Dory, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wired1_79-3"><a href="#cite_note-wired1-79"><span>[</span>79<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Avenue Q</i> star <a href="/wiki/Stephanie_D%27Abruzzo" title="Stephanie D'Abruzzo">Stephanie D'Abruzzo</a> also appeared on the recording, as Sheldon/Deb.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wired1_79-4"><a href="#cite_note-wired1-79"><span>[</span>79<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Finding Nemo</i> was the first non-musical animated film to which Disney added songs to produce a stage musical.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wired1_79-5"><a href="#cite_note-wired1-79"><span>[</span>79<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2009, <i>Finding Nemo – The Musical</i> was honored with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thea_(award)#15th_Annual_Thea_Award_Recipients" title="Thea (award)">Thea Award</a> for Best Live Show from the <a href="/wiki/Themed_Entertainment_Association" title="Themed Entertainment Association">Themed Entertainment Association</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81"><span>[</span>81<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Finding_Nemo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><span id="Sequel"></span><span id="Sequels"></span></p>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Finding_Dory" title="Finding Dory">Finding Dory</a></div>
<p>In 2005, after disagreements between Disney's <a href="/wiki/Michael_Eisner" title="Michael Eisner">Michael Eisner</a> and Pixar's <a href="/wiki/Steve_Jobs" title="Steve Jobs">Steve Jobs</a> over the distribution of Pixar's films, Disney announced that they would be creating a new animation studio, <a href="/wiki/Circle_7_Animation" title="Circle 7 Animation">Circle 7 Animation</a>, to make sequels to the seven Disney-owned Pixar films (which consisted of the films released between 1995 and 2006).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82"><span>[</span>82<span>]</span></a></sup> The studio had put <i><a href="/wiki/Toy_Story_3" title="Toy Story 3">Toy Story 3</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Monsters_University" title="Monsters University">Monsters, Inc. 2</a></i> into development, and had hired screenwriter Laurie Craig to write a draft for <i>Finding Nemo 2</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83"><span>[</span>83<span>]</span></a></sup> Circle 7 was subsequently shut down after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Iger" title="Robert Iger">Robert Iger</a> replaced Eisner as CEO of Disney and arranged the acquisition of Pixar.</p>
<p>In July 2012, it was reported that <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Stanton" title="Andrew Stanton">Andrew Stanton</a> was developing a sequel to <i>Finding Nemo</i>, to be titled <i>Finding Dory</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84"><span>[</span>84<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sequel_85-0"><a href="#cite_note-sequel-85"><span>[</span>85<span>]</span></a></sup> with Victoria Strouse writing the script and scheduled to be released in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FN2_86-0"><a href="#cite_note-FN2-86"><span>[</span>86<span>]</span></a></sup> The same day the news of a potential sequel broke, director Andrew Stanton called into question the accuracy of these reports. The message said, "Didn't you all learn from <i><a href="/wiki/Chicken_Little_(2005_film)" title="Chicken Little (2005 film)">Chicken Little</a></i>? Everyone calm down. Don't believe everything you read. Nothing to see here now. #skyisnotfalling".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87"><span>[</span>87<span>]</span></a></sup> According to the report by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> published in August 2012, Ellen DeGeneres was in negotiations to reprise her role of Dory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88"><span>[</span>88<span>]</span></a></sup> In September 2012, Stanton confirmed, saying, "What was immediately on the list was writing a second <i><a href="/wiki/John_Carter_(film)" title="John Carter (film)">Carter</a></i> movie. When that went away, everything slid up. I know I'll be accused by more sarcastic people that it's a reaction to <i>Carter</i> not doing well, but only in its timing, but not in its conceit".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89"><span>[</span>89<span>]</span></a></sup> In February 2013, it was confirmed by the press that Albert Brooks would reprise the role of Marlin in the sequel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90"><span>[</span>90<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2013, Disney announced the sequel, <i>Finding Dory</i>, confirming that Ellen DeGeneres and Albert Brooks would be reprising their roles as Dory and Marlin, respectively. It was scheduled to be released on November 25, 2015,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91"><span>[</span>91<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92"><span>[</span>92<span>]</span></a></sup> but the film's ending was revised after Pixar executives viewed <a href="/wiki/Blackfish_(film)" title="Blackfish (film)"><i>Blackfish</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93"><span>[</span>93<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94"><span>[</span>94<span>]</span></a></sup> On September 18, 2013, it was announced that the film would be pushed back to a June 17, 2016, release. Pixar's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good_Dinosaur" title="The Good Dinosaur">The Good Dinosaur</a></i> was moved to the November 25, 2015 slot to allow more time for production of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slashfilm.com_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-slashfilm.com-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
