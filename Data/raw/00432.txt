<p><b>Baxter Humby</b> (born October 26, 1972) is a Canadian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kickboxer" title="Kickboxer">kickboxer</a> known as "The One Armed Bandit" due to his missing right hand, which was amputated at birth just below his elbow after becoming entangled with the <a href="/wiki/Umbilical_cord" title="Umbilical cord">umbilical cord</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> He is the only man in the world to win world titles with only one hand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paradisewarriorretreat.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-paradisewarriorretreat.com-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baxter_Humby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baxter Humby was born in <a href="/wiki/Winnipeg" title="Winnipeg">Winnipeg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, in 1974.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paradisewarriorretreat.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-paradisewarriorretreat.com-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baxter_Humby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kickboxing">Kickboxing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baxter_Humby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Kickboxing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Baxter is the current IMTC (International <a href="/wiki/Muay_Thai" title="Muay Thai">Muay Thai</a> Council) World Super Welterweight Champion. He holds a number of different title belts including WBC Super Welterweight National Champion (2010), IKKC USA Kickboxing Champion, IMTC World Middleweight Champion and IKBA International Kickboxing Champion.</p>
<p>His interest in sports led him to take up running at age 11 and martial arts at 17. He ran for the Canadian National Track Team (Paralympics) in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> in 1992 and in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> in 1994.</p>
<p>He won the Canadian Super Welterweight Kickboxing Championship in 1996, and afterwards, in 1997, moved to California to pursue a professional <a href="/wiki/Kickboxing" title="Kickboxing">kickboxing</a> career.</p>
<p>He currently trains with Muay Thai World Champion trainers, Master Tom Khamvongsa and David Huey, at the Muay Thai Academy in North Hollywood, CA.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Films">Films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baxter_Humby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, Humby travelled to <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> to star in the film "One Arm Hero". It was produced by martial arts film star Carter Wong (<i><a href="/wiki/Big_Trouble_in_Little_China" title="Big Trouble in Little China">Big Trouble in Little China</a></i>) and directed by Li Man Sing.</p>
<p>Baxter Humby worked as a stunt double for <a href="/wiki/Tobey_Maguire" title="Tobey Maguire">Tobey Maguire</a> in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_3" title="Spider-Man 3">Spider-Man 3</a></i>, during a fight scene in which <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man" title="Spider-Man">Spider-Man</a> punches through <a href="/wiki/Sandman_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Sandman (Marvel Comics)">Sandman</a>'s chest.</p>
<p>Humby played the role of Eduardo Romero, a corrupt Mexican official, on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Shield" title="The Shield">The Shield</a></i>.</p>
