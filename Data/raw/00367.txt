<p>The <b>Batcycle</b> is the fictional personal <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle" title="Motorcycle">motorcycle</a> of <a href="/wiki/Comic_book" title="Comic book">comic book</a> <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">superhero</a> <a href="/wiki/Batman" title="Batman">Batman</a>. In the comic book universe, Batman's personal Batcycle is a modified street-bike with a 786 cc liquid-cooled <a href="/wiki/V4_engine" title="V4 engine">V-4 engine</a>. It contains a computer-controlled <a href="/wiki/Carburetor" title="Carburetor">carburetor</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bulletproof" title="Bulletproof">bulletproof</a> wind-guard.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_media">In other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcycle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Live-action">Live-action</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcycle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Live-action">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Batman_.28TV_series.29"><i>Batman</i> (TV series)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcycle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Batman (TV series)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Batcycle.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="234" data-file-width="250" height="206" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/eb/Batcycle.jpg/220px-Batcycle.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/e/eb/Batcycle.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/e/eb/Batcycle.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Batcycle.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Batcycle from <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_(1966_film)" title="Batman (1966 film)">Batman</a></i> (1966).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Batcycle made its first appearance in <a href="/wiki/1966_in_television" title="1966 in television">1966</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Batman_(TV_series)" title="Batman (TV series)"><i>Batman</i></a> TV series. It was a 1965 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harley_Davidson" title="Harley Davidson">Harley Davidson</a> with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Side_car" title="Side car">side car</a>, but it was taken on <a href="/wiki/Lease" title="Lease">lease</a> and was only used for the first season <a href="/wiki/Episode" title="Episode">episode</a> "Not Yet, He Ain't".</p>
<p>Later that year, a new Batcycle was introduced. It was produced by Kustomotive, conceived by Dan Dempski, designed by Tom Daniel, and built by Dan and Korky Korkes using a <a href="/wiki/Yamaha_Motor_Company" title="Yamaha Motor Company">Yamaha</a> Catalina 250. It was leased to <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a> starting on April 18, 1966 for <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">$</a>50 a week with an additional $350 up front. The new Batcycle was first used in the 1966 film <a href="/wiki/Batman_(1966_film)" title="Batman (1966 film)"><i>Batman</i></a> and continued to appear in the rest of the TV series. Hubie Kerns and Victor Paul did most of the stunt work with the motorcycle throughout the series. The total amount paid to Kustomotive was $2,500. When the series was canceled, Kustomotive used the Batcycle in <a href="/wiki/Auto_show" title="Auto show">car shows</a>, paying royalties to Greenway, 20th Century Fox, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Periodical_Publications" title="National Periodical Publications">National Periodical Publications</a>. Kustomotive built four replicas of the Batcycle for tours.</p>
<p>Robin's sidecar (a detachable self-propelled vehicle) was authenticated by <a href="/wiki/Burt_Ward" title="Burt Ward">Burt Ward</a> on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Treasure" title="Hollywood Treasure">Hollywood Treasure</a></i> and sold at auction for $30,000.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Batblade_.28Batman_.26_Robin.29">Batblade (<i>Batman &amp; Robin</i>)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcycle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Batblade (Batman &amp; Robin)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <b>Batblade</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> is the name of a specialized <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle" title="Motorcycle">motorcycle</a> used by <a href="/wiki/Batgirl" title="Batgirl">Batgirl</a> (<a href="/wiki/Alicia_Silverstone" title="Alicia Silverstone">Alicia Silverstone</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/1997_in_film" title="1997 in film">1997</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_%26_Robin_(film)" title="Batman &amp; Robin (film)">Batman &amp; Robin</a></i>. This vehicle can function under the most extreme weather conditions.</p>
<p>The Batblade was built on the body of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drag_racer" title="Drag racer">drag racer</a>: the rear tire comes not from a motorcycle, but from a car. Originally, the equipment for fast pursuits was conceived, and, with the help of the special effects, it functions also on smooth underground and under most extreme weather conditions.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Dark_Knight_and_The_Dark_Knight_Rises"><i>The Dark Knight</i> and <i>The Dark Knight Rises</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcycle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Dark Knight and The Dark Knight Rises">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Batpod.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" data-file-width="1024" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Batpod.jpg/220px-Batpod.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Batpod.jpg/330px-Batpod.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Batpod.jpg/440px-Batpod.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Batpod.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Batpod from <i>The Dark Knight</i> and <i>The Dark Knight Rises</i>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Batcycle is known as the <b>Batpod</b> for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_(film)" title="The Dark Knight (film)">The Dark Knight</a></i> (2008) and "<a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_Rises" title="The Dark Knight Rises">The Dark Knight Rises</a>" (2012).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The bike has 20" front and rear tires and is powered by a high-performance, water-cooled, single-cylinder engine - geared toward the lower end for faster acceleration and with no exhaust pipes. The exhaust is routed through the hollow steel/aluminum/magnesium tubing used for the frame of the bike.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-popmech_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-popmech-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> The Batpod is steered by the shoulders instead of hands, and the rider's arms are protected by shields. The two foot pegs are set 3½ feet apart on either side of the tank, which the rider lies on, belly down.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> The engine noise was designed around the <a href="/wiki/Shepard_tone" title="Shepard tone">shepard tone</a>, for which sound effects came, in part, from the sound of the <a href="/wiki/Tesla_Roadster" title="Tesla Roadster">Tesla Roadster</a>'s electric motor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The Batpod ejects out of the <a href="/wiki/Batmobile" title="Batmobile">Tumbler</a>'s front end, with the Tumbler's front wheels becoming the Batpod's front and rear wheels. Because the Tumbler is ordered to <a href="/wiki/Self-destruct" title="Self-destruct">self-destruct</a>, the Batpod allows Batman to continue his pursuit. For the film, the bike is armed with grappling hooks, cannons, and machine guns. Six models were constructed for the film's production to anticipate some of them crashing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> One action sequence in the film shows the wheels rolling against their normal <a href="/wiki/Rotation" title="Rotation">axes</a>, seemingly for added stability in sharp turns or other maneuvers. This also allows for instant changes in direction - if the driver approaches a wall, the Batpod's central frame will rotate to keep the driver upright (the inspiration for this useful chassis feature may well have been the innovative wheel configuration on a <a href="/wiki/Omni_wheel" title="Omni wheel">Killough Platform</a> into omnidirectionally mobile chasses). The Batpod's chassis also elongates, allowing the rider to pass under low-hanging obstacles, as when Batman slaloms underneath a tractor trailer the Joker is driving.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-popmech_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-popmech-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> The term "Batpod" is mentioned by <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Pennyworth" title="Alfred Pennyworth">Alfred Pennyworth</a> only once in the movie.</p>
<p>The Batpod is used again in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_Rises" title="The Dark Knight Rises">The Dark Knight Rises</a></i> (2012). At first, it serves as Batman's primary vehicle. Later, <a href="/wiki/Catwoman" title="Catwoman">Selina Kyle</a> uses the Batpod during the final battle against <a href="/wiki/Bane_(comics)" title="Bane (comics)">Bane</a>'s forces for the fate of Gotham. Batman uses the <a href="/wiki/Batplane" title="Batplane">Bat</a> to fight Bane's stolen Tumblers from the air while Selina uses the Batpod to open a tunnel to allow Gotham civilians to escape, to shoot Bane, and to destroy at least two of Bane's Tumblers while tracking the nuclear bomb Bane and <a href="/wiki/Talia_al_Ghul" title="Talia al Ghul">Talia al Ghul</a> intended to use to destroy the city. During filming on August 9, 2011, a stunt performer collided with an IMAX camera while filming a chase scene involving Kyle's Batpod.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Animation">Animation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcycle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Animation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Batman:_The_Animated_Series"><i>Batman: The Animated Series</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcycle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Batman: The Animated Series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Batcycle was one of the many vehicles used by Batman and Robin in <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_The_Animated_Series" title="Batman: The Animated Series">Batman: The Animated Series</a></i>. The Batcycle was themed to look like Batman's other vehicles, the <a href="/wiki/Batmobile" title="Batmobile">Batmobile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Batplane" title="Batplane">Batwing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Batboat" title="Batboat">Batboat</a>. Batman would use it in certain episodes, as would Robin. Perhaps the most important part the Batcycle played in the series was in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_Mask_of_the_Phantasm" title="Batman: Mask of the Phantasm">Batman: Mask of the Phantasm</a></i>, where Batman jumped off the Batcycle so it would crash into a giant fan that was being controlled by <a href="/wiki/Joker_(comics)" title="Joker (comics)">the Joker</a>. The Batcycle saw a little more use from Robin, particularly in the episode "<a href="/wiki/Robin%27s_Reckoning" title="Robin's Reckoning">Robin's Reckoning</a>." Robin would continue to use a variation of the Batcycle in later seasons after he had become <a href="/wiki/Nightwing" title="Nightwing">Nightwing</a>. Batman always donned a helmet, modeled to accommodate his cowl's ears, before driving the Batcycle.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Batman"><i>The Batman</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcycle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The Batman">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Batman_(TV_series)" title="The Batman (TV series)">The Batman (TV series)</a></div>
<p>The Batcycle makes only a few appearances in <i>The Batman</i>. The first is in the episode, "The Cat and the Bat," in which <a href="/wiki/Catwoman" title="Catwoman">Catwoman</a> steals Batman's utility belt and inadvertently sends the Batmobile careening out of control on autopilot. Batman uses the Batcycle to catch-up with and retrieve the other vehicle. The Batcycle's second appearance on the program is in the episode, "RPM". The Batmobile is wrecked during a pursuit of <a href="/wiki/Gearhead" title="Gearhead">Gearhead</a>, and Batman must use the Batcycle to continue the battle against the villain when the rebuilt Batmobile is not fully operational in time. The Batcycle ends up destroyed after Gearhead infects its computerized system with a <a href="/wiki/Nanotechnology" title="Nanotechnology">nanotech</a> virus during this same episode.</p>
<p>Robin uses a variation of the Batcycle featuring his own colors in <i>The Batman</i>. Robin also has a similarly styled bike in the <a href="/wiki/Teen_Titans_(TV_series)" title="Teen Titans (TV series)"><i>Teen Titans</i></a> animated series.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Toys">Toys</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcycle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Toys">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Art_Asylum" title="Art Asylum">Art Asylum</a> created a <a href="/wiki/Toy" title="Toy">toy</a> version of the Batcycle for C3 Sets Wave 2 of <a href="/wiki/Minimates" title="Minimates">Minimates</a> in 2005. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LEGO" title="LEGO">LEGO</a> paired the Batcycle with "<a href="/wiki/Harley_Quinn" title="Harley Quinn">Harley Quinn</a>'s Hammer Truck" for their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LEGO_Batman" title="LEGO Batman">LEGO Batman</a> line. In 2008, Mattell's Hot Wheels line released a 1/50 scale of the 1966 Batcycle, and in 2009, released a 1/12 scale of the same model. Both featured detachable sidecars. An upgraded 'Elite' version of the 1/12 model is also expected, with even greater detail. The Batblade has also been mentioned as a candidate for the 2010 series of 1/50 Bat-vehicles.</p>
