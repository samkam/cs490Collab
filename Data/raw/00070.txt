<p><i><b>And Then There Were None</b></i> (a.k.a. <i><b>Ten Little Indians</b></i>) is a 1974 film version of the <a href="/wiki/Agatha_Christie" title="Agatha Christie">Agatha Christie</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mystery_novel" title="Mystery novel">mystery novel</a> <a href="/wiki/And_Then_There_Were_None" title="And Then There Were None">of the same name</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Two previous films were released in <a href="/wiki/And_Then_There_Were_None_(1945_film)" title="And Then There Were None (1945 film)">1945</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ten_Little_Indians_(1965_film)" title="Ten Little Indians (1965 film)">1965</a>, and a videotaped made-for-<a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> version was broadcast in 1959.</p>
<p>This was the second of three versions of Christie's novel to be adapted to the screen by producer <a href="/wiki/Harry_Alan_Towers" title="Harry Alan Towers">Harry Alan Towers</a>; the aforementioned 1965 version, this one made in 1974, and another in 1989.</p>
<p>It follows the script of the <a href="/wiki/Ten_Little_Indians_(1965_film)" title="Ten Little Indians (1965 film)">1965 version</a>, right down to calling the <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Reed" title="Oliver Reed">Oliver Reed</a> character "Hugh" (a name change made to accommodate <a href="/wiki/Hugh_O%27Brian" title="Hugh O'Brian">Hugh O'Brian</a> in the earlier version) instead of "Phillip," which was character's name in the novel and play.</p>
<p>This particular adaptation is set in an abandoned hotel in the Iranian desert; the film was shot in the Shah Abbas Hotel (now known as the <a href="/wiki/Abbasi_Hotel" title="Abbasi Hotel">Abbasi Hotel</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> during its pre-<a href="/wiki/Iranian_Revolution" title="Iranian Revolution">revolution</a> days. (The 1965 version was set at a snowed-in mountain chalet, and the 1989 one in the African savanna.)</p>
<p>The film is an hour and thirty-eight minutes long, and was the first version of the novel to be filmed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colour" title="Colour">colour</a>.</p>
<p>Some versions of the film feature a pre-credit sequence that shows the guests arriving by plane at an airport in Iran, where they subsequently board a helicopter to be transported to the hotel. This prologue was cut from the U.S. release.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Herbert_Lom" title="Herbert Lom">Herbert Lom</a>, who plays Dr. Armstrong here, also starred in the 1989 version as the General.</p>
<p>The film was directed by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Collinson_(film_director)" title="Peter Collinson (film director)">Peter Collinson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=And_Then_There_Were_None_(1974_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A group of 10 people, strangers to one another, have all traveled to a hotel located deep in the deserts of Iran. Upon arrival they discover that their host is mysteriously absent. They are accused via a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magnetic_tape_sound_recording" title="Magnetic tape sound recording">tape recording</a> by the host, U.N. Owen, someone none of them has ever met, of having committed various crimes in the past which went unpunished by the law.</p>
<p>As guests start to die, the remainder deduce that their unseen host is determined to murder them all. Since a search proves that there is no one hiding, they realize that the murderer must be one of them.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast_and_characters">Cast and characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=And_Then_There_Were_None_(1974_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cast and characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Charles_Aznavour" title="Charles Aznavour">Charles Aznavour</a> as Michel Raven, entertainer. Raven is accused of having run over two people in Paris while driving under the influence of alcohol.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/St%C3%A9phane_Audran" title="Stéphane Audran">Stéphane Audran</a> as Ilona Morgan, actress. Ilona is accused of having been responsible for the death of her husband.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Elke_Sommer" title="Elke Sommer">Elke Sommer</a> as Vera Clyde, secretary. Mr. Owen accuses her of having murdered her sister's fiancé.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gert_Fr%C3%B6be" title="Gert Fröbe">Gert Fröbe</a> as Wilhelm Blore, police official. Accused of committing perjury to frame an innocent man, who subsequently died in prison.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Herbert_Lom" title="Herbert Lom">Herbert Lom</a> as Edward Armstrong, doctor. Accused of having operated on a woman while drunk, inadvertently causing her death.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Reed" title="Oliver Reed">Oliver Reed</a> as Hugh Lombard, businessman. Accused of murdering the young woman who was to bear his child out of wedlock.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Richard_Attenborough" title="Richard Attenborough">Richard Attenborough</a> as Arthur Cannon, judge. Accused by Mr. Owen of having sentenced an innocent man to death by hanging.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Orson_Welles" title="Orson Welles">Orson Welles</a> as "U.N. Owen" — the Voice on the Tape</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Maria_Rohm" title="Maria Rohm">Maria Rohm</a> as Elsa Martino, servant. Accused of causing the death of her invalid employer, assisted by her husband.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alberto_de_Mendoza" title="Alberto de Mendoza">Alberto de Mendoza</a> as Otto Martino, servant. Accused of helping his wife murder their invalid employer.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Adolfo_Celi" title="Adolfo Celi">Adolfo Celi</a> as André Salvé, military general. Accused of having been responsible for the deaths of five men who were under his command.</li>
</ul>
