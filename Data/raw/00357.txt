<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Alice in Wonderland (Disney franchise)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><i><b>Alice in Wonderland</b></i> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Company" title="Walt Disney Company">Disney</a> franchise, commencing in 1951 with the theatrical release of the film <i><a href="/wiki/Alice_in_Wonderland_(1951_film)" title="Alice in Wonderland (1951 film)">Alice in Wonderland</a></i>. The film is an adaption of the books by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lewis_Caroll" title="Lewis Caroll">Lewis Caroll</a>, which featured his character <a href="/wiki/Alice_(Alice%27s_Adventures_in_Wonderland)" title="Alice (Alice's Adventures in Wonderland)">Alice</a>. Disney has done various things with the property, and most notably reimagined the story in 2010 with a live-action film.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Films">Films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alice_in_Wonderland_.28animated.29"><i>Alice in Wonderland</i> (animated)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Alice in Wonderland (animated)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/Alice_in_Wonderland_(1951_film)" title="Alice in Wonderland (1951 film)">Alice in Wonderland</a></b></i> is a 1951 British-American animated fantasy comedy-adventure film produced by Walt Disney Productions and based primarily on Lewis Carroll's Alice's Adventures in Wonderland with several additional elements from Through the Looking-Glass. The 13th in the Walt Disney Animated Classics series, the film was released in New York City and London on July 26, 1951. The film features the voices of Kathryn Beaumont (who later voiced Wendy Darling in the 1953 Disney film Peter Pan) as Alice, and Ed Wynn as the Mad Hatter. The theme song, "Alice in Wonderland", has since become a jazz standard.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alice_in_Wonderland_.28live-action.29"><i>Alice in Wonderland</i> (live-action)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Alice in Wonderland (live-action)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/Alice_in_Wonderland_(2010_film)" title="Alice in Wonderland (2010 film)">Alice in Wonderland</a></b></i> is a 2010 American fantasy film[5] directed by Tim Burton and written by Linda Woolverton. Released by Walt Disney Pictures, the film stars Mia Wasikowska as Alice Kingsleigh with Johnny Depp, Anne Hathaway and Helena Bonham Carter. The film was shot in the United Kingdom and the United States.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alice_in_Wonderland:_Through_the_Looking_Glass"><i>Alice in Wonderland: Through the Looking Glass</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Alice in Wonderland: Through the Looking Glass">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/Alice_in_Wonderland:_Through_the_Looking_Glass" title="Alice in Wonderland: Through the Looking Glass">Alice in Wonderland: Through the Looking Glass</a></b></i> is an upcoming American fantasy film directed by James Bobin and written by Linda Woolverton. It is the sequel to the 2010 film Alice in Wonderland. The film stars Mia Wasikowska, Johnny Depp, Helena Bonham Carter, Anne Hathaway, Sacha Baron Cohen and Rhys Ifans. The film is scheduled to be released by Walt Disney Pictures on May 27, 2016.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="TV_series">TV series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: TV series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Adventures_in_Wonderland"><i>Adventures in Wonderland</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Adventures in Wonderland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/Adventures_in_Wonderland" title="Adventures in Wonderland">Adventures in Wonderland</a></b></i> is a live-action musical television series based on Walt Disney's animated classic Alice in Wonderland. In the series, Alice (played by Elisabeth Harnois), was portrayed as a girl who can come and go from Wonderland simply by walking through her mirror (a reference to Wonderland's source material Lewis Carroll's Through the Looking-Glass).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mickey_Mouse_Clubhouse"><i>Mickey Mouse Clubhouse</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mickey Mouse Clubhouse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An episode of <i><b><a href="/wiki/Mickey_Mouse_Clubhouse" title="Mickey Mouse Clubhouse">Mickey Mouse Clubhouse</a></b></i>, entitled "Mickey's Adventures in Wonderland", is based on the film.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Once_Upon_a_Time_in_Wonderland"><i>Once Upon a Time in Wonderland</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Once Upon a Time in Wonderland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_in_Wonderland" title="Once Upon a Time in Wonderland">Once Upon a Time in Wonderland</a></b></i> is an American fantasy-drama series that aired on ABC from October 10, 2013 to April 3, 2014. It was created by Edward Kitsis, Adam Horowitz, Zack Estrin, and Jane Espenson for ABC Studios. The program is a spin-off of Once Upon a Time and aired on ABC at 8:00 pm Eastern/7:00 pm Central on Thursday nights in the 2013–14 television season beginning October 10, 2013.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">Video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alice_in_Wonderland_.282000.29">Alice in Wonderland (2000)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Alice in Wonderland (2000)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Alice_in_Wonderland_(2000_video_game)" title="Alice in Wonderland (2000 video game)">Alice in Wonderland</a></b> is a platform video game developed by Digital Eclipse Software and published by Nintendo for the Game Boy Color. It was released in North America on October 4, 2000. The game follows the plot of the 1951 animated Disney film of the same name.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alice_in_Wonderland_.282010.29">Alice in Wonderland (2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Alice in Wonderland (2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Alice_in_Wonderland_(2010_video_game)" title="Alice in Wonderland (2010 video game)">Alice in Wonderland</a></b> is an action adventure video game published by Disney Interactive Studios. It was announced on July 23, 2009, that a video game based on the film would be released in the same week as the film for the Wii, Nintendo DS, Windows PC and Zeebo, with the soundtrack being composed by veteran video game music composer Richard Jacques. The Wii, DS, and PC versions were released on March 2, 2010.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kingdom_Hearts">Kingdom Hearts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Kingdom Hearts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Wonderland appears as a world in some of the games, as do characters from the 1951 film version.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Theme_park_attractions">Theme park attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Theme park attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alice.27s_Curious_Labyrinth">Alice's Curious Labyrinth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Alice's Curious Labyrinth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Alice%27s_Curious_Labyrinth" title="Alice's Curious Labyrinth">Alice's Curious Labyrinth</a></b> is a hedge maze attraction at the Disneyland Park within Disneyland Paris. It opened in 1992 with the Park, and belongs to the British part of Fantasyland.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mad_Tea_Party">Mad Tea Party</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Mad Tea Party">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Mad_Tea_Party" title="Mad Tea Party">Mad Tea Party</a></b> is a spinning tea cup ride at all five Disneyland-style theme parks around the world. The ride theme is inspired by the Unbirthday Party scene in Walt Disney's Alice In Wonderland. The ride has gained infamy over the years for the number of guests who get motion sickness as a result of the spinning component to the ride.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alice_in_Wonderland_.28ride.29">Alice in Wonderland (ride)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Alice in Wonderland (ride)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disneyland_attraction)" title="Alice in Wonderland (Disneyland attraction)">Alice in Wonderland</a></b> is a dark ride in Fantasyland at Disneyland in Anaheim, California. Based on the animated Disney adaptation of the same name, the attraction resides next to a second ride, the Mad Tea Party, based on a scene in that same adaptation. The presence of two rides based on the film is unusual in that Walt Disney said he regretted making it because it lacked a connection to the audience's hearts.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mad_T_Party">Mad T Party</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Mad T Party">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Mad_T_Party" title="Mad T Party">Mad T Party</a></b> is a nighttime event at Disney California Adventure, part of the Disneyland Resort in Anaheim, California. Anchored in the Hollywood Studios subsection of the Hollywood Land section of the park, Mad T Party premiered on June 15, 2012, the same day the park was re-dedicated. The event, which succeeded ElecTRONica, is inspired by Tim Burton's Alice in Wonderland.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stage_version">Stage version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Stage version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alice_in_Wonderland_Jr.">Alice in Wonderland Jr.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Alice in Wonderland Jr.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Alice in Wonderland Jr. (page does not exist)">Alice in Wonderland Jr.</a></b> is a one act stage version solely meant for middle and high school productions. It includes the majority of the film's songs and others including Song of the South's "Zip-a-Dee-Doo-Dah", two new reprises of "I'm Late!", and three new numbers entitled "Ocean of Tears", "Simon Says", and "Who Are You?" respectively. This 60–80 minute version is owned by Music Theatre International in the Broadway, Jr. Collection.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alice_in_Wonderland_soundtrack">Alice in Wonderland soundtrack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Alice in Wonderland soundtrack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Almost_Alice">Almost Alice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_in_Wonderland_(Disney_franchise)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Almost Alice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Almost_Alice" title="Almost Alice">Almost Alice</a></b> is a concept album of various artists' music inspired by Tim Burton's film, Alice in Wonderland. The album is also notable for featuring songs that were inspired from quotes directly from Lewis Carroll's original novel Alice's Adventures in Wonderland.The album was released by Buena Vista Records on March 2, 2010. It debuted at No. 5 on the Billboard 200 US albums chart. On June 1, 2010, iTunes released a deluxe edition of the album.</p>
