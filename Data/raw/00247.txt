<h1 class="ptitle" itemprop="name">‘Avatar’ Sequels Filming in New Zealand; Cameron Talks New Trilogy</h1>
<div style="margin-top:10px;"></div>
<span class="alignleft by">
    Published 1 year ago  
    by 
    <link href="https://plus.google.com/u/1/107168973425084682525/posts?rel=author" rel="author"/>
<span itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span itemprop="name"><a href="http://screenrant.com/author/sandy/" rel="author" title="Posts by Sandy Schaefer">Sandy Schaefer</a></span></span>
<span class="single-date" content="December 16th, 2013"></span>
</span>
<div style="padding-top: 20px;height: 43px;margin-left: 0px;">
<div style="float:left;">

<p align="center" class="ggnoads"><img alt="avatar sequels new zealand Avatar Sequels Filming in New Zealand; Cameron Talks New Trilogy" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-402065" height="320" src="http://cdn.screenrant.com/wp-content/uploads/avatar-sequels-new-zealand.jpg" title="Avatar Sequels Filming in New Zealand; Cameron Talks New Trilogy" width="620"/></p>
<p>James Cameron’s vision for the future of the <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/avatar/"><em>Avatar</em></a> franchise was originally two sequels (and maybe a prequel down the line), but that expanded to encompass an entire new movie trilogy over this past year. The plan is for shooting (including, but not restricted to, motion-capture photography) to get underway by <a href="http://screenrant.com/avatar-2-3-4-filming-start-date/">October 2014</a> – two months before Cameron <a href="http://screenrant.com/avatar-sequels-release-dates-sandy-97896/">originally expected</a> <em>Avatar 2</em> to reach theaters, approximately one year after the first installment obliterated global box office records (for perspective).</p>
<p>The filmmaker adjusted his projected release date for <em>Avatar 2</em> from 2014 to 2015 over a year ago (before <a href="http://screenrant.com/avatar-2-3-4-sequels-2016-2017-2018/">the trilogy was announced</a>). Back then, he was preparing to film (at least) part of the sequel in Wellington production studios in New Zealand – where Peter Jackson has been shooting <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/the-hobbit/"><em>The Hobbit</em></a> trilogy over the last couple of years – not so long after Cameron had purchased a sizable portion of undeveloped real estate (read: farmland) in the country.</p>
<p>Cameron’s Lightstorm Entertainment, along with 20th Century Fox Studio and the New Zealand government, has officially confirmed that the entire new <em>Avatar</em> movie trilogy will be produced in the country, in a deal that is reportedly worth more than NZ$500 million ($412 million) and will ensure that <em>“around 90 percent”</em> of the live-action production crew on the trilogy is composed of New Zealanders.</p>

<p>Hiring will take place over the forthcoming months, as Cameron is currently overseeing the script writing for <em>Avatar 2, 3 and 4</em> from his 2,500 acres of farmland around Lake Pounui, which is a <em>“20-minute helicopter ride from the NZ capital of Wellington.”</em> (For a more thorough breakdown of the deal, read the article over at <a href="http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/new-avatar-trilogy-be-filmed-665834" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.hollywoodreporter.com']);" target="_blank"><em>THR</em></a>.)</p>
<p><img alt="james cameron avatar sequels trilogy 570x294 Avatar Sequels Filming in New Zealand; Cameron Talks New Trilogy" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-402066" height="294" src="http://cdn.screenrant.com/wp-content/uploads/james-cameron-avatar-sequels-trilogy-570x294.jpg" title="Avatar Sequels Filming in New Zealand; Cameron Talks New Trilogy" width="570"/></p>
<p>Odds are that Cameron will end up being credited as the co-screenwriter on all three <em>Avatar</em> sequels, with his scripting collaborators including Josh Friedman (<a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/the-sarah-connor-chronicles/"><em>The Sarah Connor Chronicles</em></a>) on <em>Avatar 2</em>, Rick Jaffa and Amanda Silver (<a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/dawn-of-the-planet-of-the-apes/"><em>Dawn of the Planet of the Apes</em></a>) on <em>Avatar 3</em> and Shane Salerno (<a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/savages/"><em>Savages</em></a>) on <em>Avatar 4</em>.</p>
<p>In an <a href="http://www.news-sentinel.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20131216/ENT/131219716/1009/SPORTS" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.news-sentinel.com']);" target="_blank"><em>AP</em></a> interview, Cameron promised that the <em>Avatar</em> sequels will chart new territories and native inhabitants of Pandora, but revealed that the films won’t be heading off-planet (as he originally thought would be required):</p>
<blockquote><p>“It’s going to be a lot of new imagery and a lot of new environments and creatures across Pandora. We’re blowing it out all over the place. At first I thought I was going to take it onto other worlds as well, in the same solar system, but it turned out not to be necessary. I mean the Pandora that we have imagined will be a fantasy land that is going to occupy people for decades to come, the way I see it.”</p></blockquote>
<p>Of course, most would agree that it’s the stories that are the wild card factor here, not so much the expanded worldview of Pandora in these films – something that <a href="http://screenrant.com/james-cameron-writing-avatar-2-3/">Cameron is all too aware of.</a> Indeed, in said <em>AP</em> interview, he also indicated the scope of the narrative in the new <em>Avatar</em> trilogy will far outstrip that in the original film:</p>
<blockquote><p>“[The first 'Avatar'] was very Jake-centric. His story seen through his eyes. We spread it around quite a bit more as we go forward. It’s really the story of his family, the family that he creates on Pandora. His extended family. So think of it as a family saga like ‘The Godfather.’”</p></blockquote>
<p>Plot details on the new <em>Avatar</em> trilogy are tightly under lock and key right now, but we anticipate some variation on the ‘settlers vs. natives in space’ narrative of the first movie – not least of all, because Stephen Lang will reprise as Colonel Quaritch (through <a href="http://screenrant.com/avatar-2-3-4-sequels-cast-stephen-lang-colonel-quaritch/">means that have yet to be explained</a>), and Cameron has said that the memorable, if two-dimensional, military antagonist from <em>Avatar</em> will <em>“evolve into really unexpected places”</em> in the sequels.</p>
<p>_____</p>
<p><strong><em>Avatar 2</em></strong> is slated to open in theaters by December 2016, followed by <strong><em>Avatar 3</em></strong> in December 2017 and <strong><em>Avatar 4</em></strong> in December 2018.</p>
