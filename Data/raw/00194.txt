<p><i><b>The Eagle Has Landed</b></i> is a book by British writer <a href="/wiki/Jack_Higgins" title="Jack Higgins">Jack Higgins</a>, set during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and first published in <a href="/wiki/1975_in_literature" title="1975 in literature">1975</a>. It was made into a <a href="/wiki/The_Eagle_Has_Landed_(film)" title="The Eagle Has Landed (film)">film of the same name</a> in <a href="/wiki/1976_in_film" title="1976 in film">1976</a> starring <a href="/wiki/Michael_Caine" title="Michael Caine">Michael Caine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Donald_Sutherland" title="Donald Sutherland">Donald Sutherland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jenny_Agutter" title="Jenny Agutter">Jenny Agutter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Duvall" title="Robert Duvall">Robert Duvall</a>. The plot has similarities with that of <i><a href="/wiki/Went_the_Day_Well%3F" title="Went the Day Well?">Went the Day Well?</a></i>, a film made during World War II.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Eagle_Has_Landed&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The book makes use of the <a href="/wiki/False_document" title="False document">false document</a> technique, and opens with Higgins describing his discovery of the concealed grave of thirteen German paratroopers in an English graveyard. What follows was inspired by the real life rescue of Hitler's ally <a href="/wiki/Benito_Mussolini" title="Benito Mussolini">Benito Mussolini</a> by <a href="/wiki/Otto_Skorzeny" title="Otto Skorzeny">Otto Skorzeny</a>. A similar idea is considered by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hitler" title="Hitler">Hitler</a>, with the strong support of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Himmler" title="Himmler">Himmler</a>. <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Canaris" title="Wilhelm Canaris">Admiral Wilhelm Canaris</a>, head of the <a href="/wiki/Abwehr" title="Abwehr">Abwehr</a> (German military intelligence), is ordered to make a <a href="/wiki/Feasibility_study" title="Feasibility study">feasibility study</a> of the seemingly impossible task of capturing British Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> and bringing him to the Reich.</p>
<p>Canaris realizes that although Hitler will soon forget the matter, Himmler will not. Fearing Himmler may try to discredit him, Canaris orders one of his officers, <a href="/wiki/Oberst" title="Oberst">Oberst</a> Radl, to undertake the study, despite feeling that it is all just a waste of time and effort.</p>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Unteroffizier" title="Unteroffizier">Unteroffizier</a> on Radl's staff finds that one of their spies, code named <i>Starling</i>, has provided a tantalizing piece of intelligence. "At any other time, in any other place, this information would be useless", Radl said. "And then <a href="/wiki/Synchronicity" title="Synchronicity">synchronicity</a> rears its disturbing head." Winston Churchill is scheduled to spend a relaxing weekend at a country house near the village of Studley Constable, <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a>. There Joanna Grey, an <a href="/wiki/Afrikaner" title="Afrikaner">Afrikaner</a> woman and longtime <a href="/wiki/Abwehr" title="Abwehr">Abwehr</a> agent, lives. She detests England because she was abused and raped by British soldiers, and her husband, daughter, and parents were killed during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglo-Boer_War" title="Anglo-Boer War">Anglo-Boer War</a>. As a result of her reports, Radl devises a detailed plan to intercept Churchill and return with him to Germany. Although Radl is certain the plan has real possibilities, Admiral Canaris orders him to abandon it.</p>
<p>Himmler, however, has already learned of the scheme and summons Radl. He orders him to proceed, but without notifying Canaris. In response, Radl arranges for <a href="/wiki/Liam_Devlin" title="Liam Devlin">Liam Devlin</a>, a member of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Republican_Army" title="Irish Republican Army">Irish Republican Army</a>, to be smuggled to Norfolk by way of <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>. Posing as a wounded veteran of the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a>, he contacts Mrs. Grey, who arranges a position for him as <a href="/wiki/Game_warden" title="Game warden">game warden</a> to the estate of Studley Grange. While awaiting further developments, Devlin becomes romantically involved with Molly Prior, a girl from the village.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, Radl selects of a team of commandos to carry out the operation, led by a disgraced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fallschirmj%C3%A4ger_(Nazi_Germany)" title="FallschirmjÃ¤ger (Nazi Germany)">FallschirmjÃ¤ger</a> commander, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Colonel" title="Lieutenant Colonel">Lieutenant Colonel</a> Kurt Steiner. While returning from the Eastern Front, Steiner had intervened when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SS" title="SS">SS</a> soldiers were rounding up <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">Jews</a> at a railway station in Poland. To the outrage of the SS and <a href="/wiki/Polizei" title="Polizei">Polizei</a>, he took one of their men hostage and helped a teenage Jewess to escape on a passing freight train. For this he was court-martialled, along with his men, who backed his actions. Too highly decorated to face a firing squad, Steiner and his men were allowed to transfer to a penal unit in the <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Islands</a>. There they are forced to make high-risk attacks with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manned_torpedo" title="Manned torpedo">manned torpedoes</a> against Allied ships in the English Channel.</p>
<p>Radl travels to <a href="/wiki/Alderney" title="Alderney">Alderney</a> and recruits Steiner and his surviving men. Steiner's father, General Steiner, is being tortured by the <a href="/wiki/Gestapo" title="Gestapo">Gestapo</a> for his alleged ties to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_Resistance" title="German Resistance">German Resistance</a>. This serves as an additional incentive for the Colonel to accept the mission. Radl relocates Steiner and his men to an airfield on the north western coast of <a href="/wiki/Holland" title="Holland">Holland</a>, where they familiarise themselves with the British weapons and equipment they will be using. The team will be air dropped into Norfolk via a captured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C-47" title="C-47">C-47</a> Dakota with Allied markings. The commandos outfit themselves as <a href="/wiki/Polish_Armed_Forces_in_the_West" title="Polish Armed Forces in the West">Free Polish</a> troops, as few of them speak English; the plan is to infiltrate Studley Constable, capture Churchill, rendezvous with an <a href="/wiki/E-boat" title="E-boat">E-boat</a> at the nearby coast and make their escape. As part of the ruse they arm themselves with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sten_gun" title="Sten gun">Sten guns</a>, <a href="/wiki/M1_Garand" title="M1 Garand">M1 Garands</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bren_gun" title="Bren gun">Bren guns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Revolver" title="Revolver">revolvers</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Browning_Hi-Power" title="Browning Hi-Power">Browning Hi-Powers</a> instead of German weaponry.</p>
<p>At first, the plan seems to go off without a hitch. Then, however, one of Steiner's NCOs rescues a young girl who fell into a mill race. He is killed by the water wheel and his German uniform (worn, by Himmler's order, under the Polish uniforms, as protection against being executed as spies) is seen by several of the villagers. Determined to continue the mission, Steiner arranges for the locals to be rounded up, but the sister of Father Vereker, the local priest, escapes and alerts a nearby unit of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Army_Rangers" title="US Army Rangers">US Army Rangers</a>. Colonel Robert Shafto, an inexperienced but glory-seeking officer, rallies his forces to retake the hostages. Without notifying headquarters, he orders a foolhardy assault in which many Americans are killed. After the Colonel is shot in the head by Mrs. Grey, Major Kane organizes a second, successful attack.</p>
<p>Steiner, his second-in-command Ritter von Neumann, and Devlin manage to escape with the aid of a local girl, Molly Prior, who had become romantically involved with the Irishman. Determined to finish the mission, Steiner allows Devlin and Neumann to escape without him and decides to make one last attempt at Churchill. He succeeds in reaching Churchill, but hesitates, is shot and supposedly killed. (However, Steiner reappears alive in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Eagle_Has_Flown" title="The Eagle Has Flown">The Eagle Has Flown</a></i>, a sequel.) In Germany, Radl has had a heart attack, implied to be fatal, although at about the same time, Himmler, upon discovering that the mission has failed, orders Radl's arrest for <a href="/wiki/High_treason" title="High treason">high treason</a>.</p>
<p>As in many novels of Higgins, this story is surrounded by a 'frame story' with a prologue and epilogue. The author, whilst doing historical research in <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a>, supposedly meets various surviving characters. Some paperback editions have more historical backstory than others, including a meeting with an older Liam Devlin in a Belfast hotel. The final revelation comes from an aged and terminally ill Father Vereker: "Churchill" had been an impersonator and even if the mission had succeeded, it would not have mattered.</p>

<li>After the success of <i>The Eagle Has Landed</i>, Higgins wrote a quasi-sequel called <i><a href="/wiki/The_Eagle_Has_Flown" title="The Eagle Has Flown">The Eagle Has Flown</a></i>, which was published in 1991.</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Eagle_Has_Landed&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Liam_Devlin" title="Liam Devlin">Liam Devlin</a> is featured in several later Jack Higgins books, older and acting as a mentor to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sean_Dillon_(Jack_Higgins_character)" title="Sean Dillon (Jack Higgins character)">Sean Dillon</a> and Martin Brosnan. In Higgins' novel <i>Confessional</i>, Devlin allies with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MI6" title="MI6">MI6</a> in order to prevent a rogue <a href="/wiki/KGB" title="KGB">KGB</a> assassin from murdering <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">Pope</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Paul_II" title="John Paul II">John Paul II</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Schutzstaffel" title="Schutzstaffel">S.S.</a> <a href="/wiki/British_Free_Corps" title="British Free Corps">British Free Corps</a> unit is described in the book. In the novel, a British S.S. Officer named Harvey Preston is attached to Steiner's unit to add credibility. A rabid Nazi<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Disputed statement"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (October 2012)">dubious</span></a> <span class="metadata">â <a href="/wiki/Talk:The_Eagle_Has_Landed#Dubious" title="Talk:The Eagle Has Landed">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup> and convicted con-man prior to his enlistment, Preston is viewed with disgust by Steiner, Devlin, and their fellow commandos. After Steiner, Neumannn, and Devlin escape, Preston is lynched inside the village's Roman Catholic Church by a mentally ill resident of Studley Constable.</li>
<li>Lt. Colonel Kurt Steiner is not a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nazi" title="Nazi">Nazi</a> stereotype. Steiner is a veteran of the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Campaign" title="Norwegian Campaign">Invasion of Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Fort_Eben-Emael" title="Battle of Fort Eben-Emael">Albert Canal</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Crete" title="Battle of Crete">Battle of Crete</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leningrad" title="Battle of Leningrad">Leningrad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Stalingrad" title="Battle of Stalingrad">Stalingrad</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dnieper-Carpathian_Offensive" title="Dnieper-Carpathian Offensive">the Ukraine</a>.</li>
