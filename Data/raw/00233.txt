<p><b>Stephen Lang</b> (born July 11, 1952) is a <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a>-nominated <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Playwright" title="Playwright">playwright</a>. He started in <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway theatre</a> but is more widely known for his film roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Gods_and_Generals_(film)" title="Gods and Generals (film)">Gods and Generals</a></i> (2003), <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar_(2009_film)" title="Avatar (2009 film)">Avatar</a></i> (2009) and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terra_Nova_(TV_show)" title="Terra Nova (TV show)">Terra Nova</a>" (2011)</p>
<p>From 2004 to 2006, Lang was a co-<a href="/wiki/Artistic_director" title="Artistic director">artistic director</a> of <a href="/wiki/Actors_Studio" title="Actors Studio">Actors Studio</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stephen_Lang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lang was born in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, the youngest child of Theresa (<a href="/wiki/Married_and_maiden_names" title="Married and maiden names">née</a> Volmer, dec. 2008) and <a href="/wiki/Eugene_Lang" title="Eugene Lang">Eugene Lang</a>, a prominent entrepreneur and philanthropist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-filmr-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> He has two elder siblings - <a href="/wiki/Jane_Lang" title="Jane Lang">Jane</a>, an attorney and activist, and David, who served as an executive at REFAC, the company Eugene founded in 1952.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-people-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Lang's father has donated much of his net worth (in excess of $150 million) to charity and will not leave an inheritance to his children, believing they each needed to learn to become self-sufficient.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Lang's mother was of <a href="/wiki/Irish_Catholic" title="Irish Catholic">Irish Catholic</a> descent, and his father is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">Jewish</a> (Lang's paternal grandparents were immigrants from <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-people-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Lang attended school in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica_Estates,_Queens" title="Jamaica Estates, Queens">Jamaica Estates, Queens</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> For high school he attended George School, a Quaker boarding school in Newtown, PA. He graduated from <a href="/wiki/Swarthmore_College" title="Swarthmore College">Swarthmore College</a> in 1973 with a degree in English Literature; on May 30, 2010, Swarthmore awarded him an Honorary Degree from College in recognition of his distinguished career in theatre, television, and film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swarthmore_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-swarthmore-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> His youngest son, Noah, received his bachelor's degree during the same ceremony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swarthmore_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-swarthmore-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> He also holds an Honorary Doctorate in Humane Letters from <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville_University" title="Jacksonville University">Jacksonville University</a> and was an artist in residence at <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_University" title="Northeastern University">Northeastern University</a> in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Lang has been married to Kristina Watson, a costume designer and teacher, since June 1, 1980. They have four children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-filmr-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-people-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stephen_Lang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lang played Harold (Happy) Loman in the 1984 Broadway revival of <i><a href="/wiki/Death_of_a_Salesman_(1985_film)" title="Death of a Salesman (1985 film)">Death of a Salesman</a></i> and the 1985 television film with <a href="/wiki/Dustin_Hoffman" title="Dustin Hoffman">Dustin Hoffman</a> as <a href="/wiki/Willy_Loman" title="Willy Loman">Willy Loman</a>, and appeared in the first <a href="/wiki/Hannibal_Lecter" title="Hannibal Lecter">Hannibal Lecter</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Manhunter_(film)" title="Manhunter (film)">Manhunter</a></i> (1986), as reporter <a href="/wiki/Freddy_Lounds" title="Freddy Lounds">Freddy Lounds</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> He played attorney David Abrams in the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Crime_Story_(TV_series)" title="Crime Story (TV series)">Crime Story</a></i> (1986–88). He played the title role in the NBC movie <i>Babe Ruth</i> (1991). He later played the "One Armed Man" in the 2000 revival of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fugitive_(TV_series)" title="The Fugitive (TV series)">The Fugitive</a></i> starring Tim Daly. The series was a modest success but lasted only one season because of its large production budget.</p>
<p>In 1992, he was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a> for his lead role in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Speed_of_Darkness_(play)" title="The Speed of Darkness (play)">The Speed of Darkness</a>.</i> His film role in <a href="/wiki/Last_Exit_to_Brooklyn_(film)" title="Last Exit to Brooklyn (film)"><i>Last Exit to Brooklyn</i></a> (1989) garnered him widespread critical acclaim, but its limited release prevented the film from reaching a wider audience. On stage, he was the first to play the role of Colonel Nathan Jessup in <i><a href="/wiki/A_Few_Good_Men_(play)" title="A Few Good Men (play)">A Few Good Men</a>,</i> a role made famous on film (1992) by <a href="/wiki/Jack_Nicholson" title="Jack Nicholson">Jack Nicholson</a>. He is the winner of over half a dozen theatre awards including the Drama Desk and Helen Hayes awards.</p>
<p>In films, he played <a href="/wiki/George_Pickett" title="George Pickett">Maj. Gen. George E. Pickett</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Gettysburg_(1993_film)" title="Gettysburg (1993 film)">Gettysburg</a></i> (1993) and as the lead role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thomas_%22Stonewall%22_Jackson" title='Thomas "Stonewall" Jackson'>Thomas "Stonewall" Jackson</a> in the Gettysburg <a href="/wiki/Prequel" title="Prequel">prequel</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Gods_and_Generals_(film)" title="Gods and Generals (film)">Gods and Generals</a></i> (2003), both from director <a href="/wiki/Ronald_F._Maxwell" title="Ronald F. Maxwell">Ronald F. Maxwell</a>. He considers <i>Gods and Generals</i> to be his finest performance. Arguably, his most famous film role, before Avatar, was his portrayal of the villainous <a href="/wiki/Ike_Clanton" title="Ike Clanton">Ike Clanton</a> in the successful Western <i><a href="/wiki/Tombstone_(film)" title="Tombstone (film)">Tombstone</a></i> (1993) with <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Russell" title="Kurt Russell">Kurt Russell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Val_Kilmer" title="Val Kilmer">Val Kilmer</a>.</p>

<p>Shortly before <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Miller" title="Arthur Miller">Arthur Miller</a>'s death in February 2005, Lang appeared in his long-time friend's last play, titled <i>Finishing the Picture</i>. It premiered in 2004 at <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Goodman_Theatre" title="Goodman Theatre">Goodman Theatre</a>, where Lang had the second run of his own play <i>Beyond Glory,</i> which had premiered in Arlington, Virginia, early in 2004, and his Tony-nominated portrayal for <i>The Speed of Darkness.</i> Lang also performed <i>Beyond Glory</i>, a one-man show, for troops deployed overseas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-parade_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-parade-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2006, he played the role of Colonel Littlefield in <a href="/wiki/John_Patrick_Shanley" title="John Patrick Shanley">John Patrick Shanley</a>'s play <i><a href="/wiki/Defiance_(play)" title="Defiance (play)">Defiance</a></i>. He brought <i>Beyond Glory</i> to Roundabout's Off-Broadway Laura Pels Theatre in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> Since its New York City premiere, <i>Beyond Glory</i> has been nominated for a Drama Desk Award and a Lucille Lortel Award both for outstanding solo performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Lang has a role in the ESPN mini-series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Bronx_is_Burning" title="The Bronx is Burning">The Bronx is Burning</a></i>, as well as roles in independent features <i>Save Me</i> and <i>From Mexico With Love</i>. He plays a lead role in <a href="/wiki/James_Cameron" title="James Cameron">James Cameron</a>'s sci-fi epic <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar_(2009_film)" title="Avatar (2009 film)">Avatar</a></i> as the villainous Colonel Quaritch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, he appeared in Michael Mann's film <i><a href="/wiki/Public_Enemies_(2009_film)" title="Public Enemies (2009 film)">Public Enemies</a></i> as Texas Ranger Charles Winstead,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> the man widely considered to have fired the shots that killed <a href="/wiki/John_Dillinger" title="John Dillinger">John Dillinger</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> and in Grant Heslov's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Men_Who_Stare_at_Goats" title="The Men Who Stare at Goats">The Men Who Stare at Goats</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Bridges" title="Jeff Bridges">Jeff Bridges</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Spacey" title="Kevin Spacey">Kevin Spacey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ewan_McGregor" title="Ewan McGregor">Ewan McGregor</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Clooney" title="George Clooney">George Clooney</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Also in 2009, he narrated the <a href="/wiki/Audiobook" title="Audiobook">audiobook</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Road_Rage_(audiobook)" title="Road Rage (audiobook)">Road Rage</a></i>—which combines the short stories "<a href="/wiki/Duel_(1971_film)" title="Duel (1971 film)">Duel</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Matheson" title="Richard Matheson">Richard Matheson</a> and "Throttle" by <a href="/wiki/Stephen_King" title="Stephen King">Stephen King</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joe_Hill" title="Joe Hill">Joe Hill</a>—and guest-starred in the <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Criminal_Intent" title="Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent">Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent</a></i> Season 8 finale, "<a href="/wiki/Revolution_(Law_%26_Order:_Criminal_Intent)" title="Revolution (Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent)">Revolution</a>" in the role of Axel Kaspers. In 2010 he performed the narration for "The Gettysburg Story: Battlefield Auto Tour,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> the top-selling audio tour of the <a href="/wiki/Gettysburg_Battlefield" title="Gettysburg Battlefield">Gettysburg Battlefield</a> at <a href="/wiki/Gettysburg_National_Military_Park" title="Gettysburg National Military Park">Gettysburg National Military Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gettysburg,_Pennsylvania" title="Gettysburg, Pennsylvania">Gettysburg</a>, PA. Written and produced by filmmaker <a href="/wiki/Jake_Boritt" title="Jake Boritt">Jake Boritt</a> and based on works by historian <a href="/wiki/Gabor_Boritt" title="Gabor Boritt">Gabor Boritt</a> it tells the story of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Gettysburg" title="Battle of Gettysburg">Battle of Gettysburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Gettysburg_Address" title="Gettysburg Address">Gettysburg Address</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gettysburg_National_Cemetery" title="Gettysburg National Cemetery">Gettysburg National Cemetery</a>. Lang is also the narrator of the companion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Television" title="Public Television">Public Television</a> documentary <i>The Gettysburg Story</i> presented by <a href="/wiki/Maryland_Public_Television" title="Maryland Public Television">Maryland Public Television</a>.</p>
<p>Lang played the villain Khalar Zym in the 2011 <i><a href="/wiki/Conan_the_Barbarian" title="Conan the Barbarian">Conan the Barbarian</a></i> reboot starring Jason Momoa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> He also played one of the leads, Nathaniel Taylor, in the <a href="/wiki/Steven_Spielberg" title="Steven Spielberg">Steven Spielberg</a>-produced<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Terra_Nova_(TV_series)" title="Terra Nova (TV series)">Terra Nova</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2012, he signed on to play Mary Shannon's estranged father in a three episode arc on the final season of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USA_(TV_network)" title="USA (TV network)">USA</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/In_Plain_Sight" title="In Plain Sight">In Plain Sight</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Lang starred in the 2013 horror film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Monkey%27s_Paw_(2013_film)" title="The Monkey's Paw (2013 film)">The Monkey's Paw</a></i>.</p>
<p>Lang has been confirmed as reprising his role as Colonel Miles Quaritch in the upcoming sequels to <i>Avatar</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>He plays <a href="/wiki/Increase_Mather" title="Increase Mather">Increase Mather</a>, in a recurring role, on <a href="/wiki/WGN_America" title="WGN America">WGN America</a>'s first original scripted series, <i><a href="/wiki/Salem_(TV_series)" title="Salem (TV series)">Salem</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
