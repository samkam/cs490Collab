<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>The Avengers</i> (soundtrack)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><i><b>The Avengers (Original Motion Picture Soundtrack)</b></i> is the <a href="/wiki/Film_score" title="Film score">film score</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Studios" title="Marvel Studios">Marvel Studios</a> film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Avengers_(2012_film)" title="The Avengers (2012 film)">The Avengers</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Silvestri" title="Alan Silvestri">Alan Silvestri</a>, was released by <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Records" title="Hollywood Records">Hollywood Records</a> on May 1, 2012. A separate <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack" title="Soundtrack">soundtrack</a> and <a href="/wiki/Concept_album" title="Concept album">concept album</a> titled, <i><b>Avengers Assemble (Music from and Inspired by the Motion Picture)</b></i> by various artists was also released on the same date.</p>

<p>In November 2011, Marvel announced that <a href="/wiki/Alan_Silvestri" title="Alan Silvestri">Alan Silvestri</a>, who <a href="/wiki/Film_score" title="Film score">scored</a> <i>Captain America: The First Avenger</i>, would write and compose the score for <i>The Avengers</i> (making him the first composer to score more than one movie in the Marvel Cinematic Universe). Silvestri stated, "This is actually a very unique experience [for me]. I’ve worked on films where there have been a number of stars and certainly worked on films where there have been characters of equal weight in terms of their level of importance and profile in the film, but this one is somewhat extreme in that regard because each of these characters has their own world and it’s a very different situation. It’s very challenging to look for a way to give everyone the weight and consideration they need, but at the same time the film is really about the coming together of these characters, which implies that there is this entity called the Avengers which really has to be representative of all of them together."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Silvestri_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Silvestri-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Silvestri recorded the score with the <a href="/wiki/London_Symphony_Orchestra" title="London Symphony Orchestra">London Symphony Orchestra</a> at <a href="/wiki/Abbey_Road_Studios" title="Abbey Road Studios">Abbey Road Studios</a> in London, England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SXSW1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-SXSW1-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Avengers</i> director <a href="/wiki/Joss_Whedon" title="Joss Whedon">Joss Whedon</a> described the score as "old school", saying "the score is very old-fashioned, which is why [Silvestri] was letter perfect for this movie because he can give you the heightened emotion, the [<a href="/wiki/Hans_Zimmer" title="Hans Zimmer">Hans Zimmer</a>] school of 'I’m just feeling a lot right now!' but he can also be extraordinarily cue and character specific, which I love."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SXSW1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-SXSW1-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Silvestri reused his theme for <i>Captain America</i> and introduced new ones - including the theme for Black Widow, which is "a lonely, plucked theme with an Eastern European flavor to define this character".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The score album was released on May 1, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Soundtrack_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Soundtrack-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>In March 2012, American <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Soundgarden" title="Soundgarden">Soundgarden</a> announced through their official <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> page that they have written a song to be included on the film's soundtrack, entitled "Live to Rise".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Soundgarden_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Soundgarden-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Additionally, Indian rock band <a href="/wiki/Agnee" title="Agnee">Agnee</a> released a music video for their single "Hello Andheron", which served as the theme song for the Indian release of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agnee_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Agnee-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> The following day, Marvel released the album's full tracklisting, which was released by <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Records" title="Hollywood Records">Hollywood Records</a> on May 1, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Soundtrack_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Soundtrack-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> A cover of <a href="/wiki/AC/DC" title="AC/DC">AC/DC</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Shoot_to_Thrill" title="Shoot to Thrill">Shoot to Thrill</a>", performed by <a href="/wiki/Theory_of_a_Deadman" title="Theory of a Deadman">Theory of a Deadman</a>, was originally to be included on the album, but was removed for unknown reasons.</p>
