<div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"><strong>The Beatles</strong> are the latest group to take part in the ongoing Why Music Matters campaign.<br/><br/>
Launched last year, <a href="http://www.whymusicmatters.org">Why Music Matters</a> aimed to prove to fans that their record collections still had an innate worth. Aiming to re-open discussion about how music was released and where fans obtained it, the campaign wanted to provide an ethical dimension to the music industry in the 21st century.<br/><br/>
Finding support from all four major labels, <a href="http://http://www.whymusicmatters.org">Why Music Matters</a> also won acclaim from artists. Launching a series of animated short films, the campaign has found support from Cee-Lo Green, Elbow and more.<br/><br/>
The short films are building up, with the catalogue containing everyone from Blind Willie Johnson to The Jam to Nick Cave and Sigur Ros.<br/><br/>
Now <a href="http://www.clashmusic.com/artists/the-beatles">The Beatles</a> have shown their support. The legendary Scousers have got behind the campaign, with the band providing permission for an inventive new animation using their acclaimed music.<br/><br/>
The film was animated and directed by Lee Gingold, and follows a central character - ow he grew up listening to their music and how it has marked the various milestones in his life, the trials and tribulations, the passion, the story behind the music. <br/><br/>
