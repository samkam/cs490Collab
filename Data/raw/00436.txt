<h2 class="short-teaser">
Joss Whedon discusses his many influences on Avengers: Age of Ultron, grounding the villain's powers, and defends Spider-Man 3...</h2><div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"><p> Right: Oscars and awards done with for another year. It's nearly blockbuster season now. And few are bigger or enjoying more anticipation than Joss Whedon's <em>Avengers: Age Of Ultron</em>, the sequel to the third highest grossing film of all time.</p>
<p>For his encore, Joss Whedon appears not to be resting on his laurels. Indeed, in his latest interview with Empire, <em>The Avengers</em> director certainly made it clear his ambitions were fairly high, even purely in terms of influence.</p>
<p>"I know I reference <em>Godfather Part II</em> a lot" he said. "'Don’t reference the greatest movie ever made because that’s Icarus, you moron!’ But at the same time, it is a huge touchstone for me, because you get everything you got from the first <em>Godfather</em> movie in a very different movie, in a movie that structurally couldn’t be more different, and thematically and in intent and in mood, and yet nobody ever goes ‘it wasn’t <em>Godfather</em>-y enough'. I need to give people an exciting ride about heroic people, and that’s certainly part of why I signed on, but at the same time, a richer, deeper, darker movie is not a bad thing".</p>
<p>In that vein, Whedon also revealed that he has a new kind of villain that he is grounding to be the megalomaniacal foil to his superheroes.</p>
<p>"The powers in the comic books – they’re always like 'and then I can reverse the polarity of your ions’ – well, we have to ground things a lot more. With Ultron, we have to make him slightly less omnipotent, because he’d win. Bottom line. Also, having weaknesses and needs, and foibles, and alliances, and actually caring what people think of him, all these things, are what make him a character and not just a tidal wave. A movie about a tidal wave can be great, but it’s different than a conflict between one side and the other...[so] the powers he has are slightly different – he can control certain things, he’s not just firing repulsors".</p>
<p>And then Whedon sprung to the defence of Sam Raimi's maligned (including by Raimi himself!) <em>Spider-Man 3.</em></p>
<p>When discussing Quicksilver and Scarlet Witch, he revealed that "they’re already good to go by the time we’re up and running. You don’t want to fall into <em>Spider-Man 3</em> territory – and I say that as a guy who actually thinks pretty well of that movie; there’s some great stuff in that movie – but there comes a point where you’re overloaded with front-story, back-story, origin story, and it becomes very hard to juggle".</p>
<p>The full Empire interview is <a href="http://www.empireonline.com/interviews/interview.asp?IID=2017" target="_blank">here</a>.</p>
<p><em>Avengers: Age Of Ultron</em> lands in UK cinemas at the end of April.</p>
