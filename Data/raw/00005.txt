<li class="date">July 2013</li>
<li class="user">Posted By Thomas Wiseman IV</li>
<li class="comments">0 Comments</li>
</ul>
</dd>
</dl>
<p>For those of you havent seen it, we have a library made for students. We have two computers filled with TV shows and reading in English, and bookshelves full of books for all levels. All of this is available for students to take home!</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://englishconnect-spb.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/A_Tale_of_Two_Cities__91605_zoom.jpg"><img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-3175" height="300" src="http://englishconnect-spb.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/A_Tale_of_Two_Cities__91605_zoom-181x300.jpg" width="181"/></a></p>
<p>One of the books we have is <i>A Tale of Two Cities</i> by Charles Dickens, the famous British author. It has sold over <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Tale_of_Two_Cities" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://en.wikipedia.org']);">200 million</a> copies, and I read it in the 9th grade. I liked it  its a complex story with believable characters. Like all of the Dickens novels Ive read, there is a lot about morality and virtue.</p>
<p>The story takes place before and during the French Revolution. In the main part of the story, Charles Darnay, who used to be a French aristocrat, becomes a victim of the Revolution. There is blood, drama, revenge, and romance  everything you could want! <a href="http://www.goodreads.com/book/show/1953.A_Tale_of_Two_Cities#other_reviews" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.goodreads.com']);">Here</a> you can read reviews of it.</p>
<p>Its not easy to read in English  the language is very rich. In our library, its under the Advanced books. However, we also have a version of the book re-written for Beginner students! Come in and take it home with you for a good read!</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://englishconnect-spb.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Charles_Dickens_circa_1860s.png"><img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-3176" height="300" src="http://englishconnect-spb.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Charles_Dickens_circa_1860s-232x300.png" width="232"/></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Read my book!  Charles Dickens</p>
