<p><i><b>Spider-Man 3</b></i> is an <a href="/wiki/Action_game" title="Action game">action game</a> loosely based on the <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_3" title="Spider-Man 3"><i>Spider-Man 3</i> film</a> and released for the <a href="/wiki/Game_Boy_Advance" title="Game Boy Advance">Game Boy Advance</a>, <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a>, <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_DS" title="Nintendo DS">Nintendo DS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a>, <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a> on May 4, 2007. A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PSP" title="PSP">PSP</a> version was released on October 17, 2007. The Game Boy Advance, PlayStation 2, Nintendo DS &amp; Wii versions were developed by <a href="/wiki/Vicarious_Visions" title="Vicarious Visions">Vicarious Visions</a> while the Microsoft Windows version was developed by <a href="/wiki/Beenox" title="Beenox">Beenox</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> and the Xbox 360 and PlayStation 3 versions were developed by <a href="/wiki/Treyarch" title="Treyarch">Treyarch</a>.</p>
<p>Planned releases for <a href="/wiki/Xbox_(console)" title="Xbox (console)">Xbox</a> and <a href="/wiki/GameCube" title="GameCube">GameCube</a> of the game were planned, but later cancelled due to low sales for Xbox and limited resources for GameCube along with <i><a href="/wiki/Shrek_the_Third_(video_game)" title="Shrek the Third (video game)">Shrek the Third</a></i>.</p>
<p>The game's plot expands on the film by including additional characters and elements from the Spider-Man comics and the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Universe" title="Marvel Universe">Marvel Universe</a>. Depending on the platform, different villains from the comics are featured, but all versions of the game feature the film's main villains: <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Brock" title="Eddie Brock">Venom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Osborn" title="Harry Osborn">New Goblin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sandman_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Sandman (Marvel Comics)">Sandman</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spider-Man_3_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Xbox_360.2C_PlayStation_3.2C_and_Microsoft_Windows">Xbox 360, PlayStation 3, and Microsoft Windows</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spider-Man_3_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Xbox 360, PlayStation 3, and Microsoft Windows">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A new gang called the H-Bombers have blown up the Carlyle building, and Spider-Man comes in to save the day. He beats the bombers and saves a woman from being killed. The next day, Peter Parker narrates: "Just another day in the life of your friendly neighborhood Spider-Man! Lately, things have been going my way. I got the girl and New York finally likes me. Not that there are problems, but there is this new guy <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Brock" title="Eddie Brock">Eddie Brock</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Daily_Bugle" title="Daily Bugle">Daily Bugle</a>, and he is really starting to get on my nerves. Harry, my best friend, won't talk to me. On top of that, new gangs have been dividing up the city, still its nothing I can handle. One weird thing, there hasn't been any big super villian around since Doc Ock. I had this bad feeling that the sky is gonna fall or something and soon."</p>
<p>Spider-Man finds out that the H-Bombers aren't the only gang terrorising the city. He finds out that there are other three gangs in the city: The Apocalypse, the Arsenic Candy, and the Dragon Tails. However, he makes short work of the Apocalypse, who attacked the subway and the power plant, and the Arsenic Candy, who attacked a theater, a factory and an abandoned warehouse with hostages. The H-Bombers later attack the subway station too, but Spidey was able to stop them.</p>
<p>Spider-Man takes <a href="/wiki/Mary_Jane_Watson" title="Mary Jane Watson">Mary Jane Watson</a>, his girlfriend, on a thrill ride, and stops by central park. Mary Jane asks how things been with Harry Osborn, and Peter responds that he has tried everything and Harry thinks he killed his Dad, Norman Osborn. Mary Jane tries to comfort him and tell him it wasn't his fault. Nearby, a symbiote inside a meteor crashes nearby and attaches to Peter's shoe. Meanwhile, Harry sneaks into the secret Goblin lair, and goes into a chamber filled with green smoke, and puts on his Goblin armor.</p>
<p>Peter's college science teacher <a href="/wiki/Lizard_(comics)" title="Lizard (comics)">Dr. Connors</a>, records a tape explaining that he has studied lizard DNA and that lizards can grow new limbs after they are accidentally cut off. So he decides to inject lizard DNA into him to grow a right arm (which he lost when he was in the U.S. army). The experiment turns out okay and Connors regrows his arm, but the experiment is far more than imagined and transforms into a giant lizard and knocks over the camera that he was using to record his investigation. Peter walks pass Dr. Connors' lab and hears strange noises coming from there. He finds out what happened and follows Connors into the sewers, where the Lizard already turned other people into lizards, too. Spider-Man then follows and defeats the Lizard but the Lizard escapes deeper into the sewers, and Spidey can't go any further. Peter changes into his clothes and worries until Harry (now the New Goblin) appears out of nowhere and attacks Peter. They go on a high-fly battle on New Goblin's glider, but Goblin is defeated and knocked unconscious by Peter's webbing, which he attached to a building. Peter brings Harry to a local hospital, where he will be treated. Meanwhile, an escaped convict <a href="/wiki/Sandman_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Sandman (Marvel Comics)">Flint Marko</a> tries to escape from the police and falls into a cockpit full of sand where scientist are mutating sand and Flint turns into sand.</p>
<p>Spider-Man foils the H-Bombers plans once again, who were planting bombs all over Financial District. The H-Bombers decide to attack the Daily Bugle kidnaps <a href="/wiki/J._Jonah_Jameson" title="J. Jonah Jameson">J. Jonah Jameson</a>. It was revealed that Luke Carlyle, one of the richest people in New York, was The Mad Bomber, the leader of the H-Bombers all along. Spidey foils the H-Bombers' plans and saves Jameson from The Mad Bomber.</p>
<p>Spider-Man finds out about a science corporation called MechaBioCon, where a scientist named Dr Stillwell captured and tested <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mac_Gargan/Scorpion&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mac Gargan/Scorpion (page does not exist)">Mac Gargan/Scorpion</a> (who escaped his first encounter with Spider-Man in the subway station two years ago. Spid</p>
<p>Spider-Man goes to the city to chase and battle Scorpion in the city and then goes to sleep due to a very tiring day.</p>
<p>During his sleep, the symbiote consumes Peter, leading Peter to have a new jet-black suit. The new symbiote suit makes him stronger, more agile, and more aggressive. Marko, now the Sandman, recently robbed a bank, and is headed for the subways. Spider-Man pursues him, and eventually defeats him, washing him away in a sewer line.</p>
<p>Brock and Peter both are given an assignment by their boss, <a href="/wiki/J._Jonah_Jameson" title="J. Jonah Jameson">J. Jonah Jameson</a>: catch Spider-Man robbing something, and the one who does gets a promotion. Brock attempts to cheat by paying someone to pose as Spider-Man. The real Spider-Man appears, now influenced by the black suit, breaks Brock's camera and punches him. Brock tells him that he has many more cameras, and automatically photographs Spider-Man punching him. Spider-Man is able to recover the cameras and Brock swears revenge.</p>
<p>Peter gives Mary Jane a ride back to her apartment after a horrible date, where under the influence of the black suit, he alienates her. After acting too bossy and asking her to shut up, she decides to call off the relationship, which saddens Peter. Peter decides to go to a church, where he attempts to remove the symbiote suit, and successfully releases its bond to him. Unfortunately, Brock secretly finds and watches Peter unmask himself, and attaches to the symbiote, becoming Venom.</p>
<p>Brock finds a still-alive Sandman, and blackmails him to work together to defeat Spider-Man, or he will kill Sandman's daughter, Penny. Sandman eventually agrees. They kidnap MJ, so Peter suits up again in the red suit as Spider-Man. He goes to the construction site where Venom and Sandman are holding MJ captive in a taxi. He fights Venom and Sandman while Harry, recovered from his head injury, decides to help Peter rescue MJ as the New Goblin. Harry arrives and rescues MJ, and fights a giant Sandman which he defeats. Harry attempts to help Spider-Man fight Venom, but is killed in the process. Peter eventually defeats Venom by weakening him with sonic vibrations then dropping the two of them off a building. He survives as Venom is impaled by a steel bar.</p>
<p>Peter mends his relationship with MJ, and Sandman, still alive, is reunited with Penny, who has just been rescued by police.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wii.2C_PlayStation_2.2C_and_PlayStation_Portable">Wii, PlayStation 2, and PlayStation Portable</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spider-Man_3_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wii, PlayStation 2, and PlayStation Portable">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Note: these versions of the game contain a similar story to the next-gen versions of the game.</p>
<p>Spider-Man spots a building on fire which a gang called the H-Bombers blown up. Spidey defeats the bombers and rescues hostages inside the building. The next day, Peter Parker narrates "Just another day in the life of your friendly neighborhood Spider-Man! Lately, things have been going my way. I got the girl and New York finally likes me. Not that there are problems, but there is this new guy Eddie Brock at the Daily Bugle, and he is really starting to get on my nerves. Harry, my best friend won't talk to me. On top of that, new gangs have been dividing up the city. Still, its nothing I can handle. One weird thing is that there hasn't been any big super villain around since Doc Ock. I had this bad feeling that the sky is gonna fall or something and soon..."</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Game_Boy_Advance_and_Nintendo_DS">Game Boy Advance and Nintendo DS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spider-Man_3_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Game Boy Advance and Nintendo DS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As Spider-Man swings through the air, he spots a building on fire. He enters it and defuses a <a href="/wiki/Bomb" title="Bomb">bomb</a> which has been planted there. He then finds New Goblin flying around, and defeats him, knowing that it is his friend, Harry Osborn. He then retreats to save civilians who are trapped in a building.</p>
<p>After rescuing them, he is encountered by Sandman. Knowing that he cannot defeat Sandman, he flees to his home, where the alien symbiote takes over his body. Peter wakes up with the black suit, and uses it to his advantage. He then finds that <a href="/wiki/Electro_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Electro (Marvel Comics)">Electro</a> has kidnapped the Senator, and Mad Bomber has planted explosives throughout the city. After foiling their plans, and defeating them, he swings off to find Sandman, and gives chase. He then defeats Flint by opening a door which releases a large amount of water onto him. Spider-Man, thinking he killed Flint, tries to get the black suit off him, knowing that its influence is corrupting his mind. The black suit then is removed when sound waves drive it off, onto Eddie Brock, who desires revenge on Peter and Spider-Man, and gives himself the name "Venom". Spider-Man then finds that Sandman is still alive and well, and now has <a href="/wiki/Quicksand" title="Quicksand">quicksand</a> powers at his usage. He is then defeated once again by Peter. Venom then comes, and battles Spidey, but is defeated when dropped from the building. Spider-Man proceeds to call for medical attention, and the symbiote escapes into the night, with the screenshot saying "The End?".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="TV_game">TV game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spider-Man_3_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: TV game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Levels 1, 2, and 3 begin with Spider-Man chasing the New Goblin around the city. The New Goblin throws <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bombs" title="Bombs">bombs</a> at Spider-Man and he has to shoot them with webs to make the bombs blow up. The glider can blow up if Spider-Man shoots webbing at it. Spidey wakes up with a black suit and fights the Sandman and his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thugs" title="Thugs">thugs</a>. Spidey has to retrieve all the diamonds that Sandman stole and save people who are getting their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Backpacks" title="Backpacks">backpacks</a> stolen. (Levels 4-9). In Levels 10-12 <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Brock" title="Eddie Brock">Eddie Brock</a> becomes <a href="/wiki/Venom_(comics)" title="Venom (comics)">Venom</a> and teams up with the Sandman. They find a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Construction_site" title="Construction site">construction site</a> and fight Spider-Man there. They kidnap Mary Jane and Spidey has to save her.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mobile_phone">Mobile phone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spider-Man_3_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mobile phone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This version of the game does not include any of the actors of the film of the other platforms. The plot changes drastically compared with the <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_3" title="Spider-Man 3">film</a> and the other consoles, especially because none of the characters appear side of the other consoles, and this version follows a single thematic thread. As in most of the consoles, the player can return to any level using the <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man" title="Spider-Man">Spider-Man</a>'s traditional suit or black suit.</p>
<p>The game starts with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peter_Parker" title="Peter Parker">Peter</a> going to <a href="/wiki/Mary_Jane_Watson" title="Mary Jane Watson">Mary Jane</a>'s play, but he is late for fighting some thugs on the streets. Mary Jane is disappointed to Peter by his double life as <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man" title="Spider-Man">Spider-Man</a> and asks him if he can be <i>more like <a href="/wiki/Harry_Osborn" title="Harry Osborn">Harry</a></i>. Then appears Harry Osborn, with the New Goblin costume to take revenge on Peter for the death of his father. Spider-Man defeats Harry and gets the <a href="/wiki/Symbiote_(comics)" title="Symbiote (comics)">symbiote</a>.</p>
<p>Possessed by the symbiote, Peter ruins the career of his fellow reporter <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Brock" title="Eddie Brock">Eddie Brock</a>, defeats his former friend Harry, and almost kills <a href="/wiki/Sandman_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Sandman (Marvel Comics)">Flint Marko</a>. Realizing this, he decides to pull out the black suit, which falls into the hands of Eddie Brock, who becomes <a href="/wiki/Venom_(comics)" title="Venom (comics)">Venom</a>. Venom kidnaps Mary Jane and decides to take revenge on Peter. Spider-Man defeats Venom, who promises that they will meet again, and rescues Mary Jane. Peter and Mary Jane finally get reconciled.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spider-Man_3_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The game retains many of the gameplay elements from the <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_2_(video_game)" title="Spider-Man 2 (video game)">previous game</a>. Among the holdovers from the previous game is the freely explorable <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> map, which is larger in area than the one in <i>Spider-Man 2</i>. There have been various changes to the movement and combat system, including the usage of motion sensitive controls on the Wii version. The Nintendo DS version's combat system is entirely operated using the DS's touch screen - the <a href="/wiki/D-pad" title="D-pad">d-pad</a> is only used for moving Spider-Man. Players are also now able to interact at certain points during some <a href="/wiki/Cutscene" title="Cutscene">cutscenes</a>.</p>
<p>Another prominent feature is the ability to play as the black-suited Spider-Man from the film, which allows access to new attacks and special "Rage" moves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GSpot2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-GSpot2-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Microsoft_Windows.2C_PlayStation_3.2C_and_Xbox_360">Microsoft Windows, PlayStation 3, and Xbox 360</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spider-Man_3_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Microsoft Windows, PlayStation 3, and Xbox 360">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Spider-man_3_360.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="279" data-file-width="470" height="148" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/7/7f/Spider-man_3_360.jpg/250px-Spider-man_3_360.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/7/7f/Spider-man_3_360.jpg/375px-Spider-man_3_360.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/7/7f/Spider-man_3_360.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Spider-man_3_360.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A shot of Black-Suited Spider-Man by the alien symbiote.</div>

</div>
<p>The Treyarch-developed Xbox 360, PlayStation 3 and PC versions differ from the versions developed by Vicarious Visions in that initially players cannot change into the black suit at will. Instead, players change into the Black Suit at a certain point in the storyline. Players are also able to swing freely and enter the Bugle for assignments like in the <i>Spider-Man 2</i> game and can enter into Peter's apartment. Like many other PC games, the PC version can be modified, although few mods for this game have released. Scorpion, Rhino, and Kingpin are exclusive villains to this version. Peter Parker [in civilian clothes] is also playable through a glitch.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PlayStation_2.2C_PSP.2C_and_Wii">PlayStation 2, PSP, and Wii</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spider-Man_3_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: PlayStation 2, PSP, and Wii">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Vicarious Visions developed the game for the <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a>, <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> and <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a>. The game follows the movie, but has different side missions when compared to the Treyarch versions. Vicarious Visions allows players to switch between the red and black suit at will, although using the black suit has negative consequences. These versions include two exclusive villains: <a href="/wiki/Morbius,_the_Living_Vampire" title="Morbius, the Living Vampire">Morbius, the Living Vampire</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Shriek_(comics)" title="Shriek (comics)">Shriek</a>. Activision released <i>Spider-Man 3: Special Edition</i> for the PS2 which includes a bonus DVD featuring "Spider-Man 3: The Story Behind the Web", an exclusive interview with Stan Lee, the <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_3" title="Spider-Man 3">Spider-Man 3</a></i> movie trailers, and a collectible lenticular card with an image from the movie. Otherwise, the PS2 and Wii versions are largely the same, although the Wii version does utilize the <a href="/wiki/Wii_Remote" title="Wii Remote">Wii Remote</a>'s motion sensor controls for swinging and combat. In this version, there is an alternate ending in which Venom loses control of the symbiote instead of being impaled by a steel bar. The <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a> version is a full port of the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> version of the game by <a href="/wiki/Vicarious_Visions" title="Vicarious Visions">Vicarious Visions</a> and features a free-roaming city, <a href="/wiki/Morbius,_the_Living_Vampire" title="Morbius, the Living Vampire">Morbius, the Living Vampire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shriek_(comics)" title="Shriek (comics)">Shriek</a>, plus the black suit. The player can also land on the ground, unlike <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_2_(video_game)" title="Spider-Man 2 (video game)">Spider-Man 2</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a>, where he cannot land on the ground, similar to the original Spider-Man movie game.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Special_Editions">Special Editions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spider-Man_3_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Special Editions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Special Editions of the game were available to PS2 and PS3 owners. The PS3 Collector's Edition artwork shows the fully colored Black Costume of Spider-Man instead of a combination of both Spidey-Suits. Common features for the PS2 and PS3 were an interview with <a href="/wiki/Avi_Arad" title="Avi Arad">Avi Arad</a>, chief creative officer of <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Entertainment" title="Marvel Entertainment">Marvel Entertainment</a> and founder of <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Studios" title="Marvel Studios">Marvel Studios</a>, <i>Spider-Man 3</i> movie webisodes, a behind the scenes <a href="/wiki/Featurette" title="Featurette">featurette</a> with the cast, and a collectable lenticular card with the movie's images. The PS3 Collectors Edition came with a DLC which featured the ability to play as the <i>New Goblin</i> (Note: he will only be unlockable after you defeat him in the game.) The New Goblin DLC was later made available for download from <a href="/wiki/Xbox_Live" title="Xbox Live">Xbox Live</a> and <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Network" title="PlayStation Network">PlayStation Network</a> users.</p>

<p><i>Spider-Man 3</i> was met with average to mixed reviews. <a href="/wiki/GameRankings" title="GameRankings">GameRankings</a> and <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> gave it 63% and 68 out of 100 for the Game Boy Advance version;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRGBA_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-GRGBA-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCGBA_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-MCGBA-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> 78.17% and 79 out of 100 for the DS version;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRDS_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-GRDS-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCDS_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-MCDS-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> 62% and 53 out of 100 for the PlayStation 2 version;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRPC_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-GRPC-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCPC_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-MCPC-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> 53% and 60 out of 100 for the PC version;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRPS2_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-GRPS2-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCPS2_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-MCPS2-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> 66.24% and 63 out of 100 for the Xbox 360 version;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRX360_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-GRX360-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCX360_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-MCX360-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> 50.67% and 50 out of 100 for the Wii version;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRWII_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-GRWII-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCWII_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-MCWII-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> 63.03% and 62 out of 100 for the PlayStation 3 version;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRPS3_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-GRPS3-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCPS3_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-MCPS3-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> and 54.66% and 52 out of 100 for the PSP version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRPSP_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-GRPSP-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCPSP_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-MCPSP-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> The key criticism for these versions is the game's similarity to its predecessor.</p>
<p>The Wii, PS2, and PSP versions have been criticized for a short story mode and disappointing graphics, with <a href="/wiki/GamesRadar" title="GamesRadar">GamesRadar</a> suggesting that they were technically inferior to the preceding game from 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup> The Wii version has however been praised for the use of the Remote and Nunchuk in its gameplay, which is considered to be the Wii version's strongest point. The Xbox 360, PC and PS3 versions, despite receiving only average reviews, have been universally better received than the PS2, PSP, and Wii versions. The Wii version was given a "D+" grade by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wire_(magazine)" title="The Wire (magazine)">The Wire</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <i><a href="/wiki/X-Play" title="X-Play">X-Play</a></i> gave the Wii version 1 out of 5 (the first Wii game to get 1 out of 5),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCWII_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-MCWII-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> the PS3 and Xbox 360 versions 3 out of 5,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup> and the DS version 4 out of 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The game was also criticized for not being the same version on each system. Some criticism has surfaced due to having New Goblin only fully playable in the PS3 and Xbox 360. It was also criticized for having a lot of glitches. However, <i><a href="/wiki/Game_Informer" title="Game Informer">Game Informer</a></i> liked the PS3 and Xbox 360 versions of game, giving them an eight out of ten.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GI_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-GI-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> For the DS version, <a href="/wiki/GameSpot" title="GameSpot">GameSpot</a> praised the number of moves, Tobey Maguire's voice acting, and the variety of missions, but criticized the soundtrack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GSpot_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-GSpot-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/ScrewAttack" title="ScrewAttack">ScrewAttack</a> named the Wii version of the game the 7th worst superhero game.</p>
