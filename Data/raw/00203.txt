<td colspan="2"><span class="postbody"><img border="0" src="http://i195.photobucket.com/albums/z261/PHOENIX1974/new/eaglelanded.jpg"/>
<br/>
<br/>
(The spoilers have landed! There are a few below...)
<br/>
<br/>
We all know that Churchill was never captured by an elite German paratroop unit during World War II and whisked away back to Germany. Still it's funny. When things are presented from a certain view (like whether you're watching a nature program from the lion's or antelope's point of view) you are right behind whichever side's eyes you see through. So it is with <span style="font-style: italic">The Eagle Has Landed</span>. I wanted those guys to get Churchill so bad. It wouldn't have altered the course of the war, and these people know it - but just for the novelty of it. It would have been like a Bond movie, with Churchill tied down to some kind of laser apparatus and Hitler glowering over him, laughing maniacally. They could have traded wisecracks. But, of course...never happened.
<br/>
<br/>
<span style="font-style: italic">The Eagle Has Landed</span> is an enjoyable enough book, with well fleshed out characters that we care very much about, from both sides. The bookends are a little silly, with Higgins himself discovering the secret German mission that was hushed up from both sides after the war. I'm sure more than one naive reader believed the whole thing was a true story, but I'm sure Higgins realised that most people would understand that nearly everyone was with him in knowing it was pure fiction which included the author. Something like the reality-based docufilms that are popular today, melding reality with fantasy. There is also a mountain of narrative to get through before the mission starts, some three quarters of the book planning the damn thing which we already know is going to fail.  But it does add tension, and it does mean we invest an awful lot ourselves in the success of what these people set out to do. We know Radl, from the Abwehr, is dying, and would sure love it if he finds some kind of peace in knowing he pulled it off. We know how fine and honorable Steiner and Devlin are...and dammit...they deserve it after all the shit they've gone through! Of course, there are the less palatable characters like Himmler himself. But their character arcs aren't as tied in to the success of this one mission as are the others.
<br/>
<br/>
It's been a long time since I saw the film <span style="font-style: italic">The Eagle Has Landed</span>, but as my memory was refreshed I saw just how faithful the film was to the novel. There is a lot missing, but what is there fits in neatly - so much so that seeing the film after reading the book could almost be an anticlimax. Produced shorty after the novel was published, it cuts a few of the novel's characters loose, such as Steiner's father and the British Free Corps asshole Preston (and of course forgoes the bookends) but it's director John Sturges never makes it his own. Of course, Jack Higgins himself had a hand in adapting the screenplay, as if often the case. We also get to see Churchill shot at the end of the film I believe, where it's revealed that the Churchill visiting the quiet country town he's meant to be nabbed from is actually a double - meaning the mission would have been a failure anyway. It's a double blow for those reading the book as well - even though Steiner doesn't manage to kill Churchill in it, it's still revealed that this Churchill was a double. Kind of makes you feel reading the whole friggin' book was a waste of time.
<br/>
<br/>
There's another interesting note to be made here about reading a novel <span style="font-style: italic">after</span> you've seen the film. I couldn't for the life of me divorce the visage of Michael Caine from the character of Kurt Steiner no matter how much I tried while reading it. It's a curse in a way. Luckily I'd forgotten that Donald Sutherland plays Devlin, or that Robert Duvall plays Radl. I was able to give them their own unique faces.
<br/>
<br/>
Anyway, if you're looking for a war/espionage read you could do a lot worse. Same goes for watching the film. As I remember, it wasn't half bad.<br/>_________________<br/><span style="font-style: italic">I'm a rapper. A rapper. A rap-rap-rapper.
