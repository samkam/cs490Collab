<p><i><b>The Eagle Has Landed</b></i> is a 1976 British film directed by <a href="/wiki/John_Sturges" title="John Sturges">John Sturges</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Michael_Caine" title="Michael Caine">Michael Caine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Donald_Sutherland" title="Donald Sutherland">Donald Sutherland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Duvall" title="Robert Duvall">Robert Duvall</a>.</p>
<p>Based on the novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Eagle_Has_Landed" title="The Eagle Has Landed">The Eagle Has Landed</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Jack_Higgins" title="Jack Higgins">Jack Higgins</a>, the film is about a German plot to kidnap <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> during the height of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>. <i>The Eagle Has Landed</i> was Sturges' final film, and received positive reviews and was successful upon its release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes-review_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-review-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Eagle_Has_Landed_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film begins with captured Second World War film footage of the rescue from Italy of Mussolini by German paratroopers. Inspired by the <a href="/wiki/Gran_Sasso_raid" title="Gran Sasso raid">rescue</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hitler" title="Hitler">Hitler</a>'s ally <a href="/wiki/Benito_Mussolini" title="Benito Mussolini">Benito Mussolini</a> by <a href="/wiki/Otto_Skorzeny" title="Otto Skorzeny">Otto Skorzeny</a>, a similar idea is considered by Hitler, with the support of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Himmler" title="Himmler">Himmler</a> (<a href="/wiki/Donald_Pleasence" title="Donald Pleasence">Donald Pleasence</a>). <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Canaris" title="Wilhelm Canaris">Admiral Canaris</a> (<a href="/wiki/Anthony_Quayle" title="Anthony Quayle">Anthony Quayle</a>), head of the <a href="/wiki/Abwehr" title="Abwehr">Abwehr</a> (German military intelligence), is ordered to make a feasibility study of the seemingly impossible task of capturing the British Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> and bringing him to Germany.</p>
<p>Canaris considers the idea a joke, but realises that although Hitler will soon forget the matter, Himmler will not. Fearing Himmler may try to discredit him, Canaris orders one of his officers, <a href="/wiki/Oberst" title="Oberst">Oberst</a> Radl (<a href="/wiki/Robert_Duvall" title="Robert Duvall">Robert Duvall</a>) to undertake the study, despite feeling that it is a waste of time.</p>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Unteroffizier" title="Unteroffizier">Unteroffizier</a> on Radl's staff, Karl, finds that one of their spies, code named <i>Starling</i>, has provided tantalising intelligence – Winston Churchill is to visit an airfield near the (fictitious) village of Studley Constable in <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a>, where Joanna Grey (<a href="/wiki/Jean_Marsh" title="Jean Marsh">Jean Marsh</a>), a German <a href="/wiki/Sleeper_agent" title="Sleeper agent">sleeper agent</a> lives. Radl comes up with a scheme that could work, called 'Eagle', where the kidnapping will proceed with German troops leading the action. He also is convinced that 'synchronicity' is behind it all, where actions and conditions merge at the proper moment, at the proper time. Himmler summons Radl and unofficially tells him to proceed, without notifying Canaris but with Hitler's knowledge, providing Radl with an 'authorising' letter signed by Hitler himself. Radl recruits <a href="/wiki/Liam_Devlin" title="Liam Devlin">Liam Devlin</a> (<a href="/wiki/Donald_Sutherland" title="Donald Sutherland">Donald Sutherland</a>), a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irish_Republican_Army_(1922-1969)" title="Irish Republican Army (1922-1969)">IRA</a> lecturing at a <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> university, to the mission.</p>
<p>Radl looks for a suitable officer to lead the mission and chances upon the highly decorated and experienced, but conflicted and anti-Nazi, <a href="/wiki/Fallschirmj%C3%A4ger" title="Fallschirmjäger">Fallschirmjäger</a> officer <a href="/wiki/Oberst" title="Oberst">Oberst</a> Kurt Steiner (<a href="/wiki/Michael_Caine" title="Michael Caine">Michael Caine</a>). While returning from the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Front_(World_War_II)" title="Eastern Front (World War II)">Eastern Front</a>, Steiner intervened when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SS" title="SS">SS</a> soldiers rounded up <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">Jews</a> at a railway station in <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, and attempted to save the life of a teenage girl who was shot while trying to escape. For this, he was court-martialled, along with a platoon of his men. Rather than face the firing squad, the men were allowed to transfer to a penal unit on the <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Island</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alderney" title="Alderney">Alderney</a>, where they made high risk attacks with <a href="/wiki/Neger" title="Neger">Neger</a> Manned Torpedoes against <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">English Channel</a> convoys.</p>
<p>Radl travels to Alderney and, with the help of Devlin, recruits Steiner and his surviving men. The team will parachute into England from a captured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C-47_Skytrain" title="C-47 Skytrain">C-47</a> with Allied markings. The commandos outfit themselves as Polish paratroopers, as few of them speak English. The plan is to infiltrate Studley Constable, complete their mission, rendezvous with an <a href="/wiki/E-boat" title="E-boat">E-boat</a> on the nearby coast and escape. The paratroopers land and the operation proceeds.</p>
<p>Radl visits Himmler to announce, "The eagle has landed.", but then Himmler destroys the authorising document he'd given Radl, and Radl then knows the plan must succeed or he is in serious trouble.</p>
<p>As the Germans take up positions in the town, the plan is foiled when a paratrooper rescues a local girl from certain death by a water wheel. He instead is killed in the process and his German uniform (worn under the Polish uniforms at the prior insistence of Steiner) is revealed to the onlooking villagers. The locals are rounded up into the village church, but Pamela Vereker (<a href="/wiki/Judy_Geeson" title="Judy Geeson">Judy Geeson</a>), the sister of the village priest Father Vereker (<a href="/wiki/John_Standing" title="John Standing">John Standing</a>), escapes to alert a unit of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Rangers" title="United States Army Rangers">United States Army Rangers</a> stationed nearby. Inexperienced, glory-seeking Colonel Pitts (<a href="/wiki/Larry_Hagman" title="Larry Hagman">Larry Hagman</a>) tries to foil the German plan almost single-handed but is killed by treasonous Nazi sympathizer Joanna Grey in her house, while his poorly-planned assault on the church fails with heavy casualties. Pitt's young deputy, Captain Clark (<a href="/wiki/Treat_Williams" title="Treat Williams">Treat Williams</a>) then organises a second, successful attack.</p>
<p>Steiner's men sacrifice themselves to delay the Americans while Devlin, Steiner and his wounded second-in-command Neustadt (<a href="/wiki/Sven-Bertil_Taube" title="Sven-Bertil Taube">Sven-Bertil Taube</a>) escape, with the aid of local girl Molly Prior (<a href="/wiki/Jenny_Agutter" title="Jenny Agutter">Jenny Agutter</a>) who was romantically involved with Devlin. Instead of boarding the escape boat, Steiner vows to make one last attempt at Churchill.</p>
<p>Receiving radioed-news of the apparent failure of the operation, Radl orders his assistant Karl to leave Alderney immediately and return home to Germany, in order to avoid the fate Radl knows is forthcoming. Radl is then arrested and <a href="/wiki/Summary_execution" title="Summary execution">summarily executed</a> by firing squad, under the pretext that he "exceeded his orders to the point of treason". In this way Himmler distances himself from the failed mission.</p>
<p>Steiner does manage to infiltrate the country house and apparently succeeds in killing Churchill before being shot and killed himself. Captain Clark, on scene, is stunned by Churchill's death, but is then informed by security personnel that "Churchill" was actually George Fowler, an impersonator – the real Prime Minister is at the <a href="/wiki/Tehran_Conference" title="Tehran Conference">Tehran Conference</a>. Clark and a superior officer are also advised that, "No one will ever know about this. This never happened. It did not occur."</p>
<p>Devlin, evading capture, leaves a love letter for Molly, and disappears to live in obscurity.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Eagle_Has_Landed_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting_and_production">Casting and production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Eagle_Has_Landed_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Casting and production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Caine was originally offered the part of Devlin but did not want to play an IRA man so he asked if he could have the role of Steiner. <a href="/wiki/Richard_Harris" title="Richard Harris">Richard Harris</a> stepped into the part of Devlin but then it was felt he was too associated with financing the real IRA so Donald Sutherland was given the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sturges_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-sturges-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Tom Mankiewicz thought the script was the best he had ever written "but John Sturges, for some reason, had given up" and did a poor job. He said editor <a href="/wiki/Anne_V._Coates" title="Anne V. Coates">Anne V. Coates</a> was the one who saved the movie and made it watchable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tom_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-tom-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Michael Caine had initially been excited at the prospect of working with Sturges. During shooting, Sturges confessed to Caine that he was only doing the film to earn enough money to go fishing. Caine wrote later in his autobiography: "The moment the picture finished he took the money and went. [Producer] Jack Wiener later told me [that Sturges] never came back for the editing nor for any of the other good post-production sessions that are where a director does some of his most important work."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming_locations">Filming locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Eagle_Has_Landed_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Filming locations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cornwall was used to represent the <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Islands</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Berkshire" title="Berkshire">Berkshire</a> for <a href="/wiki/East_Anglia" title="East Anglia">East Anglia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-movie-locations_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-movie-locations-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The majority of the film, set in the fictional village of Studley Constable, was filmed at <a href="/wiki/Mapledurham" title="Mapledurham">Mapledurham</a> on the A329 in <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">Oxfordshire</a> and features the village church, <a href="/wiki/Mapledurham_Watermill" title="Mapledurham Watermill">Mapledurham Watermill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mapledurham_House" title="Mapledurham House">Mapledurham House</a>, which represented the Manor House where Winston Churchill was taken.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-movie-locations_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-movie-locations-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> A fake waterwheel was added to the 15th century structure for the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-movie-locations_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-movie-locations-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Mock buildings such as shops and a pub were constructed on site in Mapledurham while interiors were filmed at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twickenham_Studios" title="Twickenham Studios">Twickenham Studios</a>. The "Landsvoort Airfield" scenes were filmed at <a href="/wiki/RAF_St_Mawgan" title="RAF St Mawgan">RAF St Mawgan</a>, five miles from <a href="/wiki/Newquay" title="Newquay">Newquay</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-movie-locations_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-movie-locations-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The sequence set in <a href="/wiki/Alderney" title="Alderney">Alderney</a> was filmed in <a href="/wiki/Charlestown,_Cornwall" title="Charlestown, Cornwall">Charlestown</a>, near <a href="/wiki/St_Austell" title="St Austell">St Austell</a> in Cornwall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-movie-locations_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-movie-locations-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Some of the filming took place at <a href="/wiki/Rock,_Cornwall" title="Rock, Cornwall">Rock</a> in Cornwall. The railway station sequence where Steiner and his men make their first appearance was filmed in <a href="/wiki/Rovaniemi" title="Rovaniemi">Rovaniemi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-movie-locations_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-movie-locations-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The parachuting scenes were carried out by members of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/REME" title="REME">REME</a> (Royal Electrical and Mechanical Engineers) Parachute Display Team. The exit shots were filmed from a DC3 at <a href="/wiki/Dunkeswell_Aerodrome" title="Dunkeswell Aerodrome">Dunkeswell Airfield</a> in Devon. The landings onto the beach were filmed on <a href="/wiki/Holkham" title="Holkham">Holkham Beach</a> in Norfolk.</p>
<p>In the movie there is a scene where Radl and Karl look at an Ordnance Survey map with a magnifying glass, at an area just south east of present day Wells Next The Sea. This seems to suggest that the filmmakers wanted 'Studley Constable' to be located between Warham, Wighton and Binham. This is only a few miles from Holkham where the parachute drop was actually filmed. Reading of the book suggests the fictional village may have actually been based on nearby Cley Next The Sea.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Eagle_Has_Landed_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The original running time for the film was 134 minutes in Europe, while 11 minutes were cut by the producers before the film was released in the United States. The US 131 minutes <a href="/wiki/NTSC" title="NTSC">NTSC</a> DVD version is close to the original European cinema version, and most European <a href="/wiki/PAL" title="PAL">PAL</a> DVD versions also have that length (130 minutes).</p>
<p>In 2004 <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Communications" title="Carlton Communications">Carlton Visual Entertainment</a> released a two-disc PAL version which contains two versions of the film: the regular DVD version as well as an extended 145 minute version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-censorship_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-censorship-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> This 15 minutes longer version contains a number of scenes that had been deleted even before the European cinema release:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-censorship_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-censorship-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>An alternative opening: originally the film was intended to start with Heinrich Himmler (<a href="/wiki/Donald_Pleasence" title="Donald Pleasence">Donald Pleasence</a>) arriving at <a href="/wiki/Hohenschwangau_Castle" title="Hohenschwangau Castle">Schloss Hohenschwangau</a> for a conference with Hitler, Canaris, Bormann and Goebbels. This deleted opening would have preceded the scenes under the opening credits which are a long aerial shot of a staff car leaving the castle in question. The deleted scene explains why Schloss Hohenschwangau appears in the credits but does not appear in the film.</li>
<li>Extended scene when Radl arrives at Abwehr headquarters, he discusses his health with a German Army doctor (played by <a href="/wiki/Ferdy_Mayne" title="Ferdy Mayne">Ferdy Mayne</a>).</li>
<li>Scene at a Berlin University where Liam Devlin is a lecturer.</li>
<li>Scene in Landsvoort where Steiner and von Neustadt discuss the mission and its merits and consequences.</li>
<li>Devlin's arrival at Studley Constable is now extended where he and Joanna Grey discuss their part in the mission.</li>
<li>Devlin drives his motorbike through the centre of the village and on to the cottage, where he inspects the barn before returning to the village.</li>
<li>Scene where Devlin reads poetry to Molly Prior.</li>
<li>Extended scene in which Molly interrupts Devlin shortly after he receives the army vehicles.</li>
<li>Scene on the boat at the end that shows the fate of von Neustadt. (This scene is visible in one of the special edition DVDs extras, in the action photo gallery)</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Eagle_Has_Landed_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film was a success and ITC made two more films with the same production team, <i><a href="/wiki/Escape_to_Athena" title="Escape to Athena">Escape to Athena</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Green_Ice" title="Green Ice">Green Ice</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grade_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-grade-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Eagle_Has_Landed_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In his review for <i>The New York Times</i>, Vincent Canby called the film "a good old-fashioned adventure movie that is so stuffed with robust incidents and characters that you can relax and enjoy it without worrying whether it actually happened or even whether it's plausible."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes-review_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-review-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Canby singled out the writing and directing for praise:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Tom Mankiewicz's screenplay, based on a novel by Jack Higgins, is straightforward and efficient and even intentionally funny from time to time. Mr. Sturges ... obtains first-rate performances building the tension until the film's climactic sequence, which, as you might suspect, concludes with a plot twist. ... With so many failed suspense melodramas turning up these days, it's refreshing to see one made by people who know what they're about.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes-review_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-review-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
