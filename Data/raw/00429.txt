The film follows two dinosaur experts -- Dr. Alan Grant (Sam Neill) and Dr. Ellie Sattler Laura Dern) -- as they are invited by eccentric millionaire John Hammond (Richard Attenborough) to preview his new amusement park on an island off Costa Rica. By cloning DNA harvested

Accompanied by cynical scientist Ian Malcolm (Jeff Goldblum), who is obsessed with chaos theory, and Hammond's two grandchildren (Ariana Richards and Joseph Mazzello), they are sent on a tour through Hammond's new resort in computer controlled touring cars. But as a tropical storm hits the island, knocking out the power supply, and an unscrupulous employee (Wayne Knight) sabotages the system so that he can smuggle dinosaur embryos out of the park, the dinosaurs start to rage out of control. Grant then has to bring Hammond's grandchildren back to safety as the group is pursued by the gigantic man-eating beasts. ~ Paul Brenner, Rovi</span>

<p class="comment" style="line-height:1.25">
                    








































"Jurassic Park" is one of those films that will always stand the test of time. This timeless film follows a team of scientists as they discover through dinosaur fossils that their DNA can be recreated. And so, their idea to open a park on an island to bring in families for their own viewing pleasure begins. This film, because of it's backstory, is very believable, and it is what most of today's big budget films are missing (a believable human element). I love every second of this film, from it's fantastic characters, to it's giant plot, to it's "larger-than-life" characters. This will forever be one of my favourite films. "Jurassic Park" is a phenomenal piece of filmmaking.

<p class="comment" style="line-height:1.25">
                    








































Steven Spielberg's 1993 "dinosaurs on the loose" action-fest is almost comparable to Star Wars in the way it opened audiences' mind to the possibilities of visual effects (particularly, the previously little-used CGI). Even by today's standards the CGI and animatronic effects still hold a candle to modern efforts, breathing vivid life and personality into the prehistoric beasts. Due to seeing this movie in the theaters at a young age, certain scenes such as the first T-rex appearance and any suspenseful sequence with the velociraptors are forever burned in my memory. <br/><br/>But how does the actual movie hold up once the nostalgia goggles are taken off? Well, the film's biggest asset (aside from the entertaining visuals) is by far Spielberg's self-assured direction and it's tight pacing. There is very little wasted time when it comes to establishing the plot and key exposition points before diving into the awe-inspiring action sequences. Also, the touchingly emotional score by the ever-reliable John Williams is absolutely breath taking and is as iconic as the film itself. <br/><br/>While never boring, this sci-fi adventure is little different from the "big dumb Hollywood spectacles" that infest modern-day summers. The characters are rather unremarkable and under-written. John Hammond, the fun-loving park manager that is played delightfully by Richard Attenborough, is the only person that goes through anything resembling an arc but even then it's nothing spectacular. The story seems mostly concerned with getting to the next action set piece as soon as possible with little breathing room for the characters to develop. Due to this, actors such as Sam Neil, Laura Dern, and Martin Ferrero are given very little to work with outside of clunky and cliché exposition-laden dialogue (the "show don't tell rule" is broken quite a few times in this film). Jeff Goldblum fairs a little better as Ian Malcolm despite being mostly regulated to one-dimensional comic relief, and meanwhile Wayne Knight gives way to some pretty funny moments.  <br/><br/>The script tries to bring up ethical questions pertaining to "science vs. nature", but it's half-baked and practically dropped by the time the second-half kicks in. It also does not help how the characters act very stupid at times (Malcolm during the T-rex's debut appearance) and some pretty blatant continuity errors (the T-rex somehow sneaking up on velociraptors despite previously making tremors wherever it went).<br/><br/>In the end, this influential blockbuster delivers on its still-impressive special effects and infectious thrills, but stumbles with it's straightforward-to-a-fault narrative and thin characterization. While I'll always have a soft-spot for this flick, it's just a shame that as an adult what I remember so fondly makes up only a fraction of it's actual quality.


                </p>

<p class="comment" style="line-height:1.25">
                    








































If I had to rank my top 10-20 favorite movies of all time, "Jurassic Park" would have to be on that list(probably around 10).  It has everything.  Action, adventure, great acting, a great plot, and 20 years later is holds up amazingly well.  If you don't know the story, then run to the theater and see this now, because you will be missing out by not seeing this at least once on the big screen.  I saw a teaser in IMAX when I saw the new "Oz" movie, and immediately knew I wanted to see this again.  I wanted to see it at IMAX, but due to where I was the day we were gonna go, I could only see it in 3D.  The 3D is done well, but it doesn't really add anything to enhance the movie, unless you see it in IMAX.  The teaser I saw was the T-Rex scene, and let me tell you that was something special.  You felt like you were right there in the car with the kids and wanted to hold your ears shut too at how loud the T-Rex roars.  The effects on this movie are still awe inspiring and better than most movies made today.  Time has been very kind to "Jurassic Park".  I've heard that 2014 will bring part 4, and after rewatching this, it can't come soon enough

require(["jquery", "underscore","globals", "typeahead", "bootstrap"], function($,_, RT) {  // Typeahead and bootstrap don't return anything
    _.templateSettings = {
        interpolate: /\<\@\=(.+?)\@\>/gim,
        evaluate: /\<\@(.+?)\@\>/gim
    };
    RT.adRefresh = _.throttle(function() {
        googletag.pubads().refresh();
    }, 5000);
    /* search box code */
    
    var searchLoading = function() {
        var searchBtn = $(".navbar-searchbox .searchBtn"),
            searchBtnIcon = $("<em class='glyphicon glyphicon-search'></em>"),
            loadingBtnIcon = $("<div class='loading' style='width:16px;height:16px;'></div>"),
            query = "",
            loadingData = function() {
                if ($("em", searchBtn).length > 0) {
                    searchBtn.html(loadingBtnIcon);
                }
                $(".more.pull-right").each(function() {
                    $(this).removeClass("show");
                });
            },
            stopLoadingData = function() {
                if ($(".loading", searchBtn).length > 0) {
                    searchBtn.html(searchBtnIcon);
                }
                $(".more.pull-right").last().addClass("show");
            },
            updateQuery = function(newQuery) {
                query = newQuery;
            },
            getQuery = function() {
                return query;
            };
        return {
            loadingData: loadingData,
            stopLoadingData: stopLoadingData,
            query: getQuery,
            updateQuery: updateQuery
        };
    }();
    
    var movieResults = new Bloodhound({
            datumTokenizer: Bloodhound.tokenizers.obj.whitespace('value'),
            queryTokenizer: Bloodhound.tokenizers.whitespace,
            remote: {
                url: "/search/json/?catCount=2&q=",
                replace: function(url, query) {
                    searchLoading.updateQuery(query);
                    return url + query;
                },
                filter: function(media) {
                    return $.map(media.movies, function(data) {
                        data.type = "movie";
                        return { name: data.name, value: data, query: searchLoading.query() };
                    });
                },
                ajax: {
                    beforeSend: function() {
                        searchLoading.loadingData();
                    },
                    complete: function() {
                        searchLoading.stopLoadingData();
                    }
                }
            },
            limit:2
        }),
        tvResults = new Bloodhound({
            datumTokenizer: Bloodhound.tokenizers.obj.whitespace('value'),
            queryTokenizer: Bloodhound.tokenizers.whitespace,
            remote: {
                url: "/search/json/?catCount=2&q=",
                replace: function(url, query) {
                    searchLoading.updateQuery(query);
                    return url + query;
                },
                filter: function(media) {
                    return $.map(media.tvResults, function(data) {
                        data.type = "tv";
                        return { name: data.name, value: data, query: searchLoading.query() };
                    });
                }
            },
            limit:2
        }),
        actorResults = new Bloodhound({
            datumTokenizer: Bloodhound.tokenizers.obj.whitespace('value'),
            queryTokenizer: Bloodhound.tokenizers.whitespace,
            remote: {
                url: "/search/json/?catCount=2&q=",
                replace: function(url, query) {
                    searchLoading.updateQuery(query);
                    return url + query;
                },
                filter: function(media) {
                    return $.map(media.actors, function(data) {
                        data.type = "actor";
                        return { name: data.name, value: data, query: searchLoading.query() };
                    });
                }
            },
            limit:2
        });

    movieResults.initialize();
    tvResults.initialize();
    actorResults.initialize();

    $("#search-term").typeahead({
        highlight:true,
        minLength:2
    }, {
        name: "movies",
        source: movieResults.ttAdapter(),
        displayKey: function(data) {
            return data.name;
        },
        templates: {
            header: "<h3>Movies</h3>",
            suggestion: _.template("<div class='searchResult movieResult'><a class='media' href='/m/<@= value.vanity @>/?search=<@= query @>'><div class='pull-left searchResultImage'><img class='media-object' src='<@= value.image @>'/></div><div class='media-body'><p class='media-heading linkColor noSpacing small'><@= value.name @> (<@= value.year @>)</p><p class='small subtle noSpacing'><@= value.subline @></p></div></a></div>"),
            footer: _.template("<p class='more pull-right viewAllSearchResults'><a href='/search/?search=<@= query @>'>View All Search Results <span class='fontelloIcon icon-angle-right'></span></a></p>")
        }
    }, {
        name: "tv",
        source: tvResults.ttAdapter(),
        displayKey: function(data) {
            return data.name;
        },
        templates: {
            header: "<h3>TV Series</h3>",
            suggestion: _.template("<div class='searchResult tvResult'><a class='media' href='/tv/<@= value.vanity @>/?search=<@= query @>'><div class='pull-left searchResultImage'><img class='media-object' src='<@= value.image @>'/></div><div class='media-body'><p class='media-heading linkColor noSpacing small'><@= value.name @> (<@= value.startYear @> - <@= value.endYear @>)</p><p class='small subtle noSpacing'><@= value.subline @></p></div></a></div>"),
            footer: _.template("<p class='more pull-right viewAllSearchResults'><a href='/search/?search=<@= query @>'>View All Search Results <span class='fontelloIcon icon-angle-right'></span></a></p>")
        }
    }, {
        name: "actors",
        source: actorResults.ttAdapter(),
        displayKey: function(data) {
            return data.name;
        },
        templates: {
            header: "<h3>Celebrities</h3>",
            suggestion: _.template("<div class='searchResult celebrityResult'><a class='media' href='/celebrity/<@= value.vanity @>/?search=<@= query @>'><div class='pull-left searchResultImage'><img class='media-object' src='<@= value.image @>'/></div><div class='media-body'><p class='media-heading linkColor noSpacing small'><@= value.name @></p></div></a></div>"),
            footer: _.template("<p class='more pull-right viewAllSearchResults'><a href='/search/?search=<@= query @>'>View All Search Results <span class='fontelloIcon icon-angle-right'></span></a></p>")
        }
    }).on("typeahead:selected", function(evt, data) {
        debugger;
        if ("movie" == data.value.type) {
            document.location.href = "/m/" + data.value.vanity + "/?search=" + searchLoading.query();
        } else if ("tv" == data.value.type) {
            document.location.href = "/tv/" + data.value.vanity + "/?search=" + searchLoading.query();
        } else {
            document.location.href = "/celebrity/" + data.value.vanity + "/?search=" + searchLoading.query();
        }
    }).on("typeahead:opened", function() {
        $(".twitter-typeahead").css("position", "static");
        $(".twitter-typeahead .tt-input").css({"position":"absolute","width":$(".twitter-typeahead").width()});
        $(".twitter-typeahead .tt-hint").css("position", "relative");
        $(".tt-dropdown-menu").css("top","36px");
    });
    var onResize = function() {
        $(".twitter-typeahead").css("width", $(".navbar-searchbox").width()-$(".navbar-searchbox .input-group-btn").width());
        $(".twitter-typeahead .tt-input").css({"width":$(".twitter-typeahead").width()});
    };
    $(window).resize(onResize);
    $(window).on("orientationchange", onResize);
    $(".header_main .navbar-searchbox .searchBtn").click(function() {
        var searchQuery = $(".header_main .navbar-searchbox .twitter-typeahead pre").text();
        searchQuery = searchQuery.replace(/\ /g, "+");
        window.location.href = "/search/?search="+searchQuery;
    });
    
    $("[rel=tooltip], [data-toggle=tooltip]").tooltip();
});
</script>
