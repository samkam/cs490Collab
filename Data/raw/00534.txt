<p>"<b>Music</b>" is a song by American singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>. It is the titular track from her eighth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Music_(Madonna_album)" title="Music (Madonna album)">Music</a></i> (2000), and was released as the <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> on August 21, 2000, by <a href="/wiki/Maverick_Records" title="Maverick Records">Maverick Records</a>. The song was also included on the compilation albums <i><a href="/wiki/GHV2" title="GHV2">GHV2</a></i> (2001) and <i><a href="/wiki/Celebration_(Madonna_album)" title="Celebration (Madonna album)">Celebration</a></i> (2009). Written and produced by Madonna and <a href="/wiki/Mirwais_Ahmadza%C3%AF" title="Mirwais Ahmadzaï">Mirwais Ahmadzaï</a>, the nexus of "Music" is about people having fun at a party, and Madonna claims that music makes the people come together. Musically, "Music" is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disco_music" title="Disco music">disco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electropop" title="Electropop">electropop</a> song. "Music" provides a cross-section of Madonna's artistic range, as she sings in several genres, some natural and some electronically manipulated that refuse confinement.</p>
<p>"Music" received positive reviews from international critics, who praised the production, <a href="/wiki/Catchiness" title="Catchiness">catchiness</a> and club-friendly nature of the song, also comparing it with some of Madonna's older songs. "Music" peaked number one in 22 other countries, including Australia, Canada, Italy, New Zealand, Norway and the United Kingdom. The song reached number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. "Music" also has the longest running number-one spot at <i>Billboard</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Play" title="Hot Dance Club Play">Hot Dance Club Play</a> of the decade, with a longevity of five weeks at number one. The song was the second most successful dance single of the decade in the United States, behind Madonna's own "<a href="/wiki/Hung_Up" title="Hung Up">Hung Up</a>" (2005) single. The song was also the last number one hit on the Canadian RPM singles chart.</p>
<p>The music video, directed by <a href="/wiki/Jonas_%C3%85kerlund" title="Jonas Åkerlund">Jonas Åkerlund</a>, portrays Madonna and her friends having a party in her limousine, driven by comedian <a href="/wiki/Ali_G" title="Ali G">Ali G</a>. Promoting "Music", Madonna performed the song during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Europe_Music_Awards_2000" title="MTV Europe Music Awards 2000">Europe Music Awards</a> in 2000, wearing a T-shirt with "<a href="/wiki/Kylie_Minogue" title="Kylie Minogue">Kylie Minogue</a>" written on it, and at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/43rd_Grammy_Awards" title="43rd Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a> in the following year. The song was also performed on the four Madonna's following <a href="/wiki/List_of_Madonna_concert_tours" title="List of Madonna concert tours">concert tours</a>. Most recently, "Music" was performed during the halftime of the <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLVI" title="Super Bowl XLVI">Super Bowl XLVI</a>. "Music" was nominated at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/43rd_Grammy_Awards" title="43rd Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a> in the category of "<a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Record_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Record of the Year">Record of the Year</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Female_Pop_Vocal_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Female Pop Vocal Performance">Best Female Pop Vocal Performance</a> in 2001.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_(Madonna_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>After the critical and commercial success of her album <i><a href="/wiki/Ray_of_Light" title="Ray of Light">Ray of Light</a></i> (1998), Madonna intended to embark on a new concert tour in September 1999, but due to the delay of her film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Next_Best_Thing" title="The Next Best Thing">The Next Best Thing</a></i>, the tour was canceled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> After filming and promoting <i>The Next Best Thing</i>, her record company encouraged her to return to the studio and record new music before going on the road. These sessions became the <i>Music</i> album. Before the album was released, Madonna recorded a statement to her fans: "Hey Mr. DJ, put a record on... Hi, it's Madonna. You've probably been hearing about my new record, 'Music', for a while. Well, I just wanted to make sure you knew that the single is gonna drop very soon. I worked on it with a French guy named Mirwais, and he is the shit. The album will be released worldwide on September 19, and I hope you like my music."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>"Music" features production by <a href="/wiki/Mirwais_Ahmadza%C3%AF" title="Mirwais Ahmadzaï">Mirwais Ahmadzaï</a>, who Madonna has called a "genius."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Madonna and Mirwais Ahmadzaï were introduced through Madonna's business partner <a href="/wiki/Guy_Oseary" title="Guy Oseary">Guy Oseary</a>. In an interview with <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a>, Madonna said, "Guy Oseary, my partner at Maverick [Records], was given a demo by a French artist called Mirwais," she said. "[He] slipped it to me and said, 'What do you think [of him] as an artist to sign at Maverick?' [...] I just said 'Oh my God, this is what I want.' I just flipped over it and said, 'Please find out if he wants to work with me.'"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> On May 27, 2000, an unauthorized incomplete copy of the song leaked onto the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-napster_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-napster-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Snippets ranging from 30 seconds to upwards of three minutes of the song were leaked via fan sites and Napster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtv-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Madonna's spokeswoman Caresse Norman reacted a statement: "The music was stolen and was not intended for release for several months. It is still a work in progress. Ultimately those sites that offered a download of Madonna's music are violating her rights as an artist."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-mtv-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> To counter the trend of <a href="/wiki/Music_piracy" title="Music piracy">music piracy</a>, Madonna actively campaigned against Napster with fellow performers from other genres, including <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a> and drummer <a href="/wiki/Lars_Ulrich" title="Lars Ulrich">Lars Ulrich</a> of <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-napster_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-napster-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>"Music" begins with Madonna's androgynous voice saying "Hey Mr. DJ, put a record on, I want to dance with my baby". Above this lyric, Madonna's electronically manipulated voice asks "Do you like to boogie woogie?" In the book <i>Music, Space, and Place</i> it is stated that for the "boogie woogie" lyric Mirwais decided to use an old EMS2000 vocoder to twist Madonna's voice to achieve what Mirwais described as an effect of going "in fits and starts."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> According to the book <i>Madonna's Drowned Worlds: New Approaches to Her Cultural Transformations</i> (ed. Santiago Fouz-Hernández and Freya Jarman-Ivens), "Music" is a "disco anthem, and the beat commands [the people] to get up and dance."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-catch_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-catch-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The song's lyric "I wanna dance with my baby" may further reinforce a connection with gay listeners because of its casual, campy style.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-catch_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-catch-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The book also says that the song is an expression to her public and became one of Madonna's catchiest singles of her career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-catch_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-catch-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The song has been compared to Madonna's early 80s dance hit "<a href="/wiki/Everybody_(Madonna_song)" title="Everybody (Madonna song)">Everybody</a>," because in both songs Madonna declares that music has the power to overcome divisions of race, gender, and sexuality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-catch_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-catch-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The song is written in the key of <a href="/wiki/G_minor" title="G minor">G minor</a> and Madonna's voice spans from G<sup>3</sup> to D<sup>5</sup>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-notes_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-notes-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> It is set in common time with a moderately fast <a href="/wiki/Tempo" title="Tempo">tempo</a> of 120 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beats_per_minute" title="Beats per minute">beats per minute</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-notes_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-notes-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> According to <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i>, Madonna's vocal in the song is not the focal point, but instead a "thread that binds a mind-blowing melange of hyperactive beats, grooves, and stinging percussive elements."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Billboard</i> adds that the song is also "saturated" with the influence of <a href="/wiki/Cameo_(band)" title="Cameo (band)">Cameo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herbie_Hancock" title="Herbie Hancock">Herbie Hancock</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nitzer_Ebb" title="Nitzer Ebb">Nitzer Ebb</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_(Madonna_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:30%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"Music" is a stunning enterprise, a ballsy testament to [Madonna's] insistence on being a style-setter and one of the industry's most savvy-and now critically accountable-tunesmiths. Call this one dance, pop, even alternative...listeners will be shocked then mesmerized by this composition, showcasing yet another side of an artist, who after twenty years, continues to be a true industry artisan and the by-the-book definition of "evolutionary."</p>
</div>
</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">—-<i>Billboard Magazine's</i> review of the song<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></div>
</div>
<p>The song received positive reaction from critics, who praised the production, catchiness and club-friendly nature of the song. <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Thomas_Erlewine" title="Stephen Thomas Erlewine">Stephen Thomas Erlewine</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a> called it "a thumping track which sounds funkier, denser, sexier with each spin".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> Jim Farber of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_New_York_Daily_News" title="The New York Daily News">The New York Daily News</a></i> gave the song a very positive review, stating that it is "everything a single should be. Pithy, simple and maddeningly catchy, it's her most instantly embraceable single since "<a href="/wiki/Holiday_(Madonna_song)" title="Holiday (Madonna song)">Holiday</a>" (1983). Farber goes on to say that it's the song's chorus that "kills," and the "power of dance music" theme of the song is used to comment on current trends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> In a review of the album, <i><a href="/wiki/Vibe_(magazine)" title="Vibe (magazine)">Vibe</a></i> called the track "a bouncing parade of synthesizers that pose the question: 'Do forty-something baby-mamas still have the divine right to get down?' (The answer is yes). And so, as the vocoders gurgle and the analog synths invoke Zapp, a slowly building crescendo of rhythm announces Madonna's return."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> said "the song recaptures the simple perfection of "Holiday" and brings her career full circle."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Slant_Magazine" title="Slant Magazine">Slant</a></i> magazine praised the song in a review of the album: "["Music"] is a retro hands-in-the-air club song reminiscent of <a href="/wiki/Debbie_Deb" title="Debbie Deb">Debbie Deb</a>'s "When I Hear Music" and Madonna's own "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Into_The_Groove" title="Into The Groove">Into The Groove</a>" (1985) [and] is the singer's best dancefloor-beckoning track since "<a href="/wiki/Vogue_(Madonna_song)" title="Vogue (Madonna song)">Vogue</a>" (1990). She sings "Music makes the people come together" like a track off of her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Madonna_(album)" title="Madonna (album)">debut album</a>, and as an added bonus she uses words like "bourgeoisie" and "acid-rock" with equal abandon."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Ethan Brown of <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_(magazine)" title="New York (magazine)">New York</a></i> magazine stated that the song "elicits memories of past pop odes to dance culture," adding that the songs production is a "giddy mix of electro-bounce, campy vocoder chants, and funky keyboard squeals."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> In her favorable review of the song, Charlotte Robinson of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Popmatters" title="Popmatters">Popmatters</a> stated that the track "reasserts the freewheeling, fun spirit that made Madonna so damn appealing when she debuted nearly twenty years ago," adding that the song is an example of that "indefinable thing that is what we love about Madonna."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><i>Billboard Magazine</i> gave a very enthusiastic review of the song, stating "the first listen's gonna blast you like a brick wall, as ears and minds try to make sense of the bombast of sonic missiles blasting from your speakers-but successive plays will make clear just how inventive this upstanding composition is."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Billboard</i> adds that "it's guaranteed you've never heard Madonna-nor any other popular artist-pull it together this way."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> The song was named the 66th best song of the 2000s decade by <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>, which said that "despite all the new pop starlets out there trying to jump her train, Madonna definitely [is] not slackening pace. When she dropped "Music," she was older than <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina</a> combined, yet she took them to school with vintage electro-boom, Eurodisco flourishes from French producer <a href="/wiki/Mirwais_Ahmadza%C3%AF" title="Mirwais Ahmadzaï">Mirwais</a>, and her own inimitable sass."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> Apart from winning various other awards, "Music" earned two <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nominations in 2001, for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Record_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Record of the Year">Record of the Year</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Female_Pop_Vocal_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Female Pop Vocal Performance">Best Female Pop Vocal Performance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_reception">Commercial reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_(Madonna_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Commercial reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>"Music" was Madonna's 12th number one single on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and is her most recent number-one hit on that chart to date. By reaching number one, it made Madonna the second artist to achieve number one hits on the Hot 100 in the 1980s, 1990s and 2000s. The first was <a href="/wiki/Janet_Jackson" title="Janet Jackson">Janet Jackson</a>, who had done the same achievement with "<a href="/wiki/Doesn%27t_Really_Matter" title="Doesn't Really Matter">Doesn't Really Matter</a>", which preceded "Music" as the Hot 100's number one single. "Music" was also the longest-running number one song on the <i>Billboard</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Play" title="Hot Dance Club Play">Hot Dance Club Play</a> in the 2000s, with a longevity of five weeks at number one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> It eventually became the second most-successful dance song of the 2000s in the United States, reaching number two on the Hot Dance Club Play Decade-end Chart, behind Madonna's own "<a href="/wiki/Hung_Up" title="Hung Up">Hung Up</a>" (2005).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dance_decade_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-dance_decade-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> "Music" was certified platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaa_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-riaa-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> In the United Kingdom, "Music" debuted on the UK Singles Chart at number one,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ukpeak_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-ukpeak-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> and was the 24th best selling single of 2000 in the United Kingdom. According to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Official_Charts_Company" title="The Official Charts Company">The Official Charts Company</a>, the song has sold 410,000 copies there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The song peaked number one on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Singles_Chart" title="Canadian Singles Chart">Canadian Singles Chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-all_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-all-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> "Music" was also a success in Australia and New Zealand. The song debuted at number one on the Australian <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Charts" title="ARIA Charts">ARIA Singles Chart</a>, where it stayed for three consecutive weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aus_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-aus-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> In New Zealand, "Music" made its debut at number 33 on the New Zealand Singles Chart,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nz_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-nz-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> and ascended to the top ten, finally peaking at number one on the week of October 1, 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nz_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-nz-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> Making its debut at its peak position of number 8, "Music" charted for a total of 20 weeks in France, before falling out on January 6, 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fra_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-fra-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> On the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Top_40" title="Dutch Top 40">Dutch Top 40</a> chart, the song made its debut at number 87.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-neth_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-neth-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> The following week it went at number 85 and its peak was on the number 4, before the ending of its 17-week run.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-neth_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-neth-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> On August 31, 2000, "Music" debuted at number two on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swedish_Singles_Chart" title="Swedish Singles Chart">Swedish Singles Chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swe_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-swe-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> In Switzerland, the song debuted at number one, and spent 21 weeks in the chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swi_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-swi-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup> Overall, "Music" reached number one in 25 countries worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>The music video for "Music" was directed by Swedish director <a href="/wiki/Jonas_%C3%85kerlund" title="Jonas Åkerlund">Jonas Åkerlund</a>, who previously directed Madonna's "<a href="/wiki/Ray_of_Light_(song)" title="Ray of Light (song)">Ray of Light</a>" video in 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.C3.85kerlund_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-.C3.85kerlund-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> The video was shot in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>, in April 2000, and stars Madonna and her longtime backup singer <a href="/wiki/Niki_Haris" title="Niki Haris">Niki Haris</a> and actress <a href="/wiki/Debi_Mazar" title="Debi Mazar">Debi Mazar</a> as well as comedian <a href="/wiki/Sacha_Baron_Cohen" title="Sacha Baron Cohen">Sacha Baron Cohen</a>, as his famous character <a href="/wiki/Ali_G" title="Ali G">Ali G</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup> Madonna's manager, Caresse Norman, said about Baron Cohen's collaboration: "We came up with the video idea, and she thought he would be great in it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup> Jonas Åkerlund has stated that originally they were planning on having <a href="/wiki/Chris_Rock" title="Chris Rock">Chris Rock</a> be in the music video, but he wanted Sacha Baron Cohen instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.C3.85kerlund_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-.C3.85kerlund-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> He said that at that time Baron Cohen wasn't known at all in America so he had to get everybody tapes of the <i><a href="/wiki/Da_Ali_G_Show" title="Da Ali G Show">Da Ali G Show</a></i> so they could see the Ali G character. He added that once they got a chance to see the show, "everybody loved it" and Sacha was on a plane right away to Los Angeles to shoot the video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.C3.85kerlund_34-2"><a href="#cite_note-.C3.85kerlund-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<blockquote>
<p>When we did the 'Music' video, it was a weird time. She was pregnant and we didn't want her to look pregnant – so we had to work around that. We had an idea to do a fun party video with her and her girls, make it a little bling and cowboy hats and all that. We wanted to have some comedy in there and I wanted to do some animation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.C3.85kerlund_34-3"><a href="#cite_note-.C3.85kerlund-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>

<p>The music video debuted on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> on August 2, 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pr_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-pr-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> The video starts with Madonna along with her friends boarding a limousine driven by Ali G. As the music starts G is directed to take them to a club, and later to a strip-club, where he is denied entrance. An animated section follows where Madonna, as a superheroine character with superpowers, flies above rooftops, swims underwater, and DJs at a club with a dozen arms like a Hindu deity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guilbert_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guilbert-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> Madonna's animated character also attacks various neon signs with the names of many of her successful songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pr_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-pr-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> The video ends with Madonna and her friends traveling in the limousine with many of the strippers and G engaging in a rendezvous with them. In a humorous twist, throughout the video Madonna wears a gold necklace around her neck that says "Mommy."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guilbert_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-Guilbert-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the extended version of the music video, there is a variation at the end of the animated section. Ali G briefly interrupts the song to demonstrate his rap skills to persuade Madonna to include him on her next single. An annoyed Madonna asks him to stop and to turn the song back on. The video won several awards, including "Best Pop Clip of the Year" at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_Music_Awards" title="Billboard Music Awards">Billboard Music Awards</a> in 2000 and "Best Dance Video" at the International Dance Music Awards in 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> The music video was parodied on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MADtv" title="MADtv">MADtv</a></i>. The parody was called <i>Movies</i>, and featured <a href="/wiki/Mo_Collins" title="Mo Collins">Mo Collins</a> as Madonna.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup> The video poked fun at Madonna's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Madonna_films" title="List of Madonna films">filmography</a>, with an animated 'Madonna' attacking signs that had the names of her movies.</p>

<p>The first live performance of "Music" was at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Europe_Music_Awards_2000" title="MTV Europe Music Awards 2000">MTV Europe Music Awards 2000</a>. She performed the song wearing a T-shirt with "<a href="/wiki/Kylie_Minogue" title="Kylie Minogue">Kylie Minogue</a>" written on it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> She also performed the song at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/43rd_Grammy_Awards" title="43rd Grammy Awards">2001 Grammy Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup> For the <a href="/wiki/Drowned_World_Tour" title="Drowned World Tour">Drowned World Tour</a> in the same year, "Music" was used as the final encore; Madonna wore tight black jeans and a customised <a href="/wiki/Dolce_%26_Gabbana" title="Dolce &amp; Gabbana">Dolce &amp; Gabbana</a> halter top that proclaims "Mother" in the front and "F*cker" in the back. She sang the song surrounded by her dancers, while imagery of her all-time music videos were displayed on backdrop screens. Also, incorporated were elements from <a href="/wiki/Kraftwerk" title="Kraftwerk">Kraftwerk</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Trans-Europe_Express_(song)" title="Trans-Europe Express (song)">Trans-Europe Express</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-omh_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-omh-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> "Music" was later added to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Re-Invention_Tour" title="Re-Invention Tour">Re-Invention Tour</a> in 2004 as the fourth song of the Scottish segment; this mix of the song was slower, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hip-hop" title="Hip-hop">hip-hop</a>-based.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slant_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-slant-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup> The same version was used at the 2005 <a href="/wiki/Live_8" title="Live 8">Live 8</a> benefit concert in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Confessions_Tour" title="Confessions Tour">Confessions Tour</a> in 2006, the song was mixed with <a href="/wiki/The_Trammps" title="The Trammps">The Trammps</a>' "<a href="/wiki/Disco_Inferno" title="Disco Inferno">Disco Inferno</a>", thus creating the hedonistic "Music Inferno". This also included a sample of her song "Where's The Party" at the beginning of the song, while dancers rollerskated circles around.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rs_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-rs-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stylus_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-stylus-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>"Music" was part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hard_Candy_Promo_Tour" title="Hard Candy Promo Tour">Hard Candy Promo Tour</a>, in support of the <i><a href="/wiki/Hard_Candy_(Madonna_album)" title="Hard Candy (Madonna album)">Hard Candy</a></i> album; this version sampled <a href="/wiki/Fedde_le_Grand" title="Fedde le Grand">Fedde le Grand</a>'s "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Put_Your_Hands_Up_for_Detroit" title="Put Your Hands Up for Detroit">Put Your Hands Up for Detroit</a>", getting a <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">Dance</a>-version of the song. It was the last song performed during the <i>Old School</i> segment of her <a href="/wiki/Sticky_%26_Sweet_Tour" title="Sticky &amp; Sweet Tour">Sticky &amp; Sweet Tour</a> in 2008, where Madonna used the version of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hard_Candy_Promo_Tour" title="Hard Candy Promo Tour">promotional tour</a>, with a sample of <a href="/wiki/Indeep" title="Indeep">Indeep</a>'s "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Last_Night_a_DJ_Saved_My_Life_(song)" title="Last Night a DJ Saved My Life (song)">Last Night A DJ Saved My Life</a>" at the beginning of the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mashup_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-mashup-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup> Madonna included the song in the setlist for her performance at halftime of <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLVI" title="Super Bowl XLVI">Super Bowl XLVI</a>, interpolated with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LMFAO_(group)" title="LMFAO (group)">LMFAO</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Party_Rock_Anthem" title="Party Rock Anthem">Party Rock Anthem</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Sexy_and_I_Know_It" title="Sexy and I Know It">Sexy and I Know It</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-au.eonline.com_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-au.eonline.com-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-au.eonline.com_51-1"><a href="#cite_note-au.eonline.com-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> A brief excerpt of the song was included during the "Turning Up the Hits" interlude on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MDNA_Tour" title="MDNA Tour">MDNA Tour</a>.</p>
<p>In 2004, Canadian Hard Rock band <a href="/wiki/Out_of_Your_Mouth" title="Out of Your Mouth">Out of Your Mouth</a> released a <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover version</a>. In 2007, <a href="/wiki/The_Dynamics" title="The Dynamics">The Dynamics</a> released an eight-minute soul cover on their album <i>Versions Excursions</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup> Remixed <a href="/wiki/Eurodance" title="Eurodance">eurodance</a> cover versions of the song have appeared on the <i><a href="/wiki/Dancemania" title="Dancemania">Dancemania</a></i> series albums. Including an uptempo cover remix by <a href="/wiki/CJ_Crew" title="CJ Crew">Nancy And The Boys</a> on the 2001 album <i><a href="/wiki/Dancemania_Speed" title="Dancemania Speed">Dancemania Speed 6</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup> French metal band <a href="/wiki/Eths" title="Eths">Eths</a> covered the song on their 2012 album <i>III</i>.</p>
