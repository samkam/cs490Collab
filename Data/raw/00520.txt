                  Presley Pictures</a> , <a class="style9" href="http://video.elvispresley.com.au/">Elvis Video</a>, <a class="style9" href="http://mp3audio.elvispresley.com.au/">Elvis MP3 Audio</a>, <a class="style9" href="http://elvispresleyphotos.com/">Elvis Photos</a> + <a class="style9" href="http://biography.elvis.com.au/">Elvis 
                    Presley Biography</a>, <a class="style9" href="http://movies.elvispresley.com.au/">Elvis' Movies</a> and <a class="style9" href="#" onclick="flvFPW1('http://www.elvis.com.au/admin/GracelandCam/Gcam1.htm','Cam','width=390,height=430',1);return document.MM_returnValue">Graceland

<p><span class="style1"><span class="style2"><strong>Elvis Presley Music</strong> </span> contains <strong><a href="http://www.elvispresleymusic.com.au/music_index.html">music videos</a></strong>, </span><span class="style2"><strong> video and audio interviews</strong> </span><span class="style1">and a</span><span class="style2"><strong> 'day by day'</strong> </span><span class="style1"><strong> <a href="elvis_presley_1935_1953.html">biography of Elvis Presley's life</a></strong> from</span><span class="style2"><strong> 1935 to 1977</strong>, </span><span class="style1"><strong></strong> including all concerts, movies and important events.</span></p>
<p class="style1"><strong><a href="http://www.elvispresleymusic.com.au/pictures/index.html"><img align="left" alt="Elvis Presley" border="0" height="261" src="images/elvis_presley.jpg" width="202"/></a></strong> Elvis was born on <a href="elvis_presley_1935_1953.html"><strong>January 8, 1935</strong></a>, in what has been described as a 'two-room shotgun  house' in East Tupelo, Mississippi.</p>
<p class="style1">In <a href="elvis_presley_1953_1955.html"><strong>1953</strong></a>, Elvis made his first demo recording for <strong>Sam Phillips</strong>'  Memphis-based <strong><a href="articles/cd_elvis_at_sun.html">Sun Records</a></strong>  and  between <a href="elvis_presley_1953_1955.html"><strong>1953 and 1955</strong></a>, Elvis recorded a number of regional hits  for <strong>Sun</strong>. In November 1955, <a href="http://coloneltomparker.elvis.com.au/"><strong>Colonel Tom Parker</strong></a> arranged the purchase of Presley's contract by the  much larger RCA Records.</p>
<p class="style1">For Elvis Presley, <a href="elvis_presley_1956.html"><strong>1956</strong></a> was a year like no other.</p>
<p class="style1">In January, he was a <strong>regional sensation</strong>, but by year’s end he had become a <strong>national and international phenomenon</strong>. He made his first two albums for RCA (both million sellers), recorded classics  like 'Heartbreak Hotel',  'Don't  Be Cruel',  'Hound Dog',  and 'Love Me Tender</p>'. He appeared on <strong>national television 11 times</strong>, signed a <strong>seven- year contract with Paramount Pictures</strong>, and stared in his first movie, <strong>Love Me Tender</strong>. Elvis' appearances on national television were pivotal events for America because his unconventional appearance and performing style caused nationwide controversy. 
<p class="style1">Elvis Presley outraged adults, mesmerized the teenagers of the new youth generation, and soon became the leader of the cultural revolution sweeping across the country.<br/>
</p>
<p><span class="style1">In <a href="elvis_presley_1957.html"><strong>1957</strong></a>, Elvis made two movies, <strong>Loving You</strong>  and <strong>Jailhouse Rock</strong>, and also recorded soundtracks for both. 'Jailhouse Rock' included a production number of the title song that was the </span><span class="style4">prototype for the music video</span><span class="style1">, a recording industry format that became a standard feature with the advent of <strong class="style4">MTV</strong>  in the early 1980s.</span></p>
<p class="style1">In  March <a href="elvis_presley_army_1958_1960.html"><strong>1958</strong></a>, Elvis was inducted into the <strong>United States Army</strong>  for a  two-year stint. </p>
<p class="style1">The Memphis Draft Board granted Elvis a deferment so filming of his fourth movie, <strong>King Creole</strong> could be   finished. Thousands of fans wrote pleading letters, begging that  their hero not be drafted. Thousands more (female) fans reportedly wept  when their hero's locks were sheared in a regulation military crew cut.  But Elvis returned to civilian life two years later, and more hit  records and movies followed.</p>

<p class="style1">Elvis Presley's fame grew. He continued to star in films like <strong>Blue Hawaii</strong> and <strong>Viva Las Vegas</strong>  throughout the 1960s. While Elvis was not a trained actor, his charisma filled the big screen.</p>
<p class="style1">Elvis continued to  enjoy commercial success during the 1960s, although changing tastes  brought artists associated with Motown and the 'British Invasion' more  to the fore with younger listeners. Elvis' audience aged with him, and  for many, Presley symbolized the America of their youth.</p>
<p class="style1">The<strong> December 3, 1968</strong>,    broadcast of his television special, <a href="video/elvis_68_comeback_special.html"><strong>Elvis</strong></a>,</p> turned his career around by   introducing him to hipper recording material and new directions.
<p class="style1">Starting <strong>January 13, <a href="elvis_presley_1967_1969.html">1969</a></strong>, in Memphis, Tennessee, Elvis recorded at <strong>Chips Moman's</strong> <strong>American Sound Studios</strong>... In twelve days, he cut thirty-six sides. Four of   them were singles - 'In the Ghetto,' 'Suspicious Minds,' 'Don't Cry Daddy,' and   'Kentucky Rain,' and all but the last were gold, even though Kentucky Rain was a   substantial hit. And the two albums that came out of it [From Elvis in   Memphis</p> and From Memphis to Vegas/From Vegas to Memphis] went   platinum.
<p><span class="style1">When Elvis   returned to the live stage after the success of his</span> <span class="style1">1968 television special </span><span class="style1">and   the wrap-up of his Hollywood movie contract obligations, he opened at the   International Hotel in <strong>Las Vegas</strong>  in the summer of 1969 for a 4-week, 57-show   engagement that <strong>broke all existing Las Vegas attendance records</strong>. He returned to   the International a few months later in early 1970, during the slow winter   season in Vegas, and broke his own attendance record. Right after that came a   record-breaking six-show engagement at the Astrodome in Houston, where Elvis   played to a total of 207,494 people.</span></p>
<p class="style1">During his 'concert years' from 1969 to   1977, Elvis gave nearly 1,100 concert performances.</p>
<p class="style1">Elvis   Presley's three network television   specials - <a href="video/elvis_68_comeback_special.html"><strong>Elvis</strong></a> (1968), <strong><a href="video/aloha_from_hawaii.html">Elvis:   Aloha from Hawaii, via Satellite</a></strong>  (1973), and <strong><a href="video/elvis_1977.html">Elvis   in Concert</a></strong>  (1977) - stand among the most highly rated specials of   their time. Elvis - Aloha from Hawaii, via   Satellite, was seen in 40 countries by 1 billion to 1.5 billion people and made   television history. It was seen on television in more American homes than man's   first walk on the moon.</p>
<p class="style1">His talent, good looks, sensuality, charisma, and good humor endeared him to   millions, as did the humility and human kindness he demonstrated throughout his   life. Known the world over by his first name, he is regarded as one of the most   important figures of twentieth century popular culture.</p>
<p class="style1">His music, personality and verve touched millions, from the American  teens of the 1950s to Japanese Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi and U.S. Presedent George Bush who paid their respects in June 2007 as just two of the 750,000 annual visitors to  Graceland. (See <a href="http://www.elvis.com.au/presley/news/bush_koizumi_at_graceland.shtml" target="_blank"><strong>related article</strong></a>)</p>
<p class="style1"> Among his many awards and accolades   were <strong>14 Grammy nominations</strong>  (<span class="style4">3 wins</span>) from the National Academy of Recording Arts   &amp; Sciences, the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award, which he received at age   36, and his being named One of the<strong> <a href="http://elvispresleyphotos.com/jaycees_award_1971_january_16.html">Ten Outstanding Young Men of the Nation for   1970</a></strong>  by the United States Jaycees.</p>
<p class="style1">Elvis'  greatest legacy, though, is the music, and the rockers and other  musicians who built on it.</p>
<p class="style1"><a href="http://www.elvis.com.au/presley/article_thenightspringsteenjumpedthefence.shtml"><strong>Bruce  Springsteen</strong></a> said: 'It was like he whispered his dream in all our ears  and then we dreamed it'. Beatles vocalist and rhythm guitarist <strong>John Lennon</strong>  said, 'Before Elvis there was nothing'. It has been estimated that The King has  sold more than <a class="style9" href="http://www.elvis.com.au/presley/one_billion_record_sales.shtml">1 billion recordings</a>.</p>
