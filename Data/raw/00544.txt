<section class="review read-more" itemprop="review" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">
<meta content="" itemprop="reviewRating" itemref="microdata-rating" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
<h4 class="review-author headline">
            Review
                            by <span itemprop="author">Stephen Thomas Erlewine</span>
</h4>
<div class="text" itemprop="reviewBody">
<p>
                    Filled with vocoders, stylish neo-electro beats, dalliances with trip-hop, and, occasionally, eerie synthesized atmospherics, <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/music-mw0000088531">Music</a> blows by in a kaleidoscopic rush of color, technique, style, and substance. It has so many layers that it's easily as self-aware and earnest as <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/ray-of-light-mw0000033767">Ray of Light</a>, where her studiousness complemented a record heavy on spirituality and reflection. Here, she mines that territory occasionally, especially as the record winds toward its conclusion, but she applies her new tricks toward celebrations of music itself. That's not only true of the full-throttle dance numbers but also for ballads like "I Deserve It" and "Nobody's Perfect," where the sentiments are couched in electronic effects and lolling, rolling beats. Ultimately, that results in the least introspective or revealing record <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/madonna-mn0000237205">Madonna</a> has made since <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/like-a-prayer-mw0000198738">Like a Prayer</a>, yet that doesn't mean she doesn't invest herself in the record. Working with a stable of producers, she has created an album that is her most explicitly musical and restlessly creative since, well, <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/like-a-prayer-mw0000198738">Like a Prayer</a>. She may have sacrificed some cohesion for that willful creativity but it's hard to begrudge her that, since so much of the album works. If, apart from the haunting closer "Gone," the <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/orbit-mn0000405080">Orbit</a> collaborations fail to equal <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/ray-of-light-mw0000033767">Ray of Light</a> or "Beautiful Stranger," they're still sleekly admirable, and they're offset by the terrific <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/guy-sigsworth-mn0000546133">Guy Sigsworth</a>/<a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/mark-spike-stent-mn0000233507">Mark "Spike" Stent</a> midtempo cut "What It Feels Like for a Girl" and <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/madonna-mn0000237205">Madonna</a>'s thriving partnership with <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/mirwais-mn0000499745">Mirwais</a>. This team is responsible for the heart of the record, with such stunners as the intricate, sensual, folk-psych "Don't Tell Me," the eerily seductive "Paradise (Not for Me)," and the thumping title track, which sounds funkier, denser, sexier with each spin. Whenever she works with <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/mirwais-mn0000499745">Mirwais</a>, <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/music-mw0000088531">Music</a> truly comes alive with the spark and style.                 </p>
