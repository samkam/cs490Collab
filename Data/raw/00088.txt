<p><i><b>The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe</b></i> is the <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack" title="Soundtrack">soundtrack</a> of the <a href="/wiki/The_Chronicles_of_Narnia:_The_Lion,_the_Witch_and_the_Wardrobe" title="The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe">film of the same name</a>. <a href="/wiki/Harry_Gregson-Williams" title="Harry Gregson-Williams">Harry Gregson-Williams</a> composed the soundtrack, which was released on December 13, 2005 (see <a href="/wiki/2005_in_music" title="2005 in music">2005 in music</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Chronicles_of_Narnia:_The_Lion,_the_Witch_and_the_Wardrobe_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gregson-Williams was the first composer who director <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Adamson" title="Andrew Adamson">Andrew Adamson</a> approached for the film because they had worked together on <i><a href="/wiki/Shrek" title="Shrek">Shrek</a></i> (2001) and <i><a href="/wiki/Shrek_2" title="Shrek 2">Shrek 2</a></i> (2004). Because Adamson had to shoot the film in New Zealand for an extended period and Gregson-Williams was working on scores for films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Heaven_(film)" title="Kingdom of Heaven (film)">Kingdom of Heaven</a></i>, he initially only prepared for the project by re-reading the book and reading the film script. It wasn't until he saw the first cut of the film that, as he put it, "I could see the look of it, the look of the characters. I found that what I felt emotionally, when compared to the book, was quite different and it was then at that point that I could really 'get under the skin' of the project." He said that although re-reading the book helped him enter "that headspace", the script also provided "a lot of good information" because it included several scenes that weren't in the book.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_and_recording">Writing and recording</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Chronicles_of_Narnia:_The_Lion,_the_Witch_and_the_Wardrobe_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Writing and recording">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Score">Score</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Chronicles_of_Narnia:_The_Lion,_the_Witch_and_the_Wardrobe_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Score">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the recording process, Gregson-Williams employed the 75-piece <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hollywood_Studio_Symphony_Orchestra" title="Hollywood Studio Symphony Orchestra">Hollywood Studio Symphony Orchestra</a>, along with a 140-member choir (from <a href="/wiki/Abbey_Road_Studios" title="Abbey Road Studios">Abbey Road Studios</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, England; mostly members of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bach_Choir" title="Bach Choir">Bach Choir</a>) and numerous other solo musicians such as electric violinist <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Marsh" title="Hugh Marsh">Hugh Marsh</a> and vocalist <a href="/wiki/Lisbeth_Scott" title="Lisbeth Scott">Lisbeth Scott</a> (at his Wavecrest Studio).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SoundtrackNet_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-SoundtrackNet-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> He composed the original score and then spent late September through early November 2005 conducting the Hollywood Orchestra and overseeing the recording of the English <a href="/wiki/Choir" title="Choir">choir</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SoundtrackNet_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-SoundtrackNet-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> For "color", he employed instruments used in ancient <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk music</a>, and to underscore critical dramatic moments, he added choral textures and, occasionally, a solo voice. The score includes instances of <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Though frequent comparisons were made between it and <a href="/wiki/Howard_Shore" title="Howard Shore">Howard Shore</a>'s score for <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_(film_series)" title="The Lord of the Rings (film series)"><i>The Lord of the Rings</i> film trilogy</a> (2001–03), Gregson-Williams said he was not influenced by Shore during the scoring of <i>The Chronicles of Narnia</i>. He also said he composed over 100 minutes of music for the original score, although less than sixty minutes made the final cut.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> There is a bootleg copy of the complete recordings circulating on the internet, although no official version of this soundtrack has been released. The complete recordings comprises all music composed by Williams for the movie.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The score features many uses of themes or <a href="/wiki/Leitmotif" title="Leitmotif">leitmotifs</a>, the most prominent of which are a motif for the Pevensie children, a motif for Narnia itself, and the "Heroic motif".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The heroic motif, however, is not heard until track nine ("To Aslan's Camp").</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Songs">Songs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Chronicles_of_Narnia:_The_Lion,_the_Witch_and_the_Wardrobe_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Songs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gregson-Williams co-wrote a song with <a href="/wiki/Imogen_Heap" title="Imogen Heap">Imogen Heap</a>, "Can't Take It In", which Heap recorded for the soundtrack. Originally, <a href="/wiki/Dido_(singer)" title="Dido (singer)">Dido</a> submitted a song for the soundtrack, but it was considered unsatisfactory. Heap was approached after a suggestion from the soundtrack's supervisor, who managed a band with whom Heap had toured. The song was written, recorded, produced and mixed in a week;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Heap said she needed "like a year more than I got" and considered the experience of handing in the song an hour after completing it "[p]retty scary". According to her, the creation of the song was difficult because <i>The Chronicles of Narnia</i> is a family film, so she "had to be quite descriptive and not too electronic" when making the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The soundtrack includes the song "<a href="/wiki/Wunderkind_(song)" title="Wunderkind (song)">Wunderkind</a>", which <a href="/wiki/Alanis_Morissette" title="Alanis Morissette">Alanis Morissette</a> wrote and recorded in three days; it was produced by <a href="/wiki/Mike_Elizondo" title="Mike Elizondo">Mike Elizondo</a>. According to her, after watching a rough cut of the film the song "seemed to flow from [me]", and that it "really touches me deeply".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Also included is "Winter Light", written and recorded by <a href="/wiki/Tim_Finn" title="Tim Finn">Tim Finn</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Evanescence" title="Evanescence">Evanescence</a> lead singer <a href="/wiki/Amy_Lee" title="Amy Lee">Amy Lee</a> claimed she had been approached to write music for the film, both of which were rejected. However, the producers of the film stated that they had never approached Lee about penning music for the film, and that it was "news to them". No Evanescence music had ever been planned for the score of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blabbermouth_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-blabbermouth-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Response">Response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Chronicles_of_Narnia:_The_Lion,_the_Witch_and_the_Wardrobe_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album debuted on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> at number 43,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> selling 20,000 copies in its first week. In its second week on the chart it dropped to number 65 before moving to number 57 in its third week after the holiday box office success of the film. It descended to number 59 in week four. "Wunderkind" was issued as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_single" title="Radio single">radio single</a> in some parts of Asia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The album received 3 stars out of a possible 5 from <i>Filmtracks</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> 3 stars out of a possible 5 from <i>Allmusic</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> and 3 stars out of a possible 4 from <i>MovieMusic UK</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> The soundtrack received two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a> award nominations: "<a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Original_Score" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Original Score">Best Original Score</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Original_Song" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song">Best Original Song</a>" (for "Wunderkind").</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Track_listing">Track listing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Chronicles_of_Narnia:_The_Lion,_the_Witch_and_the_Wardrobe_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Track listing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
