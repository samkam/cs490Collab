<p><b>Ariana Clarice Richards</b> (born September 11, 1979) is an American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a> and professional <a href="/wiki/Painting" title="Painting">painter</a>. She is best known for her roles as a child actress such as Carol Wetherby in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Prancer_(film)" title="Prancer (film)">Prancer</a></i>, Mindy Sterngood in <i><a href="/wiki/Tremors_(film)" title="Tremors (film)">Tremors</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Jurassic_Park_characters#Lex_Murphy" title="List of Jurassic Park characters">Lex Murphy</a> in the blockbuster film <i><a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park_(film)" title="Jurassic Park (film)">Jurassic Park</a></i>. She has won several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Young_Artist_Awards" title="Young Artist Awards">Young Artist Awards</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ariana_Richards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Richards was born in <a href="/wiki/Healdsburg,_California" title="Healdsburg, California">Healdsburg, California</a>, the daughter of Darielle (<a href="/wiki/Married_and_maiden_names" title="Married and maiden names">née</a> Garrison), a public relations representative and film producer, and Gary Richards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Her sister, Bethany, is also an actress. Her maternal grandmother, who was of Italian ancestry, was a descendant of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a> painter <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Crivelli" title="Carlo Crivelli">Carlo Crivelli</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Richards is also a descendant of boxer <a href="/wiki/John_L._Sullivan" title="John L. Sullivan">John L. Sullivan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Richards graduated from <a href="/wiki/Skidmore_College" title="Skidmore College">Skidmore College</a>, earning a degree in Fine Art and Drama, with distinction. She continued with instruction at <a href="/wiki/Art_Center_College_of_Design" title="Art Center College of Design">Art Center College of Design</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pasadena,_California" title="Pasadena, California">Pasadena</a> and also attended <a href="/wiki/Western_Oregon_University" title="Western Oregon University">Western Oregon University</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ariana_Richards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Richards made her acting debut in 1987's <i>Into the Homeland</i>, a made-for-cable release that starred <a href="/wiki/Powers_Boothe" title="Powers Boothe">Powers Boothe</a>. Her most notable appearance was as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Jurassic_Park#Alexis_Murphy" title="List of characters in Jurassic Park">Lex Murphy</a> in the first <i><a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park_(film)" title="Jurassic Park (film)">Jurassic Park</a></i> film; she reprised the role very briefly in the sequel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lost_World:_Jurassic_Park" title="The Lost World: Jurassic Park">The Lost World: Jurassic Park</a></i>. She also appeared as Mindy Sterngood in the first <i><a href="/wiki/Tremors_(film)" title="Tremors (film)">Tremors</a></i> film and reprised her role in the second sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Tremors_3:_Back_to_Perfection" title="Tremors 3: Back to Perfection">Tremors 3: Back to Perfection</a></i>, which went <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Straight_to_video" title="Straight to video">straight to video</a>. She has been featured in other films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Angus_(film)" title="Angus (film)">Angus</a></i>, where she played a high school cheerleader; she also appeared on television episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Golden_Girls" title="The Golden Girls">The Golden Girls</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Empty_Nest_(TV_series)" title="Empty Nest (TV series)">Empty Nest</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Boy_Meets_World" title="Boy Meets World">Boy Meets World</a></i>.</p>
<p>In addition to acting in movies, Richards appeared in the 1998 music video "<a href="/wiki/Brick_(song)" title="Brick (song)">Brick</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Ben_Folds_Five" title="Ben Folds Five">Ben Folds Five</a>, portraying a high school student having an abortion. She appeared in the September 2006 issue of the British movie magazine <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Empire_(magazine)" title="Empire (magazine)">Empire</a></i> discussing a possible return to acting.</p>
<p>In 2013, Richards starred in the 2013 film <i>Battledogs</i>, shot in <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2014, Richards was voted #98 on <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s 100 Greatest Child Stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ariana_Richards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1993, Richards released the album <i>First Love</i> on the <a href="/wiki/Pony_Canyon" title="Pony Canyon">Pony Canyon</a> label in Japan. The album was a mix between teen-pop and dance friendly ballads of the early 1990s. There was also a single released in very small volume. Ariana and her mother wrote the words to the track "You're the Reason".</p>
<p>Richards returned briefly to the world of music in September, 2008 and recorded a cover version of <a href="/wiki/David_Foster" title="David Foster">David Foster</a>'s "The Prayer" in a duet with <a href="/wiki/Chris_M._Allport" title="Chris M. Allport">Chris M. Allport</a>, whom she has known since a young age.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Art">Art</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ariana_Richards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Art">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Richards has become a successful artist. Her paintings, usually of landscapes and figures, tend to be in the style of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Impressionists" title="Impressionists">impressionists</a>. In October 2005, she won first place in the National Professional Oil Painting Competition (sponsored by American Artist Magazine) for the painting <i>Lady of the Dahlias</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>She currently resides in <a href="/wiki/Salem,_Oregon" title="Salem, Oregon">Salem, Oregon</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> and is apprenticing under a professional painter.</p>
