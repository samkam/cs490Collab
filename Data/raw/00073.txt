<div id="greet_block"><noscript><div class="greet_block wpgb_cornered"><div class="greet_text"><div class="greet_image"><a href="http://www.linussblanket.com/feed/rss/" rel="nofollow"><img alt="WP Greet Box icon" src="http://www.linussblanket.com/wp-content/plugins/wp-greet-box/images/rss_icon.png"/></a></div>Hello there! If you are new here, you might want to <a href="http://www.linussblanket.com/feed/rss/" rel="nofollow"><strong>subscribe to the RSS feed</strong></a> for you'll always know what books I'm in the midst of reading, what books I'm loving, and which books I'm giving away.<div style="clear:both"></div><div class="greet_block_powered_by">Powered by <a href="http://omninoggin.com/projects/wordpress-plugins/wp-greet-box-wordpress-plugin/" style="text-decoration:none;" title="WP Greet Box WordPress Plugin">WP Greet Box</a> <a href="http://omninoggin.com/" style="text-decoration:none;" title="WordPress Plugin">WordPress Plugin</a></div></div></div><div style="clear:both"></div></noscript></div><p style="text-align: left;"><em><a href="http://www.amazon.com/Then-There-Agatha-Christie-Collection/dp/0007136838/tag=linsbla-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/Then-There-Agatha-Christie-Collection/dp/0007136838/tag=linsbla-20', 'And Then There Were None']);">And Then There Were None</a><a href="http://www.amazon.com/Then-There-Agatha-Christie-Collection/dp/0007136838/tag=linsbla-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/Then-There-Agatha-Christie-Collection/dp/0007136838/tag=linsbla-20', ' by Agatha Christie']);"> by Agatha Christie</a></em> has a special way of getting under my skin and making me look over my <a href="http://www.linussblanket.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/And-Then-There-Were-None.jpg"><img alt="" class="alignright size-full wp-image-5491" height="300" src="http://www.linussblanket.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/And-Then-There-Were-None.jpg" title="And Then There Were None, Agatha Christie" width="300"/></a>shoulder the way all good suspenseful psychological murder mysteries will do. This book  creeped me out as a child and didn’t fail to do so this time around.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><em>And Then There Were None</em> begins with ten strangers  journeying to spend time on Indian Island after each receives a carefully worded invitation to spend  time on the island.  None of the travelers have any real idea of who will meet them, and each only vaguely remembers the friend from whom they have received their correspondence – but they have heard rumors of an actress that may or not own the property, and on wild goings on at the property.  Each has a strong motivation for wanting to be anywhere but home.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">The visitors to the island are surprised to realize that neither host nor hostess are on the island, and when a terrible recording comes on a dinner announcing crimes that each of them have purportedly committed, mysterious deaths start to occur.  It doesn’t take the group long to figure out that they have been lured to the island to be executed for crimes that has long gone unpunished.  They also realize that the executioner has to be one of them.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">The beginning of the novel bored me a bit as all the characters were being introduced. They were hard to keep track of, and my adult self agreed with my twelve-year-old self in wanting them to get along with it.  I was grateful when some of the characters were picked off because there were just too many stories.  It was a curious experince to watch events unfold because while I didn’t like any of the victims, there was a  terror in imagining myself in their position, and that occupied me more than caring about them.  I also wanted to see who was next, and in what unexpected way their murder would occur and match up with the nursery rhyme.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><em>And Then There Were None</em> has a terrifying premise, and Christie proves to be the master of suspense – she is able to build the spine tingling factor in spite of a rather implausible plot.  Would you really go traipsing off to some island without talking to your host?  Without knowing them well?  Without having spoken to them in years?  All of these folks do and while I didn’t buy it completely, it ultimately doesn’t matter. The story works anyway.  I was on the edge of my seat the entire time that I was reading.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">I’m not sure if this is one of these novels where you actually have the clues to figure it out beforehand.  In spite of having read this book before it was long enough ago that while I remembered how the killer was doing things, I didn’t remember who it was.  I had a strong suspicion, though.  The manner in which the killer is revealed did leave something to be desired, but the beauty of what Christie accomplished with this one still remains intact and is definitely worth a read. Recommended.</p>
