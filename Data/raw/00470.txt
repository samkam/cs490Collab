<p><b>Dileep A. Rao</b> (born July 29, 1973) is an American actor who has appeared in feature films and television series. He starred in <a href="/wiki/Sam_Raimi" title="Sam Raimi">Sam Raimi</a>'s horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Drag_Me_to_Hell" title="Drag Me to Hell">Drag Me to Hell</a></i> (2009), <a href="/wiki/James_Cameron" title="James Cameron">James Cameron</a>'s science fiction film <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar_(2009_film)" title="Avatar (2009 film)">Avatar</a></i> (2009), and <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Nolan" title="Christopher Nolan">Christopher Nolan</a>'s thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Inception" title="Inception">Inception</a></i> (2010).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dileep_Rao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rao was born in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> to a physicist mother and an engineer father, both of Indian descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> He grew up in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yanbu,_Saudi_Arabia" title="Yanbu, Saudi Arabia">Yanbu, Saudi Arabia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver</a>, Colorado, and <a href="/wiki/Claremont,_California" title="Claremont, California">Claremont, California</a>. He has one sister who is a professor at <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a>. He graduated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Claremont_High_School_(Claremont,_California)" title="Claremont High School (Claremont, California)">Claremont High School</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_San_Diego" title="University of California, San Diego">University of California, San Diego</a>, with a B.A., later receiving an <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Fine_Arts" title="Master of Fine Arts">M.F.A.</a> from the <a href="/wiki/American_Conservatory_Theater" title="American Conservatory Theater">American Conservatory Theater</a> in San Francisco where his class included <a href="/wiki/Anna_Belknap" title="Anna Belknap">Anna Belknap</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Banks" title="Elizabeth Banks">Elizabeth Banks</a>.</p>
<p>Rao's first role after graduating was in the American premiere of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_Ink_(play)" title="Indian Ink (play)">Indian Ink</a></i> by Tom Stoppard. Rao moved to Los Angeles and began working in regional theater including at the Berkeley Rep, <a href="/wiki/South_Coast_Repertory" title="South Coast Repertory">South Coast Repertory</a>, and for the Manhattan Theater Club.</p>
<p>He competed on <i><a href="/wiki/Jeopardy!" title="Jeopardy!">Jeopardy!</a></i> on June 7, 2002, and won $34,400.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> On June 8, 2008, Rao was randomly selected from over 1600 entrants to play the <a href="/wiki/NPR" title="NPR">NPR</a> <a href="/wiki/Weekend_Edition" title="Weekend Edition">Weekend Edition</a> Sunday puzzle on air with <a href="/wiki/Will_Shortz" title="Will Shortz">Will Shortz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, he appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar_(2009_film)" title="Avatar (2009 film)">Avatar</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Drag_Me_to_Hell" title="Drag Me to Hell">Drag Me to Hell</a></i>. He was nominated for several awards as part of the ensemble cast in <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Nolan" title="Christopher Nolan">Christopher Nolan</a>'s 2010 film <i><a href="/wiki/Inception" title="Inception">Inception</a></i>. He played a pharmacologist in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="High_average_box_office">High average box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dileep_Rao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: High average box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of February 22, 2011, the average U.S. domestic box office of Dileep Rao's movies was over $363 million and $1.2 billion worldwide. This is an extremely high average, especially for an actor who has appeared in multiple movies. Among actors tracked by <a href="/wiki/Box_Office_Mojo" title="Box Office Mojo">Box Office Mojo</a>, the highest average for an entertainment figure with multiple movies is <a href="/wiki/Emma_Watson" title="Emma Watson">Emma Watson</a> with an average of $252 million. <a href="/wiki/Lee_Unkrich" title="Lee Unkrich">Lee Unkrich</a> is reported with an "average" of over $400 million, but this is from a voice appearance in a single film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
