<h1 class="title"><a href="http://www.cziplee.com/journal/czipread-of-the-week-zooms-in-on-author-dan-brown/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to CzipRead of the Week zooms in on author, Dan Brown">CzipRead of the Week zooms in on author, Dan Brown</a></h1>
				April 30th, 2013			<div class="entry">
<p>We had just restocked our fiction shelves with Dan Brown’s titles that now come with an updated cover version but just as an exciting content.  Next month will be the upcoming release by Brown’s latest called, <a href="http://www.prweb.com/releases/2013/3/prweb10562806.htm" target="_blank" title="Dan Brown Inferno">Inferno</a>!  There’s so much anticipation for this read, we foresee many sleepless nights this month of May!</p>
<p>For those who aren’t too familiar with Dan Brown’s titles…..</p>
<p><img alt="Dan Brown Angels and Demons CzipLee Bangsar" class="alignnone wp-image-2218" height="480" src="http://www.cziplee.com/journal/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Angels-And-Demons_novel.jpg" width="320"/></p>
<p>This is the book that apparently started it all to the lead of The Da Vinci Code.  It’s a story about a world-renowned Harvard symbologist Robert Langdon had been summoned to a Swiss research facility to analyze a cryptic symbol. What was discovered turned out to be unimaginable:  a deadly vendetta against the Catholic Church by a centuries old underground organization—the Illuminati. In great desperation to save the Vatican from a powerful time bomb, the symbologist joins forces in Rome with the beautiful and mysterious scientist Vittoria Vetra. Together they embarked on a frantic hunt through sealed crypts, dangerous catacombs, deserted cathedrals, and the most secretive vault on earth…the long forgotten Illuminati lair.</p>
<p><img alt="Dan Brown Deception Point CzipLee Bangsar" class="alignnone wp-image-2219" height="499" src="http://www.cziplee.com/journal/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/deception-point-400x624.jpg" width="320"/></p>
<p>Next up, yet another pulse-pounding fiction title from Mister Brown himself.  In this book, a bunch of experts unearthed the evidence of scientific trickery which is a bold deception that threatens to plunge the world into controversy.  But of course, there are those out there who would go through great lengths to prevent the truth from being exposed.  And so the chase pursues.</p>
<p><img alt="Dan Brown Digital Fortress" class="alignnone wp-image-2220" height="480" src="http://www.cziplee.com/journal/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Digital-Fortress_novel.jpg" width="320"/></p>
<p>This book is one of those techno triller read that explores the theme of government surveillance of electronically stored information on the private lives of citizens, and the possible civil liberties nd ethical implications when operating such technology.  We can’t help but think, only those who are that much into anagrams and computer viruses would enjoy this book.</p>
<p><img alt="Dan Brown The Da Vinci Code CzipLee Bangsar" class="alignnone wp-image-2221" height="480" src="http://www.cziplee.com/journal/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/The-DaVinci-Code_novel.jpg" width="320"/></p>
<p>If anything, most of us would have known of Dan Brown as a result of this book.  Every page was entirely convincing which made it a rare reading experience to find yourself doubting what’s real, and what’s not.  And of course, we know that this book resulted in a boost of tourists in Paris after the book and movie made a big hit.</p>
<p><img alt="Dan Brown The Lost Symbol CzipLee Bangsar" class="alignnone wp-image-2222" height="480" src="http://www.cziplee.com/journal/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/The-Lost-Symbol_novel.jpg" width="320"/></p>
<p>Yet another exciting novel on symbols and the lost world of esoteric yet inconceivable truth.  This book is set within the hidden chambers, tunnels, and temples of Washington, D.C., an intelligent, lightning-paced story with many surprises.</p>
<p>So if we’ve got you hooked, this is definitely an author to look-up to.  His manner and approach of writing these stories are amazingly well panned out.  The end of each read tends to makes you think and reconsider pre-existing knowledge, when what you thought you once knew turned out not to be so.</p>
