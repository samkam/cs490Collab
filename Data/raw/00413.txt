<p><b>Robert "Bob" Peck</b> (23 August 1945 – 4 April 1999) was an English stage, television and film actor who was best known for his roles as Ronald Craven in the television serial <i><a href="/wiki/Edge_of_Darkness" title="Edge of Darkness">Edge of Darkness</a></i> and as gamekeeper <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Muldoon_(fictional_character)" title="Robert Muldoon (fictional character)">Robert Muldoon</a> in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park_(film)" title="Jurassic Park (film)">Jurassic Park</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Peck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Robert Peck was born into a working-class family in <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, on 23 August 1945. He went to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Modern_School" title="Leeds Modern School">Leeds Modern School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lawnswood" title="Lawnswood">Lawnswood</a>. Peck was educated at the <a href="/wiki/Leeds_College_of_Art" title="Leeds College of Art">Leeds College of Art</a> where he received a Diploma in Art and Design.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Peck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stage_career">Stage career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Peck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Stage career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Before breaking into film and television work, Peck was a regular actor with the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Shakespeare_Company" title="Royal Shakespeare Company">Royal Shakespeare Company</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Ian_McKellen" title="Ian McKellen">Ian McKellen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Donald_Sinden" title="Donald Sinden">Donald Sinden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Judi_Dench" title="Judi Dench">Judi Dench</a>. Between 1979–80 he played <a href="/wiki/Iago" title="Iago">Iago</a> alongside Donald Sinden in <i><a href="/wiki/Othello" title="Othello">Othello</a></i>, in both <a href="/wiki/Stratford-upon-Avon" title="Stratford-upon-Avon">Stratford</a> and London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> He made a memorable appearance on stage in the Royal Shakespeare Company production of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_and_Adventures_of_Nicholas_Nickleby_(play)" title="The Life and Adventures of Nicholas Nickleby (play)">The Life and Adventures of Nicholas Nickleby</a></i>, originally by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Dickens" title="Charles Dickens">Charles Dickens</a>, playing two characters: John Browdie and Sir Mulberry Hawk — he appeared playing the two characters in a later television adaptation of the play. He played the character of <a href="/wiki/Macduff_(Macbeth)" title="Macduff (Macbeth)">Macduff</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Nunn" title="Trevor Nunn">Trevor Nunn</a>'s acclaimed 1976 stage and television versions of <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Macbeth" title="Macbeth">Macbeth</a></i>, and re-appeared in another production of Macbeth in 1982. According to Peck's fellow Royal Shakespeare Theatre performer and veteran actor Sir Ian McKellen, Peck is the actor he considers he "learned the most from".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Edge_of_Darkness"><i>Edge of Darkness</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Peck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Edge of Darkness">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Peck's television career began in the 1970s, with his first television roles being in the BBC's <i>Thirty-Minute Theatre</i> anthology series in 1972, in which he appeared in the episode "Bypass". He also appeared in various other successful television productions such as <i><a href="/wiki/Z-Cars" title="Z-Cars">Z-Cars</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Play_for_Today" title="Play for Today">Play for Today</a></i>. He also appeared in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Royal_Flash_(film)" title="Royal Flash (film)">Royal Flash</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parker_(1986_film)" title="Parker (1986 film)">Parker</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1985 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_writer" title="Television writer">television writer</a> <a href="/wiki/Troy_Kennedy_Martin" title="Troy Kennedy Martin">Troy Kennedy Martin</a> - who had previously written the screenplay for the film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Italian_Job_(1969_film)" title="The Italian Job (1969 film)">The Italian Job</a></i> and created the popular <a href="/wiki/Police_procedural" title="Police procedural">police procedural</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Z-Cars" title="Z-Cars">Z-Cars</a></i> (in which Peck had appeared during the 1970s) - cast Peck in the starring role of policeman Ronald Craven in his television miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Edge_of_Darkness" title="Edge of Darkness">Edge of Darkness</a></i>. This <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crime_drama" title="Crime drama">crime drama</a>/<a href="/wiki/Political_thriller" title="Political thriller">political thriller</a> follows Peck's character as he attempts to unravel the truth behind the brutal murder of his daughter, portrayed in the series by <a href="/wiki/Joanne_Whalley" title="Joanne Whalley">Joanne Whalley</a>. Another of Peck's co-stars in the series was US actor <a href="/wiki/Joe_Don_Baker" title="Joe Don Baker">Joe Don Baker</a>, along with fellow UK actors <a href="/wiki/Charles_Kay" title="Charles Kay">Charles Kay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ian_McNeice" title="Ian McNeice">Ian McNeice</a>.</p>
<p>The series was broadcast on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC Two</a> in six episodes from 4 November to 9 December 1985, in six parts entitled "Compassionate Leave", "Into the Shadows", "Burden of Proof", "Breakthrough", "Northmoor" and "Fusion". During its run the show attracted four million viewers and spiralled Peck to fame, bagging him a <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_Television_Award_for_Best_Actor" title="British Academy Television Award for Best Actor">British Academy Television Award for Best Actor</a> at the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_of_Film_and_Television_Arts" title="British Academy of Film and Television Arts">British Academy of Film and Television Arts</a> Awards. After the series ended, Peck had become a figure of popularity and a national favourite.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_success">Film success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Peck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Film success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After gaining popularity for his starring role in <i><a href="/wiki/Edge_of_Darkness" title="Edge of Darkness">Edge of Darkness</a></i>, Peck had become a national favourite and began appearing in films. After a few theatre appearances, Peck made his first appearance as a main character in a film again playing a policeman, John Graham, based in <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a> 1950, who takes under his wing the son of a murdered black priest in the 1987 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Kitchen_Toto" title="The Kitchen Toto">The Kitchen Toto</a></i>. He also appeared in the 1987 film <i><a href="/wiki/On_the_Black_Hill_(film)" title="On the Black Hill (film)">On the Black Hill</a></i>, adapted from the 1982 novel <a href="/wiki/On_the_Black_Hill" title="On the Black Hill">of the same name</a> by <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Chatwin" title="Bruce Chatwin">Bruce Chatwin</a>. However, the film role that really launched his career as a film actor was his portrayal of the <a href="/wiki/Android_(robot)" title="Android (robot)">android</a> Byron in the 1989 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Post-apocalyptic" title="Post-apocalyptic">post-apocalyptic</a> science-fiction <a href="/wiki/Adventure_film" title="Adventure film">adventure film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Slipstream_(1989_film)" title="Slipstream (1989 film)">Slipstream</a></i>, in which he appeared alongside other big names including <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hamill" title="Mark Hamill">Mark Hamill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Paxton" title="Bill Paxton">Bill Paxton</a>, <a href="/wiki/F._Murray_Abraham" title="F. Murray Abraham">F. Murray Abraham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Kingsley" title="Ben Kingsley">Ben Kingsley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robbie_Coltrane" title="Robbie Coltrane">Robbie Coltrane</a>. During the late-1980s he also appeared in television shows including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Storyteller" title="The Storyteller">The Storyteller</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jim_Henson_Hour" title="The Jim Henson Hour">The Jim Henson Hour</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Screen_One" title="Screen One">Screen One</a></i>.</p>
<p>Peck's image and popularity increased with appearances in films including the 1990 film <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_of_the_Flies_(1990_film)" title="Lord of the Flies (1990 film)">Lord of the Flies</a></i> as the Marine Officer. He also appeared in the television shows <i><a href="/wiki/Screen_Two" title="Screen Two">Screen Two</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Screenplay_(TV_series)" title="Screenplay (TV series)">Screenplay</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/A_TV_Dante" title="A TV Dante">A TV Dante</a></i>. He also appeared in the television movies <i><a href="/wiki/The_Black_Velvet_Gown" title="The Black Velvet Gown">The Black Velvet Gown</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/An_Ungentlemanly_Act" title="An Ungentlemanly Act">An Ungentlemanly Act</a></i>. In 1993, Peck made his biggest film appearance, when he was cast as park gamekeeper Robert Muldoon in the blockbuster smash hit <i><a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park_(film)" title="Jurassic Park (film)">Jurassic Park</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Steven_Spielberg" title="Steven Spielberg">Steven Spielberg</a> from the 1990 novel <a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park_(novel)" title="Jurassic Park (novel)">of the same name</a> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Crichton" title="Michael Crichton">Michael Crichton</a>.</p>
<p>After appearing in Steven Spielberg's <i>Jurassic Park</i>, Peck appeared in the popular television show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_Indiana_Jones_Chronicles" title="The Young Indiana Jones Chronicles">The Young Indiana Jones Chronicles</a></i> in 1993 playing General Targo in one episode. He also played Italian <a href="/wiki/Romantic_music" title="Romantic music">Romantic</a> composer <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Verdi" title="Giuseppe Verdi">Giuseppe Verdi</a> in a television movie documenting the life and success of the composer.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_years">Later years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Peck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Later years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the later years of his life and career, Peck appeared in more films portraying the roles of Captain Sebastian Belger in <i><a href="/wiki/Merisairas" title="Merisairas">Merisairas</a></i>, Françoise's father in <i><a href="/wiki/Surviving_Picasso" title="Surviving Picasso">Surviving Picasso</a></i>, Ravn in <i><a href="/wiki/Smilla%27s_Sense_of_Snow_(film)" title="Smilla's Sense of Snow (film)">Smilla's Sense of Snow</a></i>, Harry Briggs in <i><a href="/wiki/FairyTale:_A_True_Story" title="FairyTale: A True Story">FairyTale: A True Story</a></i> and Denton (based on <a href="/wiki/Lancelot_Dent" title="Lancelot Dent">Lancelot Dent</a>) in the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Opium_War_(film)" title="The Opium War (film)">The Opium War</a></i> (Chinese name <i>Yapian zhangzheng</i>). He also appeared in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Direct-to-TV_film" title="Direct-to-TV film">direct-to-TV film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Scold%27s_Bridle#Television_adaptation" title="The Scold's Bridle">The Scold's Bridle</a></i> (1998).</p>
<p>In 2000, a year after Peck's death from cancer, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stop-motion" title="Stop-motion">stop-motion</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animated_film" title="Animated film">animated film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Miracle_Maker_(2000_film)" title="The Miracle Maker (2000 film)">The Miracle Maker</a></i>, was released, in which Peck voiced the character of <a href="/wiki/Joseph_of_Arimathea" title="Joseph of Arimathea">Joseph of Arimathea</a>. The film was dedicated to Peck's memory.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Peck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Peck won a British Academy Television Award for Best Actor at the British Academy of Film and Television Arts in 1986 for his portrayal of maverick policeman Ronald Craven in the television miniseries <i>Edge of Darkness</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Peck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Peck married actress <a href="/wiki/Jill_Baker" title="Jill Baker">Jill Baker</a> in 1982, and they had three children Hannah (born 1983), George (born 1986) and Milly (born 1990). Peck and Baker shared a seventeen-year marriage until his death in 1999.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Peck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 1994, Peck was diagnosed with an undisclosed type of cancer. Peck was said to be undergoing <a href="/wiki/Chemotherapy" title="Chemotherapy">chemotherapy</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_therapy" title="Radio therapy">radio therapy</a> and his agent claimed that he was making a recovery. Nevertheless, Peck died at his home in London, England on 4 April 1999, at the age of 53.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Peck's funeral took place in London, and his close friend and <i>Edge of Darkness</i> co-star Ian McNeice read a eulogy at the service. He was cremated in London and his ashes were given to his family. He is survived by his widow and three children.</p>
