<p><i><b>Music from and Inspired by Spider-Man 3</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack_album" title="Soundtrack album">soundtrack album</a> to <a href="/wiki/Sam_Raimi" title="Sam Raimi">Sam Raimi's</a> <a href="/wiki/2007_in_film" title="2007 in film">2007</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_3" title="Spider-Man 3">Spider-Man 3</a></i>. It was released on May 1, 2007. A special edition version is available only on the soundtrack's official website. A digital edition of the album is also in the planning stages, with the release date to be announced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The soundtrack's website allows the user to listen to the first song from the soundtrack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-official-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Unlike the first two <i>Spider-Man</i> soundtrack releases, the album does not feature any of the film's score by Christopher Young. The entire concept of this soundtrack is that each song was written (or recorded in the case of The Flaming Lips) for the soundtrack exclusively.</p>
<p>The special edition of the album is available only on the soundtrack's website, and it contains a bonus track (the "<a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_(theme_song)" title="Spider-Man (theme song)">Theme from Spider-Man</a>" covered by <a href="/wiki/The_Flaming_Lips" title="The Flaming Lips">The Flaming Lips</a>), a 32-page embossed hardcover book featuring movie stills and all five collectable movie cards inside 8"x8" box made from a replica of the rubberized black Spider-Man suit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-official-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Additionally, the digital version was made available for pre-order on iTunes and does contain "The Theme from Spider-Man" by The Flaming Lips.</p>
<p>Following its release, the soundtrack debuted at number 33 on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, selling about 21,000 copies in its first week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spider-Man_3_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The European version of the album features the track "Cut Off the Top (Timo Maas Dirty Rocker Remix)" by <a href="/wiki/Beatsteaks" title="Beatsteaks">Beatsteaks</a>. It is 3:03 long and appears as the fifth track, between "Pleased to Meet You" and "Red River".</li>
<li>The album does not include the <a href="/wiki/James_Brown" title="James Brown">James Brown</a> song "People Get Up and Drive Your Funky Soul", which is played in the film during the scene in which Peter, under the influence of the alien symbiote, struts and dances in the street.</li>
</ul>
