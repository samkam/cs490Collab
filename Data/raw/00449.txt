<h1 class="ptitle" itemprop="name">‘Sinister Six’ Gets 2016 Release Date; ‘Amazing Spider-Man 3′ in 2018</h1>
<div style="margin-top:10px;"></div>
<span class="alignleft by">
    Published 7 months ago  
    by 
    <link href="https://plus.google.com/u/1/107168973425084682525/posts?rel=author" rel="author"/>
<span itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span itemprop="name"><a href="http://screenrant.com/author/sandy/" rel="author" title="Posts by Sandy Schaefer">Sandy Schaefer</a></span></span>
<span class="single-date" content="July 23rd, 2014"></span>
</span>
<div style="padding-top: 20px;height: 43px;margin-left: 0px;">
<div style="float:left;">

<div class="addthis_jumbo_share" style="margin-bottom:10px;"></div>

<div class="entry" itemprop="articleBody" style="clear:both;">
<p align="center" class="ggnoads"><img alt="Sinister Six Movie Spider Man cameo Sinister Six Gets 2016 Release Date; Amazing Spider Man 3 in 2018" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-442300" height="350" src="http://cdn.screenrant.com/wp-content/uploads/Sinister-Six-Movie-Spider-Man-cameo.jpg" title="Sinister Six Gets 2016 Release Date; Amazing Spider Man 3 in 2018" width="620"/></p>
<p>This past week, reports began to circulate that the <em>Amazing Spider-Man</em> spinoff <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/sinister-six/"><strong><em>Sinister Six</em></strong></a> – a film about a team of Spider-Man super villains - might be in serious danger of being cancelled. Not so, it turns out, as Sony/Columbia Pictures has announced an official Fall 2016 release date for writer/director Drew Goddard’s <em>Sinister Six</em> movie. Meanwhile, <em>Amazing Spider-Man 1 &amp; 2</em> director Marc Webb’s<a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/the-amazing-spider-man-3/"><strong><em> Amazing Spider-Man 3</em></strong></a> has been pushed to 2018 (presumably, the May 2018 spot that Sony is currently holding onto).</p>
<p><span id="more-474428"></span><p><div id="zergnet-widget-22204"></div>
<script language="javascript" type="text/javascript">

		var znscr = document.getElementsByTagName('script')[0];

	})();

<p>Goddard stepped down as the showrunner for Marvel Studios’ upcoming <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/daredevil/"><em>Daredevil</em> </a>series a couple months back, in order to concentrate his efforts on getting <em>Sinister Six</em> ready. However, <a href="http://screenrant.com/amazing-spider-man-3-delayed-sinister-six-venom/">recent comments</a> made by certain other prominent members of the <em>Amazing Spider-Man</em> franchise <em>“brain trust”</em> – Roberto Orci and Alex Kurtzman, to be exact – indicated that Sony’s original plans for the series’ future may’ve changed (worst case scenario, that the blueprints for building <a href="http://screenrant.com/venom-movie-amazing-spider-man-sinister-six-sony/">a Shared Spider-Man movie universe</a> may’ve been abandoned), in reaction to <em>Amazing Spider-Man 2</em>‘s overall shaky critical reception and box office drop-off from its predecessor.</p>
<p>Sony, however, has now revealed its hand of cards, not so coincidentally as the <a href="http://screenrant.com/comic-con/">2014 International Comic-Con</a> gets up and running. <em>Sinister Six</em> now has a November 11th, 2016 release date – one week before WB releases its Harry Potter spinoff, <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/fantastic-beasts-and-where-to-find-them/"><em>Fantastic Beasts &amp; Where to Find Them</em></a>. The month of November has long served franchises such as Harry Potter and James Bond well (among others), while <em>Thor: The Dark World</em> proved last year that superhero movies can also thrive at the box office during that frame.</p>
<p><img alt="amazing spider man 2 andrew garfield Sinister Six Gets 2016 Release Date; Amazing Spider Man 3 in 2018" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-400179" height="320" src="http://cdn.screenrant.com/wp-content/uploads/amazing-spider-man-2-andrew-garfield.jpg" title="Sinister Six Gets 2016 Release Date; Amazing Spider Man 3 in 2018" width="620"/></p>
<p>However, there are some pressing questions to ask about <em>Sinister Six</em>. For one, will the movie feature Andrew Garfield as Peter Parker/Spidey, seeing how the actor previously said he’s only <a href="http://screenrant.com/andrew-garfield-amazing-spider-man-4-venom-movie/">contracted for three Spider-Man films</a> - and if the final movie that’s part of Garfield’s current deal is meant to be <em>Amazing Spider-Man 3</em> (as would make the most sense), does that mean <em>Sinister Six</em> won’t actually feature his Spider-Man?</p>
<p>Likewise, what big name actors are gong to be recruited to fill <a href="http://screenrant.com/sinister-six-movie-roster/">the Sinister Six roster</a> that already includes Rhino (Paul Giamatti) and the Harry Osborn Green Goblin (Dane DeHaan) – not to mention, potentially Felicity Jones as Felicia Hardy/Black Cat, while Jamie Foxx as Electro remains a (slight) possibility? And how will the turnout for a <em>Wild Bunch</em>-style movie with Spider-Man villains compare to other Spider-Man films, especially given that the moviegoing public has shown signs of Spidey fatigue of late? (Not to mention, there’s still that <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/venom/"><em>Venom</em></a> movie, waiting in the wings…) The <a href="http://screenrant.com/superhero-shared-movie-universe-problems/">sustainability of a Spider-Man Shared Universe</a> is questionable, to say the least.</p>
<p><img alt="Sinister Six Amazing Spider Man 2 Sinister Six Gets 2016 Release Date; Amazing Spider Man 3 in 2018" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-396360" height="320" src="http://cdn.screenrant.com/wp-content/uploads/Sinister-Six-Amazing-Spider-Man-2.jpg" title="Sinister Six Gets 2016 Release Date; Amazing Spider Man 3 in 2018" width="620"/></p>
<p>Having said all that, the good news is that <a href="http://screenrant.com/amazing-spider-man-3-release-date-delay-sinister-six/">releasing <em>Sinister Six</em> before <em>Amazing Spider-Man 3</em></a> frees up the latter from having to devote too much of its running time to world-building (a la <em>The Amazing Spider-Man 2</em>). Plus, Marvel Studios <a href="http://screenrant.com/marvel-studios-release-dates-2017-2018-2019/">left the May 2018 frame</a> open for Sony’s next <em>Amazing Spider-Man</em> installment – which means that Webb’s film will have more time to develop, fewer larger franchise duties to fulfill, <em>and</em> less direct competition to face at the box office.</p>
<p><strong><em>Sinister Six</em></strong> opens in U.S. theaters on November 11th, 2016. <strong><em>The Amazing Spider-Man 3</em></strong> is arriving in 2018, presumably on the May 4th, 2018 date once reserved for <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/the-amazing-spider-man-4/"><em>Amazing Spider-Man 4</em></a>.</p>
<p>Source: Sony/Columbia Pictures</p>

<p>I’ve held off commenting on the recent talk about what’s going to happen regarding the Spider-Man franchise mostly because I couldn’t believe Sony would be seriously considering pulling the plug.</p>
<p>You can’t deny that they should be concerned but it’s not like the movie bombed. Even with a $200 million dollar budget grossing over $700 million has to have turned a profit.<br/>
Maybe it’s because I’m a little older than your average commenter but it blows my mind that when a movie makes that much money it can still be considered a disappointment.</p>
<p>Of course it didn’t help that the final product wasn’t that great but I don’t think the blame falls solely on the filmmakers.<br/>
The marketing was awful. By the time the movie was released it felt like there was nothing left to see. I understand trying to reach as many viewers as possible but you couldn’t avoid Spider-Man marketing unless you cut yourself off every form of media.<br/>
I live in a relatively smaller New England town and there were billboards here. That never happens. Seriously, never. So by the time the movie was released you almost wanted to not see it just as an F-you to Sony.</p>
<p>As for the movie itself I think a shakeup needs to happen on the creative side.<br/>

Also as it’s been said a million times already there’s been to much world building and not enough straight forward storytelling. Sony isn’t the only Studio making this mistake. Marvel has made it more than once and it seems like WB/DC may be about to make the same mistake with BvS.</p>
<p>A movie, any movie needs a beginning, middle and end. Not beginning, setup, middle, foreshadowing, end, and then a button scene.<br/>
There’s no satisfaction from seeing 1/3 of a story.</p>
