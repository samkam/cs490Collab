<div class="StoryContent">
		

	Get ready to feel old. Twenty-four years have passed since Michael Crichton published his bestselling novel, <i>Jurassic Park</i>. Steven Spielbergs 1993 adaptation of Crichtons book recently celebrated its 20th anniversary with a 3D re-release. And the last time you were able to see a <i>Jurassic Park</i> movie in theaters before that was 2001. That all changes right now.<br><br>
<script data-config="http://config.playwire.com/25130/videos/v2/3118922/zeus.json" data-height="338" data-width="600" src="//cdn.playwire.com/bolt/js/zeus/embed.js" type="text/javascript"></script><br>

Universal is bringing the mighty dinosaurs of the <i>Jurassic</i> franchise back to life for a new installment in the ongoing series. Expect a lot of changes on screen, however, for <i>Jurassic World</i>. Very few original players from the initial franchise are coming back, as far as we know. The pieces are starting to fall into place on the fourth movie, so we figured we would dig into the sequel and fill you in on all of the latest details. Weve done this in the past for wildly anticipated movies like <i><a href="http://www.cinemablend.com/new/Batman-Superman-Movie-What-We-Know-So-Far-38745.html">Batman vs. Superman</a></i>, <i><a href="http://www.cinemablend.com/new/Star-Wars-Episode-7-What-We-Know-So-Far-36488.html">Star Wars: Episode VII</a></i> and, most recently, Marvels <i><a href="http://www.cinemablend.com/new/Guardians-Galaxy-What-We-Know-So-Far-41679.html">Guardians of the Galaxy</a></i>. <br><br>

For now, lets extract DNA from the frozen specimens of <i>Jurassic World</i> news and tell you what we know so far. <br><br>
<img src="http://www.cinemablend.com/images/news/41703/_1392673279.jpg">
<div class="liststyle">Release Date</div>
As far back as <a href="http://www.cinemablend.com/new/Jurassic-Park-4-Dead-Neverending-Story-Remake-Lives-13591.html">2009</a>, we were running stories about a fourth <i>Jurassic</i> movie, even as the sequel toiled away in development hell. Steven Spielberg had the sequel <a href="http://www.cinemablend.com/new/Steven-Spielberg-Says-Jurassic-Park-4-His-Schedule-28325.html">on his calendar</a>. <i>Park III</i> director <a href="http://www.aintitcool.com/node/42999">Joe Johnston</a> had real ideas about where he wanted the series to go. The sequel, as we know it, finally got fast-tracked  for real  in <a href="http://www.cinemablend.com/new/Jurassic-Park-4-Coming-2014-35078.html">January 2013</a>, when the studio said the wheels were turning toward a release. We entertained a 2014 date, but Universally <a href="http://www.cinemablend.com/new/Jurassic-Park-4-Gets-An-Official-Title-Release-Date-39382.html">officially</a> circled June 12, 2015, and that date  so far  has stuck. 

<br><br>
<img src="http://www.cinemablend.com/images/news/41703/_1392673292.jpg">
<div class="liststyle">The Director </div>
Spielberg never planned to come back. When Johnston passed on the opportunity to once again helm a dinosaur thriller, Universal started fishing for worthy replacements. They landed on <a href="http://www.deadline.com/2013/03/colin-trevorrow-to-helm-jurassic-park-4-for-universal-and-steven-spielberg/">Colin Trevorrow</a>, a relative newcomer who made a name for himself (to a certain extent) with the shaggy-dog time travel romance <i><a href="http://www.cinemablend.com/new/Top-10-Movies-2012-Sean-List-34702.html">Safety Not Guaranteed</a></i>, with Aubrey Plaza, Jake Johnson and Mark Duplass. I say "to a certain extent" because while <i>Safety</i> charmed the film-festival circuit, it didnt necessarily turn Trevorrow into a household name. <i>Jurassic World</i> still needed star power.  

<br><br>
<img src="http://www.cinemablend.com/images/news/41703/_1424383395.jpg">
<div class="liststyle">Chris Pratt</div>
<i>Jurassic Park</i> had Sam Neill (who returned for part three). <i>The Lost World</i> had Jeff Goldblum. Trevorrow needed a rugged leading man for <i>Jurassic World</i>. He finally settled on the busiest man in show business at the moment, <i>Parks and Rec</i> co-star <a href="http://www.prrecordgazette.com/2014/01/26/chris-pratt-confirms-jurassic-world-jurassic-park-was-really-kind-of-my-star-wars">Chris Pratt</a>. The charismatic young actor is coming off turns in the massively successful <i><a href="http://www.cinemablend.com/reviews/The-LEGO-Movie-6727.html">The LEGO Movie</a></i>, where he voiced the main character, Emmet. Oh yeah, then Pratt was the star (<a href="http://www.cinemablend.com/new/Guardians-Galaxy-Adds-Chris-Pratt-Star-Lord-35546.html">and Star-Lord</a>) of Marvels <i>Guardians of the Galaxy</i>, which happened to be the highest-grossing film of 2014. Pratts basically on fire right now, and his role in <i>Jurassic World</i> can only heat his white-hot career even further.
