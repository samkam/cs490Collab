<p><b>Frodo Baggins</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Character_(arts)" title="Character (arts)">fictional character</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tolkien%27s_legendarium" title="Tolkien's legendarium">J. R. R. Tolkien's legendarium</a>. He serves as the primary <a href="/wiki/Protagonist" title="Protagonist">protagonist</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings" title="The Lord of the Rings">The Lord of the Rings</a></i>. Frodo is a <a href="/wiki/Hobbit" title="Hobbit">hobbit</a> of <a href="/wiki/Shire_(Middle-earth)" title="Shire (Middle-earth)">the Shire</a> who inherits the <a href="/wiki/One_Ring" title="One Ring">One Ring</a> from <a href="/wiki/Bilbo_Baggins" title="Bilbo Baggins">Bilbo Baggins</a> and undertakes the quest to destroy it in the fires of <a href="/wiki/Mount_Doom" title="Mount Doom">Mount Doom</a>. He is also mentioned in Tolkien's posthumously published works, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Silmarillion" title="The Silmarillion">The Silmarillion</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Unfinished_Tales" title="Unfinished Tales">Unfinished Tales</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concept_and_creation">Concept and creation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frodo_Baggins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concept and creation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frodo did not appear until the third draft of <i>A Long-Expected Party</i> (the first chapter of <i>The Lord of the Rings</i>), when he was named Bingo (after a family of toy <a href="/wiki/Koala" title="Koala">koala</a> owned by Tolkien's children), son of Bilbo Baggins and Primula Brandybuck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> In the fourth draft, he was renamed Bingo Bolger-Baggins, son of Rollo Bolger and Primula Brandybuck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Tolkien did not change the name to Frodo until the third phase of writing, when much of the narrative, as far as the hobbits' arrival in <a href="/wiki/Rivendell" title="Rivendell">Rivendell</a>, had already taken shape.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Prior to this, the name "Frodo" had been used for the character who eventually became <a href="/wiki/Peregrin_Took" title="Peregrin Took">Peregrin Took</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Internal_history">Internal history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frodo_Baggins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Internal history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frodo_Baggins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Frodo is introduced in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fellowship_of_the_Ring" title="The Fellowship of the Ring">The Fellowship of the Ring</a></i> as the adoptive heir of <a href="/wiki/Bilbo_Baggins" title="Bilbo Baggins">Bilbo Baggins</a>. The chapter "A Long-expected Party" relates that Frodo's parents <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drogo_Baggins" title="Drogo Baggins">Drogo Baggins</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primula_Brandybuck" title="Primula Brandybuck">Primula Brandybuck</a> had been killed in a boating accident when Frodo was 12; Frodo subsequently spent the next nine years living with his maternal family, the <a href="/wiki/Brandybuck_Clan" title="Brandybuck Clan">Brandybucks</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brandy_Hall" title="Brandy Hall">Brandy Hall</a>. At the age of 21 he was adopted by his cousin,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>nb 1<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bilbo_Baggins" title="Bilbo Baggins">Bilbo</a>, who brought him to live at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bag_End" title="Bag End">Bag End</a>. By coincidence he and Bilbo shared the same birthday, the 22nd of 'September'. It was Bilbo who introduced the <a href="/wiki/Elvish_languages_(Middle-earth)" title="Elvish languages (Middle-earth)">Elvish languages</a> to Frodo, and they often shared long walking trips together.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_and_relations">Family and relations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frodo_Baggins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Family and relations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>We have seen Frodo's family connections to Bilbo. Frodo and Merry are, meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Cousin#First_cousins" title="Cousin">first cousins once removed</a>. Their common ancestors are <a href="/wiki/List_of_Hobbits#G" title="List of Hobbits">Gorbadoc Brandybuck</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Hobbits#M" title="List of Hobbits">Mirabella Took Brandybuck</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Fellowship_of_the_Ring"><i>The Fellowship of the Ring</i></span><i><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frodo_Baggins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Fellowship of the Ring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></i></h3>
<p><i>The Fellowship of the Ring</i> opens as Frodo came of age (at 33 years old) and Bilbo left the <a href="/wiki/Shire_(Middle-earth)" title="Shire (Middle-earth)">Shire</a> for good on his one hundred and eleventh birthday. Frodo inherited Bag End and <a href="/wiki/One_Ring" title="One Ring">Bilbo's ring</a>, which were both introduced in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit" title="The Hobbit">The Hobbit</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Gandalf" title="Gandalf">Gandalf</a>, at this time, was not certain about the origin of the Ring, so he warned Frodo to avoid using it and to keep it secret. Frodo kept the Ring hidden for 17 years, resulting in it giving him the same longevity of Bilbo, until Gandalf returned to tell him that it was the One Ring of the Dark Lord <a href="/wiki/Sauron" title="Sauron">Sauron</a>, who desired to use it to conquer <a href="/wiki/Middle-earth" title="Middle-earth">Middle-earth</a>.</p>
<p>Realizing that he was a danger to the Shire as long as he remained there with the Ring, Frodo decided to leave home and take the Ring to <a href="/wiki/Rivendell" title="Rivendell">Rivendell</a>, home of <a href="/wiki/Elrond" title="Elrond">Elrond</a>, a mighty <a href="/wiki/Elf_(Middle-earth)" title="Elf (Middle-earth)">Elf</a> lord. He left the Shire with three companions: his gardener <a href="/wiki/Samwise_Gamgee" title="Samwise Gamgee">Samwise Gamgee</a> and his cousins <a href="/wiki/Meriadoc_Brandybuck" title="Meriadoc Brandybuck">Meriadoc Brandybuck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peregrin_Took" title="Peregrin Took">Peregrin Took</a>. They escaped just in time, for Sauron's most powerful servants, the Nine <a href="/wiki/Nazg%C3%BBl" title="Nazgûl">Nazgûl</a>, had entered the Shire as Black Riders, looking for Bilbo and the Ring. They followed Frodo's trail across the Shire and nearly intercepted him.</p>
<p>Frodo and his companions escaped the Black Riders by travelling through the <a href="/wiki/Old_Forest" title="Old Forest">Old Forest</a>, but they were misled by the magic of <a href="/wiki/Old_Man_Willow" title="Old Man Willow">Old Man Willow</a> until they were rescued by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Bombadil" title="Tom Bombadil">Tom Bombadil</a>, who gave them shelter and guided them on their way. After leaving Bombadil, they were caught in fog on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barrow_Downs" title="Barrow Downs">Barrow Downs</a> by <a href="/wiki/Barrow-wight" title="Barrow-wight">Barrow-wights</a> and were entranced under a spell. Frodo broke loose from the spell, attacked the barrow-wight and summoned Tom Bombadil, who again rescued the hobbits and set them on their way.</p>
<p>At the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inn_of_the_Prancing_Pony" title="Inn of the Prancing Pony">Inn of the Prancing Pony</a> in the village of <a href="/wiki/Bree_(Middle-earth)" title="Bree (Middle-earth)">Bree</a>, Frodo received a delayed <a href="/wiki/List_of_Middle-earth_objects#Gandalf.27s_letter" title="List of Middle-earth objects">letter from Gandalf</a>, and met <a href="/wiki/Aragorn" title="Aragorn">Aragorn</a>, also called Strider, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ranger_of_the_North" title="Ranger of the North">Ranger of the North</a>. Strider became the hobbits' guide while journeying through the wilderness towards Rivendell. The One Ring slipped onto Frodo's finger inadvertently in the Prancing Pony's common room, turning Frodo invisible. This attracted the attention of Sauron's agents, who ransacked the hobbits' rooms in the night. The group, under Aragorn's guidance, quickly fled through the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minor_places_in_Middle-earth#Midgewater_Marshes" title="Minor places in Middle-earth">Midgewater Marshes</a> and again escaped the Nazgûl.</p>
<p>While encamped at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amon_S%C3%BBl" title="Amon Sûl">Amon Sûl</a>, they were found and attacked by five Nazgûl. The chief of the Nazgûl, known as the <a href="/wiki/Witch-king_of_Angmar" title="Witch-king of Angmar">Witch-king of Angmar</a>, stabbed Frodo with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Morgul-blade" title="Morgul-blade">Morgul-blade</a>, before Aragorn routed all five of them with fire. A piece of this blade remained in Frodo's shoulder and, working its way towards his heart, threatened to turn him into a wraith under the control of the Witch-king. With the help of his companions and <a href="/wiki/Glorfindel" title="Glorfindel">Glorfindel</a>, Frodo was able to evade the remaining Ringwraiths and reach Rivendell. Although almost overcome by his wound, once there he was healed over time by Elrond; it was said and later seen that the wound would never completely heal, however, as it was as much spiritual as physical.</p>
<p>In Rivendell, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Council_of_Elrond" title="Council of Elrond">Council of Elrond</a> met and resolved to destroy the Ring by casting it into <a href="/wiki/Mount_Doom" title="Mount Doom">Mount Doom</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mordor" title="Mordor">Mordor</a>, the realm of Sauron. Frodo, realizing that he was destined for this task, stepped forward to be the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ring-bearer" title="Ring-bearer">Ring-bearer</a>. A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fellowship_of_the_Ring_(characters)" title="Fellowship of the Ring (characters)">Fellowship of nine companions</a> was formed to guide and protect him: the hobbits, Gandalf, Aragorn, the <a href="/wiki/Dwarf_(Middle-earth)" title="Dwarf (Middle-earth)">dwarf</a> <a href="/wiki/Gimli_(Middle-earth)" title="Gimli (Middle-earth)">Gimli</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Elf_(Middle-earth)" title="Elf (Middle-earth)">elf</a> <a href="/wiki/Legolas" title="Legolas">Legolas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mirkwood" title="Mirkwood">Mirkwood</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Boromir" title="Boromir">Boromir</a>, a man of <a href="/wiki/Gondor" title="Gondor">Gondor</a>. Together they set out from Rivendell. Frodo was armed with <a href="/wiki/Sting_(Middle-earth)" title="Sting (Middle-earth)">Sting</a>, Bilbo's Elvish knife, and wore Bilbo's coat of Dwarven mail made of <i><a href="/wiki/Mithril" title="Mithril">mithril</a></i>. The company, seeking a way over the <a href="/wiki/Misty_Mountains" title="Misty Mountains">Misty Mountains</a>, first tried the Pass of <a href="/wiki/Caradhras" title="Caradhras">Caradhras</a>, but abandoned it in favour of the <a href="/wiki/Moria_(Middle-earth)" title="Moria (Middle-earth)">mines of Moria</a>. In Moria Frodo was stabbed by an <a href="/wiki/Orc_(Middle-earth)" title="Orc (Middle-earth)">Orc</a>-spear, but his coat of <i><a href="/wiki/Mithril" title="Mithril">mithril</a></i> armour saved his life. They were led through the mines by Gandalf, until he fell battling a <a href="/wiki/Balrog" title="Balrog">Balrog</a>, and then by Aragorn to <a href="/wiki/Lothl%C3%B3rien" title="Lothlórien">Lothlórien</a>. There <a href="/wiki/Galadriel" title="Galadriel">Galadriel</a>, the Lady of the Woods, gave Frodo an Elven cloak and a phial carrying the Light of <a href="/wiki/E%C3%A4rendil" title="Eärendil">Eärendil</a> to aid him on his dangerous quest.</p>
<p>Having then travelled some miles down the <a href="/wiki/Anduin" title="Anduin">Anduin</a> by boat, the Fellowship reached <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parth_Galen" title="Parth Galen">Parth Galen</a>. There, Boromir, having fallen to the lure of the Ring, tried to take it by force from Frodo. Frodo escaped by putting on the Ring and becoming invisible. This event broke the Fellowship; Boromir was later slain defending Merry and Pippin from invading Orcs, who captured the two hobbits. Aragorn, Gimli and Legolas gave him a hero's funeral before setting out after the two hobbits. Frodo chose to continue the quest alone, but Sam followed his master, joining him on the journey to Mordor.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Two_Towers"><i>The Two Towers</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frodo_Baggins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The Two Towers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Frodo and Sam made their way through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emyn_Muil" title="Emyn Muil">Emyn Muil</a>, followed by the creature <a href="/wiki/Gollum" title="Gollum">Gollum</a>, who had been tracking the Fellowship since Moria, seeking to reclaim the Ring he had possessed for centuries. After Gollum attacked the hobbits, Frodo subdued him with Sting. He then took pity on Gollum, and spared his life (just as Bilbo had done in <i>The Hobbit</i>), instead binding him to a promise to guide them through the <a href="/wiki/Dead_Marshes" title="Dead Marshes">Dead Marshes</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Morannon" title="Morannon">Black Gate</a>, which Gollum did. Gollum said that there was "another way" into Mordor, and Frodo, over Sam's objections, allowed him to lead them south into <a href="/wiki/Ithilien" title="Ithilien">Ithilien</a>. It was there that they met <a href="/wiki/Faramir" title="Faramir">Faramir</a>, younger brother of Boromir, who took them to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henneth_Ann%C3%BBn" title="Henneth Annûn">Henneth Annûn</a>. There Frodo allowed Gollum to be captured by Faramir, saving Gollum's life but leaving him feeling betrayed by his "master". After giving them provisions, Faramir allowed the two hobbits and Gollum to go on their way, but warned Frodo of Gollum's treachery.</p>
<p>The three of them passed near to <a href="/wiki/Minas_Morgul" title="Minas Morgul">Minas Morgul</a>, where the pull of the Ring became almost unbearable. There, they began the long climb up the Endless Stair, and at the top entered the tunnel, not knowing it was the home of the giant spider <a href="/wiki/Shelob" title="Shelob">Shelob</a>. Gollum hoped to deliver the hobbits to her and retake the Ring from her leavings. Shelob stung Frodo, rendering him unconscious, but Sam drove her off with Sting and the Phial of Galadriel. After attempting unsuccessfully to wake Frodo, and unable to find any signs of life, Sam concluded that he was dead and decided that his only option was to take the Ring and continue the quest. However, Orcs from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cirith_Ungol" title="Cirith Ungol">Cirith Ungol</a> soon found Frodo's body and knew that he was not dead. Planning to interrogate him after his awakening, they carried him into the tower at the head of the pass.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Return_of_the_King"><i>The Return of the King</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frodo_Baggins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The Return of the King">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sam rescued Frodo from the Orcs of Cirith Ungol. After a brief confrontation in which Frodo became enraged that Sam had taken the Ring, Sam restored the Ring to him. The two of them, dressed in scavenged Orc-armour, set off for Mount Doom, trailed by Gollum. They witnessed the plains of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gorgoroth_(Middle-earth)" title="Gorgoroth (Middle-earth)">Gorgoroth</a> empty at the approach of the Armies of the West, but at one point they barely escaped being drafted into an Orc-band. With the Ring getting closer to its master, Frodo became progressively weaker as its influence grew. After running out of water, they left all unnecessary baggage behind to travel light. As they reached Mount Doom, Gollum reappeared and attacked Frodo, who beat him back. While Sam fought with Gollum, Frodo went on to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sammath_Naur" title="Sammath Naur">Sammath Naur</a> within the volcano, where Sauron had forged the Ring. Here, however, Frodo lost the will to destroy the Ring, and instead put it on, claiming it for himself. Gollum got past Sam and attacked the invisible Frodo, biting off his finger, and finally regained his "precious". As he danced around in elation, Gollum lost his balance and fell with the Ring into the lava. The Ring was thus destroyed, and with it Sauron's power. Frodo and Sam were rescued by the reborn Gandalf and several <a href="/wiki/Eagle_(Middle-earth)" title="Eagle (Middle-earth)">Great Eagles</a> as Mount Doom erupted.</p>
<p>After reuniting with the Fellowship and attending Aragorn's coronation as King of <a href="/wiki/Gondor" title="Gondor">Gondor</a>, the four hobbits returned to the Shire to find it taken over by a gang of ruffians, led initially by Frodo's cousin, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lotho_Sackville-Baggins" title="Lotho Sackville-Baggins">Lotho Sackville-Baggins</a>, and then by the fallen <a href="/wiki/Wizard_(Middle-earth)" title="Wizard (Middle-earth)">wizard</a> <a href="/wiki/Saruman" title="Saruman">Saruman</a>. The four travellers roused their fellow hobbits and led them in driving the ruffians out. There they witnessed the deaths of both Saruman and his henchman <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%ADma_Wormtongue" title="Gríma Wormtongue">Gríma</a>.</p>
<p>Frodo never completely recovered from the physical, emotional and psychological wounds he suffered during the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Ring" title="War of the Ring">War of the Ring</a>. He was taken ill on the anniversaries of his wounding on <a href="/wiki/Weathertop" title="Weathertop">Weathertop</a> and his poisoning by Shelob. He briefly served as Deputy Mayor of the Shire, but spent most of his time writing the tale of his travels. Two years after the Ring was destroyed, Frodo and Bilbo as Ring-bearers were granted passage to <a href="/wiki/Valinor" title="Valinor">Valinor</a> — where Frodo might find peace. They boarded a ship at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grey_Havens" title="Grey Havens">Grey Havens</a> and together with Gandalf, Elrond and Galadriel, the Keepers of the <a href="/wiki/Three_Rings" title="Three Rings">Three Rings</a>, they passed over the sea and departed Middle-earth. Having no children of his own Frodo left his estate, along with the <a href="/wiki/Red_Book_of_Westmarch" title="Red Book of Westmarch">Red Book of Westmarch</a>, to Sam.</p>
<p>According to Appendix D of <i>The Lord of the Rings</i>, Frodo’s birthday was made a festival in <a href="/wiki/Minas_Tirith" title="Minas Tirith">Minas Tirith</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".22The_Sea-Bell.22">"The Sea-Bell"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frodo_Baggins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title='Edit section: "The Sea-Bell"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/The_Sea-Bell" title="The Sea-Bell">The Sea-Bell</a>" was published in Tolkien's 1962 collection of verse <i><a href="/wiki/The_Adventures_of_Tom_Bombadil" title="The Adventures of Tom Bombadil">The Adventures of Tom Bombadil</a></i> with the sub-heading <i>Frodos Dreme</i>. Tolkien suggests that this enigmatic, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narrative_poem" title="Narrative poem">narrative poem</a> represents the despairing dreams that visited Frodo in the Shire in the years following the destruction of the Ring. It relates the otherwise unnamed speaker's journey to a mysterious land across the sea, where he tries but fails to make contact with the people who dwell there. He descends into despair and near-madness, eventually returning to his own country, to find himself utterly alienated from those he once knew.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics_and_appearance">Characteristics and appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frodo_Baggins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Characteristics and appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gandalf described Frodo as "taller than some and fairer than most, [with] a cleft in his chin: perky chap with a bright eye."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> He had thick, curly brown hair like most other hobbits, and had fair skin due to his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fallohide" title="Fallohide">Fallohide</a> ancestry.</p>
<p>Bilbo and Frodo shared a common birthday on 22 September, but Bilbo was 78 years Frodo's senior. At the opening of <i>The Fellowship of the Ring</i>, Frodo and Bilbo were celebrating their thirty-third and one hundred and eleventh birthdays, respectively. Frodo inherited the Ring at this point, and due to its influence he still appeared about 33 during the War of the Ring, though his age was closer to 50.</p>
<p>Frodo, like Bilbo and his <a href="/wiki/Took_clan" title="Took clan">Took</a> ancestors, was considered by many others in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hobbiton" title="Hobbiton">Hobbiton</a> to be a little odd. His interest in the outside world and fascination with Elves and faraway places (like those to which Bilbo travelled in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit" title="The Hobbit">The Hobbit</a></i>) were uncommon among hobbits.</p>
<p>When the hobbits were waylaid by the <a href="/wiki/Barrow-wight" title="Barrow-wight">Barrow-wight</a>, they found long <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weapons_of_Middle-earth#Barrow-blades" title="Weapons of Middle-earth">daggers</a> made by <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BAnedain" title="Dúnedain">Dúnedain</a> in the wight's treasure. These served as short-swords for the hobbits, but Frodo's was broken when he resisted the Witch-king at the Ford of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bruinen" title="Bruinen">Bruinen</a>. Later, Bilbo gave Frodo <a href="/wiki/Sting_(Middle-earth)" title="Sting (Middle-earth)">Sting</a>, a magic Elven dagger, and a coat of <i><a href="/wiki/Mithril" title="Mithril">mithril</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chain_mail" title="Chain mail">chain mail</a>. The mail saved him from injury or death on four occasions, deflecting a spear-point and an Orc-arrow in the <a href="/wiki/Moria_(Middle-earth)" title="Moria (Middle-earth)">Mines of Moria</a>, another Orc-arrow along the Anduin, and finally Saruman's knife at Bag End. Frodo wounded the Barrow-wight and a cave <a href="/wiki/Troll_(Middle-earth)" title="Troll (Middle-earth)">troll</a>, but never killed anyone.</p>
<p>Like other members of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fellowship_of_the_Ring_(characters)" title="Fellowship of the Ring (characters)">Fellowship of the Ring</a>, Frodo received in Lórien a special cloak from <a href="/wiki/Galadriel" title="Galadriel">Galadriel</a>, which allowed him to blend in with natural surroundings. Galadriel also gave him a vial that reflected the light of <a href="/wiki/E%C3%A4rendil" title="Eärendil">Eärendil</a>, the Evenstar, (and, by extension, of the <a href="/wiki/Two_Trees_of_Valinor" title="Two Trees of Valinor">Two Trees of Valinor</a>).</p>
<p>He was very compassionate, pitying Gollum and allowing him to guide him and Sam to Mordor despite Sam's distrust of the creature. This act of kindness later proved to be a factor in the quest's success in destroying the Ring.</p>
<p>The influence of the Ring and the wound by the Morgul-blade seems combined to have given him the ability to see into the spirit world, for instance he sees faraway events in his dreams on several occasions. He also can see the ring of power worn by Galadriel and he seems to be able to sense danger.</p>
<p>Before he set out for the Shire from Gondor, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arwen_Evenstar" title="Arwen Evenstar">Arwen Evenstar</a> gave him a white jewel on a silver chain, saying that if the memory of his dark journey and his painful wounds continued to trouble him he could depart to Valinor for a time. The book states that he "wore always a white gem like a star, that he often would finger."</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Names_and_titles">Names and titles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frodo_Baggins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Names and titles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Frodo is referred to by several names and titles in <i>The Lord of the Rings</i>. On leaving the Shire he uses the alias 'Mr Underhill'. <a href="/wiki/Gildor_Inglorion" title="Gildor Inglorion">Gildor Inglorion</a> calls him 'Elf-friend' in acknowledgement of his ability to speak Elvish. After the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Council_of_Elrond" title="Council of Elrond">Council of Elrond</a> he is given the title 'Ring-bearer'. After the fulfilment of the quest he is referred to by the bards as 'Nine-fingered Frodo' or 'Frodo of the Nine Fingers', after Gollum, in an effort to steal the Ring, bit off his finger in order to get it off of the invisible Frodo.</p>
<p>Frodo is the only prominent hobbit whose name is not explained in Tolkien’s Appendices to <i>The Lord of the Rings</i>. In his letters Tolkien states that it is derived from <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Germanic_name#Frod" title="Germanic name">fród</a></i> meaning "wise by experience". A character from <a href="/wiki/Norse_mythology" title="Norse mythology">Norse mythology</a> called <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%B3%C3%B0i" title="Fróði">Fróði</a> is mentioned in <i><a href="/wiki/Beowulf" title="Beowulf">Beowulf</a></i>, where it is rendered in Old English as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Froda" title="Froda">Froda</a>. Tolkien did mention he changed final a's to final o's in male Hobbit names.</p>
<p>In the early drafts of <i>The Lord of the Rings</i> the principal character is called Bingo Baggins; the name Frodo is given to another hobbit. In the drafts of the final chapters of <i>The Lord of the Rings</i> published by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Tolkien" title="Christopher Tolkien">Christopher Tolkien</a> as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sauron_Defeated" title="Sauron Defeated">Sauron Defeated</a></i>, Gandalf names Frodo <i>Bronwe athan Harthad</i> (Endurance Beyond Hope) after the destruction of the Ring. Tolkien states that Frodo’s name in the fictional language of <a href="/wiki/Westron" title="Westron">Westron</a> was <i>Maura Labingi</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> His name in <a href="/wiki/Sindarin" title="Sindarin">Sindarin</a> (another of Tolkien's invented languages) appears to have been <i>Iorhael</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> which is derived from <i>ior</i> meaning 'old' and <i>hael</i> meaning "wise". In <i>The Return of the King</i> he is also referred to by the name 'Daur', a Sindarin word meaning "noble" <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> (or perhaps "Wise by experience," if it means the same as "Frodo" does.)</p>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Bakshi" title="Ralph Bakshi">Ralph Bakshi</a>'s 1978 <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_(1978_film)" title="The Lord of the Rings (1978 film)">animated version</a> of <i>The Lord of the Rings</i>, Frodo was voiced by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Guard" title="Christopher Guard">Christopher Guard</a>. <a href="/wiki/Billy_Barty" title="Billy Barty">Billy Barty</a> was the model for Frodo, as well as Bilbo and Sam, in the live-action recordings Bakshi used for <a href="/wiki/Rotoscoping" title="Rotoscoping">rotoscoping</a>.</p>
<p>In the 1980 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rankin/Bass" title="Rankin/Bass">Rankin/Bass</a> animated version of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Return_of_the_King_(1980_film)" title="The Return of the King (1980 film)">The Return of the King</a></i>, made for television, the character was voiced by <a href="/wiki/Orson_Bean" title="Orson Bean">Orson Bean</a>, who had previously played Bilbo in the same company's adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit_(1977_film)" title="The Hobbit (1977 film)">The Hobbit</a></i>.</p>
<p>In the 1981 <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> radio <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_(1981_radio_series)" title="The Lord of the Rings (1981 radio series)">serial of <i>The Lord of the Rings</i></a>, Frodo is played by <a href="/wiki/Ian_Holm" title="Ian Holm">Ian Holm</a>, who later played Bilbo in <a href="/wiki/Peter_Jackson" title="Peter Jackson">Peter Jackson</a>'s <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_(film_series)" title="The Lord of the Rings (film series)">film adaptation</a> of <i>The Lord of the Rings</i>.</p>
<p>In the 1993 television <a href="/wiki/Miniseries" title="Miniseries">miniseries</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hobitit" title="Hobitit">Hobitit</a></i> by Finnish broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Yle" title="Yle">Yle</a>, Frodo is played by Taneli Mäkelä.</p>
<p>In <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_(film_series)" title="The Lord of the Rings (film series)">The Lord of the Rings</a></i> film trilogy (2001-2003) directed by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Jackson" title="Peter Jackson">Peter Jackson</a>, Frodo is played by American actor <a href="/wiki/Elijah_Wood" title="Elijah Wood">Elijah Wood</a>. The timeline in Jackson's movie trilogy is simplified and much shorter than in the novel; in the movie Frodo sets out on his adventure a few months after inheriting Bag End and Bilbo's possessions, including the Ring. Consequently he is the same age as his friends - Sam, Merry, and Pippin - when he begins his adventure. Dan Timmons writes in the <a href="/wiki/Mythopoeic_Society" title="Mythopoeic Society">Mythopoeic Society</a>'s <i>Tolkien on Film: Essays on Peter Jackson’s The Lord of the Rings</i> (Mythopoeic Press, 2005) that the themes and internal logic of the Jackson films are undermined by the portrayal of Frodo, whom he considers a weakening of Tolkien's original.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timmons_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-timmons-14"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> Elijah Wood reprised his role of Frodo in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit:_An_Unexpected_Journey" title="The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey">The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey</a></i>, the first part of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit_(film_series)" title="The Hobbit (film series)">The Hobbit</a></i> trilogy (2012-2014).</p>
<p>On stage, Frodo was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/James_Loye" title="James Loye">James Loye</a> in the three-hour stage production of <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_of_the_Rings_(musical)" title="Lord of the Rings (musical)">The Lord of the Rings</a></i>, which opened in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> in 2006, and was brought to London in 2007. In the United States, Frodo was portrayed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Joe_Sofranko&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Joe Sofranko (page does not exist)">Joe Sofranko</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">Cincinnati</a> productions of <i>The Fellowship of the Ring</i> (2001), <i>The Two Towers</i> (2002), and <i>The Return of the King</i> (2003) for <a href="/wiki/Clear_Stage_Cincinnati" title="Clear Stage Cincinnati">Clear Stage Cincinnati</a>. In Chicago, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Patrick_Blashill&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Patrick Blashill (page does not exist)">Patrick Blashill</a> played Frodo in the <a href="/wiki/Lifeline_Theatre" title="Lifeline Theatre">Lifeline Theatre</a> production of <i>The Two Towers</i> in 1999.</p>
