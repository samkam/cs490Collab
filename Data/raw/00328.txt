<div class="header"><h2 class="size48 fontBook header_text_color2574">The creators of the </h2></div>
<div class="views-field views-field-field-synopsis-text-color-value"> <span class="field-content"><style>

</style></span> </div>
<div class="views-field views-field-field-synopsis-value"> <div class="field-content synopsis-text-color2574"><p>beloved <em>Toy Story </em>films re-open the toy box and bring moviegoers back to the delightful world of our favorite gang of toy characters in <em>Toy Story 3</em>. As Andy prepares to depart for college, Buzz, Woody, and the rest of his loyal toys are troubled about their uncertain future. <em>Toy Story 3</em>  is a comical adventure that lands the toys in a room full of untamed tots who can't wait to get their sticky little fingers on these "new" toys. It's pandemonium as the toys try to stay together, ensuring that "no toy gets left behind."</p>

<div class="views-field views-field-field-synopsis-text-color-value"> <span class="field-content"><style>

</style></span> </div>
<div class="views-field views-field-field-synopsis-value"> <div class="field-content synopsis-text-color2577"><p>since <em>Toy Story 2</em>.  As director Lee Unkrich took on the third installment, he realized that <em>Toy Story 3  </em>needed to make viewers feel like all three movies “were part of one grand story.” The character of a corrupt teddy bear and a day care storyline were revived from early <em>Toy Story  </em>iterations, and the core conflict had always seemed inevitable.</p>
<p>“When you’re broken, you can be fixed. When you’re lost, you can be found. When you’re stolen, you can be recovered. But there’s no way to fix being outgrown by a child,” explained Executive Producer John Lasseter.</p>
