<p>"<b>What It Feels Like for a Girl</b>" is a song by <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>, taken from her eighth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Music_(Madonna_album)" title="Music (Madonna album)">Music</a></i> (2000). The song was released as the third single from the album on April 17, 2001 by <a href="/wiki/Maverick_Records" title="Maverick Records">Maverick Records</a>, with two other versions being also released: a <a href="/wiki/Dance" title="Dance">dance</a>-remix produced by <a href="/wiki/Above_%26_Beyond_(band)" title="Above &amp; Beyond (band)">Above &amp; Beyond</a> and a Spanish version titled <i>Lo Que Siente La Mujer</i>. It was written by Madonna, <a href="/wiki/Guy_Sigsworth" title="Guy Sigsworth">Guy Sigsworth</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Torn" title="David Torn">David Torn</a>, who was credited later, while production was handled by Madonna, Sigsworth and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mark_Stent" title="Mark Stent">Mark Stent</a>. Lyrically, it conveys society's <a href="/wiki/Double_standard" title="Double standard">double standard</a> toward women, addressing hurtful myths about female inferiority. To emphasize the message, the song opens with a spoken word sample of <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Gainsbourg" title="Charlotte Gainsbourg">Charlotte Gainsbourg</a> from the movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cement_Garden" title="The Cement Garden">The Cement Garden</a></i>.</p>
<p>The song received acclaim from most music critics, who agreed it was a highlight from the album, while also remarking that the song was one of her most mature musical ventures of her career. Commercially, "What It Feels Like for a Girl" was successful in most music charts around the world, peaking in the top ten in countries including Australia, Canada, Denmark, Finland, Japan and the United Kingdom. Though it was a more low peak on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, peaking at number 23, it managed to achieve success on component charts in the United States, topping the US <a href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Songs" title="Hot Dance Club Songs">Hot Dance Club Play</a> chart.</p>
<p>An accompanying music video for the song was filmed by Madonna's then-husband <a href="/wiki/Guy_Ritchie" title="Guy Ritchie">Guy Ritchie</a> and premiered on March 22, 2001. It features Madonna in a blonde bob wig as she was portraying a reckless woman on a crime spree. The video was criticized for its depiction of violence and abuse, which caused <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> to ban it before 9pm. The single was also released on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> and <a href="/wiki/VHS" title="VHS">VHS</a> containing the music video. The song was used as a video interlude and was performed in Spanish on the <a href="/wiki/Drowned_World_Tour" title="Drowned World Tour">Drowned World Tour</a>. The song was also covered by the Fox TV series <a href="/wiki/Glee_(TV_series)" title="Glee (TV series)"><i>Glee</i></a>, which was commercially featured on the episode "<a href="/wiki/The_Power_of_Madonna" title="The Power of Madonna">The Power of Madonna</a>", as well as the following EP.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_recording">Background and recording</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=What_It_Feels_Like_for_a_Girl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and recording">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In September 1999, it was announced that Madonna had reteamed with producer <a href="/wiki/William_Orbit" title="William Orbit">William Orbit</a> for a possible follow-up to her Grammy-winning, multiplatinum 1998 album <i><a href="/wiki/Ray_of_Light" title="Ray of Light">Ray of Light</a></i>, according to the singer's spokesperson. Madonna was also signed to be on the movie "<a href="/wiki/The_Next_Best_Thing" title="The Next Best Thing">The Next Best Thing</a>", also contributing to its soundtrack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> In January 2000, Madonna announced that she was going to experiment with electronica on her then-upcoming album and was said to be venturing into the popular European dance style known as <a href="/wiki/Trance" title="Trance">trance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The next month, Madonna released a cover of <a href="/wiki/Don_McLean" title="Don McLean">Don McLean</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/American_Pie_(song)" title="American Pie (song)">American Pie</a>" as a single to the soundtrack of "The Next Best Thing",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> while in April 2000, it was announced that French musician <a href="/wiki/Mirwais_Ahmadza%C3%AF" title="Mirwais Ahmadzaï">Mirwais Ahmadzaï</a> was creating a melding of dance and pop songs with a disco feel for the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Later, it was also announced that she added <a href="/wiki/Guy_Sigsworth" title="Guy Sigsworth">Guy Sigsworth</a> to work with her on the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billaugust_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-billaugust-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Sigsworth created a demo backing track that was finished enough to impress, but not so finished as to restrict Madonna's freedom to write over the top of it. According to him, "What It Feels Like For a Girl" was done very quickly. "I sent her a backing track, and she wrote a top line to it, we put it together and four days later we had a record. And what was kind of good for me was that we decided on day one that we liked all the noises I'd already used on the demo, and it was more a matter of repositioning them in the arrangement around M's top line. So the whole job, apart from recording the voice, was moving things around in <a href="/wiki/Pro_Tools" title="Pro Tools">Pro Tools</a>," he claimed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-soundonsound_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-soundonsound-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> He remarked that he sent her two sketches and that she chose the more unusual one. "And at the start of it, I put this sample from a movie of the actress <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Gainsbourg" title="Charlotte Gainsbourg">Charlotte Gainsbourg</a>. I just knew that M would fall in love with that, and she did," he declared.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-soundonsound_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-soundonsound-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>"What It Feels Like For a Girl" was written by Madonna and <a href="/wiki/Guy_Sigsworth" title="Guy Sigsworth">Guy Sigsworth</a>, while production was done by Madonna, Sigsworth, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mark_Stent" title="Mark Stent">Mark "Spike" Stent</a>. <a href="/wiki/David_Torn" title="David Torn">David Torn</a> was included as an additional composer to the track, after Madonna found out Sigsworth had sampled Torn's <i><a href="/wiki/Cloud_About_Mercury" title="Cloud About Mercury">Cloud About Mercury</a></i> album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The song is set in the <a href="/wiki/Key_(music)" title="Key (music)">key</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eb_major" title="Eb major">Eb major</a>, with Madonna's vocals spanning from the lower octave of <a href="/wiki/G_(musical_note)" title="G (musical note)">G3</a> to the higher note of <a href="/wiki/B_(musical_note)" title="B (musical note)">Bb4 note</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-musicnotes_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-musicnotes-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Jose F. Promis considers the song a "semi-ballad".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusicsingle_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusicsingle-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Lyrically, "What It Feels Like for a Girl" condemns male chauvinism by addressing hurtful myths about female inferiority,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pluggedin_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-pluggedin-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> with Madonna commenting on female role-playing in society.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billaugust_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-billaugust-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The beginning of the song opens with a dialogue from the 1993 British film, <a href="/wiki/The_Cement_Garden_(film)" title="The Cement Garden (film)"><i>The Cement Garden</i></a>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Birkin" title="Andrew Birkin">Andrew Birkin</a> and starring his niece, <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Gainsbourg" title="Charlotte Gainsbourg">Charlotte Gainsbourg</a>. In the dialogue, Gainsbourg says:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"Girls can wear jeans and cut their hair short, wear shirts and boots. 'Cause it's OK to be a boy. But for a boy to look like a girl is degrading. 'Cause <i>you</i> think that <i>being</i> a girl is degrading. But secretly you'd love to know what it's like... Wouldn't you? What it feels like for a girl."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-popmatters_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-popmatters-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Later, the song is build from a groovy verse section, driven by a "cool beat" and filtered bass licks, towards a dreamy chorus washed over by tidal keys and pads, as noted by Edward Cheung from <a href="/wiki/PopMatters" title="PopMatters">PopMatters</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-popmattersbest_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-popmattersbest-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> "Strong inside but you don't know it / Good little girls they never show it / When you open up your mouth to speak / Could you be a little weak?", she sings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdnow_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-cdnow-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Village_Voice" title="The Village Voice">The Village Voice</a>'</i>s Phil Dellio found the song "the perfect answer record to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Virgin_Suicides" title="The Virgin Suicides">The Virgin Suicides</a></i> (where boys indeed stand on the side of the street looking uncomprehendingly on girls), thanks in no small part to the gossamer-like synthesizer percolating in the background.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-village_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-village-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_remixes">Release and remixes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=What_It_Feels_Like_for_a_Girl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Release and remixes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Planned to be the album's second single,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billaugust_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-billaugust-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> "What It Feels Like For a Girl" was eventually released as the album's third single on April 17, 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-madonna.com_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-madonna.com-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Two new versions of the song were released to further promote the track. The first was a <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> version of the song titled <i>Lo Que Siente la Mujer</i>, especially recorded for inclusion on the single as its <a href="/wiki/A-side_and_B-side" title="A-side and B-side">B-side</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-madonna.com_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-madonna.com-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> and eventually released as a promo-only single in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. Later it was added to the two-disc Tour Edition of <i>Music</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> and the Bonus Track edition of the album in some territories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> The second was a <a href="/wiki/Trance_music" title="Trance music">trance</a> version created by <a href="/wiki/Above_%26_Beyond_(band)" title="Above &amp; Beyond (band)">Above &amp; Beyond</a>, which was also dubbed as the single's version as well as being featured in its music video and the song's <a href="/wiki/CD_single" title="CD single">CD single</a>. The CD single also features a remix produced by British DJ <a href="/wiki/Paul_Oakenfold" title="Paul Oakenfold">Paul Oakenfold</a>, which transformed the song into "a massive, deep arena club stomper," as noted by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a>'s Jose F. Promis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusicsingle_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusicsingle-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Thunderpuss_controversy">Thunderpuss controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=What_It_Feels_Like_for_a_Girl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Thunderpuss controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The acclaimed remixing duo <a href="/wiki/Thunderpuss" title="Thunderpuss">Thunderpuss</a> was hired to do a remix of "What It Feels Like for a Girl". However, when they were almost finishing the mix, it was rejected because someone renamed an amateur remix of the song as "Thunderpuss Mix" and spread it on the internet. The label thought the remix was leaked from Thunderpuss studio, after an employee claimed that he saw the Thunderpuss remix in rotation on the internet already.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about.com_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-about.com-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> Barry Harris of Thunderpuss claimed in an interview for <a href="/wiki/About.com" title="About.com">About.com</a> that the situation "started this fire within the record company and Madonna, and we were like 'no, it's not our mix', we have not done anything. They just cancelled our entire mix, period, and that was it, the end of story."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about.com_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-about.com-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=What_It_Feels_Like_for_a_Girl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>"What It Feels Like for a Girl" received acclaim from most music critics. <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Thomas_Erlewine" title="Stephen Thomas Erlewine">Stephen Thomas Erlewine</a> in his review for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a></i> picked the song as one of the album's highlights, calling it a "terrific midtempo cut",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic1_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic1-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> Larry Flick of <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> considered the song "a hook-laden midtempo jam."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billaugust_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-billaugust-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> In a separate review, Chuck Taylor also of <i>Billboard</i> named the song "one of the more substantive - and mature - musical ventures of her career," while panning its remix version for "reduc[ing] the song to a mindless trickle of beats without any hints of the verses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billreview_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-billreview-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> Sal Cinquemani of <i><a href="/wiki/Slant_Magazine" title="Slant Magazine">Slant Magazine</a></i> observed that on the track Madonna exposes her soul,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slant_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-slant-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> while Barry Walters of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> wrote that the song is "as musically gentle as it is lyrically barbed."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rollingstone_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-rollingstone-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> Garry Crossing of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dot_(music)" title="Dot (music)">Dot</a></i> labeled it a "tender, sensitive, flirtatious, sexy and oddly vulnerable-feeling ode to the female perspective."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dot_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-dot-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> Regis D'Angiolini of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CD_Now" title="CD Now">CD Now</a></i> praised the track, calling it "one of Madonna's best songs in recent years, thanks to a catchy refrain, keyboard hook, and clever lyrics."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdnow_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-cdnow-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>While naming it "a big, warm, soulful jacuzzi of a song," Danny Eccleston of <i><a href="/wiki/Q_(magazine)" title="Q (magazine)">Q</a></i> picked the song as a standout track and claimed that the song has "the vaguest melodic echo of <a href="/wiki/Basement_Jaxx" title="Basement Jaxx">Basement Jaxx</a>’s terrific '<a href="/wiki/Red_Alert_(song)" title="Red Alert (song)">On &amp; On</a>'."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-q_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-q-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> Cynthia Funchs of <i><a href="/wiki/PopMatters" title="PopMatters">PopMatters</a></i> picked the song as her favorite on the album, calling it "outstanding" with "sweet, enchanted beats."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-popmatters_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-popmatters-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> David Browne of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> noted that the song is "an older, wiser <i><a href="/wiki/Into_the_Groove" title="Into the Groove">Into the Groove</a>,</i> and it's a beautiful keeper," but went on to call the track "a muted sparkler with a softly padding beat and genuinely empathetic lyrics about teenagers."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-ew-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> While reviewing her compilation <a href="/wiki/GHV2" title="GHV2">GHV2</a>, Sal Cinquemani of <i>Slant</i> perceived that the track was "largely lost amid the conventional sonics of Music's final single," writing that it "is GHV2's least dynamic offering," grading the song "C-".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slantghv2_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-slantghv2-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> However, Charlotte Robinson of <i>PopMatters</i> disagreed, claiming that the inclusion of the song, along with "Don't Tell Me", represented nicely that <i>Music</i> was another triumph.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-popmattersghv2_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-popmattersghv2-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2013 Scott Kearnan of <a href="/wiki/Boston.com" title="Boston.com">Boston.com</a> included the track at number twenty-six on his list of "30 Ultimate Madonna Singles." In his review of the song Kearnan wrote "Madonna’s covered plenty of ground about how women are treated in the world, but she’s rarely this unfiltered."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> Louis Virtel of <a href="/wiki/AfterEllen.com_and_TheBacklot.com" title="AfterEllen.com and TheBacklot.com">The Backlot</a> included the song on his list of "The 100 Greatest Madonna Songs," commenting that the song's "tenderness and power resonated like no Madonna ballad since '<a href="/wiki/Take_a_Bow_(Madonna_song)" title="Take a Bow (Madonna song)">Take a Bow</a>'.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> Mark Graham of <i><a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a></i> placed the song at number 34 on his list of "53 Favorite Madonna Songs", in honor of Madonna's 53rd birthday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vh1_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-vh1-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=What_It_Feels_Like_for_a_Girl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"What It Feels Like for a Girl" debuted at number 73 on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> edition of May 5, 2001, moving to number 46 the following week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billdebut_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-billdebut-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup> The song peaked at number 23 on the May 19, 2001 edition, becoming that week's greatest gainer/sales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billpeak_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-billpeak-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> On the same week, it topped the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Play" title="Hot Dance Club Play">Hot Dance Club Play</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billpeak_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-billpeak-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> According to her official website, "it would have gone higher on the Billboard Hot 100 had there been a traditional commercial single release - and therefore more sales points to move it up the chart. However, only a maxi-single was issued, hence its rather middling chart placement."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-madonna.com_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-madonna.com-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Elsewhere, "What It Feels Like for a Girl" was successful. In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, the song managed to be the third song from <i>Music</i> to peak inside the top-ten, reaching number 7 on April 28, 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uk_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-uk-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, the song debuted and peaked at number 6, on May 6, 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aria_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-aria-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup> It was certified <a href="/wiki/List_of_music_recording_certifications" title="List of music recording certifications">gold</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Recording_Industry_Association" title="Australian Recording Industry Association">Australian Recording Industry Association</a> (ARIA).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ARIA_Annual_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-ARIA_Annual-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The song was also very successful in <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, peaking at number four,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-finland_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-finland-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, where it peaked at number two,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-italy_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-italy-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> and in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, where it topped the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PROMUSICAE" title="PROMUSICAE">PROMUSICAE</a> chart on April 21, 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spain_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-spain-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> It debuted at number 50 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_New_Zealand" title="Recording Industry Association of New Zealand">New Zealand Singles Chart</a>, until it rose to number 15, on May 27, 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nz_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-nz-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> However, in some countries, "What It Feels Like for a Girl" failed to reach the top-twenty. In <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, the song reached numbers 26 and 22, respectively, becoming her lowest-charting single since "<a href="/wiki/Nothing_Really_Matters" title="Nothing Really Matters">Nothing Really Matters</a>" (1999) in both countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-austria_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-austria-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sweden_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-sweden-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, the song debuted and peaked at number 40 on the <a href="/wiki/Syndicat_National_de_l%27%C3%89dition_Phonographique" title="Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique">French Singles Chart</a>, until descending several times on the charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-french_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-french-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=What_It_Feels_Like_for_a_Girl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>A music video for the song was directed by Madonna's then-husband, filmmaker <a href="/wiki/Guy_Ritchie" title="Guy Ritchie">Guy Ritchie</a> in February 2001 and filmed throughout various parts of <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, including one location on W. Olympic Blvd and S. Wooster St. The video uses the dance remix version of the song, produced by Above &amp; Beyond, instead of the album's original version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvnewsvid_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtvnewsvid-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> According to Madonna, the video "shows my character acting out a fantasy and doing things girls are not allowed to do," she said in a written statement distributed by her record label, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warner_Bros" title="Warner Bros">Warner Bros</a>. "This is an angry song and I wanted a matching visual with an edgy dance mix."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvnewsvid_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-mtvnewsvid-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> It premiered on <a href="/wiki/Oxygen_(TV_channel)" title="Oxygen (TV channel)">Oxygen</a> TV channel, on March 22, 2001, but was banned from TV broadcasts in many parts of the world, including the most famous <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> and <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>, due to its violent content. It was only aired on MTV and VH1 just once time, during a news segment at a more mature hour of 11:30 p.m. ET.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup> While the video failed to air on both networks, Madonna released it as a <a href="/wiki/DVD_single" title="DVD single">DVD single</a> on April 24, 2001,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> becoming the biggest-selling DVD single of 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> According to the Andrew Morton, writer of the book <i>Madonna</i>, the video is "entirely consistent with the themes that she has been exploring for the last twenty years, namely the relationship between the sexes, the ambiguity of gender, and the unresolved conflict, for women in a patriarchal society of being fully female and sexual while exercising control over their lives."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-book_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-book-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>As stated by Eden Miller of <i><a href="/wiki/PopMatters" title="PopMatters">PopMatters</a></i>, the music video for "What It Feels Like for a Girl" opens with Madonna in a motel room, getting ready for a night out, which was seen as "simple and pleasant" enough. The video takes its first turn when Madonna picks up an old woman to accompany her in her drive around the city, and it quickly changes from there. Madonna rams her <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet_Camaro" title="Chevrolet Camaro">Camaro</a> into a car full of young men who were looking at her suggestively. From here, she tasers a man at an <a href="/wiki/Automated_teller_machine" title="Automated teller machine">automated teller machine</a> (ATM) and takes his money, pulls a realistic-looking water gun on two police officers and sprays them with water. After mowing down some roller-hockey players in a parking lot, she steals another car and blows up a gas station, all while still accompanied by the old woman. Finally, Madonna slams her stolen car into a cement pole in an apparent act of murder/suicide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-plot_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-plot-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup> Although having a vision of violence, "Madonna's violent acts in the video are either shown from a distance or take place off screen, and although ideas and events are clearly indicated, nothing graphic or gratuitous appears," as noted by Miller.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-plot_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-plot-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception_and_controversy">Reception and controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=What_It_Feels_Like_for_a_Girl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reception and controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The music video was heavily criticized for being overly violent and graphic. Madonna's spokesperson said that there was a lot of violence because it tells the story of a woman who had probably been abused. Madonna also explained that her character was acting out a "fantasy and doing things that girls are not allowed to do."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup> The video was received only early hours play on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> However, it was not the first time that Madonna had a video rejected for heavy rotation by MTV, since in 1990, the channel refused to show "<a href="/wiki/Justify_My_Love" title="Justify My Love">Justify My Love</a>" because of its steamy images, and in 1992 MTV only showed "<a href="/wiki/Erotica_(song)" title="Erotica (song)">Erotica</a>" late at night.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup> The decision to ban the video was a source of argument, since it appeared to be no more violent than some television shows that aired at the time. Ironically, the video went into heavy rotation on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oh!_Oxygen" title="Oh! Oxygen">Oh! Oxygen</a> and was streamed on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/America_Online" title="America Online">America Online</a> frequently. It was also later played frequently on VH1 Madonna programs, but in an edited format. According to Eden Miller of <i><a href="/wiki/PopMatters" title="PopMatters">PopMatters</a></i>, the reason why the video was possibly banned from <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> was due to the scene where it featured Madonna pointing a <a href="/wiki/Gun" title="Gun">gun</a> towards the police, as MTV had previously banned videos that contained guns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-plot_49-2"><a href="#cite_note-plot-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup> Miller explained that "while Madonna is one of MTV's darlings, she's still a woman, and the idea of a woman taking her aggressions out on men is something the network can just not have. That's what is so disturbing [...] And that really is what it feels like for a girl."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-plot_49-3"><a href="#cite_note-plot-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/MSN" title="MSN">MSN</a> listed the song on "Madonna's Most Shocking Moments" and said the song was supporting a "pro-feminist image".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
