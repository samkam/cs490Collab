<p><i><b>Anne of Green Gables</b></i> is a 1908 novel by Canadian author <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Maud_Montgomery" title="Lucy Maud Montgomery">Lucy Maud Montgomery</a>. Written for all ages, it has been considered a children's novel since the mid-twentieth century. It recounts the adventures of <a href="/wiki/Anne_Shirley" title="Anne Shirley">Anne Shirley</a>, an 11-year-old orphan girl who is mistakenly sent to Matthew and Marilla Cuthbert, a middle-aged brother and sister who had intended to adopt a boy to help them on their farm in <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a>. The novel recounts how Anne makes her way with the Cuthberts, in school, and within the town.</p>
<p>Since publication, <i>Anne of Green Gables</i> has sold more than 50 million copies and has been translated into 20 languages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Numerous sequels were written by Montgomery, and since her death another sequel has been published, as well as an authorized prequel. The original book is taught to students around the world.</p>
<p>It has been adapted as film, made-for-television movies, and animated and live-action television series. Anne Shirley was played by <a href="/wiki/Megan_Follows" title="Megan Follows">Megan Follows</a> in the 1985 Canadian produced movie. Plays and musicals have also been created, with productions annually in Canada since 1964 of the first musical production, which has toured in Canada, the United States, Europe and Japan. Others have been produced in Canada and the United States.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anne_of_Green_Gables&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In writing the novel, Montgomery was inspired by notes she had made as a young girl, about a couple who were mistakenly sent an orphan girl instead of the boy they had requested yet decided to keep her. She drew upon her own childhood experiences in rural Prince Edward Island. Montgomery used a photograph of <a href="/wiki/Evelyn_Nesbit" title="Evelyn Nesbit">Evelyn Nesbit</a> as the model for the face of Anne Shirley, which she had clipped from New Yorkâs <i><a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Magazine_(New_York)" title="Metropolitan Magazine (New York)">Metropolitan Magazine</a></i> and put on the wall of her bedroom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Montgomery was also inspired by the "formula Ann" orphan stories which were popular at the time and distinguished her character by spelling her name as "Anne." She based other characters such as <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Blythe" title="Gilbert Blythe">Gilbert Blythe</a> in part on people she knew. She said that she wrote the novel in the twilight of the day, while sitting at her window and overlooking the fields of <a href="/wiki/Cavendish,_Prince_Edward_Island" title="Cavendish, Prince Edward Island">Cavendish</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_summary">Plot summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anne_of_Green_Gables&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Anne, a young orphan from the fictional community of Bolingbroke, <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> (based upon the real community of <a href="/wiki/New_London,_Prince_Edward_Island" title="New London, Prince Edward Island">New London</a>), is sent to Prince Edward Island after a childhood spent in strangers' homes and orphanages. Marilla and Matthew Cuthbert, siblings in their fifties and sixties, had decided to adopt a boy from the orphanage to help Matthew run their farm. They live at <a href="/wiki/Green_Gables" title="Green Gables">Green Gables</a>, their <a href="/wiki/Avonlea_(Anne_of_Green_Gables)" title="Avonlea (Anne of Green Gables)">Avonlea</a> farmhouse on Prince Edward Island. Through a misunderstanding, the orphanage sends Anne Shirley.</p>
<p>Anne is described as bright and quick, eager to please, talkative, and extremely imaginative. She has a pale face with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freckles" title="Freckles">freckles</a> and usually braids her <a href="/wiki/Red_hair" title="Red hair">red hair</a>. Marilla at first says the girl must return to the orphanage, but after a few days she decides to let her stay. Marilla feels that she could be a good influence on the girl and had also learned that another disagreeable woman in town might take Anne in instead.</p>
<p>As a child of imagination, Anne takes much joy in life and adapts quickly, thriving in the close-knit farming village. Her talkativeness initially drives the prim, duty-driven Marilla to distraction, although Matthew falls for her charm immediately. Anne says that they are "kindred spirits."</p>
<p>The book recounts Anne's adventures in making a home: the country school where she quickly excels in her studies; her friendship with Diana Barry (her best or "bosom friend" as Anne fondly calls her); her budding literary ambitions; and her rivalry with classmate <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Blythe" title="Gilbert Blythe">Gilbert Blythe</a>, who teases her about her red hair. For that he earns her instant hatred, although he apologizes many times. As time passes, Anne realizes she no longer hates Gilbert but cannot bring herself to speak to him.</p>
<p>The book also follows Anne's adventures in quiet, old-fashioned Avonlea. Episodes include her play time with friends (Diana, Jane Andrews and Ruby Gillis), her run-ins with the unpleasant Pye sisters (Gertie and Josie), and domestic mishaps such as dyeing her hair green (while intending to dye it black) or accidentally getting Diana drunk (by giving her what she thinks is raspberry cordial but is currant wine).</p>
<p>At fifteen, Anne goes to Queen's Academy to earn a teaching license, along with Gilbert, Ruby, Josie, Jane and several other students. She obtains her license in one year instead of the usual two and wins the Avery Scholarship for the top student in English. Her attainment of this scholarship would allow her to pursue a Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) degree at the fictional Redmond College (based on the real <a href="/wiki/Dalhousie_University" title="Dalhousie University">Dalhousie University</a>) on the mainland in Nova Scotia.</p>
<p>Near the end of the book, Matthew dies of a heart attack after learning that all of his and Marilla's money has been lost in a <a href="/wiki/Bank_failure" title="Bank failure">bank failure</a>. Out of devotion to Marilla and Green Gables, Anne gives up the Avery Scholarship to stay at home and help Marilla, whose eyesight is diminishing. She plans to teach at the Carmody school, the nearest school available, and return to Green Gables on weekends. In an act of friendship, Gilbert Blythe gives up his teaching position at the Avonlea School to work at White Sands School instead, knowing that Anne wants to stay close to Marilla after Matthew's death. After this kind act, Anne and Gilbert's friendship is cemented, and Anne looks forward to the next "bend in the road."</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anne_of_Green_Gables&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Anne_Shirley" title="Anne Shirley">Anne Shirley</a></b>âAn imaginative, talkative, red-headed orphan who comes to live with Matthew Cuthbert and Marilla Cuthbert, who are siblings.</p>
<p><b>Marilla Cuthbert</b>âMatthew's sister, is an austere woman who objects to Anne's imaginative, unusual ways, finds herself embracing Anne's ways. Though she is conservative in her rules, she does love Anne and has the glimmerings of a sense of humor and a hidden soft side.</p>
<p><b>Matthew Cuthbert</b>âMarilla's brother, a shy, awkward man who takes a liking to Anne from the start. The two become fast friends. He is a good listener. Because Marilla has primary responsibility for rearing the girl, he has no qualms about "spoiling" her and indulging her in pretty clothes and other frivolities.</p>
<p><b>Diana Barry</b>âAnne's bosom friend and kindred spirit. Anne and Diana become best friends from the moment they meet. She is the only girl of Anne's age who lives close to Green Gables. Anne admires Diana for being pretty and for her amiable disposition. Diana lacks Anne's powerful imagination but is a loyal friend.</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Blythe" title="Gilbert Blythe">Gilbert Blythe</a></b>âA handsome classmate who tried to get Anne's attention by pulling her hair and calling her "Carrots" (unaware of her sensitivity about her red hair). Anne reacted by refusing to have anything to do with him for the next few years. Although Gilbert repeatedly apologized, Anne rebuffed him for years. However, Gilbert never abandoned his quest for her friendship (and eventually, love). Anne had unknowingly forgiven him when he had saved her from drowning, but had only just let her pride down when he gave up his job as teacher at the Avonlea school for her, to enable her to live at Green Gables with Marilla.</p>
<p><b>Mrs. Rachel Lynde</b>âA neighbour of Matthew and Marilla, and the nosiest person in town. She soon warms to the freckle-faced orphan. She is industrious and helpful, and does work for the church. She is married and has raised ten children, but her husband, Thomas Lynde, is mentioned briefly and never speaks.</p>
<p><b>Miss Muriel Stacy</b>âAnne's energetic new teacher. Her warm and sympathetic nature appeals to her students, but Avonlea's conservative parents disapprove of her liberal teaching methods. She forms a special relationship with Anne, who views her as a mentor. Miss Stacy encourages Anne to develop her character and intellect, and helps prepare her for the entrance exam at Queen's Academy, where she finishes in a tie for first with Gilbert Blythe.</p>
<p><b>Josie Pye</b>âA classmate generally disliked by the other girls (as are her siblings). Josie is vain, dishonest and jealous of Anne's popularity.</p>
<p><b>Jane Andrews</b>âOne of Anne's friends from school, she is plain and sensible. She does well enough academically to join Anne's class at Queen's.</p>
<p><b>Ruby Gillis</b>âAnother of Anne's friends. Having several "grown up" sisters, Ruby loves to share her knowledge of beaus with her friends. Ruby is portrayed as traditionally beautiful with long golden hair, and she suffers from consumption/<a href="/wiki/Tuberculosis" title="Tuberculosis">tuberculosis</a>.</p>
<p><b>Reverend and Mrs. Allan</b>âThe minister and his wife also befriend Anne, with Mrs. Allan becoming particularly close. She is described as pretty.</p>
<p><b>Minnie May Barry</b>âDiana's baby sister, whose life is saved by Anne when she comes down with <a href="/wiki/Croup" title="Croup">croup</a>.</p>
<p><b>Mr. &amp; Mrs. Barry</b>âDiana's parents. Mr. Barry farms, and, near the end of the book, offers to rent some tracts to help out Anne and Marilla, after Matthew's passing. Mrs. Barry has a severe personality, expecting her children to follow strict rules. After Anne accidentally gets Diana drunk, Mrs. Barry rejects the girl, forbidding Diana to have anything to do with Anne... until after she saves Minnie May, of course.</p>
<p><b>Miss Josephine Barry</b>âDiana's aunt. Initially portrayed in a negative light, she is quickly charmed by Anne's imagination, and eventually invites her and Diana to tea. She refers to Anne as 'the Anne-girl'and even sends Anne beaded slippers as a Christmas present.</p>
<p><b>Mr. Phillips</b>âAnne's first teacher at Avonlea, whom she despises (he spelled Anne's name without an 'E', among other things). She refuses to attend school for a long time, after Mr. Phillips punished only her among 12 pupils who arrived late. He is described as lacking discipline, and "courts" one of his pupils openly... (less frowned upon then as opposed to more contemporary times).</p>

<p>The province and tourist facilities have highlighted the local connections to the internationally popular novels. <i>Anne of Green Gables</i> has been translated into 36 languages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> "Tourism by Anne fans is an important part of the Island economy".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Merchants offer items based on the novels.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Green_Gables" title="Green Gables">Green Gables</a> farmhouse is located in <a href="/wiki/Cavendish,_Prince_Edward_Island" title="Cavendish, Prince Edward Island">Cavendish, Prince Edward Island</a>. Many tourist attractions on <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a> have been developed based on the fictional Anne, and provincial licence plates once bore her image.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Balsam Hollow, the forest that inspired the Haunted Woods and Campbell Pond, the body of water which inspired The Lake of Shining Waters, both described in the book, are located in the vicinity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PEI_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-PEI-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> In addition, the <a href="/wiki/Confederation_Centre_of_the_Arts" title="Confederation Centre of the Arts">Confederation Centre of the Arts</a> has featured the wildly successful <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anne_of_Green_Gables_(musical)" title="Anne of Green Gables (musical)">Anne of Green Gables musical</a> on its mainstage every summer for the past 48 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The novel has been very popular in Japan, which is known as <i>Red-haired Anne</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> where it has been included in the national school curriculum since 1952. 'Anne' is revered as "an icon" in Japan, especially since 1979 when this story had been broadcast as <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anne_of_Green_Gables_(anime)" title="Anne of Green Gables (anime)">Anne of Green Gables</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Japanese couples travel to Prince Edward Island to have civil wedding ceremonies on the grounds of the Green Gables farm. Some Japanese girls arrive as tourists with red-dyed hair styled in pigtails, to look like Anne.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2014, <a href="/wiki/Asadora" title="Asadora">Asadora</a> '<a href="/wiki/Hanako_to_Anne" title="Hanako to Anne">Hanako to Anne</a>'(Hanako Muraoka is the first translator in Japan) was broadcast and Anne became popular among old and youngï¼alikeï¼.</p>
<p>The Avonlea theme park near Cavendish and the Cavendish Figurines shop have trappings so that tourists may dress like the book's characters for photos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> Souvenir shops throughout Prince Edward Island offer numerous foods and products based on details of the 'Anne Shirley' novels. Straw hats for girls with sewn-in red braids are common, as are bottles of raspberry cordial soda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> In the first book, Lucy Maud Montgomery established the cordial soda as the favorite beverage of Anne, who declared: "I just love bright red drinks!"</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy_and_honors">Legacy and honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anne_of_Green_Gables&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Legacy and honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bala%27s_Museum" title="Bala's Museum">Bala's Museum</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Bala,_Ontario" title="Bala, Ontario">Bala, Ontario</a>, Canada, is dedicated to Lucy M. Montgomery information and heritage. The house museum is located in the former home of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fanny_Pike&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fanny Pike (page does not exist)">Fanny Pike</a>, where Montgomery and her family stayed for a vacation in 1922. She based her novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blue_Castle" title="The Blue Castle">The Blue Castle</a></i> on the region, changing the town's name to Deerwood. This was the only book she wrote that was set in other than <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stage">Stage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anne_of_Green_Gables&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Stage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anne_of_Green_Gables_-_The_Musical" title="Anne of Green Gables - The Musical">Anne of Green Gables - The Musical</a></i>. This show has been performed annually in the summer since 1964, when the <a href="/wiki/Confederation_Centre_of_the_Arts" title="Confederation Centre of the Arts">Confederation Centre of the Arts</a> opened. Its annual <a href="/wiki/Charlottetown_Festival" title="Charlottetown Festival">Charlottetown Festival</a> features Canada's longest-running main stage musical production, which has had a total audience of more than 2 million. <i>Anne of Green Gables - The Musical</i> was composed by Canadians <a href="/wiki/Don_Harron" title="Don Harron">Don Harron</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norman_Campbell" title="Norman Campbell">Norman Campbell</a>, with lyrics by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Elaine_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Elaine Campbell (page does not exist)">Elaine Campbell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mavor_Moore" title="Mavor Moore">Mavor Moore</a>. The production has been performed before <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom">Queen Elizabeth II</a> and has also toured across Canada, the United States, and Europe. It had a run in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>'s <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">West End</a> in 1969. The Charlottetown Festival production performed at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1970_World%27s_Fair" title="1970 World's Fair">1970 World's Fair</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Osaka,_Japan" title="Osaka, Japan">Osaka, Japan</a>. <a href="/wiki/Walter_Learning" title="Walter Learning">Walter Learning</a> directed and organized a successful national tour of the musical in Japan in 1991.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>The Guild in Charlottetown, Prince Edward Island, hosts <i>Anne and Gilbert, The Musical</i>. Written by <a href="/wiki/Nancy_White_(singer-songwriter)" title="Nancy White (singer-songwriter)">Nancy White</a>, Bob Johnston and Jeff Hochhauser, the production is based on Montgomery's sequels featuring Anne Shirley.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><i>The Nine Lives of L.M. Montgomery</i>, a musical adapted from Montgomery's novel and her life, opened at Kings Playhouse in <a href="/wiki/Georgetown,_Prince_Edward_Island" title="Georgetown, Prince Edward Island">Georgetown, Prince Edward Island</a> on June 20, 2008, the 100th anniversary of the book's publication. With book and lyrics by Adam-Michael James and music by <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy</a>-nominated composer <a href="/wiki/Leo_Marchildon" title="Leo Marchildon">Leo Marchildon</a>, the musical depicts events from Montgomeryâs life and features as characters heroines from all of her novels. Anne figures prominently, and is shown from age 12 into her 40s. Gilbert Blythe also appears. The showâs second production was at the Carrefour Theatre in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlottetown,_Prince_Edward_Island" title="Charlottetown, Prince Edward Island">Charlottetown, Prince Edward Island</a> and opened July 11, 2009. Both years, the musical was nominated for The Prince Edward Island Museum and Heritage Foundation's Wendell Boyle Award. In July 2010, a concert version of the show toured Prince Edward Island, with four performances at Green Gables.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Theatreworks USA, a New York-based children's theatre company, produced an <i>Anne of Green Gables</i> musical in 2006 at the <a href="/wiki/Lucille_Lortel_Theatre" title="Lucille Lortel Theatre">Lucille Lortel Theatre</a>. A revived production, with musical contributions from <a href="/wiki/Gretchen_Cryer" title="Gretchen Cryer">Gretchen Cryer</a>, is planned to tour grade-schools.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>The Peterborough Players, based in <a href="/wiki/Peterborough,_New_Hampshire" title="Peterborough, New Hampshire">Peterborough, New Hampshire</a>, staged an adaptation by Joseph Robinette of <i>Anne of Green Gables</i> in August 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
