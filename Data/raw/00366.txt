<p>The <b>Batcave</b> is the secret headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/DC_Comics" title="DC Comics">DC Comics</a> <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">superhero</a> <a href="/wiki/Batman" title="Batman">Batman</a>, the alternate identity of playboy <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bruce_Wayne" title="Bruce Wayne">Bruce Wayne</a>, consisting of a series of subterranean <a href="/wiki/Cave" title="Cave">caves</a> beneath his residence, <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Manor" title="Wayne Manor">Wayne Manor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dc-ency_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-dc-ency-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dc-batc_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-dc-batc-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publication_history">Publication history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Publication history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally, there was only a secret tunnel that ran underground between Wayne Manor and a dusty old barn where the <a href="/wiki/Batmobile" title="Batmobile">Batmobile</a> and Batmicrolite were kept. Later, in <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_(comic_book)" title="Batman (comic book)">Batman</a></i> #12 (Augustâ€“September 1942), <a href="/wiki/Bill_Finger" title="Bill Finger">Bill Finger</a> mentioned "secret underground hangars." In 1943, the writers of the first <a href="/wiki/Batman_(serial)" title="Batman (serial)">Batman</a> movie serial, titled <i>Batman</i>, gave the Caped Crusader a complete underground crime lab and introduced it in the second chapter entitled "The Bat's Cave". The entrance was via a secret passage through a grandfather clock and included bats flying around.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bob_Kane" title="Bob Kane">Bob Kane</a>, who was on the movie set, mentioned this to Bill Finger who was going to be the initial scripter on the Batman daily newspaper strip. Finger included with his script a clipping from <i><a href="/wiki/Popular_Mechanics" title="Popular Mechanics">Popular Mechanics</a></i> that featured a detailed cross section of underground hangars. Kane used this clipping as a guide, adding a study, crime lab, workshop, hangar and garage. This illustration appeared in the <i>Batman</i> "<a href="/wiki/Comic_strip" title="Comic strip">dailies</a>" on October 29, 1943<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> in a strip entitled "The Bat Cave!"</p>
<p>In this early version the cave itself was described as Batman's underground study and, like the other rooms, was just a small alcove with a desk and <a href="/wiki/Filing_cabinet" title="Filing cabinet">filing cabinets</a>. Like in the movie serial, the Batman's symbol was carved into the rock behind the desk and had a candle in the middle of it. The entrance was via a bookcase which led to a secret elevator.</p>
<p>The Batcave made its comic book debut in <i><a href="/wiki/Detective_Comics" title="Detective Comics">Detective Comics</a></i> #83 in January 1944.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Over the decades, the cave has expanded along with its owner's popularity to include a vast trophy room, supercomputer and forensics lab. There has been little consistency as to the floor plan of the Batcave or its contents. The design has varied from artist to artist and it is not unusual for the same artist to draw the cave layout differently in various issues.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fictional_history">Fictional history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fictional history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The cave was discovered and used long before by Bruce Wayne's ancestors as a storehouse as well as a means of <a href="/wiki/Underground_Railroad" title="Underground Railroad">transporting escaped slaves</a> during the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">Civil War</a> era. The 18th century frontier hero Tomahawk once discovered a gargantuan bat (owned by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Morgaine_le_Fey" title="Morgaine le Fey">Morgaine le Fey</a> of Arthurian legend) inside what can be assumed will become the Batcave.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Wayne himself rediscovered the caves as a boy when he fell through a dilapidated well on his estate, but he did not consider the cave as a potential base of operations until he rediscovered it yet again when he returned to Gotham to become Batman. In addition to a base the Batcave serves as a place of privacy and tranquility, much like <a href="/wiki/Superman" title="Superman">Superman's</a> <a href="/wiki/Fortress_of_Solitude" title="Fortress of Solitude">Fortress of Solitude</a>.</p>
<p>In earlier versions of the storyline, Bruce Wayne discovered the cave as an adult. In "The Origin of the Batcave" in <i>Detective Comics</i> #205 (March 1954), Batman tells Robin he had no idea the cave existed when he purchased the house they live in. He discovered the cave by accident when testing the floor of an old barn on the rear of the property, and the floor gave way. This story also established that a frontiersman named Jeremy Coe used the cave as a headquarters 300 years earlier. Bruce Wayne discovering the cave as an adult remained the case at least through <i>Who's Who</i> #2 in 1985.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Use">Use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Upon his initial foray into crime-fighting, Wayne used the caves as a sanctum and to store his then-minimal equipment. As time went on, Wayne found the place ideal to create a stronghold for his war against crime, and has incorporated a plethora of equipment as well as expanding the cave for specific uses.</p>
<p>Often, Bruce Wayne is depicted as having discovered the cave as a child, falling into it during an exploration of the Wayne Manor estate grounds in his youth. This was shown in the movies <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_Forever" title="Batman Forever">Batman Forever</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_Begins" title="Batman Begins">Batman Begins</a></i>, as a young Bruce Wayne fell through the wooden cover of an abandoned water well.</p>
<p>The cave is accessible in several ways. It can be reached through a <a href="/wiki/Secret_passage" title="Secret passage">secret door</a> in Wayne Manor itself, which is almost always depicted as in the main study, often behind a grandfather clock which unlocks the secret door when the hands are set to the time that Bruce Wayne's parents were murdered, 10:47 P.M. In the 1960s Batman TV show, the cave entrance has been shown to be behind a bookcase which was revealed when Bruce Wayne (actor Adam West) activated a red control switch hidden in a bust of William Shakespeare; when the secret switch is turned, the bookcase slides to one side, revealing the "Bat-Poles", which allow Bruce Wayne and his ward Dick Grayson (actor Burt Ward) to change into their Batman and Robin costumes en route as they slide down to the cave. An entrance under Bruce Wayne's chair in his office in Wayne Enterprises, as shown in <i>Batman Forever</i>, connects to a miles-long tunnel which Bruce travels through in a high-speed personal transportation capsule. In <i>Batman Begins</i> and <i>The Dark Knight Rises</i>, the cave is accessible through a secret door disguised as part of a large display case and is unlocked by pressing a sequence of keys on the nearby grand piano.</p>
<p>Another secret entrance, covered by a hologram, waterfall or a camouflaged door, allows access to a service road for the Batmobile. Another alternate entrance is the dry well where Bruce originally discovered the Batcave, highlighted especially during the <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_Knightfall" title="Batman: Knightfall">Knightfall</a></i> comic book storyline. At one point, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robin_(Tim_Drake)" title="Robin (Tim Drake)">Tim Drake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dick_Grayson" title="Dick Grayson">Dick Grayson</a> use the dry well to get into the cave, which they had been locked out of by <a href="/wiki/Azrael_(comics)" title="Azrael (comics)">Jean Paul Valley</a> during his time as Batman, and Bruce Wayne used it to infiltrate the cave and confront the insane Valley in the final battle between the two men for the title of the Batman. Lured into the narrow tunnel, Valley was forced to remove the massive Bat-armor he had developed, thus allowing Wayne, who was wearing his normal, slim Batsuit, to force him out of costume and renounce his claim to the title.</p>
<p>The location of the cave is known not only to Batman, but to several of his allies. In addition to the so-called "Batman Family", members of the <a href="/wiki/Justice_League" title="Justice League">Justice League</a> and the original <a href="/wiki/Outsiders_(comics)" title="Outsiders (comics)">Outsiders</a> are aware of the cave's location. Essentially, anyone who is aware of Batman's secret identity also knows the location of the Batcave, much like how people who have knowledge of Robin's identity have knowledge of Batman's; these, unfortunately, include such villains as <a href="/wiki/Ra%27s_al_Ghul" title="Ra's al Ghul">Ra's al Ghul</a>, who makes occasional visits to the Batcave to confront his long-time nemesis, and <a href="/wiki/David_Cain_(comics)" title="David Cain (comics)">David Cain</a>, who infiltrated the cave during the <i><a href="/wiki/Bruce_Wayne:_Fugitive" title="Bruce Wayne: Fugitive">Bruce Wayne: Fugitive</a></i> comic book storyline when he framed Bruce Wayne for murder.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Batcave serves as Batman's command center, where he monitors all crisis points in <a href="/wiki/Gotham_City" title="Gotham City">Gotham</a> and the world.</p>
<p>The cave's centerpiece is a supercomputer whose specs are on par with any of those used by leading national security agencies; it permits global surveillance and also connects to a massive information network as well as storing vast amounts of information, both on Batman's foes and his allies. A series of satellite link-ups allows easy access to Batman's information network anywhere around the globe. The systems are protected against unauthorized access, and any attempt to breach their security immediately sends an alert to Batman or <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Gordon" title="Barbara Gordon">Oracle</a>. Despite the power of Batman's computers, the <a href="/wiki/Justice_League_Watchtower" title="Justice League Watchtower">Justice League Watchtower</a> is known to have more powerful computers (composed of <a href="/wiki/Krypton_(comics)" title="Krypton (comics)">Kryptonian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thanagar" title="Thanagar">Thanagarian</a> and Martian technology), and Batman does occasionally use them if he feels his computers are not up to the task; on occasion he also consults Oracle for assistance.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Batcomputer" title="Batcomputer">Batcomputer</a> as presented in <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_%26_Robin_(film)" title="Batman &amp; Robin (film)">Batman &amp; Robin</a></i> is powerful beyond the realm of realistic computer systems, as <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Pennyworth" title="Alfred Pennyworth">Alfred Pennyworth</a> is able to program a replication of himself (his "brain algorithms") that is capable of conversation.</p>

<p>Additionally, the cave features state-of-the-art facilities such as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forensics" title="Forensics">crime lab</a>, various specialized laboratories, mechanized workshops, personal <a href="/wiki/Gym" title="Gym">gymnasium</a>, a vast library, parking, docking and hangar space (as appropriate) for his various vehicles as well as separate exits for each type, trophies of past campaigns, a large <a href="/wiki/Bat" title="Bat">bat</a> colony, and a Justice League teleporter. It also has medical facilities as well as various areas used in training exercises for Batman and his allies.</p>
<p>The cave houses Batman's vast array of specialized vehicles, foremost being the famous <i><a href="/wiki/Batmobile" title="Batmobile">Batmobile</a></i> in all its incarnations (mostly for nostalgia, but also for contingencies, as all are serviceable and in excellent working condition). The <a href="/wiki/Batman:_The_Animated_Series" title="Batman: The Animated Series">1990s DC animated series</a> gave rise to the idea that Batman keeps a fleet of regular cars of various models and utility vehicles such as an ambulance when the Batmobile would be too conspicuous for a given mission. Other vehicles within the complex include various motorcycles, <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">air</a>- and <a href="/wiki/Watercraft" title="Watercraft">watercraft</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Batplane" title="Batplane">The Bat-Wing</a>, a single-occupant <a href="/wiki/Jet_aircraft" title="Jet aircraft">supersonic jet</a>, and the Subway Rocket (which debuted in <i>Detective Comics</i> #667).</p>
<p>The cave is sometimes powered by a nuclear reactor, but most often by a hydroelectric generator made possible by an underground river.</p>
<p>Later comics, specifically the <i>Cataclysm</i> storyline, suggest that Batman has incorporated safeguards against <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquakes</a> and even a potential nuclear catastrophe, outfitting the cave as a virtual <a href="/wiki/Bomb_shelter" title="Bomb shelter">bomb shelter</a> or an enhanced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panic_room" title="Panic room">panic room</a>. The city's earthquake redesigned the caverns of the Batcave, with eight new levels now making up Batman's secret refuge of high-tech laboratory, library, training areas, storage areas, and vehicle accesses. It also includes an "island" computer platform (built on the spot where the Batmobiles' hydraulic turntable once was) with seven linked <a href="/wiki/Cray" title="Cray">Cray</a> T932 mainframes and a state-of-the-art hologram projector. There's also a selection of retractable glass maps within the computer platform. Kevlar shieldings are prepared to protect the cave's computer systems from seismic activity. With the cave's various facilities spread amid limestone stalactites and stalagmites,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dc-batc_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-dc-batc-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Batman built retractable multi-walkway bridges, stairs, elevators, and poles to access its facilities.</p>
<p>There is a containment vault solely for Lex Luthor's <a href="/wiki/Kryptonite" title="Kryptonite">Kryptonite</a> ring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> However, it was later revealed that Batman built another containment facility within the cave for a collection of variety of forms of Kryptonite.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>What is allegedly the world's last <a href="/wiki/Lazarus_Pit" title="Lazarus Pit">Lazarus Pit</a> was constructed inside the cave, although this has been contradicted by events in the pages of <i>Batgirl</i> and the <a href="/wiki/Black_Adam" title="Black Adam">Black Adam</a> miniseries.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Security_measures">Security measures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Security measures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Batcave is rigged with the most sophisticated security system in the world in order to prevent all measure of infiltration. The security measures include motion sensors, silent alarms, steel and lead mechanical doors which could lock a person in or out, and a security mode which is specifically designed to stop if not eliminate all Justice League members in the event that any of them go rogue.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Memorabilia">Memorabilia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Memorabilia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The cave stores unique memorabilia items collected from the various cases Batman has worked. Originally, these were stored in a room designed just for them; it was explained that Batman and Robin took one memento from each case. Later, the trophies were shown to be in the large main area of the cave, residing among the rest of the Batcave's furnishings.</p>
<p>The most famous and prevalently featured trophies are a full-size <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animatronic" title="Animatronic">animatronic</a> <a href="/wiki/Tyrannosaurus" title="Tyrannosaurus">Tyrannosaurus Rex</a>, a giant replica of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cent_(United_States_coin)" title="Cent (United States coin)">Lincoln penny</a>, and an oversized <a href="/wiki/Joker_(playing_card)" title="Joker (playing card)">Joker</a> <a href="/wiki/Playing_card" title="Playing card">playing card</a>. The <i>T. Rex</i> comes from an adventure on "<a href="/wiki/Dinosaur_Island" title="Dinosaur Island">Dinosaur Island</a>" (<i>Batman</i> #35, 1946); the penny was originally a trophy from Batman's encounter with a penny-obsessed villain named the <a href="/wiki/Penny_Plunderer" title="Penny Plunderer">Penny Plunderer</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/World%27s_Finest_Comics" title="World's Finest Comics">World's Finest Comics</a></i> #30, 1947), but was later retconned into being from an encounter with <a href="/wiki/Two-Face" title="Two-Face">Two-Face</a>. Other "keepsakes" in the cave come from "The Thousand and One Trophies of Batman!" (<i><a href="/wiki/Detective_Comics" title="Detective Comics">Detective Comics</a></i> #158, 1950). These three stories were reprinted in <i>Batman</i> #256.</p>
<p>Modern retellings of the items' origins can be found in <i>Batman Chronicles</i> stories in issue #8 ("Secrets of the Batcave: Dinosaur Island") and issue #19 ("The Penny Plunderers").</p>
<p>A story in <i>Batman</i> #81 featured <a href="/wiki/Two-Face" title="Two-Face">Two-Face</a> tying Batman and Robin to a giant replica of his silver dollar coin. This story was the basis for an episode of <i>Batman: The Animated Series</i> wherein Batman gains the giant coin from that encounter; this has caused widespread confusion as to the actual origin of the coin trophy.</p>
<p>Other pieces often shown in the Batcave are Two-Face's original coin, <a href="/wiki/Deathstroke" title="Deathstroke">Deathstroke</a>'s sword (the owner of which Batman has fought at least twice), the shroud of the vampiric <a href="/wiki/Monk_(comics)" title="Monk (comics)">Monk</a>, and over-sized <i><a href="/wiki/Bowling" title="Bowling">ten-pins</a></i>.</p>
<p>There is also a glass case display of <a href="/wiki/Jason_Todd" title="Jason Todd">Jason Todd</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Robin_(comics)" title="Robin (comics)">Robin</a> costume as a memorial to him, with the epitaph "A Good Soldier", which remains even after Todd's resurrection. <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Gordon" title="Barbara Gordon">Barbara Gordon</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Batgirl" title="Batgirl">Batgirl</a> costume also remains on display. In the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Horse_Comics" title="Dark Horse Comics">Dark Horse</a> two-part crossover, Grendel/Batman II, the skull of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hunter_Rose" title="Hunter Rose">Hunter Rose</a> is also put on display in the memorabilia room.</p>
<p>After the <i><a href="/wiki/Flashpoint_(comics)" title="Flashpoint (comics)">Flashpoint</a></i> comic book storyline, a letter written by a <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Wayne" title="Thomas Wayne">Thomas Wayne</a> from an alternate timeline addressed to Bruce Wayne has lain in a display case, as a reminder of Thomas Wayne's love for his son and encouraging him to move on from his tragic past.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>The Outsiders were, for a time, based out of a Batcave in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>. After <a href="/wiki/Bane_(comics)" title="Bane (comics)">Bane</a>'s attack during the <i>Knightfall</i> story arc, Bruce Wayne swore that he'd never be caught unprepared to defend <a href="/wiki/Gotham_City" title="Gotham City">Gotham City</a> ever again. When Dick Grayson assumed the Mantle of the Bat during the <i>Prodigal</i> storyline, Bruce established satellite Batcaves (most of which were not caves in the literal sense that the original one was) throughout the city on areas either owned by him, his company, or unknown or abandoned by the city, in the event that he needed a place to hide and/or resupply, which were pivotal during the <i>No Man's Land</i> storyline. One such Batcave was given to <a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Cain" title="Cassandra Cain">Batgirl</a>, below a house owned by Bruce Wayne himself, during a point where her identity was compromised after she saved a man from rogue government agents, meaning that she could not walk around without a mask. The other satellite Batcaves introduced during <i>No Man's Land</i> were:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Central Batcave</b>: Located fifty feet below the bottom of Robinson Park Reservoir, it is accessible through a secret entrance at the foot of one of the Twelve Caesars statues at the north of the park. This safehouse was put out of commission by <a href="/wiki/Poison_Ivy_(comics)" title="Poison Ivy (comics)">Poison Ivy</a>, her "Feraks", and <a href="/wiki/Clayface" title="Clayface">Clayface</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NoMansLand_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-NoMansLand-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><b>Batcave South</b>: A boiler room of a derelict shipping yard on the docks across from Paris Island. This safehouse is accessible through a number of false manholes planted throughout Old Gotham streets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NoMansLand_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-NoMansLand-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><b>Batcave South-Central</b>: Located in the Old Gotham prototype subway station, a four-block stretch of track sealed in 1896 and forgotten.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NoMansLand_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-NoMansLand-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><b>Northwest Batcave</b>: This safehouse is located in the subbasement of <a href="/wiki/Arkham_Asylum" title="Arkham Asylum">Arkham Asylum</a>. Batman secretly stocked it with emergency rations, all-terrain vehicles, and battery-powered communication equipment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NoMansLand_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-NoMansLand-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><b>Batcave East</b>: An abandoned oil refinery owned by Wayne Enterprises. It fell out of use during a gasoline crisis when the company moved all of its holdings offshore decades ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NoMansLand_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-NoMansLand-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><b>Arkham Island Batcave</b>: Over the years, Batman gathered supplies for a Batcave on Arkham Island, as a preemptive measure for any attack on the asylum. He finally utilized it during the events of the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_Arkham_Asylum" title="Batman: Arkham Asylum">Batman: Arkham Asylum</a></i>, but it was for the most part destroyed by the Titan-powered Poison Ivy. It's also implied that Joker knew about, or at least suspected the existence of this cave, as he dispatched his henchmen into the sewers of Arkham to find it, most likely because Joker has been fighting Batman for so long and knows that he would have a contingency plan for just such an emergency.</li>
</ul>
<p>Another was introduced in 2002's <i><a href="/wiki/Bruce_Wayne:_Fugitive" title="Bruce Wayne: Fugitive">Fugitive</a></i> story arc, this time in the form of an abandoned submarine.</p>

<p>Under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wayne_Foundation" title="Wayne Foundation">Wayne Foundation</a> building, there is a secret bunker. As of <i>Batman</i> #687, <a href="/wiki/Dick_Grayson" title="Dick Grayson">Dick Grayson</a> has taken to using this as his "Batcave", stating that he wishes to embody the role of Batman in a way that is specific to him as well as getting closer to the action in the city. This is similar to the bunker seen in the 2008 film <i>The Dark Knight</i>. The bunker is as well-equipped as the original Batcave, including the Subway Rocket vehicle stationed beneath the bunker.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_versions">Other versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Other versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_%26_Dracula:_Red_Rain" title="Batman &amp; Dracula: Red Rain">Batman &amp; Dracula: Red Rain</a></i>, Batman destroys the Batcave in order to eliminate Dracula's followers; having lured them into the cave after a prolonged pursuit, he sets off explosive charges to destroy the Batcave's walls at the moment the sun rises, destroying the vampires within it, before setting off additional charges to destroy Wayne Manor in order to preserve his secrets. The first sequel, <i>Bloodstorm,</i> shows that a cellar beneath a brownstone owned by Alfred Pennyworth serves as a lair/laboratory for Batman. Although Wayne Manor collapses into the remains of the cave, part of the tunnel system is still intact, with the now-vampiric Batman establishing his lair there in the story's second sequel, <i>Crimson Mist</i>, the interior including a deep chasm within walking distance of the areas where Batman kept the giant penny and the <a href="/wiki/Batmobile" title="Batmobile">Batmobile</a> when he was human. At the story's conclusion, <a href="/wiki/James_Gordon_(comics)#Vampire_Batman" title="James Gordon (comics)">Commissioner Gordon</a> sets off explosive charges to destroy the cave's roof, letting the sun into the cave once again in order to destroy, once and for all, the monster that Batman has become.</p>
<p>In the alternate reality of <i><a href="/wiki/Flashpoint_(comics)" title="Flashpoint (comics)">Flashpoint</a></i>, the Batcave- here used by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Wayne" title="Thomas Wayne">Thomas Wayne</a> rather than Bruce- is far smaller and more run-down than the traditional version, containing merely a couple of tables for Thomas to work on his equipment and a medical area, with a conventional computer in the upper manor, reflecting Thomas's more brutal and solitary M.O. as Batman as opposed to the more sophisticated training undertaken by his son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the comic book continuation of the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Smallville" title="Smallville">Smallville</a>,</i> Batman has a safe house in the form of a cargo ship, known as "Leviathan", docked at a hub in <a href="/wiki/Metropolis_(comics)" title="Metropolis (comics)">Metropolis</a>. It is registered to a <a href="/wiki/Shell_corporation" title="Shell corporation">shell corporation</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, thus protecting Bruce Wayne's secret. However, it is compromised by the <a href="/wiki/Intergang" title="Intergang">Intergang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prankster_(comics)" title="Prankster (comics)">Prankster</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mister_Freeze" title="Mister Freeze">Mister Freeze</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lex_Luthor_(Smallville)" title="Lex Luthor (Smallville)">Lex Luthor</a> is also aware of Leviathan's location due to his tracking of <a href="/wiki/Clark_Kent_(Smallville)" title="Clark Kent (Smallville)">Superman</a>'s radiation signature with his satellites.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_media">Other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Live-action">Live-action</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Live-action">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Serials">Serials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Serials">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Batcave first became part of the Batman mythos in the 1943 15-chapter movie serial <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_(serial)" title="Batman (serial)">Batman</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Wilson" title="Lewis Wilson">Lewis Wilson</a>. In this version, as later in the comics, it was just a small cave with a desk and <a href="/wiki/Filing_cabinet" title="Filing cabinet">filing cabinets</a>. It also contained bats (which were only shown as being shadows) and Batman used an enemy's phobia for them to make him talk.</p>
<p>To date, the serials are the only time the grandfather clock entrance to the Batcave has been featured in a live-action incarnation of Batman.</p>
<p>It was also featured in the 1949 serial <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_and_Robin_(serial)" title="Batman and Robin (serial)">Batman and Robin</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Robert_Lowery_(actor)" title="Robert Lowery (actor)">Robert Lowery</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Batman_.28TV_series.29"><i>Batman</i> (TV series)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Batman (TV series)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Batman_(TV_series)" title="Batman (TV series)">Batman (TV series)</a></div>
<p>The 1960s live-action Batman TV series featured the Batcave<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> extensively, and portrayed it as a large but well-lit cavern containing an atomic power generator, a chemistry lab, punch-card computers, and other electronic crime-fighting devices. In this incarnation, it primarily served as a crime lab and garage for the Batmobile. Perhaps the most famous aspect of this Batcave is that it is accessed from Wayne Manor via two Bat-Poles (one marked BRUCE and the other marked DICK), which are hidden behind a bookcase that can be opened by turning a switch hidden inside a bust of <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">Shakespeare</a>. When Bruce and Dick slide down these Bat-Poles, they are instantly outfitted in their costumes before reaching the landing pads at the bottom. The Bat-Poles can also be used to lift Bruce and Dick up from the Batcave to Wayne Manor by use of the steamjet-propelled landing pads. The Batcave is also accessible via a service elevator which is used by Alfred.</p>
<p>The actual cave that the Batmobile is shown emerging from (and sometimes entering) in the TV show is located in the man-made filming location known as the "<a href="/wiki/Bronson_Canyon" title="Bronson Canyon">Bronson Caves</a>," in <a href="/wiki/Griffith_Park" title="Griffith Park">Griffith Park</a>, below the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Sign" title="Hollywood Sign">Hollywood Sign</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Tim_Burton_films">Tim Burton films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Tim Burton films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Batman_.28film.29"><i>Batman</i> (film)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Batman (film)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Batman_(1989_film)" title="Batman (1989 film)">Batman (1989 film)</a></div>
<p>The cave is present in <a href="/wiki/Tim_Burton" title="Tim Burton">Tim Burton</a>'s <a href="/wiki/1989_in_film" title="1989 in film">1989</a> <i>Batman</i> feature film,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> and is shown to house the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Batmobile_(1989)" title="Batmobile (1989)">Batmobile</a>, which is parked at the edge of a large chasm, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Batcomputer" title="Batcomputer">Batcomputer</a> and a large vault for Batman's costume.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Batman_Returns"><i>Batman Returns</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Batman Returns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Batman_Returns" title="Batman Returns">Batman Returns</a></div>
<p>The cave is once again seen in <i>Batman Returns</i>, and Bruce gains access to it via a tube/elevator like passage from Wayne Manor, the entrance to which is hidden in an <a href="/wiki/Iron_maiden_(torture_device)" title="Iron maiden (torture device)">iron maiden</a>, and is activated by flipping a small switch hidden on a small replica of Wayne Manor in the bottom of a fish tank. Alfred also confirms, in a throwaway remark, that there is a stairway to the cave. The Batcave of this film is also shown to be more technologically advanced than in the previous film, featuring more work stations and computers. The most notable revision to the cave is a large walk-in closet carved into a wall, where Bruce stores his numerous Batsuits.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Joel_Schumacher_films">Joel Schumacher films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Joel Schumacher films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Batman_Forever"><i>Batman Forever</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Batman Forever">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Batman_Forever" title="Batman Forever">Batman Forever</a></div>
<p>In this film, the Batcave is accessed through a rotating wall in Wayne Manor's silver closet, the only room in the mansion that is kept locked. The cave can also be reached via a secret tunnel system from Bruce Wayne's office at Wayne Enterprises, through which he rides down in a capsule.</p>
<p>In addition to the standard housing of the computer and equipment, the cave was originally intended to play a larger role in this film. Alfred would reveal a second level to the cave, and an <a href="/wiki/Amnesia" title="Amnesia">amnesia</a> stricken Bruce Wayne would explore the cave to jog his memory after an attack by Two-Face. These scenes, however, were cut from the final film. The cave is also shown to have a canal inside of it, which provides access to the sea for water-based vehicles. The cave also has a lengthy tunnel used to launch the Batwing, which emerges from the cliffs underneath Wayne Manor. The cave features a rotating turntable that rises out of the floor, holding the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Batmobile_(Batman_Forever)" title="Batmobile (Batman Forever)">Batmobile</a>, and a large dome-like structure where Bruce's Batsuits and gadgets are stored.</p>
<p>The Riddler destroys most of the cave's equipment during an invasion of Wayne Manor by himself and Two-Face.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Batman_.26_Robin"><i>Batman &amp; Robin</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Batman &amp; Robin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Batman_%26_Robin_(film)" title="Batman &amp; Robin (film)">Batman &amp; Robin (film)</a></div>
<p>This incarnation of the cave features a multitude of flashing lights, mostly in neon. On the whole, this Batcave is similar to that in <i>Batman Forever</i>, only more garish in its decoration. A capsule containing Robin's <a href="/wiki/Redbird_(comics)" title="Redbird (comics)">Redbird</a> motorcycle rises out of the floor, and a long tunnel lined with neon lights leads out of the cave. The turntable holding the <a href="/wiki/Batmobile" title="Batmobile">Batmobile</a> is featured again, but in a more elaborate fashion.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Christopher_Nolan_films">Christopher Nolan films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Christopher Nolan films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Batman_Begins"><i>Batman Begins</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Batman Begins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Batman_Begins" title="Batman Begins">Batman Begins</a></div>
<p>In <i>Batman Begins</i>, the cave<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> is still unfurnished, and the only things inside are a small storage space for the Batsuit and its accessories, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Batmobile_(Batman_Begins)" title="Batmobile (Batman Begins)">Batmobile</a>. The entrance and exit for the Batmobile are on a cliff, behind a waterfall. Alfred reveals to Bruce that during the Civil War, the Waynes used the vast cavern system as part of the <a href="/wiki/Underground_Railroad" title="Underground Railroad">Underground Railroad</a>: after initially abseiling down a well (which Bruce fell down in his childhood) to get into the cave, they discover a hidden Civil War-era mechanical elevator which is still functional and leads to a hidden entrance in the mansion, which they then use as the primary means of entrance to the cave. Near the end of the film, when Bruce talks to Alfred about rebuilding the burnt-down main section of Wayne Manor, Alfred suggests they "improve the foundation", which may mean improving and furnishing the cave as they rebuild the mansion.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Dark_Knight"><i>The Dark Knight</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: The Dark Knight">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_(film)" title="The Dark Knight (film)">The Dark Knight (film)</a></div>
<p>As Wayne Manor is still under construction in <i>The Dark Knight</i>, Batman's base of operations has been relocated to a large bunker beneath a shipping yard. One access point shown is through a shipping container which houses a secret hydraulic lift. The "Bat-bunker" also contains a wire mesh cage for the Batsuit, along with the associated weapons and tools, toolbox, and spare equipment for the Batmobile. In contrast to the Batcave, the large rectangular shaped room is brightly lit by banks of overhead fluorescent lights. Storage areas for the equipment are located both under the ground and within the walls giving the room a very empty appearance with the exception of a large bank of monitors to go with a well-developed computer system. In addition, the room is equipped with furnaces which Alfred uses to burn documents after Bruce decides to turn himself in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Dark_Knight_Rises"><i>The Dark Knight Rises</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: The Dark Knight Rises">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_Rises" title="The Dark Knight Rises">The Dark Knight Rises</a></div>
<p>The Batcave reappears in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_Rises" title="The Dark Knight Rises">The Dark Knight Rises</a></i> in full working condition. To access the cave, a similar way to <i>Batman Begins</i>, tapping three keys on the piano will reveal a now modernly built elevator which takes the passenger straight to the cave. The newest addition to the cave is "<a href="/wiki/Batplane#Live-action_films" title="Batplane">The Bat</a>," a flying tank aircraft built by Wayne Enterprises' Applied Science Division and an upgraded <a href="/wiki/Batcomputer" title="Batcomputer">Batcomputer</a> as well numerous landing pads and a locking case which contains the Batsuit. Added features included that the bridges used to gain access to different sections can be submerged as well as the platforms as a form of security measures in case anyone gains unauthorized access to the cave. While submerged the only visible object is the Batcomputer which can only be accessed by either Bruce or Alfred's fingerprints and access code. The Bat-bunker appears as well, which contains weapons, supplies, and a back-up <a href="/wiki/Batsuit" title="Batsuit">Batsuit</a>. After Bruce Wayne is declared legally dead, his will is amended so that <a href="/wiki/Robin_in_other_media#The_Dark_Knight_Rises" title="Robin in other media">John Blake</a> inherits GPS coordinates that lead him to the Batcave.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Animation">Animation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Animation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_appearances">Early appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Early appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Bat-Cave was first seen in animation in various episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Batman/Superman_Hour" title="The Batman/Superman Hour">The Batman/Superman Hour</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Friends" title="Super Friends">Super Friends</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Adventures_of_Batman" title="The New Adventures of Batman">The New Adventures of Batman</a></i>. In these cartoons, the <a href="/wiki/Batcomputer" title="Batcomputer">Batcomputer</a> is present as usual. The voice of the Batcomputer was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Lou_Scheimer" title="Lou Scheimer">Lou Scheimer</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Adventures_of_Batman" title="The New Adventures of Batman">The New Adventures of Batman</a></i>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="DC_animated_universe">DC animated universe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: DC animated universe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Batman:_The_Animated_Series"><i>Batman: The Animated Series</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Batman: The Animated Series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>In the <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_The_Animated_Series" title="Batman: The Animated Series">Batman: The Animated Series</a></i> episode "Beware the Gray Ghost", the Batcave is revealed to be an exact replica of the lair used by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gray_Ghost_(fictional_character)" title="Gray Ghost (fictional character)">Gray Ghost</a>, a fiction-within-fiction character and idol to Bruce Wayne. There's also an exhibit of a collection of the Gray Ghost merchandise Bruce Wayne has collected since childhood. The Batcave gets introduced in this series as a large underground cavern. Bats are seen flying freely in the cave, with large naturally elevated platforms on which his sidekick <a href="/wiki/Robin_(comics)" title="Robin (comics)">Robin</a> practices his balance. Batman often utilises the <a href="/wiki/Batcomputer" title="Batcomputer">Batcomputer</a>, impressive technology during the time the series was produced (early to mid-1990s), to research information on villains, from an anti-venom to <a href="/wiki/Poison_Ivy_(comics)" title="Poison Ivy (comics)">Poison Ivy</a>'s plant poison to newspaper articles on the origin of <a href="/wiki/Killer_Croc" title="Killer Croc">Killer Croc</a>. Batman's numerous crime-fighting vehicles are seen parked in an adjacent compartment to the Batcave, with an adjoining not-so-secret subterranean garage which stores Bruce Wayne's mammoth collection of vintage and luxury cars.</p>
<p>In the episode "<a href="/wiki/Almost_Got_%27Im" title="Almost Got 'Im">Almost Got 'Im</a>", Two-Face uses a giant penny in an attempt to either crush Batman or kill him from the impact, depending on whichever side the giant coin landed on. Batman managed to free himself from the coin by slicing open the ropes. While telling the story of this to other Batman villains, Two-Face commented that Batman got to keep the giant coin. It is seen later in the series (and its spin-offs), in the Batcave.</p>
<p>Several entrances to the cave are seen throughout the series. In early episodes, Batman is seen using an elevator that is accessed through a secret door hidden behind a bookcase. In later episodes, he is seen using the classic grandfather clock entrance from the comics. In certain episodes, the clock-entrance is opened by setting the hands of the clock the time Bruce's parents were killed (similar to certain comic book stories), while in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Batman_Adventures" title="The New Batman Adventures">The New Batman Adventures</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_Beyond" title="Batman Beyond">Batman Beyond</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League" title="Justice League">Justice League</a></i>, the pendulum is pulled from behind the face of the clock to unlock the entrance.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="The_New_Batman_Adventures"><i>The New Batman Adventures</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: The New Batman Adventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>In the 1998 episode "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mean_Seasons" title="Mean Seasons">Mean Seasons</a>" from <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Batman_Adventures" title="The New Batman Adventures">The New Batman Adventures</a></i>, Batman and Batgirl are forced to fight a giant mechanical T-Rex. The comic book tie-in to the <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League_(TV_series)" title="Justice League (TV series)">Justice League</a></i> Batman - <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Batman_Adventures" title="Batman Adventures">Batman Adventures</a></i> #12 - features a short called "The Hidden Display" which tells how a young Dick Grayson persuades Batman into keeping a robot T-Rex early on his career, which eventually leads to the Trophy Room of the Cave. Either one of these tales could be how the animated Batman obtained the dinosaur. An extensive training area allows Barbara Gordon to take on robots as part of her training.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Batman_Beyond"><i>Batman Beyond</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Batman Beyond">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>This future Batcave of <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_Beyond" title="Batman Beyond">Batman Beyond</a></i> includes not only replicas of Batman's most famous enemies (both as wax dummies and robot combat trainers), but also a display case with the many permutations of costumes of Robin, Batgirl, Nightwing, and Batman himself. Other items which have been shown to be in the Cave include the Freeze Gun and helmet of <a href="/wiki/Mr._Freeze" title="Mr. Freeze">Mr. Freeze</a>, the puppet <a href="/wiki/Ventriloquist_(comics)" title="Ventriloquist (comics)">Scarface</a>, a 'shrine' to Bruce Wayne's childhood TV hero, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gray_Ghost_(fictional_character)" title="Gray Ghost (fictional character)">Gray Ghost</a>, and the costumes of <a href="/wiki/Harley_Quinn" title="Harley Quinn">Harley Quinn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Penguin_(comics)" title="Penguin (comics)">Penguin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Riddler" title="Riddler">Riddler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mad_Hatter_(comics)" title="Mad Hatter (comics)">Mad Hatter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Firefly_(DC_Comics)" title="Firefly (DC Comics)">Firefly</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Catwoman" title="Catwoman">Catwoman</a>.</p>
<p>The cave itself throughout the series also projected a shade of purple on person or persons while in the cave, making it appear as if Bruces black suit was purple and Terry's bat symbol composed of purple and red.</p>
<p>It also contains the original Batmobile and Batcomputer in the two part pilot, however by the subsequent episodes they had been removed and replaced with a new Batmobile which Terry uses throughout the series and feature film. How Bruce was able to refit and upgrade the Batcomputer and all other systems so quickly is never explained.</p>
<p>In the comic book series <i>Batman Beyond 2.0</i>, Terry no longer uses the Batcave due to a huge argument with Bruce. He now uses Dick Grayson's apartment as his base of operations. When Terry is seriously injured in a battle of with Rewire, he wakes up in the Batcave where Bruce has treated his injuries. He arrived there due to a built in subroutine in the suit that if the user is seriously injured the suit returns the user to the Batcave.</p>
<p>After arriving in the universe controlled by the Justice Lords Terry encounters a version of himself who is a member of the Jokerz known as " T ". Both McGinnis's arrive at Wayne Manor to find that it had been destroyed by the Justice Lords. A gang of Jokerz then proceed to attack them with T giving Terry enough time to make his way to the badly damaged Batcave where he finds an armored and more powerful version of his Batman suit. After defeating the Jokerz gang he is confronted by Justice Lord Superman.</p>
<p>Following the defeat of Lord Superman, T and Dick Grayson (of that universe) begin repairs to the Batcave and to the suit Terry found with the intention of T taking over as the new Batman and Dick becoming his mentor.</p>
<p>In the cave itself there are a number of damaged display cases which contain an unknown Batgirl suit, Justice Lord Batman suit and a Red Robin Suit.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Justice_League"><i>Justice League</i></span><i><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Justice League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></i></h5>
<p>In the <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League_(TV_series)" title="Justice League (TV series)">Justice League</a></i> animated series, the members of the League seek refuge in the Batcave during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thanagarian" title="Thanagarian">Thanagarian</a> invasion. Later, they also confront <a href="/wiki/Hawkgirl" title="Hawkgirl">Hawkgirl</a> in the cave, and use the <a href="/wiki/Batcomputer" title="Batcomputer">Batcomputer</a> to track her movements. When the Batcave comes under siege from the Thanagarians, one attempts to use Mr. Freeze's Freeze Gun on Superman; Superman repels the attack with a gust of wind, freezing the soldier. Flash also tips the infamous giant penny onto some of the attacking Thangarians ("Tails! I win!"). In a humorous scene, he also points to the T-Rex, stating "That's a giant dinosaur!", at which point Alfred states "And I thought Batman was the detective". It is assumed the League used the cave as a headquarters until the new Watchtower is built, as they decide Hawkgirl's fate in Wayne Manor in the wake of the invasion.</p>
<p>Interestingly the cave has exactly the same design as in Batman Beyond and not Batman: The Animated Series which means that Bruce Wayne was only a few years to a decade away from retiring as Batman. Also, Terry McGinnis's Batsuit is on display, albeit with a yellow Bat Symbol, which could indicate that the suit is a prototype version on Terry's Batman Beyond suit.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Batman"><i>The Batman</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: The Batman">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Batman_(TV_series)" title="The Batman (TV series)">The Batman</a></i>, the animated series that debuted in 2004, features a much more high-tech Batcave, with large computer displays and flashing blue lights. Among these displays are the "Bat-Wave" warning signals, an alternate way of calling upon the Caped Crusader before the <a href="/wiki/Bat-Signal" title="Bat-Signal">Bat-Signal</a> went into service. Bruce Wayne is seen mostly without his Batsuit or with his cowl removed while in the cave, unlike in the earlier animated series. As a throwback to the old Adam West TV show, the cave has assorted 'Bat-poles' for Batman and Robin which allowed them from level to level in a faster manner. Unlike the old series, it does not allow for instant costume changes. The elevator system is featured quite a bit as well. A similar trophy room, this time storing memorabilia seen in earlier episodes such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Riddler" title="The Riddler">The Riddler</a>'s giant hourglass and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Joker_(comics)" title="The Joker (comics)">The Joker</a>'s giant playing card trap, appears in the series. The series also shows that it was <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Pennyworth" title="Alfred Pennyworth">Alfred Pennyworth</a> who started the museum, hoping it would be useful if the city of Gotham ever fully accepted Batman, somewhat like the <a href="/wiki/Flash_Museum" title="Flash Museum">Flash Museum</a>.</p>
<p>The cave was also the location of Season 3's climatic finale, "Gotham's Ultimate Criminal Mastermind", in which the villainous robot <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D.A.V.E." title="D.A.V.E.">D.A.V.E.</a> attempts to kill Alfred using an array of trophies garnered by the Batman, putting the Dark Knight in a position where he had to choose between revealing his secret identity to all of Gotham City, or allowing Alfred to be killed by the trap. However, even the Batcave isn't impervious to damage. In one episode, a loose raccoon causes a short circuit and subsequent blackout of electricity in the cave. In the <a href="/wiki/Direct-to-video" title="Direct-to-video">direct-to-video</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Batman_vs._Dracula" title="The Batman vs. Dracula">The Batman vs. Dracula</a></i>, it said that Batman's cave is in fact part of a series of <a href="/wiki/Catacombs" title="Catacombs">Catacombs</a> under Gotham City, which Batman uses to lure Dracula to the cave and subsequently kill him with the new solar generator. On the episode "Joker's Express," it is revealed that the Batcave is also connected to some old <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mines</a> beneath the city when Gotham was a thriving coal-mining town in the late 19th century.</p>
<p>In the Season 4 episode "Artifacts", archaeologists from the future unearth the Batcave. Its titanium supports are printed with binary code, as the computer information would not survive that long. The archaeologists theorize that Thomas Wayne was Batman and that Bruce Wayne was Robin. In another segment of the episode, set in 2027, Babara Gordon (as the Oracle) is shown at the Batcomputer in the Batcave. Her wheelchair is also uncovered in the cave by the archaeologists, who believe that it was Alfred who used it.</p>
<p>Unlike in many previous incarnations of the Batcave which show only one exit/entrance, the Batmobile and other vehicles exit the cave through a variety of concealed dead-ends and disguised construction sites scattered around Gotham City. Batman also established a series of satellite Batcaves across Gotham on the show. Batcave South-Central debuted on the episode "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Strange_New_World_(The_Batman)" title="Strange New World (The Batman)">Strange New World</a>". In "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Joining,_Part_One" title="The Joining, Part One">The Joining, Part One</a>", it is revealed that <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Fox" title="Lucius Fox">Lucius Fox</a> helped the Batman in constructing the Batcave, and all of the Dark Knight's other secret safehouses throughout Gotham. Another satellite Batcave debuted on the episode "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Batman/Superman_Story,_Part_One" title="The Batman/Superman Story, Part One">The Batman/Superman Story, Part One</a>", under Wayne Industries which served as his new tech lab. In the episode, "The End of The Batman ", the villainous anti-Batman and Robin team, known as <a href="/wiki/Wrath_(comics)" title="Wrath (comics)">The Wrath</a> and his sidekick, Scorn, break into the Batcave, and attempt to kill Batman and Robin, causing large amounts of damage in the process. By the time of the two part finale involving all members of the Justice League the cave has been fully repaired.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Batman:_The_Brave_and_the_Bold"><i>Batman: The Brave and the Bold</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Batman: The Brave and the Bold">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_The_Brave_and_the_Bold" title="Batman: The Brave and the Bold">Batman: The Brave and the Bold</a></i>, the Batcave makes its first appearance in the episode "Deep Cover for Batman," when <a href="/wiki/Owlman_(comics)" title="Owlman (comics)">Owlman</a> attacks Batman inside it. In the following episode, "Game Over for Owlman," Batman brings the <a href="/wiki/Joker_(comics)" title="Joker (comics)">Joker</a>, who is at the time partnered with him, to the cave (The Joker accidentally sprayed himself with knockout gas before and after they were in the Batcave, so he couldn't possibly know where the cave's location is). A number of trophies displayed include a giant clam and oversized-slot-machine-themed electric chair, death traps formerly used by the Joker, in reference to the <a href="/wiki/Batman_(TV_series)" title="Batman (TV series)">1960s TV show</a>. The entrance to this Batcave can be seen briefly in <i>The Brave and the Bold</i> episode "Color of Revenge." It appears to be very similar to the <i>Batman</i> TV series Batcave. In The Siege of Starro Part 1, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Faceless_Hunter" title="Faceless Hunter">Faceless Hunter</a> attacks Batman in the Batcave. In the episode "Darkseid Descending", a reserve Batcave (very similar to the "Bat-Bunker" in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_(film)" title="The Dark Knight (film)">The Dark Knight</a></i>) is located inside the <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Memorial" title="Lincoln Memorial">Lincoln Memorial</a>.</p>
<p>In "Menace of the Conquer Caveman" <a href="/wiki/Booster_Gold" title="Booster Gold">Booster Gold</a> mentions that the Batcave will be converted into a historical attraction with its own built-in <a href="/wiki/Roller_coaster" title="Roller coaster">roller coaster</a> in the 25th century. In "The Last Bat on Earth", Batman goes to the Batcave to use technology from his era to defeat <a href="/wiki/Gorilla_Grodd" title="Gorilla Grodd">Gorilla Grodd</a> and his army of intelligent apes in <a href="/wiki/Kamandi" title="Kamandi">Kamandi</a>'s time. A group of humanoid humanoid "Man-Bats" made the cave their home after the Great Diaster and are driven out by Batman and Kamandi.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_Games">Video Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batcave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=36" title="Edit section: Video Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 2009 video game <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_Arkham_Asylum" title="Batman: Arkham Asylum">Batman: Arkham Asylum</a>,</i> Batman can access a secret auxiliary Batcave hidden within the cave system beneath Arkham Island after the Joker takes control of the asylum. This Batcave is small and fairly spartan (in comparison with Batman's primary Batcave), containing only two small platforms, a <a href="/wiki/Batcomputer" title="Batcomputer">Batcomputer</a>, and one of Batman's Batwing planes. Near the end of the game this cave was destroyed by Poison Ivy.</p>
<p>Although not featured in the main story, the Batcave does appear as a downloadable challenge map in the 2011 video game sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_Arkham_City" title="Batman: Arkham City">Batman: Arkham City</a></i>.</p>
<p>The Batcave is also featured in the 2012 video game <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lego_Batman_2:_DC_Superheroes" title="Lego Batman 2: DC Superheroes">Lego Batman 2: DC Superheroes</a></i> which features three parking 'areas' for land, sea and air based vehicles and their appropriate exits from the cave, the Batcomputer, used to replay past levels and 'warp' to various landmarks in Gotham and other elements shown in Batman media such as a waterfall, a Lincoln Penny and an animatronic T-Rex.</p>
<p>Also in the 2008 video game <i><a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_vs._DC_Universe" title="Mortal Kombat vs. DC Universe">Mortal Kombat vs. DC Universe</a>,</i> the Batcave is one of the fighting arenas.</p>
<p>In the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Injustice:_Gods_Among_Us" title="Injustice: Gods Among Us">Injustice: Gods Among Us</a></i>, which is also made by the creators of <i>Mortal Kombat vs. DC Universe</i>, the Batcave is a level in the game; <a href="/wiki/Green_Arrow" title="Green Arrow">Green Arrow</a> faces a villainous <a href="/wiki/Wonder_Woman" title="Wonder Woman">Wonder Woman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_Adam" title="Black Adam">Black Adam</a> in the Batcave when attempting to acquire a kryptonite weapon to defeat the corrupted Superman of an alternate reality, and the 'true' Batman faces the alternate Batman in a fight in the Batcave to convince him to go along with the plan of summoning the Superman of their world to defeat the villainous Superman of the alternate world.</p>
<p>The Batcave is accessible in the main campaign of <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_Arkham_Origins" title="Batman: Arkham Origins">Batman: Arkham Origins</a></i>. From the cave the player can use the Batwing fast travel system, switch to alternate skins and enter the challenge map rooms as opposed to selecting from the main menu as in previous Arkham games. Alfred is also present in the cave, supplying Batman with gadget upgrades. The Batcave is heavily damaged by <a href="/wiki/Bane_(comics)" title="Bane (comics)">Bane</a> during the game's climax.</p>
