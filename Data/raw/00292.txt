<p><b>The Avengers</b> are a team of superheroes assembled by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nick_Fury_(character)" title="Nick Fury (character)">Nick Fury</a> and the intelligence agency <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S.H.I.E.L.D._(Marvel_Cinematic_Universe)" title="S.H.I.E.L.D. (Marvel Cinematic Universe)">S.H.I.E.L.D.</a> through the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avenger_Initiative" title="Avenger Initiative">Avenger Initiative</a>. They exist in the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Cinematic_Universe" title="Marvel Cinematic Universe">Marvel Cinematic Universe</a> and feature all the heroes of Marvel Studios' independent productions. They first appeared as a team in 2012's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Avengers_(film)" title="The Avengers (film)">The Avengers</a>. </i>The Avengers were formed to combat threats that could not be dealt with alone.
</p><p>The team currently consists of <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Stark" title="Anthony Stark">Iron Man</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steve_Rogers_(Marvel_Cinematic_Universe)" title="Steve Rogers (Marvel Cinematic Universe)">Captain America</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Banner" title="Bruce Banner">Hulk</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thor_Odinson_(Marvel_Cinematic_Universe)" title="Thor Odinson (Marvel Cinematic Universe)">Thor</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natasha_Romanoff" title="Natasha Romanoff">Black Widow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clint_Barton" title="Clint Barton">Hawkeye</a>. They were initially brought together by <a href="/wiki/S.H.I.E.L.D." title="S.H.I.E.L.D.">S.H.I.E.L.D.</a> to combat <a href="/wiki/Loki_Laufeyson" title="Loki Laufeyson">Loki</a>, Thor's adoptive brother. The Avengers are based on the superhero team created by <a href="/wiki/Stan_Lee" title="Stan Lee">Stan Lee</a> and Steve Ditko and feature a similar roster to Mark Millar's Ultimates, the Ultimate Marvel Universe version of the team. The team was initially formed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nick_Fury" title="Nick Fury">Nick Fury</a> to "fight the battles that S.H.I.E.L.D. never could".
</p>

<p>Nick Fury assembles the team when an unexpected threat associated with Thor's brother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loki" title="Loki">Loki</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Tesseract" title="Tesseract">Tesseract</a> appears. The team initially have difficulty and refusal to obey each other; for example, Tony Stark is reluctant to working under the command of Captain America, Thor demands that Loki is his responsibility and the team is uncomfortable with the presence of Bruce Banner. When Loki attempts to destroy the team and promote his godlike supremecy to Earth in a public way, the Avengers depart from S.H.I.E.L.D.'s command and rally in defense of New York as it is invaded by the extraterrestrials known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chitauri_(Marvel_Cinematic_Universe)" title="Chitauri (Marvel Cinematic Universe)">Chitauri</a>. Captain America takes command of the team and they each work to supress the invasion force. The Hulk beats Loki into submission and Iron Man sends a nuclear weapon fired by the World Security Council into the portal through which the aliens enter Earth and their fleet is destroyed, breaking the neural connection with their ground troops and instantly killing them. The team take Loki as their prisoner and Thor takes both Loki and the Tesseract back to <a href="/wiki/Asgard" title="Asgard">Asgard</a>. The team disbands but agrees to reassemble when another global threat requires their combined efforts.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span></h2>
<ul><li>Joss Whedon talked about the power levels of the various Avengers: "<i>I had to find a way to service all the characters, to figure out why they're in the room in the first place. My biggest concern was that if I didn't pay enough attention to minutiae then the film would become generic in its concepts and visuals. Or that I'd be so concentrated on those things, I'd look around and the film wouldn't be about anything.</i>"
</li><li>On the action scenes and what Whedon terms "the hierarchy of power". "<i>If this <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steve_Rogers" title="Steve Rogers">guy</a> hits someone, he falls down. If this <a href="/wiki/Hulk" title="Hulk">guy</a> hits someone, he flies backwards. Its very complicated.</i>"
</li><li>Whedon commented on Mark Ruffalo's 'Hulk' actions: "<i>It's fun to say that Mark Ruffalo and I fought a great deal, because he's the sweetest man that I may ever have met. Yes, we did actually lay out some mats and tussle but just trying to figure out what the Hulk would be doing, what a person does in a real fight. Once he gets angry you have to keep asking "well what's making him angry now..." and go moment-to-moment. That's been the hardest thing in this movie. Mark is tremendous, I think he's going to blow people away - again - and we've gone where nobody has. We [have] Hulk in the house and everything pisses him off.</i>"
</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia"> Trivia </span></h2>
<ul><li>The roster of the team is similar to that of the Ultimates roster in the comic book miniseries <i>The Ultimates</i>. The Ultimates were conceived as the Ultimate Marvel Universe's equivalent of the mainstream Avengers. The Avengers of the Marvel Cinematic Universe bear a strong resemblance to the Ultimates in that they were formed by S.H.I.E.L.D. as a government-sponsored team to combat the threat of the Chitauri. Like in <i>The Ultimates 2</i>, the team leaves S.H.I.E.L.D. employ and becomes independent.
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Henry_Pym" title="Henry Pym">Henry Pym (Ant-Man)</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Wasp" title="The Wasp">The Wasp</a> are on the original Avengers roster in the mainstream comics, as well as their Ultimate universe versions being in the first roster of the Ultimates, but are absent from the live-action film.
</li></ul>
</li><li>The speech in the first teaser trailer was taken from the introduction of many Avengers comic books since the 1970s. In the TV series <i>The Avengers: Earth's Mightiest Heroes</i>, the new intro features Nick Fury stating a speech similar to this one.
</li><li>The movie's roster will be seen in the next animated series <i>Avengers Assemble</i> with Falcon joining the roster.
</li><li>With Quicksilver and Scarlet Witch appearing in <i>Avengers: Age of Ultron</i>, the roster continues to closely follow the comics as they both joined The Avengers along-side Captain America and Hawkeye (both of whom are founding members in the first film) after the original lineup disbanded, thus forming the second. 
</li></ul>
