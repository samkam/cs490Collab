<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://neoavatara.com/blog/?p=11248" rel="bookmark">Toy Story 3:  Movie Review</a></h1><div class="entry-info"><div class="entry-author">By <address class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://neoavatara.com/blog/?author=1" title="neoavatara">neoavatara</a></address></div> – <abbr class="published" title="2010-06-18T17:47:29+00:00">June 18, 2010</abbr></div>
<div class="entry-content clearfix">
<p style="text-align: center;"><img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="564" src="http://trailertracker.files.wordpress.com/2009/10/toy-story-3-02.jpg" width="410"/></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">
<p style="text-align: left;">It is pretty easy nowadays to write a review of a Pixar movie.  Simply write, ‘Great!’, and end it there…and you would pretty much hit the nail on the head.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">The original Toy Story movies were the coming out party for Pixar 15 years ago.  They introduced America to a new kind of animation…one that once again drew adults and children.  And this movie, as much as any, taps into that.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span id="_mce_tmp">Andy, the toy’s owner, is all grown up and off to college.  So the inevitable question:  what do you do with all those childhood memories?  Well, most of us throw them away, or give them away to charity.  It is part of moving into adulthood (except for me, who keeps on buying toys for myself…).</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span>They accidentally make their way to a day care center, where they quickly realize…they are no longer really wanted. </span><em>Their </em><span>Andy is gone.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span>A host of new toys make appearances, including Ken (of Barbie fame) and Big Baby…a scary looking doll if there was ever one (a spawn of Chucky?).  Any parent who gives a Big Baby doll to their child should be separated from all children for eternity.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span>Now, this movie is in many ways dark.  It is not like the first two movies, which were largely fun jaunts through playland.  There are serious issues at hand here, including how change affects life. There is a scene a dumpyard that I don’t think my kid understood…but it shows you the wasteland that awaits all toys.  It was, in its own way, scary and moving.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span>Ironically, the Toy Story movies are the Pixar movies I </span><em>least</em> watch.  Maybe they are too oriented for kids, I don’t know.  But this film is one that I really think I want to go back and see.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span>This movie is gorgeous in 3-D., especially IMAX, and that is certainly preferable to a regular theater if you have the opportunity.  Animation is ideal for 3D, and this may be the best of the animated 3D films out there.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span>Another Pixar movie classic….but is there any other kind?</span></p>
