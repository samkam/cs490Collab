<p><i><b>And Then There Were None</b></i> is a 1945 film adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Agatha_Christie" title="Agatha Christie">Agatha Christie</a>'s best-selling <a href="/wiki/Mystery_fiction" title="Mystery fiction">mystery</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/And_Then_There_Were_None" title="And Then There Were None">novel of the same name</a> and is directed by <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Clair" title="René Clair">René Clair</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> In the UK it had the title "Ten Little Niggers".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RT_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-RT-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The film changes certain characters' names and adheres to the ending of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stage_play" title="Stage play">stage play</a> rather than that of the novel.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The cast featured <a href="/wiki/Barry_Fitzgerald" title="Barry Fitzgerald">Barry Fitzgerald</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walter_Huston" title="Walter Huston">Walter Huston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louis_Hayward" title="Louis Hayward">Louis Hayward</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roland_Young" title="Roland Young">Roland Young</a>, <a href="/wiki/June_Duprez" title="June Duprez">June Duprez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mischa_Auer" title="Mischa Auer">Mischa Auer</a>, <a href="/wiki/C._Aubrey_Smith" title="C. Aubrey Smith">C. Aubrey Smith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Judith_Anderson" title="Judith Anderson">Judith Anderson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Haydn" title="Richard Haydn">Richard Haydn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Queenie_Leonard" title="Queenie Leonard">Queenie Leonard</a> as the people stranded on the island. The film won the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Leopard" title="Golden Leopard">Golden Leopard</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Locarno_International_Film_Festival" title="Locarno International Film Festival">Locarno International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Locarno_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Locarno-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Though it was produced by a major studio, <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a>, the copyright was allowed to lapse and the film is now in the public domain. Several different editions of varying quality have been released to home video formats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=And_Then_There_Were_None_(1945_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eight people, all total strangers to each other, are invited to small, isolated Indian Island off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, by Mr. and Mrs. Owen. They settle in at a mansion tended by two newly hired servants, Thomas and Ethel Rogers, but their hosts are absent. When the guests sit down to dinner, they notice the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Centerpiece" title="Centerpiece">centerpiece</a>, ten figurines of Indians in a circle. Afterward, Thomas Rogers puts on a <a href="/wiki/Gramophone_record" title="Gramophone record">gramophone record</a>, from which a voice accuses them all of murder:</p>
<ul>
<li>General Sir John Mandrake (<a href="/wiki/C._Aubrey_Smith" title="C. Aubrey Smith">C. Aubrey Smith</a>), of ordering his wife's lover, a lieutenant, to his death</li>
<li>Emily Brent (<a href="/wiki/Judith_Anderson" title="Judith Anderson">Judith Anderson</a>), of the death of her young nephew</li>
<li>Dr. Edward G. Armstrong (<a href="/wiki/Walter_Huston" title="Walter Huston">Walter Huston</a>), of drunkenness which resulted in a patient dying</li>
<li>Prince Nikita Starloff (<a href="/wiki/Mischa_Auer" title="Mischa Auer">Mischa Auer</a>), of killing a couple</li>
<li>Vera Claythorne (<a href="/wiki/June_Duprez" title="June Duprez">June Duprez</a>), of murdering her sister's fiance</li>
<li>Judge Francis J. Quinncannon (<a href="/wiki/Barry_Fitzgerald" title="Barry Fitzgerald">Barry Fitzgerald</a>), of being responsible for the hanging of an innocent man</li>
<li>Philip Lombard (<a href="/wiki/Louis_Hayward" title="Louis Hayward">Louis Hayward</a>), of killing 21 East African tribesmen</li>
<li>William H. Blore (<a href="/wiki/Roland_Young" title="Roland Young">Roland Young</a>), of perjury, resulting in an innocent man's death</li>
<li>Thomas (<a href="/wiki/Richard_Haydn" title="Richard Haydn">Richard Haydn</a>) and Ethel Rogers (<a href="/wiki/Queenie_Leonard" title="Queenie Leonard">Queenie Leonard</a>), of the demise of their previous employer, an invalid.</li>
</ul>
<p>It turns out that none of the ten knows or has even seen "U. N. Owen," as he signed his instructions to Rogers; they suddenly realize it stands for "unknown." The guests decide to leave, but Rogers informs them that the boat will not return until Monday, and it is only Friday.</p>
<p>Starloff admits to speeding and running down a couple. Then he takes a drink and dies from poison. The next morning, the guests learn that Mrs. Rogers has died in her sleep. Quinncannon reports that Rogers found one figurine broken after Starloff's demise. Now another is missing. With two deaths matching the <a href="/wiki/Ten_Little_Indians" title="Ten Little Indians">Ten Little Indians</a> nursery rhyme, they search the island for "Mr. Owen" without success. After General Mandrake is stabbed in the back, the judge arrives at the only explanation: Owen must be one of them.</p>
<p>Another day passes. Everyone votes secretly for whom they suspect. Only Rogers receives two votes, and is sent to spend the night in the woodshed. After locking the dining room, they give Rogers the key. The next morning, however, they find him dead, his head split open with an axe. Vera persuades Brent to reveal that she had her nephew placed in a <a href="/wiki/Reformatory" title="Reformatory">reformatory</a>, where he hanged himself. Later that day, Brent's body is found with a <a href="/wiki/Hypodermic_needle" title="Hypodermic needle">hypodermic needle</a> nearby. Armstrong discovers his is missing. Lombard admits he had a revolver, but it is lost as well.</p>
<p>At dinner, Quinncannon confesses he sentenced an innocent man to death to ruin the defending counsel's reputation. Armstrong then admits to operating while drunk, with fatal results. Blore grudgingly discloses that he perjured himself to put an innocent man in prison, where he died. Lombard merely states that the accusation against him is true. When it is Vera's turn, she excuses herself to get her coat. The others hear her shriek and rush to her. In the confusion, a single gunshot is heard. They find Vera shaken after being brushed by seaweed hanging from the ceiling, which she mistook for a cold hand. They also spot Lombard's gun, and Quinncannon dead from a shot to the head.</p>
<p>Vera insists she is innocent, but Armstrong contends that only a person who had not committed a crime would want to mete out "justice" and locks her in her room. Later that night, she wakes to find Lombard outside her window. After he gives her his gun, she lets him inside. He persuades her to admit that it was her sister who killed her own fiancé, and Vera helped her cover up the crime and unofficially took the blame, caring for her sister until she died. They hear someone going downstairs. Upon investigation, they realize that Armstrong is missing.</p>
<p>The next morning, Blore is struck while outside by stonework toppled from the floor above. Lombard takes the binoculars beside the body and sees what Blore had—a corpse on the beach. It is Armstrong. Vera pulls out the gun, certain that Lombard is the killer. Lombard tells her that his real name is Charles Morley, and that he impersonated his friend to discover if the invitation had anything to do with his friend's suicide.</p>
<p>"Owen" watches from the mansion as Vera fires and Morley drops. Returning to the mansion, Vera finds a rope and noose hanging in the parlor and discovers who Owen is: Quinncannon, very much alive. The judge tells her that all his life he had searched for perfect justice. After learning that he was terminally ill, he concocted this plan. He persuaded Armstrong to fake his (Quinncannon's) death, supposedly to help catch Owen, then murdered Armstrong. He tells Vera that she can either hang herself or be sent to the gallows (as the only possible perpetrator). He drinks poisoned whiskey, but he sees Morley before he dies and realizes that his scheme has been foiled. Vera had missed intentionally. Boat owner Fred Narracott returns to the island, asking the couple if the party is ready to leave, to which they reply, "You get them!", and run off to the boat.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Screenplay">Screenplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=And_Then_There_Were_None_(1945_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Screenplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Differences_from_the_novel">Differences from the novel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=And_Then_There_Were_None_(1945_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Differences from the novel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This adaptation of the novel took, overall, fewer liberties with Christie's plot than some of the other versions. Most of changes were made in order to comply with the strict censorship of the day, which included changing the backstories behind Miss Brent's and Vera Claythorne's crimes, since a film that would imply such themes as <a href="/wiki/Child_murder" title="Child murder">child murder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Teenage_pregnancy" title="Teenage pregnancy">teenage pregnancy</a> would never be allowed to be viewed by the general public.</p>
<p>Some of the other characters' names and crimes were also changed. Judge Francis J Quincannon was known as Justice Lawrence J Wargrave in the book. General Sir John Mandrake was called General John Gordon Macarthur and Prince Nikita Starloff replaced Anthony Marston. In the novel, Marston had killed two children—John and Lucy Combes—while driving recklessly.</p>
<p>The ending, though, is radically altered. Only the 1987 <a href="/wiki/Desyat_Negrityat" title="Desyat Negrityat">Soviet film</a> version kept the novel's ending. This film, like all the other Western versions, changed the shooting of Philip Lombard (played by <a href="/wiki/Louis_Hayward" title="Louis Hayward">Louis Hayward</a>) and the suicide of Vera Claythorne's character (played by <a href="/wiki/June_Duprez" title="June Duprez">June Duprez</a>) in favour of a more upbeat ending. Vera pretends to shoot Lombard so that the real murderer will believe he is dead.</p>
<p>In this, the film follows the altered denouement Christie herself had rewritten for her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/And_Then_There_Were_None_(1943_play)" title="And Then There Were None (1943 play)">1943 stage version</a> of the book. There is one major alteration — in the play, Vera thinks she has shot Lombard, after which the murderer appears and attacks her; Lombard, who was only grazed, comes to at the last minute and shoots the murderer as he is about to strangle the terrified girl. The film, however, simply has Vera help Lombard fake his death, then confront the culprit who commits suicide after revealing his motive and murder techniques.</p>
<p>All in all, the end result is the same; the two major characters are left alive and innocent of the crimes they were accused of. Later remakes in 1965, 1974, and 1989 (all using the title <i><a href="/wiki/Ten_Little_Indians" title="Ten Little Indians">Ten Little Indians</a></i>), also used one of these two revised finales.</p>
<p>One final alteration is the title. Christie's novel was originally titled <i>Ten Little Niggers</i> and then it was changed to <i>Ten Little Indians</i>, the title it is often known by today. In some countries, such as UK and Australia, the novel was renamed <i>And Then There Were None</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=And_Then_There_Were_None_(1945_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>And Then There Were None</i> currently holds a 100% approval rating on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remakes">Remakes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=And_Then_There_Were_None_(1945_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Remakes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Christie's mystery has been filmed a number of times, including as <i><a href="/wiki/Ten_Little_Indians_(1965_film)" title="Ten Little Indians (1965 film)">Ten Little Indians</a></i> (1965), <i><a href="/wiki/And_Then_There_Were_None_(1974_film)" title="And Then There Were None (1974 film)">Ten Little Indians</a></i> (1974), <i><a href="/wiki/Desyat_Negrityat" title="Desyat Negrityat">Desyat Negrityat</a></i> (1987) and <i><a href="/wiki/Ten_Little_Indians_(1989_film)" title="Ten Little Indians (1989 film)">Ten Little Indians</a></i> (1989), with variations to its characters and locale.</p>
