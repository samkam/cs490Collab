<p><i><b>The Da Vinci Code</b></i> is a 2006 American <a href="/wiki/Mystery_film" title="Mystery film">mystery</a>-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conspiracy_thriller" title="Conspiracy thriller">thriller</a> film produced by <a href="/wiki/John_Calley" title="John Calley">John Calley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brian_Grazer" title="Brian Grazer">Brian Grazer</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/Ron_Howard" title="Ron Howard">Ron Howard</a>. The screenplay was written by <a href="/wiki/Akiva_Goldsman" title="Akiva Goldsman">Akiva Goldsman</a> and adapted from <a href="/wiki/Dan_Brown" title="Dan Brown">Dan Brown</a>'s 2003 best-selling <a href="/wiki/The_Da_Vinci_Code" title="The Da Vinci Code">novel of the same name</a>. The film stars <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hanks" title="Tom Hanks">Tom Hanks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Audrey_Tautou" title="Audrey Tautou">Audrey Tautou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ian_McKellen" title="Ian McKellen">Ian McKellen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Molina" title="Alfred Molina">Alfred Molina</a>, <a href="/wiki/J%C3%BCrgen_Prochnow" title="Jürgen Prochnow">Jürgen Prochnow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Reno" title="Jean Reno">Jean Reno</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Bettany" title="Paul Bettany">Paul Bettany</a>.</p>
<p>In the film, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Langdon" title="Robert Langdon">Robert Langdon</a>, a professor of religious <a href="/wiki/Iconography" title="Iconography">iconography</a> and symbology from <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>, is the prime suspect in the grisly and unusual murder of Louvre curator Jacques Saunière. He escapes with the assistance of a police <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cryptology" title="Cryptology">cryptologist</a>, Sophie Neveu, and they are embroiled in a quest for the legendary <a href="/wiki/Holy_Grail" title="Holy Grail">Holy Grail</a>. He is pursued by a dogged French police captain, Bezu Fache. A noted British Grail historian, Sir Leigh Teabing, tells them the actual Holy Grail is explicitly encoded in <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci" title="Leonardo da Vinci">Leonardo da Vinci</a>'s wall painting, the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Supper_(Leonardo_da_Vinci)" title="The Last Supper (Leonardo da Vinci)">Last Supper</a></i>. Also searching for the Grail is a secret cabal within <a href="/wiki/Opus_Dei" title="Opus Dei">Opus Dei</a>, an actual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prelature" title="Prelature">prelature</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a>, who wishes to keep the true Grail a secret; the revelation of this secret would certainly destroy Christianity.</p>
<p>The film, like the book, was considered controversial. It was met with especially harsh criticism by the Roman Catholic Church for the accusation that it is behind a two-thousand-year-old coverup concerning what the Holy Grail really is and the concept that Jesus Christ and <a href="/wiki/Mary_Magdalene" title="Mary Magdalene">Mary Magdalene</a> were married and that the union produced a daughter. Many members urged the <a href="/wiki/Laity" title="Laity">laity</a> to boycott the film. Two organizations, the <a href="/wiki/Priory_of_Sion" title="Priory of Sion">Priory of Sion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Opus_Dei" title="Opus Dei">Opus Dei</a> figure prominently in the story. In the book, Dan Brown insists that the Priory of Sion and "...all descriptions of artwork, architecture, documents and secret rituals in this novel are accurate".</p>
<p>The film was met with largely negative critical response upon its release on May 19, 2006. However, this did little to hamper its box office performance; it earned US$224 million in its worldwide opening weekend and was the second highest-grossing film of 2006 behind <i><a href="/wiki/Pirates_of_the_Caribbean:_Dead_Man%27s_Chest" title="Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest">Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A man revealed to be Jacques Saunière is being pursued by a mysterious hooded character known as Silas (<a href="/wiki/Paul_Bettany" title="Paul Bettany">Paul Bettany</a>) through the Grand Gallery in the <a href="/wiki/Louvre" title="Louvre">Louvre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. Silas demands the location of the Priory's clef de voûte or "keystone." Under threat of death, Saunière finally confesses the keystone is kept in the sacristy of <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Saint-Sulpice,_Paris" title="Church of Saint-Sulpice, Paris">Church of Saint-Sulpice</a>, "beneath the Rose." Silas thanks him, and then shoots him in the stomach. Meanwhile, American symbologist Robert Langdon (<a href="/wiki/Tom_Hanks" title="Tom Hanks">Tom Hanks</a>), who is in Paris as a guest lecturer on symbols and the sacred feminine, is contacted by the French police, and summoned to the Louvre to view the crime scene. He discovers the dying Saunière has created an intricate display using black light ink and his own body and blood. Captain Bezu Fache (<a href="/wiki/Jean_Reno" title="Jean Reno">Jean Reno</a>) asks him for his interpretation of the puzzling scene.</p>
<p>Silas calls a mysterious man known as "The Teacher", revealing that he has killed all four protectors of the keystone and that all confirmed the same location. He dons a metal <a href="/wiki/Cilice" title="Cilice">cilice</a> on his thigh and proceeds to flagellate himself with a whip for the sins of murder. Facilitated by Bishop Manuel Aringarosa, Silas then travels to Saint-Sulpice and is admitted by an elderly nun; left alone, he excavates beneath the floor of the church to find a stone saying only JOB 38:11. He confronts the nun, who quotes the passage: "Hitherto shalt thou come, but no further." Realizing that he has been deceived, Silas is enraged and kills the nun.</p>
<p>Sophie Neveu (<a href="/wiki/Audrey_Tautou" title="Audrey Tautou">Audrey Tautou</a>), a cryptologist with the French police, enters the Louvre as well and slips Langdon a message which leads him to the bathroom. There, Sophie meets him and tells him that he is being tracked, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GPS" title="GPS">GPS</a> tracking dot has been (unknown by him) slipped into his jacket and that he is a primary suspect in the murder case because of a line of text found by the corpse ("P.S. find Robert Langdon"). Sophie however, believes that Saunière, who is revealed to be her grandfather, wanted to pass a hidden message on to her (Princesse Sophie, P.S., was the nickname he used for her), and that he had wanted to bring Langdon into the equation so that he could help her crack the code.</p>
<p>Buying some time by throwing the tracking device into the back of a truck, the pair begin exploring the Louvre, finding more anagram messages that Saunière had left behind. Many of these relate to Leonardo da Vinci's art, and the pair find a key with a Fleur-de-lis behind <a href="/wiki/Virgin_of_the_Rocks" title="Virgin of the Rocks">Madonna of the Rocks</a> painting. Langdon deduces from this that Saunière was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Priory_of_Sion" title="Priory of Sion">Priory of Sion</a>, a secret society associated with the <a href="/wiki/Knights_Templar" title="Knights Templar">Knights Templar</a>.</p>
<p>Pursued by the French police and cut off from the <a href="/wiki/Embassy_of_the_United_States,_Paris" title="Embassy of the United States, Paris">United States Embassy</a>, the pair escape to the <a href="/wiki/Bois_de_Boulogne" title="Bois de Boulogne">Bois de Boulogne</a> where Langdon closely inspects the key. He notices an inscription on the side - an address. The address directs them to the Depository Bank of Zurich where the key is used for a safety deposit box. In the bank, they find Saunière's deposit box and open it using the 10 digit <a href="/wiki/Fibonacci_number" title="Fibonacci number">Fibonacci numbers</a> in order (1123581321). Inside the box, they find a rosewood container, which contains a <a href="/wiki/Cryptex" title="Cryptex">cryptex</a>: a cylindrical container with five alphabetical dials which must be arranged in the correct sequence to spell out a 5-letter code word, in order to open and access the papyrus message inside. Using force to open the cryptex would break a vial of vinegar inside, which would dissolve the papyrus and destroy the message.</p>
<p>Unfortunately, the police are called by a security guard and they are forced to leave. The bank manager, Andre Vernet, assists them in escaping by taking them as passengers in an armoured van to escape the routine checks of the police. In the back of the truck Langdon and Neveu have a lengthy discussion about the cryptex and Neveu says that her grandfather often played games with her involving cryptexes. Langdon says that the cryptex might hold valuable information or another clue about what they are trying to discover. Eventually, they come to a sudden stop and Vernet forces them at gunpoint to give him the cryptex. Langdon tricks Vernet and disarms him and he and Sophie escape with the cryptex in their hands.</p>
<p>Langdon suggests that they visit his friend, Leigh Teabing (<a href="/wiki/Ian_McKellen" title="Ian McKellen">Ian McKellen</a>), for assistance to opening the cryptex. Leigh Teabing turns out to be an enthusiastic seeker of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Grail" title="Holy Grail">Holy Grail</a>, which he believes is not actually a cup but instead <a href="/wiki/Mary_Magdalene" title="Mary Magdalene">Mary Magdalene</a>. Mary was pregnant at the time of <a href="/wiki/Crucifixion_of_Jesus" title="Crucifixion of Jesus">Christ's crucifixion</a>, and Teabing tells Sophie that the Priory of Sion was formed to protect the descendants of Jesus. Jacques Saunière was believed to be a part of this society and Teabing suspects that he was training Sophie to join it also. Silas, meanwhile, breaks into Teabing's mansion and attempts to steal the cryptex. Teabing uses his cane to knock Silas out and they escape again, taking the butler, Remy Jean, and Silas with them. The group escapes in Teabing's plane, following the next clue to London. Fache learns of their destination, and alerts the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Police_Service" title="Metropolitan Police Service">London Metropolitan Police</a> to apprehend them at the airport. But Teabing manages to slip the party past the police with a trick of misdirection.</p>
<p>Teabing leads Langdon and Neveu to the <a href="/wiki/Temple_Church" title="Temple Church">Temple Church</a> in London, which is shown to be a <a href="/wiki/Red_herring" title="Red herring">red herring</a>. Silas is freed by Remy Jean, who is revealed to be a follower of The Teacher as well. The two take Teabing hostage, and Silas, believing Remy to be The Teacher, holes up in an <a href="/wiki/Opus_Dei" title="Opus Dei">Opus Dei</a> safehouse. Remy is killed by the mysterious man after deceiving Silas. Silas is attacked by the police and, in the ensuing gunfire, accidentally shoots Bishop Manuel Aringarosa. In his grief, Silas dies in police-assisted suicide and Aringarosa is taken to the hospital, as well as being arrested by Fache for using him to chase Langdon.</p>
<p>Langdon is betrayed by Teabing, who is revealed to be the true Teacher. He escapes with the Cryptex, and attempts to find the next clue at <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Newton" title="Isaac Newton">Isaac Newton</a>'s tomb in <a href="/wiki/Westminster_Abbey" title="Westminster Abbey">Westminster Abbey</a>, as Newton was another member of the Priory. Langdon and Neveu catch up with him, and chase him into the nearby chapter house. Teabing explained that he wanted to find Mary Magdalene's remains to prove he was correct about the Holy Grail and threatens to shoot Sophie if Langdon does not open the cryptex. Langdon responds by throwing the cryptex into the air. Teabing catches, but fumbles it, and it hits the ground, shattering the internal vial of vinegar. Teabing becomes distraught, as he believes the vinegar has destroyed the papyrus within the still intact cryptex.</p>
<p>After Teabing is arrested, it is revealed that Langdon had cracked the code ('<a href="/wiki/Apple" title="Apple">Apple</a>', a <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Newton#Apple_Incident" title="Isaac Newton">reference to Newton</a>) and removed the clue from the cryptex before throwing it. Using the clue, they travel to <a href="/wiki/Rosslyn_Chapel" title="Rosslyn Chapel">Rosslyn Chapel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> where Magdalene's remains had previously been hidden. The remains had since been moved, but they meet other members of the secret organization that protected her. It is revealed that Sophie is actually Magdalene's descendant and therefore is the current living descendant of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesus_Christ" title="Jesus Christ">Jesus Christ</a>. They vow to keep her safe. Langdon and Sophie part ways shortly after.</p>
<p>Back in Paris, Langdon accidentally cuts himself while shaving and the line of blood on the sink reminds him of the <a href="/wiki/Rose_Line" title="Rose Line">Rose Line</a>, which sparks an epiphany. He goes out into the streets of Paris and follows the Rose Line and finds the location of the Holy Grail, buried under the <a href="/wiki/Louvre_Pyramid" title="Louvre Pyramid">pyramid in the Louvre</a>. Langdon then kneels above Mary Magdalene's tomb as the Knights Templar did before him.</p>

<p>The film rights were purchased from Dan Brown for $6,000,000. Filming had been scheduled to start in May 2005; however, some delays caused filming to begin on June 30, 2005.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Permission to film on the premises was granted to the film by the Louvre (although, since the crew was not permitted to shine light on the <a href="/wiki/Mona_Lisa" title="Mona Lisa">Mona Lisa</a>, a replica was used instead, while the film crew used the Mona Lisa's chamber as a storage room). <a href="/wiki/Westminster_Abbey" title="Westminster Abbey">Westminster Abbey</a> denied the use of its premises, as did <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint-Sulpice_(Paris)" title="Saint-Sulpice (Paris)">Saint-Sulpice</a>. The Westminster Abbey scenes were instead filmed at <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Cathedral" title="Lincoln Cathedral">Lincoln</a> and <a href="/wiki/Winchester_Cathedral" title="Winchester Cathedral">Winchester</a> cathedrals, which both belong to the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a>. (Westminster Abbey is a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Peculiar" title="Royal Peculiar">Royal Peculiar</a>, a church or chapel under direct jurisdiction of the monarch; whereas Saint-Sulpice is a Roman Catholic institution.)</p>
<p>Due to the denial of a location permit for Saint-Sulpice,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SulpicePermit_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-SulpicePermit-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> the entire scene had to be recreated virtually by post-production company Rainmaker U.K. and though the set had been partially built, the co-ordinates were centimeters out from what the compositors had expected and so the entire process was extremely difficult to complete.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SulpiceSecret_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-SulpiceSecret-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Lincoln Cathedral reportedly received £100,000 in exchange for the right to film there, with filming there occurring between August 15 and 19, 2005, mainly within the cloisters of the cathedral. The cathedral's bell, which strikes the hour, was silent for the first time since <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> during that time. Although it remained a closed set, protesters led by a 61-year-old woman named Sister Mary Michael demonstrated against the filming. Sister Mary Michael spent 12 hours praying on her knees outside the cathedral in protest against what she saw as the blasphemous use of a holy place to film a book containing <a href="/wiki/Heresy" title="Heresy">heresy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NunProtest_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-NunProtest-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Mary Michael claimed to be a Roman Catholic nun, however the Catholic Church said she that while she was once connected to the Carmelites she left, and called her a "maverick" and a "one-women order" and stated that she is not "canonically recognized" even if she does do "good works".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Winchester Cathedral answered criticism by using its location fee to fund an exhibition, lecture series and campaign to debunk the book.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WinchesterFee_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-WinchesterFee-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The scenes for the Pope's summer residence, <a href="/wiki/Papal_Palace_of_Castel_Gandolfo" title="Papal Palace of Castel Gandolfo">Castel Gandolfo</a> were filmed on location at <a href="/wiki/Belvoir_Castle" title="Belvoir Castle">Belvoir Castle</a> in Leicestershire, England.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Shoreham_Airport" title="Shoreham Airport">Shoreham Airport</a> in West Sussex, England, was used as a filming location, with its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Art-deco" title="Art-deco">art-deco</a> terminal building utilised in a night shoot for the scenes at 'Le Bourget' Airport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Airport_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Airport-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Filming also took place elsewhere in England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LocationsUK_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-LocationsUK-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Locations included <a href="/wiki/Fairfield_Halls" title="Fairfield Halls">Fairfield Halls</a> (<a href="/wiki/Croydon" title="Croydon">Croydon</a>); the <a href="/wiki/Temple_Church" title="Temple Church">Temple Church</a> (London); <a href="/wiki/Burghley_House" title="Burghley House">Burghley House</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire" title="Lincolnshire">Lincolnshire</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Rosslyn_Chapel" title="Rosslyn Chapel">Rosslyn Chapel</a> and Rosslyn Castle (<a href="/wiki/Midlothian" title="Midlothian">Midlothian</a>, Scotland) make an appearance at the final of the film.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Studio_shoots">Studio shoots</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Studio shoots">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The filmmakers shot many of the internal scenes at <a href="/wiki/Pinewood_Studios" title="Pinewood Studios">Pinewood Studios</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PinewoodOne_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-PinewoodOne-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> the opening sequence in the cavernous <a href="/wiki/007_Stage" title="007 Stage">007 Stage</a> at Pinewood Shepperton, where the interior of the Louvre was recreated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LouvreRecreated_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-LouvreRecreated-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> In this sequence, Tom Hanks' character is taken by French police to the Louvre, where a dead body has been discovered. <a href="/wiki/David_White_(makeup_artist)" title="David White (makeup artist)">David White</a> of Altered States FX, a prosthetics and special makeup effects company, was tasked with creating a naked photorealistic silicone body for the scene. Lighting effects were utilized to obscure the body's genitalia, a technique also used on television programmes such as <i><a href="/wiki/NCIS_(TV_series)" title="NCIS (TV series)">NCIS</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCIS_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-NCIS-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Pinewood's state-of-the-art <a href="/wiki/Underwater_Stage" title="Underwater Stage">Underwater Stage</a> was used to film underwater sequences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PinewoodTwo_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-PinewoodTwo-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> The stage opened in 2005 after four years of planning and development. The water in the tank is filtered using an ultraviolet system which creates crystal clear water, and the water is maintained at 30 °C (86 °F) to create a comfortable environment to work in for both cast and crew.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Underwater_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Underwater-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Alternate versions of <a href="/wiki/Paul_Bettany" title="Paul Bettany">Paul Bettany</a>'s nude flagellation scenes were shot, in which he wears a black loincloth. Clips of these versions appear in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_(U.S._TV_channel)" title="History (U.S. TV channel)">History Channel</a>'s "Opus Dei Unveiled" documentary, aired in summer 2006.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Catholic_and_other_reactions">Catholic and other reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Catholic and other reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Vatican">The Vatican</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The Vatican">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At a conference on April 28, 2006, the secretary of the <a href="/wiki/Congregation_for_the_Doctrine_of_the_Faith" title="Congregation for the Doctrine of the Faith">Congregation for the Doctrine of the Faith</a>, a Vatican <a href="/wiki/Congregation_(Roman_Curia)" title="Congregation (Roman Curia)">curial</a> department, Archbishop <a href="/wiki/Angelo_Amato" title="Angelo Amato">Angelo Amato</a> specifically called for a boycott of the film; he said the movie is "full of <a class="extiw" href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/calumny" title="wiktionary:calumny">calumnies</a>, offences, and historical and theological errors."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BoycottCWN_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-BoycottCWN-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Cardinal <a href="/wiki/Francis_Arinze" title="Francis Arinze">Francis Arinze</a>, in a documentary called <i>The Da Vinci Code: A Masterful Deception</i>, urged unspecified legal action against the makers of the film. He was formerly Prefect of the <a href="/wiki/Congregation_for_Divine_Worship_and_the_Discipline_of_the_Sacraments" title="Congregation for Divine Worship and the Discipline of the Sacraments">Congregation for Divine Worship and the Discipline of the Sacraments</a> in the Vatican.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Opus_Dei">Opus Dei</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Opus Dei">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Stating that it does not intend to organize any boycotts, <a href="/wiki/Opus_Dei" title="Opus Dei">Opus Dei</a> (the Catholic organization that is featured prominently in the novel and the film) released a statement on February 14, 2006, asking <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Pictures" title="Sony Pictures">Sony Pictures</a> to consider editing the soon-to-be-released film so that it would not contain references that it felt might be hurtful to Catholics. The statement also said Brown's book offers a "deformed"<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> image of the church and that Opus Dei will use the opportunity of the movie's release to educate about the church.</p>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a>, April 16, 2006, Opus Dei published an open letter by the Japanese Information Office of Opus Dei mildly proposing that Sony Pictures consider including a <a href="/wiki/Disclaimer" title="Disclaimer">disclaimer</a> on the film adaptation as a "sign of respect towards the figure of Jesus Christ, the history of the Church, and the religious beliefs of viewers."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The organization also encouraged the studio to clearly label the movie as fictitious "and that any resemblance to reality is pure coincidence."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>According to a statement by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_S%C3%A1nchez_Hurtado&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Manuel Sánchez Hurtado (page does not exist)">Manuel Sánchez Hurtado</a>, Opus Dei Press Office Rome,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hurtado_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hurtado-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> in contrast to Sony Corporation's published "Code of Conduct" the company has announced that the film will not include such a disclaimer.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="American_Catholic_bishops">American Catholic bishops</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: American Catholic bishops">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Catholic_Communication_Campaign" title="Catholic Communication Campaign">U.S. Catholic bishops</a> launched a website, JesusDecoded.com, refuting the key claims in the novel that were about to be brought to the screen. The bishops are concerned about errors and serious misstatements in <i>The Da Vinci Code</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> The film has also been rated morally offensive – by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Conference_of_Catholic_Bishops%27_Office_for_Film_and_Broadcasting" title="United States Conference of Catholic Bishops' Office for Film and Broadcasting">United States Conference of Catholic Bishops' Office for Film and Broadcasting</a>, which denounced its depiction of both the Jesus-Mary Magdalene relationship and that of Opus Dei as "deeply abhorrent."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Peru">Peru</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Peru">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Peruvian_Episcopal_Conference" title="Peruvian Episcopal Conference">Peruvian Episcopal Conference</a> (CEP) declared the movie — and the book — as part of a "systematic attack on the Catholic Church".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Peruvian_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Peruvian-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Furthermore, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Lima" title="Archbishop of Lima">Archbishop of Lima</a>, the controversial Cardinal and member of Opus Dei <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juan_Luis_Cipriani" title="Juan Luis Cipriani">Juan Luis Cipriani</a>, urged his community not to see the film: "If someone goes (to see the movie), they are giving money to those who hurt the faith. It's not a problem of fiction; if truth is not respected, what arises we could call white glove terrorism."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cipriani_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cipriani-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="NOAH">NOAH</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: NOAH">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/National_Organization_for_Albinism_and_Hypopigmentation" title="National Organization for Albinism and Hypopigmentation">National Organization for Albinism and Hypopigmentation</a> (NOAH) expressed concern about Silas' character giving people with albinism a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evil_albino" title="Evil albino">bad name</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> However, the filmmakers did not change his appearance.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Censorship">Censorship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Censorship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although <i>The Da Vinci Code</i> was passed by Chinese censors, it was abruptly removed by authorities from public view in mainland China, after "a remarkable run in China, grossing over $13 million",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChinaCNN_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-ChinaCNN-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> due to protests by Chinese Catholic groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Egypt">Egypt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Egypt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Both the book and the film were banned in Egypt due to pressure from <a href="/wiki/Coptic_Orthodox_Church_of_Alexandria" title="Coptic Orthodox Church of Alexandria">Coptic Christians</a>. Some Muslims compared the film to the <a href="/wiki/Jyllands-Posten_Muhammad_cartoons_controversy" title="Jyllands-Posten Muhammad cartoons controversy">Danish cartoons that had caused a controversy earlier that year</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> Hafez Abu Saeda, of the <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_Organization_for_Human_Rights" title="Egyptian Organization for Human Rights">Egyptian Organization for Human Rights</a> stated that "This violates freedom of thought and belief ... This is fiction. It's art and it should be regarded as art."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>The biggest cinema in the <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">Faroe Islands</a>, Havnar Bio, decided to boycott the film, effectively blocking it from the other smaller cinemas, who rely on second-hand films from this source, because it seems to be blasphemous in their point of view. Havnar Bio is privately owned, and their decision is based on their own private opinion.</p>
<p>A private initiative by the individual Herluf Sørensen has arranged the movie to be played, despite the boycott by Havnar Bio. The movie opened at the <a href="/wiki/Nordic_House_in_the_Faroe_Islands" title="Nordic House in the Faroe Islands">Nordic House in the Faroe Islands</a> on the June 5, 2006.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There was a huge outcry in many states by the Christian minorities to ban the film from screening in India for the perceived anti-Christian message. Possibly the largest reaction occurred in <a href="/wiki/Kolkata" title="Kolkata">Kolkata</a> where a mob of bike-riding young men from Rippon Street, a predominantly Christian neighbourhood broke the glass of the front door display of <a href="/wiki/Oxford_Bookstore" title="Oxford Bookstore">Oxford Bookstore</a>, seized copies of the book and threw them into the pavement and the streets, waving hand-painted signs and shouting slogans which called for a ban on the release of the film. However, police dispersed the mob within a short while and the film released on the planned date without further incident. After the release, it was viewed by a large number of Christians from Kolkata, who seemed satisfied with the nature of the depiction.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Ultimately, the movie was allowed to be released without any cuts but with an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_ratings" title="Film ratings">A</a> (Adults Only) certification from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Board_for_Film_Certification" title="Central Board for Film Certification">Central Board for Film Certification</a> and a 15-second disclaimer added at the end stating that the movie was purely a work of fiction.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The film has been banned in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Punjab_(India)" title="Punjab (India)">Punjab</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goa" title="Goa">Goa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nagaland" title="Nagaland">Nagaland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meghalaya" title="Meghalaya">Meghalaya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maharashtra" title="Maharashtra">Maharashtra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tamil_Nadu" title="Tamil Nadu">Tamil Nadu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh" title="Andhra Pradesh">Andhra Pradesh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IndiaSIFY_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-IndiaSIFY-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IndiaHindu_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-IndiaHindu-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh_High_Court" title="Andhra Pradesh High Court">Andhra Pradesh High Court</a> subsequently reversed the State Government's order banning the screening of the film in the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AndhraPradesh_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-AndhraPradesh-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Central_Board_of_Film_Certification" title="Central Board of Film Certification">Indian Censor Board</a>, however, had cleared the movie for release on Friday, June 2. The <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_India" title="Supreme Court of India">Supreme Court of India</a> also rejected petitions calling for a ban on the film, saying the plot which suggested Jesus was married was fictional and not offensive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IndiaSupremeCourt_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-IndiaSupremeCourt-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jordan">Jordan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Jordan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film was banned in Jordan where authorities said the film "tarnishes the memory of Christian and Islamic figures and contradicts the truth as written in the Bible and the Koran about Jesus".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lebanon">Lebanon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Lebanon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film was banned in Lebanon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pakistan">Pakistan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Pakistan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pakistan banned <i>The Da Vinci Code</i> for showing what officials called blasphemous material about Jesus. Christian groups, along with the <a href="/wiki/Muttahida_Majlis-e-Amal" title="Muttahida Majlis-e-Amal">Muttahida Majlis-e-Amal</a> held protests against the film calling for a global ban.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PakistanBan1_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-PakistanBan1-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Philippines">Philippines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Philippines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Da_Vinci_Code_in_the_Philippines" title="The Da Vinci Code in the Philippines">The Da Vinci Code in the Philippines</a></div>
<p>The Philippine Alliance Against Pornography (PAAP) appealed to then <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Philippines" title="President of the Philippines">Philippine President</a> <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Macapagal-Arroyo" title="Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo">Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo</a> to stop the showing of <i>The Da Vinci Code</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. They branded the film as "the most <a href="/wiki/Pornography" title="Pornography">pornographic</a> and blasphemous film in history"<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and also requested the help of <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_XVI" title="Pope Benedict XVI">Pope Benedict XVI</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catholic_Bishops_Conference_of_the_Philippines" title="Catholic Bishops Conference of the Philippines">Catholic Bishops Conference of the Philippines</a> (CBCP) and other religious groups to stop the showing of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PAAP_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-PAAP-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>However, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cecille_Guidote_Alvarez&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cecille Guidote Alvarez (page does not exist)">Cecille Guidote Alvarez</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philippine_Presidential_Office" title="Philippine Presidential Office">Philippine Presidential Adviser on Culture and the Arts</a>, said the Philippine government would not interfere in the controversy about the film and leaves the decision to the <a href="/wiki/Movie_and_Television_Review_and_Classification_Board" title="Movie and Television Review and Classification Board">Movie and Television Review and Classification Board</a>'s (MTRCB) rating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTRCB_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTRCB-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> Eventually, MTRCB decided to give <i>The Da Vinci Code</i> an R-18 rating (restricted to those 18 years of age and above) despite PAAP's opposition for showing it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RatingInq_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-RatingInq-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Samoa">Samoa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Samoa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film was banned outright in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independent_State_of_Samoa" title="Independent State of Samoa">Independent State of Samoa</a> after church leaders watching a pre-release showing filed a complaint with film censors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Samoa_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Samoa-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Singapore">Singapore</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Singapore">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Singapore, the <a href="/wiki/Media_Development_Authority" title="Media Development Authority">Media Development Authority</a>, passed the unedited version of the movie, albeit with an <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=NC-16&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="NC-16 (page does not exist)">NC-16</a> rating, a restriction for children below the age of 16.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solomon_Islands">Solomon Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Solomon Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a> <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_Solomon_Islands" title="Prime Minister of the Solomon Islands">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Manasseh_Sogavare" title="Manasseh Sogavare">Manasseh Sogavare</a> said he would seek to have the film banned in his country, as it might threaten the Solomons' predominantly Christian faith:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>We profess Christian religion in the country, and that film that depicts some thoughts about this person called Jesus Christ that Christians adore as not only as a good man, but was himself God, and such a film basically undermines the very roots of Christianity in Solomon Islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SolomonIslands_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-SolomonIslands-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sri_Lanka">Sri Lanka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Sri Lanka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sri Lanka is also one of the countries that banned the film from being released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SriLanka_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-SriLanka-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> It was banned by presidential order of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mahinda_Rajapakse" title="Mahinda Rajapakse">Mahinda Rajapakse</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Thailand">Thailand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Thailand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Christian groups in this mostly Buddhist country protested the film and called for it to be banned. On May 16, 2006, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thai_Censorship_Committee&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thai Censorship Committee (page does not exist)">Thai Censorship Committee</a> issued a ruling that the film would be shown, but that the last 10 minutes would be cut. Also, some Thai subtitles were to be edited to change their meaning and passages from the Bible would also be quoted at the beginning and end of the film.</p>
<p>However, the following day, Sony Pictures appealed the ruling, saying it would pull the film if the decision to cut it was not reversed. The censorship panel then voted 6–5 that the film could be shown uncut, but that a disclaimer would precede and follow the film, saying it was a work of fiction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ThailandOne_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-ThailandOne-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ThailandTwo_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-ThailandTwo-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> This last-minute decision caused the premiere to be delayed or cancelled in some provincial theatres as the updated film reels were shipped from <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast.2Fcrew_response">Cast/crew response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Cast/crew response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tom Hanks told the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evening_Standard" title="Evening Standard">Evening Standard</a></i> that those involved with the movie "always knew there would be a segment of society that would not want this movie to be shown. But the story we tell is loaded with all sorts of <a class="extiw" href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/hooey" title="wiktionary:hooey">hooey</a> and fun kind of scavenger-hunt-type nonsense."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TeodorczukGoodridgeStandard_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-TeodorczukGoodridgeStandard-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> He said it is a mistake "to take any sort of movie at face value, particularly a huge-budget motion picture like this."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TeodorczukGoodridgeStandard_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-TeodorczukGoodridgeStandard-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> He also stated at the Cannes Film Festival that he and his wife saw no contradiction between their faith and the film, as "My heritage, and that of my wife, suggests that our sins have been taken away, not our brains."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HigginsCannesGuardian_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-HigginsCannesGuardian-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Also at Cannes, <a href="/wiki/Ian_McKellen" title="Ian McKellen">Ian McKellen</a> was quoted as saying "While I was reading the book I believed it entirely. Clever Dan Brown twisted my mind convincingly. But when I put it down I thought, 'What a load of [pause] potential <a href="/wiki/Codswallop" title="Codswallop">codswallop</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HigginsCannesGuardian_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-HigginsCannesGuardian-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup> During a May 17, 2006 interview on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Today_(NBC_program)" title="Today (NBC program)">The Today Show</a></i> with the <i>Da Vinci Code</i> cast and director, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Lauer" title="Matt Lauer">Matt Lauer</a> posed a question to the group about how they would have felt if the film had borne a prominent disclaimer that it is a work of fiction, as some religious groups wanted. (Some high-ranking <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Vatican</a> cabinet members had called for a boycott of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pullella_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pullella-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup>) McKellen<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McKellenMTV_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-McKellenMTV-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (April 2012)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup> responded, "I've often thought the Bible should have a disclaimer in the front saying 'This is fiction.' I mean, walking on water? It takes. . . an act of faith. And I have faith in this movie—not that it's true, not that it's factual, but that it's a jolly good story." He continued, "And I think audiences are clever enough and bright enough to separate out fact and fiction, and discuss the thing when they've seen it."</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions_to_the_film">Reactions to the film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Reactions to the film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cannes_Film_Festival">Cannes Film Festival</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Cannes Film Festival">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the Associated Press, during a preview for movie critics in Cannes, a line spoken by Tom Hanks "drew prolonged laughter and some catcalls". Nearing the end of the screening, "there were a few whistles and hisses, and there was none of the scattered applause even bad movies sometimes receive at Cannes."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CannesMSNBC_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-CannesMSNBC-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Protests">Protests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Protests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There have been protesters at several movie theaters across the United States on opening weekend protesting the themes of the film, citing it as <a href="/wiki/Blasphemy" title="Blasphemy">blasphemy</a> and claiming that it shames both the Catholic Church, and Jesus Christ himself. More than 200 protesters also turned out in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athens,_Greece" title="Athens, Greece">Athens, Greece</a> to protest the film's release shortly before opening day. In Manila the movie was banned from all theaters and the set by the local MTRCB as an R18 movie for the Philippines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GreekOrthodoxMAC_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-GreekOrthodoxMAC-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>, protesters also showed up at a special screening of the film the day before its widespread release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pittsburgh_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pittsburgh-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> Protests also occurred at the filming sites, but only a monk and a nun stood in a quiet protest at the Cannes premiere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HigginsCannesGuardian_41-2"><a href="#cite_note-HigginsCannesGuardian-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup> In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chennai,_India" title="Chennai, India">Chennai, India</a>, the film was banned for a two-month period to appease local Christian and Muslim groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chennai_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chennai-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Da Vinci Code</i> received mixed to negative reviews from film critics. The film currently garners a "rotten" 25% positivity rating on the film review aggregate website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, based on a sample of 220 reviews and an average rating of 4.8/10. The critics' consensus as gathered by Rotten Tomatoes is: "What makes Dan Brown's novel a best seller is evidently not present in this dull and bloated movie adaptation of <i>The Da Vinci Code</i>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CriticsRT_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-CriticsRT-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> The film was poorly received at the Cannes Film Festival, where it debuted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CannesMSNBC_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-CannesMSNBC-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Michael_Medved" title="Michael Medved">Michael Medved</a> was negative about the film, citing it as an attack on religion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Medved_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-Medved-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Lane" title="Anthony Lane">Anthony Lane</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Yorker" title="The New Yorker">The New Yorker</a></i> addressed the concerns of Catholics in his film review, stating of the film, "It is self-evident, spirit-lowering tripe that could not conceivably cause a single member of the flock to turn aside from the faith."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lane_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lane-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In his <i>Movie Guide</i>, <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Maltin" title="Leonard Maltin">Leonard Maltin</a> calls the film "a letdown in every respect."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maltin_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-Maltin-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Director Ron Howard noted that the overwhelmingly negative reviews were "frustrating" to him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Howard_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-Howard-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Conversely, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Sun-Times" title="Chicago Sun-Times">Chicago Sun-Times</a></i> (who had spoken very negatively of the novel) gave the film three out of four stars, stating, "The movie works; it's involving, intriguing and constantly seems on the edge of startling revelations." Of the storyline he also comments, "Yes, the plot is absurd, but then most movie plots are absurd. That's what we pay to see."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ebert_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ebert-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup> Ebert and co-host <a href="/wiki/Richard_Roeper" title="Richard Roeper">Richard Roeper</a> both liked it and criticized the protests of the film on their <a href="/wiki/At_the_Movies_(U.S._TV_series)" title="At the Movies (U.S. TV series)">show</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Lawrence Toppman of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Charlotte_Observer" title="The Charlotte Observer">The Charlotte Observer</a></i>, who also liked the film, gave it three and a half out of four stars and noted, "unlike most Hollywood blockbusters, this one assumes audience members will be smart."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Toppman_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-Toppman-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Although many critics gave mostly negative reviews of the film, critics of both sides acknowledged and praised the strong performances of Ian McKellen as well as Paul Bettany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MoviesOnline_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-MoviesOnline-55"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On the "Worst Movies of 2006" episode of the television show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/At_the_Movies_with_Ebert_%26_Roeper" title="At the Movies with Ebert &amp; Roeper">Ebert &amp; Roeper</a></i> (January 13, 2007), guest critic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michael_Phillips_(theatre_critic)" title="Michael Phillips (theatre critic)">Michael Phillips</a> (sitting in for the recovering Roger Ebert) listed the film at <abbr title="Number">No.</abbr> 2.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The film earned a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Razzie_Award" title="Razzie Award">Razzie Award</a> nomination for Ron Howard as <a href="/wiki/Golden_Raspberry_Award_for_Worst_Director" title="Golden Raspberry Award for Worst Director">Worst Director</a>, but lost to <a href="/wiki/M._Night_Shyamalan" title="M. Night Shyamalan">M. Night Shyamalan</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Lady_in_the_Water" title="Lady in the Water">Lady in the Water</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office_response">Box office response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Box office response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Opening_weekend">Opening weekend</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: Opening weekend">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Despite the protests and poor pre-release reviews, the film still opened with an estimated $31 million in box office sales on its opening day, averaging $7764 per screen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EstimatedCNN_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-EstimatedCNN-56"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup> During its opening weekend, moviegoers spent an estimated $77 million in America, and $224 million worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bom_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-bom-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>The Da Vinci Code</i> is the best domestic opening for both Tom Hanks and Ron Howard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HotTicket_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-HotTicket-57"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>It also enjoyed the third biggest opening weekend for that year (after <i><a href="/wiki/Pirates_of_the_Caribbean:_Dead_Man%27s_Chest" title="Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest">Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men:_The_Last_Stand" title="X-Men: The Last Stand">X-Men: The Last Stand</a></i>, and the second biggest worldwide opening weekend ever, just behind 2005's <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Episode_III:_Revenge_of_the_Sith" title="Star Wars Episode III: Revenge of the Sith">Star Wars Episode III: Revenge of the Sith</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OpeningWashPost_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-OpeningWashPost-58"><span>[</span>58<span>]</span></a></sup>) This has led some critics, particularly in the UK, to moot the idea of the 'critic-proof film'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CriticProof_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-CriticProof-59"><span>[</span>59<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Ranking_and_gross">Ranking and gross</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=36" title="Edit section: Ranking and gross">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Number 1 movie at the USA box office during its first week grossing more than $111 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bomwe_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-bomwe-60"><span>[</span>60<span>]</span></a></sup> Fifth highest gross of 2006 in the USA, and grossed $758 million worldwide in 2006 – the second highest of 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bom_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-bom-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Its worldwide total made it the 51st highest grossing film, and the highest grossing film in the franchise.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>On June 20, 2006, it became only the second movie of the year to pass the $200 million mark in the USA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PassBOM_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-PassBOM-61"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: Sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Angels_%26_Demons_(film)" title="Angels &amp; Demons (film)">Angels &amp; Demons (film)</a></div>
<p>Screenwriter <a href="/wiki/Akiva_Goldsman" title="Akiva Goldsman">Akiva Goldsman</a>, with the help of <i><a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park" title="Jurassic Park">Jurassic Park</a></i> helmer <a href="/wiki/David_Koepp" title="David Koepp">David Koepp</a>, adapted <i><a href="/wiki/Angels_%26_Demons" title="Angels &amp; Demons">Angels &amp; Demons</a></i> (a Dan Brown novel published before <i>The Da Vinci Code</i>) into a film script,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AngelsDemons_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-AngelsDemons-62"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup> which was also directed by Ron Howard. Chronologically, the book takes place before <i>The Da Vinci Code</i>. However, the filmmakers re-tooled it as a sequel. Tom Hanks reprises his role as Robert Langdon in the film, which was released in May 2009 to moderate (but generally better) reviews.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="DVD">DVD</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Da_Vinci_Code_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=38" title="Edit section: DVD">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film was released on DVD on November 14, 2006<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-widescreen_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-widescreen-63"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup> in three editions:</p>
<ol>
