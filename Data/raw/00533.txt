<p><i><b>GHV2</b></i> (an abbreviation of <i>Greatest Hits Volume 2</i>) is the second greatest hits compilation album by American singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>, released on November 12, 2001, by <a href="/wiki/Maverick_Records" title="Maverick Records">Maverick Records</a>. It contains a collection of songs from 1992 to 2001, selected by Madonna who mentioned that she only wanted "<i>songs that I could listen to five times in a row"</i> included on it. As a result, the album includes several singles which were not big hits, like "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bedtime_Story" title="Bedtime Story">Bedtime Story</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Human_Nature_(Madonna_song)" title="Human Nature (Madonna song)">Human Nature</a>", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/What_It_Feels_like_for_a_Girl" title="What It Feels like for a Girl">What It Feels like for a Girl</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Drowned_World/Substitute_for_Love" title="Drowned World/Substitute for Love">Drowned World/Substitute for Love</a>", and omits other releases like "<a href="/wiki/American_Pie_(song)" title="American Pie (song)">American Pie</a>", "<a href="/wiki/I%27ll_Remember" title="I'll Remember">I'll Remember</a>", "<a href="/wiki/You%27ll_See" title="You'll See">You'll See</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/This_Used_to_Be_My_Playground" title="This Used to Be My Playground">This Used to Be My Playground</a>". It was a follow-up to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Immaculate_Collection" title="The Immaculate Collection">The Immaculate Collection</a></i> (1990) and was released to coincide with the live home video, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drowned_World_Tour_2001_(video)" title="Drowned World Tour 2001 (video)">Drowned World Tour 2001</a></i>. The album did not contain any new recordings, but a promotional single "GHV2 Megamix" was released to promote it with mixes by <a href="/wiki/Thunderpuss" title="Thunderpuss">Thunderpuss</a>, John Rocks &amp; Mac Quayle and Tracy Young.</p>
<p>The album was originally titled <i>Greatest Hits: The Second Coming</i> but was changed prior to its release as Madonna wanted it to have a title which was memorable. The RIAA certified it Gold and Platinum on December 12, 2001, denoting one million shipments in the United States. Worldwide, the album has sold more than seven million units.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> A promotional only remix album, <i>GHV2 Remixed: The Best of 1991–2001</i>, was released to accompany it.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GHV2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album was originally titled <i>Greatest Hits: The Second Coming</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> but Madonna decided to name it change the name to <i>GHV2</i> just before it was released as "it's a title you will remember" and also because of "laziness", due to the fact that she had just finished the <a href="/wiki/Drowned_World_Tour" title="Drowned World Tour">Drowned World Tour</a> and was about to begin filming <i><a href="/wiki/Swept_Away_(2002_film)" title="Swept Away (2002 film)">Swept Away</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Other titles considered were <i>Hit Lady – Greatest Hits Volume 2</i> and <i>The Immaculate Collection Part Deux</i>. The compilation was released on the eleventh anniversary of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Immaculate_Collection" title="The Immaculate Collection">The Immaculate Collection</a></i>, featuring a selection of hits ranging from "<a href="/wiki/Erotica_(song)" title="Erotica (song)">Erotica</a>" (1992) to "<a href="/wiki/What_It_Feels_Like_for_a_Girl" title="What It Feels Like for a Girl">What It Feels Like for a Girl</a>" (2001). Madonna felt that because it was a "greatest hits", it should only contain previously released hit songs.</p>
<p>In an interview with <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jo_Whiley" title="Jo Whiley">Jo Whiley</a>, Madonna said about the selection of the <i>GHV2</i> tracks, "<i>I only wanted songs that I could listen to five times in a row."</i> Like <i><a href="/wiki/The_Immaculate_Collection" title="The Immaculate Collection">The Immaculate Collection</a></i>, there were many other popular singles excluded. "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Pie_(Madonna_version)" title="American Pie (Madonna version)">American Pie</a>", which was a big hit, was excluded as a "punishment", because Madonna regretted putting it on <i>Music</i>. "It was something a certain record company executive twisted my arm into doing, but it didn't belong on the album so now it's being punished," she said.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The cover picture is from a 2001 photo shoot for the <a href="/wiki/Drowned_World_Tour" title="Drowned World Tour">Drowned World Tour</a>, by <a href="/wiki/Regan_Cameron" title="Regan Cameron">Regan Cameron</a>. The logo in the right corner says "Madonna", and "GHV2". The Japanese lettering (モヂジラミミヂ) is the result of typing the letters 'M-A-D-O-N-N-A' on an English keyboard but with the keys re-mapped to their positions on a Japanese 'kana' keyboard. It can be pronounced as "Mo-Ji-Ji-Ra-Mi-Mi-Ji" and does not mean anything in Japanese. The proper translation for Madonna is: マドンナ.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> "GHV2" can also be seen on the cover picture on Madonna's eye. Kevin Reagan contributed artwork to the inner sleeve. A special edition of <i>GHV2</i>, featuring the same track listing, but some additional artwork, was released in December 2001. Hidden in the inner sleeve album artwork of both editions of GHV2 is a quote from "Human Nature": <i>"Express Yourself, Don't Repress Yourself."</i></p>

<p>The compilation received generally positive reviews. <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a> writer <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Thomas_Erlewine" title="Stephen Thomas Erlewine">Stephen Thomas Erlewine</a> said "During the '90s, Madonna was a true album artist, even as she was making singles as tremendous as "Take a Bow," "Deeper and Deeper," "Ray of Light," "Don't Tell Me," and the non-LP "Beautiful Stranger". Taken on their own, most of these are still pretty tremendous, but tossed together on <i>GHV2</i>, the end result is less than the sum of its parts, even if this is a good way to get all of Madge's '90s hits at once."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Christgau" title="Robert Christgau">Robert Christgau</a> said that "she gleans goodies from the overrated <i>Bedtime Stories</i> and <i>Ray of Light</i>, mixes in the glorious soundtrack-only "Beautiful Stranger" and the dismal soundtrack-only "Don't Cry for Me Argentina," and hands it all over to Mirwais for sonic tweaking I'm not interested enough to pin down", while calling it as "an essential package."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-christgau_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-christgau-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/PopMatters" title="PopMatters">PopMatters</a> said "Chronological sequencing would have made it easier to follow the course of Madonna’s musical evolution. But would these changes have made <i>GHV2</i> a significantly better album? Probably not. As it stands, it’s the best summary of Madonna’s second decade as a performer we’re going to get—at least until a massive box set appears."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pop_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-pop-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">Promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GHV2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Madonna made an appearance at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turner_prize#2001" title="Turner prize">2001 Turner Prize</a> award ceremony and mentioned that she "had a new record out called <i>GHV2</i>". She gave the award to <a href="/wiki/Martin_Creed" title="Martin Creed">Martin Creed</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> and gave a controversial speech and said, "At a time when political correctness is valued over honesty I would also like to say right on motherfuckers!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> This was on live TV before the 9 p.m. "watershed", and an attempt to "bleep" it out was too late. Channel 4 were subsequently given an official rebuke by the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Television_Commission" title="Independent Television Commission">Independent Television Commission</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001, six and five track CD samplers were released to radio stations to promote the release of <i>GHV2</i>. The six-track radio samplers consisted of "<a href="/wiki/Erotica_(song)" title="Erotica (song)">Erotica</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Bedtime_Story_(Madonna_song)" title="Bedtime Story (Madonna song)">Bedtime Story</a>", "<a href="/wiki/The_Power_of_Good-Bye" title="The Power of Good-Bye">The Power of Good-Bye</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Drowned_World/Substitute_for_Love" title="Drowned World/Substitute for Love">Drowned World/Substitute for Love</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Music_(Madonna_song)" title="Music (Madonna song)">Music</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Tell_Me_(Madonna_song)" title="Don't Tell Me (Madonna song)">Don't Tell Me</a>", while the five-track radio samplers had "<a href="/wiki/Deeper_and_Deeper" title="Deeper and Deeper">Deeper and Deeper</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Human_Nature_(Madonna_song)" title="Human Nature (Madonna song)">Human Nature</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Secret_(Madonna_song)" title="Secret (Madonna song)">Secret</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Frozen_(Madonna_song)" title="Frozen (Madonna song)">Frozen</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Music_(Madonna_song)" title="Music (Madonna song)">Music</a>".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="GHV2_Megamix">GHV2 Megamix</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GHV2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: GHV2 Megamix">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2001, <a href="/wiki/Maverick_Records" title="Maverick Records">Maverick</a> released a promotional <a href="/wiki/Megamix" title="Megamix">megamix</a> to promote the album. It was never released commercially. The "Thunderpuss GHV2 Megamix" was the most common version used by radio stations and the version used in the music video. The songs featured (in chronological order) were: "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Tell_Me_(Madonna_song)" title="Don't Tell Me (Madonna song)">Don't Tell Me</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Erotica_(song)" title="Erotica (song)">Erotica</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Secret_(Madonna_song)" title="Secret (Madonna song)">Secret</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Frozen_(Madonna_song)" title="Frozen (Madonna song)">Frozen</a>", "<a href="/wiki/What_It_Feels_Like_for_a_Girl" title="What It Feels Like for a Girl">What It Feels Like for a Girl</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Take_a_Bow_(Madonna_song)" title="Take a Bow (Madonna song)">Take a Bow</a>" (these included on the Thunderpuss Club Mix Part I), "<a href="/wiki/Deeper_and_Deeper" title="Deeper and Deeper">Deeper and Deeper</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Music_(Madonna_song)" title="Music (Madonna song)">Music</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Ray_of_Light_(song)" title="Ray of Light (song)">Ray of Light</a>" (these included on the Thunderpuss Club Mix Part II). Other remixes were produced by Johnny Rocks &amp; Mac Quayle and Tracy Young.</p>
<p>The remix went to #5 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Play" title="Hot Dance Club Play">Hot Dance Club Play</a> chart in 2001 and charted under the title "Madonna Megamix". The original Thunderpuss version has some spoken words at the beginning of "Deeper and Deeper": this was a recorded message used on the early Internet to promote the album <i><a href="/wiki/Bedtime_Stories_(Madonna_album)" title="Bedtime Stories (Madonna album)">Bedtime Stories</a></i>. The "Thunderpuss GHV2 Megamix" video is compiled of images of live performances and 23 Madonna videos. It was directed by Dago Gonzalez of <a href="/wiki/Veneno" title="Veneno">Veneno Inc.</a></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="GHV2_Remixed:_The_Best_of_1991.E2.80.932001"><i>GHV2 Remixed: The Best of 1991–2001</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GHV2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: GHV2 Remixed: The Best of 1991–2001">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 2001, <a href="/wiki/Maverick_Records" title="Maverick Records">Maverick Records</a> also released a promotional album <i>GHV2 Remixed: The Best of 1991-2001</i>, featuring remixed versions of tracks from the greatest hits compilation <i>GHV2</i>. Like the promotional megamix single release, it was not made commercially available.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> This compilation contains a remix of every song from the album <i>GHV2</i>, except "Take a Bow", "Don't Cry for Me Argentina", and "The Power of Good-Bye". The album was released as a double CD and a triple 12" set. It contains songs remixed by <a href="/wiki/Victor_Calderone" title="Victor Calderone">Victor Calderone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Junior_Vasquez" title="Junior Vasquez">Junior Vasquez</a>, BT, Sasha, <a href="/wiki/Timo_Maas" title="Timo Maas">Timo Maas</a>, and Hex Hector.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Shep_Pettibone" title="Shep Pettibone">Shep Pettibone</a> was given writing credits for "<a href="/wiki/Secret_(Madonna_song)" title="Secret (Madonna song)">Secret</a>" for the first time in the liner notes. The collection contained two previously unavailable remixes; the "Luscious Dub Mix" of "Bedtime Story", produced by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Picchiotti" title="Mark Picchiotti">Mark Picchiotti</a> and Teri Bristol, previously appeared only on the "Bedtime Story: Chapter II" promotional 12" single that was released in 1995. The album also contains a previously unreleased remix of "What It Feels Like for a Girl" produced by Chris Staropoli.</p>
<p>The artwork for the collection contained a collage of small images of Madonna, also used in the liner notes for <i>GHV2</i>. The title includes the year <i>1991</i> but does not include any recording from that year, the first being the 1992 single "Erotica". The only releases in 1991 by Madonna were from the 1990 <i><a href="/wiki/The_Immaculate_Collection" title="The Immaculate Collection">The Immaculate Collection</a></i> greatest hits collection; "<a href="/wiki/Rescue_Me_(Madonna_song)" title="Rescue Me (Madonna song)">Rescue Me</a>" (February 26, 1991), "<a href="/wiki/Crazy_for_You_(Madonna_song)" title="Crazy for You (Madonna song)">Crazy for You (Remix)</a>" (February 18, 1991, UK release) and "<a href="/wiki/Holiday_(Madonna_song)" title="Holiday (Madonna song)">Holiday</a>"/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Holiday_Collection_(EP)" title="The Holiday Collection (EP)">The Holiday Collection</a> (June 4, 1991, UK release).</p>
