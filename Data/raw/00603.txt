<p><b>Göran Bror Benny Andersson</b> (<small>Swedish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Swedish" title="Help:IPA for Swedish">[ˈjœːɾan bɾuːɾ ˈbɛnʏ ˈandɛˈʂɔn]</a></span>) (born 16 December 1946), known professionally as <b>Benny Andersson</b>, is a Swedish musician, composer, former member of the Swedish musical group <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a> (1972–1982), and co-composer of the musicals <i><a href="/wiki/Chess_(musical)" title="Chess (musical)">Chess</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kristina_fr%C3%A5n_Duvem%C3%A5la" title="Kristina från Duvemåla">Kristina från Duvemåla</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia!" title="Mamma Mia!">Mamma Mia!</a></i>. As of 2011<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Benny_Andersson&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> he is active with his own band <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benny_Anderssons_Orkester" title="Benny Anderssons Orkester">Benny Anderssons Orkester</a> (BAO!), and was executive producer for the film version of the musical <i><a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia!_(film)" title="Mamma Mia! (film)">Mamma Mia!</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Andersson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andersson was born in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> to 34-year-old civil engineer Gösta Andersson and his 26-year-old Laila. His sister Eva-Lis Andersson followed in 1948.</p>
<p>Andersson's musical background comes from his father and grandfather; they both enjoyed playing the <a href="/wiki/Accordion" title="Accordion">accordion</a>, and at six, Benny got his own. Father Gösta and grandfather Efraim taught him Swedish <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk music</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Traditional_music" title="Traditional music">traditional music</a>, and the odd <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schlager" title="Schlager">schlager</a>. Benny recalls the first records he bought were "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Du_Bist_Musik_(Caterina_Valente_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Du Bist Musik (Caterina Valente song) (page does not exist)">Du Bist Musik</a>" by Italian schlager singer <a href="/wiki/Caterina_Valente" title="Caterina Valente">Caterina Valente</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Jailhouse_Rock_(song)" title="Jailhouse Rock (song)">Jailhouse Rock</a>". He was especially impressed by the flip side "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Treat_Me_Nice_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Treat Me Nice (song) (page does not exist)">Treat Me Nice</a>" as this featured a piano. This <a href="/wiki/Sm%C3%B6rg%C3%A5sbord" title="Smörgåsbord">smörgåsbord</a> of different kinds of music was to influence and follow him through the years.</p>
<p>When Andersson was ten he got his own piano, and taught himself to play. He left school aged 15 and began to perform at youth clubs. This is when he met his first girlfriend Christina Grönvall, with whom he had two children: Peter (born 1963) and Helen (born 1965).</p>
<p>In early 1964, Benny and Christina joined "Elverkets Spelmanslag" ("The Electricity Board Folk Music Group"), the name was a punning reference to their electric instruments. The repertoire was mainly instrumentals, one of his numbers was "<a href="/wiki/Baby_Elephant_Walk" title="Baby Elephant Walk">Baby Elephant Walk</a>", and he wrote his first songs.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Hep_Stars_years_.281964.E2.80.931969.29">The Hep Stars years (1964–1969)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Andersson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Hep Stars years (1964–1969)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In October 1964 he joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Hep_Stars" title="The Hep Stars">the Hep Stars</a> as keyboardist and they made a breakthrough in March 1965 with their hit "Cadillac", eventually becoming the most celebrated of the Swedish 1960s pop bands. Andersson consolidated his place as the band's keyboardist and musical driving force as well as a teen idol. The band performed mostly covers of international hits, but Andersson soon started writing his own material, and gave the band the classic hits "No Response", "Sunny Girl", "Wedding", "Consolation", "It's Nice To Be Back" and "She Will Love You" amongst others.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Before_ABBA_.281969.E2.80.931972.29">Before ABBA (1969–1972)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Andersson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Before ABBA (1969–1972)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andersson met <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_Ulvaeus" title="Björn Ulvaeus">Björn Ulvaeus</a> in June 1966, and the two started writing songs together, their first being "Isn't It Easy To Say", eventually recorded by The Hep Stars. He also had a fruitful songwriting collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Lasse_Berghagen" title="Lasse Berghagen">Lasse Berghagen</a>, with whom he wrote several songs and submitted "Hej, Clown" for the 1969 <a href="/wiki/Melodifestivalen" title="Melodifestivalen">Melodifestivalen</a> – the Swedish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Festival" title="Eurovision Song Festival">Eurovision Song Festival</a> finals. The song finished in second place. During this contest he met vocalist <a href="/wiki/Anni-Frid_Lyngstad" title="Anni-Frid Lyngstad">Anni-Frid Lyngstad</a>, and they soon became a couple. Around the same time his songwriting companion Ulvaeus met vocalist <a href="/wiki/Agnetha_F%C3%A4ltskog" title="Agnetha Fältskog">Agnetha Fältskog</a>.</p>
<p>The personal relationships and Andersson and Ulvaeus' songwriting collaboration has led quite naturally to the very close co-operation which the four friends had during the following years. Benny and Björn scored their first hits as songwriters in the spring of 1969: "<a href="/wiki/Ljuva_sextital" title="Ljuva sextital">Ljuva sextital</a>" (a hit with Brita Borg) and "Speleman" (a hit for The Hep Stars). As the two couples began supporting each other during recording sessions, the sound of the girls' voices convinced the songwriters to model their 'group' on the likes of <a href="/wiki/Middle_of_the_road_(music)" title="Middle of the road (music)">MOR</a> acts <a href="/wiki/Blue_Mink" title="Blue Mink">Blue Mink</a>, <a href="/wiki/Middle_of_the_Road_(band)" title="Middle of the Road (band)">Middle of the Road</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Sweet" title="The Sweet">the Sweet</a>. Thus, <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a> came to life.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_ABBA_years_.281972.E2.80.931983.29">The ABBA years (1972–1983)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Andersson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The ABBA years (1972–1983)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a></div>
<p>The foursome's breakthrough came with winning the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a> for Sweden with "<a href="/wiki/Waterloo_(ABBA_song)" title="Waterloo (ABBA song)">Waterloo</a>" on 6 April 1974. During the next eight years, Andersson (together with Ulvaeus) wrote music for and produced eight studio albums with ABBA. The group achieved great success globally and scored a chain of No. 1 hits.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="After_ABBA:_Chess.2C_Kristina_and_Mamma_Mia.21">After ABBA: <i>Chess</i>, <i>Kristina</i> and <i>Mamma Mia!</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Andersson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: After ABBA: Chess, Kristina and Mamma Mia!">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After ABBA Andersson continued writing music with Ulvaeus. Their first project was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stage_musical" title="Stage musical">stage musical</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Chess_(musical)" title="Chess (musical)">Chess</a></i>, written with <a href="/wiki/Tim_Rice" title="Tim Rice">Tim Rice</a>. The <i>Chess</i> concept album – with vocals by <a href="/wiki/Elaine_Paige" title="Elaine Paige">Elaine Paige</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Dickson" title="Barbara Dickson">Barbara Dickson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Murray_Head" title="Murray Head">Murray Head</a> and Swedes <a href="/wiki/Tommy_K%C3%B6rberg" title="Tommy Körberg">Tommy Körberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_Skifs" title="Björn Skifs">Björn Skifs</a> – was released in October 1984, selling two million copies worldwide. The Paige/Dickson duet "<a href="/wiki/I_Know_Him_So_Well" title="I Know Him So Well">I Know Him So Well</a>" became a major UK No. 1 hit, and Murray Head's "<a href="/wiki/One_Night_in_Bangkok" title="One Night in Bangkok">One Night in Bangkok</a>" gave Andersson/Ulvaeus a US No. 3 hit.</p>
<p><i>Chess</i> was staged in London's West End <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Theatre" title="Prince Edward Theatre">Prince Edward Theatre</a> in May 1986, and ran for almost three years. A Broadway staging in April 1988 received disastrous reviews, and closed after just two months.</p>
<p>In 1985, Andersson produced and released an album with brother and sister <a href="/wiki/Anders_Glenmark" title="Anders Glenmark">Anders</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karin_Glenmark" title="Karin Glenmark">Karin Glenmark</a>, featuring new songs by Andersson/Ulvaeus. The duo named themselves <a href="/wiki/Gemini_(Swedish_band)" title="Gemini (Swedish band)">Gemini</a>, and a second album with more music by Björn and Benny was released in April 1987, containing the big hit "<a href="/wiki/Mio_My_Mio_(song)" title="Mio My Mio (song)">Mio My Mio</a>"; also to be found on the soundtrack to the film <i><a href="/wiki/Mio_in_the_Land_of_Faraway" title="Mio in the Land of Faraway">Mio in the Land of Faraway</a></i>, for which Andersson co-produced the music.</p>
<p>In 1987, Andersson released his first solo album <i>Klinga Mina Klockor</i> ("Chime, My Bells"). All the music was written by and performed by himself on accordion, backed by the <i><a href="/wiki/Orsa_Spelm%C3%A4n" title="Orsa Spelmän">Orsa Spelmän</a></i> (Orsa Folk Musicians) on fiddles. A second solo album followed: <i>November 1989</i>.</p>
<p>In 1990, Andersson scored a Swedish #1 hit with "Lassie", sung by female cabaret group <a href="/wiki/Ainbusk" title="Ainbusk">Ainbusk</a>, for whom he also wrote the <a href="/wiki/Svensktoppen" title="Svensktoppen">Svensktoppen</a> hits "Älska Mig" and "Drömmarnas Golv". He decided to produce an album with <a href="/wiki/Josefin_Nilsson" title="Josefin Nilsson">Josefin Nilsson</a> from this quartet, resulting in the 1993 English-language album <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shapes_(Josefin_Nilsson_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shapes (Josefin Nilsson album) (page does not exist)">Shapes</a></i>, featuring ten new Andersson/Ulvaeus compositions.</p>
<p>In 1992, he wrote the introduction melody for the European football championship, which was organised by Sweden that year.</p>
<p>From the late 1980s, Andersson had worked on an idea for an epic Swedish language musical based on his affection for traditional folk music, and in October 1995, <i><a href="/wiki/Kristina_fr%C3%A5n_Duvem%C3%A5la" title="Kristina från Duvemåla">Kristina från Duvemåla</a></i> premiered in Sweden. The musical was based on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Emigrants_(novels)" title="The Emigrants (novels)">The Emigrants</a></i> novels by Swedish writer <a href="/wiki/Vilhelm_Moberg" title="Vilhelm Moberg">Vilhelm Moberg</a>. The musical ran successfully for almost five years, before closing in June 1999. An English-language version, simply titled <i>Kristina</i>, was staged in concert at <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Hall" title="Carnegie Hall">Carnegie Hall</a> in New York City for two nights in September 2009, yielding a live recording, and at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">Royal Albert Hall</a> for one night in April 2010.</p>
<p>Andersson's next project was <i><a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia!" title="Mamma Mia!">Mamma Mia!</a></i>, a musical built around 24 of ABBA's songs, which has become a worldwide box-office blockbuster with versions in several languages currently being played in many countries, including the UK (West End premiere in April 1999), Canada (<a href="/wiki/Royal_Alexandra_Theatre" title="Royal Alexandra Theatre">Toronto</a> premiere in 2000), the USA (Broadway premiere in 2001), and Sweden (<a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish language</a> premiere in 2005).</p>
<p>For the 2004 semi-final of the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a>, staged in Istanbul thirty years after <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a> had won the contest in Brighton, Benny appeared briefly in a special comedy video made for the interval act, entitled "Our Last Video". Each of the four members of the group appeared briefly in cameo roles, as did others such as <a href="/wiki/Cher" title="Cher">Cher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rik_Mayall" title="Rik Mayall">Rik Mayall</a>. The video was not included in the official DVD release of the Eurovision Contest, but was issued as a separate DVD release. It was billed as the first time the four had worked together since the group split; however, Frida's appearance was filmed separately.</p>
<p>A film version of <i><a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia!" title="Mamma Mia!">Mamma Mia!</a></i> premiered on 18 July 2008. In April/May 2007, Andersson worked on the film soundtrack, re-recording the old ABBA songs with musicians from the old ABBA recording sessions. <i><a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia!_(film)" title="Mamma Mia! (film)">Mamma Mia! The Movie</a></i> has now become the most successful movie musical of all time and has been named the number one box office smash of 2008, and the biggest-selling DVD ever in the UK.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Benny_Anderssons_Orkester">Benny Anderssons Orkester</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Andersson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Benny Anderssons Orkester">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Benny_Andersson_Orkester.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="683" data-file-width="1024" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Benny_Andersson_Orkester.jpg/300px-Benny_Andersson_Orkester.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Benny_Andersson_Orkester.jpg/450px-Benny_Andersson_Orkester.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Benny_Andersson_Orkester.jpg/600px-Benny_Andersson_Orkester.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Benny_Andersson_Orkester.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Benny Andersson Orchestra at a concert in Minnesota in 2006.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Andersson currently performs with his own band of 16 musicians, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benny_Anderssons_Orkester" title="Benny Anderssons Orkester">BAO</a>; <i>"<b>B</b>enny <b>A</b>nderssons <b>O</b>rkester"</i> ("Benny Andersson's orchestra"), utilising the vocal talents of fellow Swedes <a href="/wiki/Helen_Sj%C3%B6holm" title="Helen Sjöholm">Helen Sjöholm</a> (from <i>Kristina from Duvemåla</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Tommy_K%C3%B6rberg" title="Tommy Körberg">Tommy Körberg</a> (of <i>Chess</i> fame), with lyrics to new material sometimes written by his song-writing partner and best friend of 40 years, Björn Ulvaeus. BAO has released five albums to huge success in Sweden, all containing hit singles.</p>
<p>BAO recently achieved a new record in Sweden on the <a href="/wiki/Svensktoppen" title="Svensktoppen">Svensktoppen</a> chart by staying there for 243 weeks with the song <i>Du är min man</i> ("You Are My Man"), sung by <a href="/wiki/Helen_Sj%C3%B6holm" title="Helen Sjöholm">Helen Sjöholm</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="New_compositions">New compositions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Andersson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: New compositions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andersson composes primarily for his 'band' BAO! with the vocalists Sjöholm and Körberg, but keeps his older material alive through re-visiting it, like the staging of 'Mamma Mia!' and the Swedish version of 'Chess'.</p>
<p>For a compilation album of the Glenmark duo <a href="/wiki/Gemini_(Swedish_band)" title="Gemini (Swedish band)">Gemini</a>, Andersson had Björn Ulvaeus write new Swedish lyrics for the re-recording of two songs from 1984 and 1987. Andersson and Ulvaeus have continuously been writing new material; most recently the two wrote seven songs for Andersson's 'BAO' 2011 album <i>O Klang Och Jubeltid</i>, performed as usual by vocalists Sjöholm, Körberg and Moreus. In July 2009, BAO, now named "The Benny Andersson Band", released their first international record, the album <i>The Story of a Heart</i>. It was a compilation of 14 tracks from Andersson's five Swedish-language releases between 1987 and 2007, including five songs now recorded with lyrics by Ulvaeus in English, and the new title song premiered on BBC2's <i>Ken Bruce Show</i>. A Swedish-language version of the title track, <i>"Sommaren Du Fick"</i> ("The Summer You Got"), was released as a single in Sweden prior to the English version, with vocals by Helen Sjöholm.</p>
<p>In the spring of 2009, Andersson also released a single recorded by the staff at his privately owned <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> hotel <i>Hotel Rival</i>, titled "<a href="/wiki/2nd_Best_to_None" title="2nd Best to None">2nd Best to None</a>", accompanied by a video showing the staff at work. In 2008, Andersson and Ulvaeus wrote a song for Swedish singer <a href="/wiki/Sissela_Kyle" title="Sissela Kyle">Sissela Kyle</a>, titled <i>"Jag vill bli gammal"</i> ("I Wanna Grow Old"), for her Stockholm stage show <i>"Your Days Are Numbered"</i>, which was never recorded and released, but did get a TV performance. Ulvaeus also contributed lyrics to ABBA's 1976 instrumental track "Arrival" for <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Brightman" title="Sarah Brightman">Sarah Brightman</a>'s cover version recorded for her 2008 album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter_Symphony" title="Winter Symphony">Winter Symphony</a></i>. New English lyrics have also been written for Andersson's 1999 song <i>"Innan Gryningen"</i> (then also named "Millennium Hymn"), with the new title "The Silence of the Dawn" for <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Dickson" title="Barbara Dickson">Barbara Dickson</a> was performed live, but not yet recorded and released. In 2007, they wrote the new song <i>"Han som har vunnit allt"</i> ("He Who's Won It All") for actor/singer <a href="/wiki/Anders_Ekborg" title="Anders Ekborg">Anders Ekborg</a>. Björn wrote English lyrics for two older songs from Benny's solo albums: "I Walk with You Mama" ("Stockholm by Night", 1989) and "After the Rain" ("Efter regnet", 1987) for opera singer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anne_Sofie_Von_Otter" title="Anne Sofie Von Otter">Anne Sofie Von Otter</a>, for her Andersson tribute album <i>I Let the Music Speak</i>. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Barbra_Dickson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Barbra Dickson (page does not exist)">Barbra Dickson</a> recorded (but not yet released) a Björn &amp; Benny song called 'The Day The Wall Came Tumbling Down'; the song eventually was released by Australian 'Mamma Mia!' musical star <a href="/wiki/Anne_Wood" title="Anne Wood">Anne Wood</a> 201 album of ABBA covers, <a href="/wiki/Divine_Discontent" title="Divine Discontent">Divine Discontent</a>. As of October 2012, Björn Ulvaeus has mentioned writing new material with Benny for a 'BAO' Christmas release (also mentioned as a BAO 'box'), and Benny is busy writing music for a Swedish language obscure musical, 'Hjälp Sökes' ('Help is Wanted') together with Kristina Lugn and Lars Rudolfsson, premiering 8 February 2013. Andersson has also written music for a documentary film about <a href="/wiki/Olof_Palme" title="Olof Palme">Olof Palme</a>, re-recording the track 'Sorgmarch' from his last album as a theme throughout the film. The song "Kärlekens Tid" -recorded 2004 by Helen Sjöholm with BAO- -has also been performed in concert in English (lyrics by Ylva Eggehorn) by opera <a href="/wiki/Baritone" title="Baritone">baritone</a> <a href="/wiki/Bryn_Terfel" title="Bryn Terfel">Bryn Terfel</a>. On 15 April 2013, it was officially announced by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EBU" title="EBU">EBU</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sveriges_Television" title="Sveriges Television">SVT</a> that Benny along with the ex-<a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a> member, <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_Ulvaeus" title="Björn Ulvaeus">Björn Ulvaeus</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swedish_people" title="Swedish people">Swedish</a> DJ and record producer, <a href="/wiki/Avicii" title="Avicii">Avicii</a>, have composed the anthem for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013_Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="2013 Eurovision Song Contest">2013 Eurovision Song Contest</a>. The song will be performed for the first time in the Final on 18 May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_music">Film music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Andersson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Film music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Benny Andersson has written music to several films for screen and television; the first attempt in the early 1970s for the obscure Swedish movie <i>The Seduction of Inga</i>: the film flopped, but the 'Björn &amp; Benny' single "She's My Kind of Girl" surprised the composers by being released in Japan and becoming a Top 10 hit (the song renamed in Japan as "The Little Girl of the Cold Wind").</p>
<p>In 1987, Andersson wrote music and co-produced the soundtrack with Anders Eljas for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Mio_in_the_Land_of_Faraway" title="Mio in the Land of Faraway">Mio in the Land of Faraway</a></i>, based on Swedish author <a href="/wiki/Astrid_Lindgren" title="Astrid Lindgren">Astrid Lindgrens</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Mio,_My_Son" title="Mio, My Son">Mio, My Son</a></i>. The title song became a huge hit in Sweden for <a href="/wiki/Gemini_(Swedish_band)" title="Gemini (Swedish band)">Gemini</a>.</p>
<p>In 2000, he wrote the music for fellow Swede (no relation) <a href="/wiki/Roy_Andersson" title="Roy Andersson">Roy Andersson</a>'s film <i><a href="/wiki/Songs_from_the_Second_Floor" title="Songs from the Second Floor">Songs from the Second Floor</a></i> (the music later re-recorded, featuring new lyrics, with BAO! with vocals by Helen Sjöholm). He also wrote the theme for Roy Andersson's next film, <i><a href="/wiki/You,_the_Living" title="You, the Living">You, the Living</a></i>, from 2007.</p>
<p>Andersson also worked on the film adaptation of <i>Mamma Mia!</i>. He also wrote the film score for the 2012 documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Palme_(film)" title="Palme (film)">Palme</a></i> about Swedish prime minister <a href="/wiki/Olof_Palme" title="Olof Palme">Olof Palme</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> He later won a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guldbagge_Award" title="Guldbagge Award">Guldbagge Award</a> for Best Original Score, for that film at the <a href="/wiki/48th_Guldbagge_Awards" title="48th Guldbagge Awards">48th Guldbagge Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sydsvenskan_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-sydsvenskan-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Andersson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Together with Ulvaeus, Andersson was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Drama_Desk_Award" title="Drama Desk Award">Drama Desk Award</a> in a category "Outstanding Music" (for the musical <i>Chess</i>), and for a <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a> in a category "Best Orchestrations" (for musical <i>Mamma Mia!</i>). Original cast recordings of both musicals were nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a>. Andersson/Ulvaeus also won a <a href="/wiki/Touring_Broadway_Awards" title="Touring Broadway Awards">Touring Broadway Award</a> for the musical "Mamma Mia" (best score).</p>
<p>During his post-ABBA career Andersson won four Swedish <a href="/wiki/Grammis" title="Grammis">Grammis</a> awards, and together with Ulvaeus received the "Special International" <a href="/wiki/Ivor_Novello" title="Ivor Novello">Ivor Novello</a> award from 'The British Academy of Composers and Songwriters', twice "The Music Export Prize" from the Swedish Ministry of Industry and Trade (2008),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> as well as the "Lifetime Achievement" award from the Swedish Music Publishers Association (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=SMFF&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="SMFF (page does not exist)">SMFF</a>). In 2002, Andersson was given an honorary professorship by the Swedish Government for his "ability to create high-class music reaching people around the world".</p>
<p>In 2007, he was elected a member of <a href="/wiki/Royal_Swedish_Academy_of_Music" title="Royal Swedish Academy of Music">Royal Swedish Academy of Music</a>, and in 2008 received an Honorary Doctorate from the <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_University" title="Stockholm University">Stockholm University</a> Faculty of Humanities for contributing importantly both to the preservation and the growth of the Swedish folk music tradition.</p>
<p>On 15 March 2010, Andersson appeared on stage in New York with former wife Anni-Frid Lyngstad to accept ABBA's award of induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. During his acceptance speech he reflected on the important influence of traditional European music and the melancholy of the Swedish soul on ABBA's brand of pop music. "If you live in a country like Sweden, with five, six months of snow, and the sun disappears totally for like two months, that would be reflected in the work of artists," he said. "It's definitely in the Swedish folk music, you can hear it in the Russian folk songs, you can hear in the music from Jean Sibelius or Edvard Grieg from Norway, you can see it in the eyes of Greta Garbo and you can hear it in the voice of Jussi Björling. And you can hear in the sound of Frida and Agnetha on some of our songs too."</p>
<p>In 2012, he received an Honorary Doctorate of Philosophy from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lule%C3%A5_Tekniska_Universitet" title="Luleå Tekniska Universitet">Luleå Tekniska Universitet</a> Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Andersson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Frida_Lyngstad_and_Benny_Andersson_1976b.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1468" data-file-width="1576" height="279" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Frida_Lyngstad_and_Benny_Andersson_1976b.jpg/300px-Frida_Lyngstad_and_Benny_Andersson_1976b.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Frida_Lyngstad_and_Benny_Andersson_1976b.jpg/450px-Frida_Lyngstad_and_Benny_Andersson_1976b.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Frida_Lyngstad_and_Benny_Andersson_1976b.jpg/600px-Frida_Lyngstad_and_Benny_Andersson_1976b.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>Andersson was engaged to Christina Grönvall after she became pregnant by him when Andersson was 16 years old. In 1963, they had a son, Peter Grönvall and, later, a daughter, Hélene. They split in 1966 and Christina never remarried. She kept custody of the children as Andersson was then at the peak of his Hep Stars' success. In the 1990s, Peter formed <a href="/wiki/One_More_Time_(band)" title="One More Time (band)">One More Time</a>, a group that enjoyed European success with the ABBA-like "Highland" and, later, as Sweden's entry to the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_1996" title="Eurovision Song Contest 1996">Eurovision Song Contest 1996</a>.</p>
<p>Andersson was engaged to <a href="/wiki/Anni-Frid_Lyngstad" title="Anni-Frid Lyngstad">Anni-Frid Lyngstad</a> of ABBA for about nine years. They married in October 1978, but divorced in 1981.</p>
<p>He married Swedish TV presenter Mona Nörklit in November 1981 and had a son, Ludvig (born January 1982), who has since followed in his father's footsteps in forming his own band.</p>
