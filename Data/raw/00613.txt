      function cycleSearchType(event) {

         var searchTypes = new Array('a', 'l', 'y', 'b', 'j', 'r', 'v', 'g', 'F', 'A', 'u', 'p', 's');
         var len = searchTypes.length;
         var currentVal = $('#searchtype').val();
         var currentIndex = searchTypes.indexOf(currentVal);
         var nextIndex = currentIndex+1;
         var prevIndex = currentIndex-1;
         if ( nextIndex == len ) {
            nextIndex = 0;
         }
         if ( prevIndex < 0 ) {
            prevIndex = len-1;
         }

         var nextType = searchTypes[nextIndex];
         var prevType = searchTypes[prevIndex];


         switch (event.keyCode) {
            case 40: 
               selectSearchType(nextType);
               event.preventDefault();
            break;

            case 38:
               selectSearchType(prevType);
               event.preventDefault();
            break;

            case 13:
               $('#mainsearch')[0].submit();
               event.preventDefault();
            break;
            default: 
               
            break;
         }

        
         return false;

      }
      function selectSearchType(type) {
         $('#searchtype').val(type);
         $('#searchtype_selected').text($('#searchtype_' + type).text());
         $('#search_options').hide();
      }

<div class="comment_body"><em class="rymfmt">"ABBA's music is among the most irresistibly melodic and tuneful ever written. For entire songs, without trying to sound cool or ambiguous, they plunge into soaring melodies. Refreshingly, they're not afraid of melodrama and dare to lure the listener into singalongs or carry him/her on the wings of effortless melodies. The songs sound so natural and easy that it is too easy to forget how original they really are. Look a little closer and you will be astounded, for this is not the stuff one can throw together spontaneously or casually. This is 'composed' pop par excellence, carefully constructed and always fresh, new and original. The composers (and co-performers) Björn and Benny can easily be ranked among such geniuses as Lennon/McCartney, Brian Wilson, Burt Bacharach and Elvis Costello, whose dedication, hard work and natural instinct has gradually turned pop into a respectable form of art. Even though ABBA's music is immediate, enjoyable and understood by anyone, it can be complex and (particularly during the second half of their career, when their relationships started to crumble) full of deep nostalgia, regret and hope. ABBA's music can be compared to Queen's music in many ways. Both bands used the studio to the limits. The music is multi-layered with interesting details added to the melodies at every step, with Abba being the more subtle of the two. Both bands strove (and mostly succeeded) in treating every single song as individual, trying to discover new melodic twists and approaches. Therefore, their music hardly ever echoes older songs, being strikingly novel and fresh. There is also a certain flamboyance, grandeur and confidence in the melodic sweep. While no one singer can quite match Freddie Mercury, Agnetha and Anna-Frid do so together, dazzling the listener with their beautiful vocals and exquisite harmonies. Their voices contrast each other and when they are weaved together, the pop tapestry becomes as subtle and ambiguous as the one woven by more elusive bands, like the Cocteau Twins."</em></div><li class="comment" id="comment_0"><div class="comment_img" style="background:url('//rymimg.com/lk/t/u/c6781ef5537895213d4f2556e9f8f4d5/882895.jpg') center no-repeat;background-size:25px;background-color:black;"></div><div class="comment_header"><a class="user" href="/~steinib">steinib</a> <span class="comment_date"></span></div></li>

<h2 class="release_page_header">Biography</h2>
<a class="artist" href="/artist/benny_andersson" title="[Artist38239]">Benny Andersson</a> and <a class="artist" href="/artist/bjorn_ulvaeus" title="[Artist81281]">Björn Ulvaeus</a> first met in 1966 when Andersson was the keyboardist for <a class="artist" href="/artist/the_hep_stars" title="[Artist42854]">the Hep Stars</a> and Ulvaeus a vocalist and lyricist for a folk outfit known as the <a class="artist" href="/artist/hootenanny_singers" title="[Artist43652]">Hootenanny Singers</a>. The two started writing music together, leading Andersson to leave the Hep Stars and record with Ulvaeus on the Hootenanny Singers' Polar label.<br/> <br/>While collaborating in 1969, they met <a class="artist" href="/artist/agnetha_faltskog" title="[Artist20116]">Agnetha Fältskog</a> and <a class="artist" href="/artist/frida" title="[Artist14789]">Anni-Frid Lyngstad</a> (also known as Frida). The four began to collaborate on each other's solo releases. It was in 1970 when the four officially began performing together under the name Festfolk. Initially there was not much interest, but the release of a single (under the cumbersome name Björn, Benny, Agnetha &amp; Anni-Frid) in 1972 started to get the group attention. Meanwhile, Faltskog and Ulvaeus had married in 1971.<br/> <br/>The minor success of the single led to the band entering the Eurovision Song Contest in 1973. Their entry, "<a class="album" href="/release/single/bjorn_and_benny__agnetha_and_anni_frid/ring_ring__bara_du_slog_en_signal____ah__vilka_tider/" title="[Album104198]">Ring Ring</a>", was the third-place winner, but it did garner them their first hit outside of their native Sweden. They re-entered the competition again the following year, this time with the song "<a class="album" href="/release/single/abba/waterloo__english_version____watch_out_f1/" title="[Album104204]">Waterloo</a>", and won first place. The single and following album became international hits, and the group, now called ABBA (an acronym of the members' first names and the name of a Swedish fish company) was poised for the typical one-hit wonder status guaranteed to Eurovision winners.<br/> <br/>Defying expectations, "Waterloo" was just the beginning. "<a class="album" href="/release/single/abba/sos___man_in_the_middle/" title="[Album104211]">SOS</a>", from their self-titled third album, became a worldwide hit, and was the beginning of string of pop hits throughout the '70s and into the early 1980s. During the height of their success, Frida and Andersson married in 1978.<br/> <br/>Fractures within the group began when Ulvaeus and Faltskog divorced in 1979. The group decided to keep going, although a Japanese tour in 1980 proved to be their last. Andersson and Frida divorced in 1981 and the band released one more full album, <em class="rymfmt"><a class="album" href="/release/album/abba/the_visitors/" title="[Album6585]">The Visitors</a></em>, and the compilation <em class="rymfmt"><a class="album" href="/release/comp/abba/the_singles__the_first_ten_years_f3/" title="[Album43169]">The Singles - The First Ten Years</a></em>, including new track and final single, "<a class="album" href="/release/single/abba/under_attack___you_owe_me_one/" title="[Album104236]">Under Attack</a>", before taking an extended leave. Over the years all four members have released a number of solo albums.<br/> <br/>Although nothing new has been released after the split up, ABBA has remained as popular as ever. A number of compilations have been released and become bestsellers, and the musical <em class="rymfmt">Mamma Mia</em>, based on a number of their songs, has become one of the most popular musicals of all time. They have even provided inspiration for an Australian cover band, <a class="artist" href="/artist/bjorn_again" title="[Artist41731]">Björn Again</a>, which has made a career out of dressing like ABBA and performing their songs.</div><div class="section_lists"><div class="release_page_header">
