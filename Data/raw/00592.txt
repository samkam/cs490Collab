<div class="pd-rating" id="pd_rating_holder_2548576_post_8870"></div><br/><p><a href="https://justwilliam1959.files.wordpress.com/2014/12/q_tips_christmas.jpg"><img alt="Q_tips_christmas" class="aligncenter wp-image-8872 size-full" height="266" src="https://justwilliam1959.files.wordpress.com/2014/12/q_tips_christmas.jpg?w=312&amp;h=266" width="312"/></a></p>
<p>Is it really December 17th? It is? Oh well Christmas will soon be over. But in the meantime we’re at day 17 of this years advent calendar blog posts. this means that we are now at the 17th letter of the alphabet, which as you know is ‘Q’. Can you guess which band will feature under the letter ‘Q’ in my ABC of <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_rock" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="British rock">British bands</a>? Really? Are you sure? I’m thinking that most of you guessed Queen, in which case you’d be wrong. Todays band proudly representing the letter ‘Q’ are the Q-Tips. (Any free advertising here for Q Magazine is purely coincidental and accidental; unless the publishers of that great magazine would like to ply me with money and gifts!).</p>
<p><a href="https://justwilliam1959.files.wordpress.com/2014/12/qtips1.jpg"><img alt="QTips1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8873" height="267" src="https://justwilliam1959.files.wordpress.com/2014/12/qtips1.jpg?w=400&amp;h=267" width="400"/></a></p>
<p>The band was formed in 1979 by the remnants of one hit wonders <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Q-Tips_%28band%29" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Q-Tips (band)">Streetband</a> who hit the charts with “Toast”. Three members of that band stayed together and they were <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Gifford" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="John Gifford">John Gifford</a> (guitar), Mick Pearl (Bass) and <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.paul-young.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Paul Young">Paul Young</a> on vocals. Yes that Paul Young the one with the spiky hair and who layed his hat anywhere he damned well wanted to. They brought in  another guitarist, Dave Laswell and Baz Watts on drums. They then added a brass section consisting of Oscar Stuart Blandamer, Tony Hughes, Richard Blanchchard and <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steve_Farr" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Steve Farr">Steve Farr</a>. The band was further augmented by keyboard player Ian Kewley. Later in their career Gifford and Blandamer were replaced by Garth Watt Roy and <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_Payne" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Nick Payne">Nick Payne</a>.</p>
<p><a href="https://justwilliam1959.files.wordpress.com/2014/12/q-tips.jpg"><img alt="Q TIPS" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8874" height="250" src="https://justwilliam1959.files.wordpress.com/2014/12/q-tips.jpg?w=250&amp;h=250" width="250"/></a></p>
<p>The band was quite short-lived, breaking up in 1982 when  Paul Young signed a solo contract. During their time they were a highly praised live act. They also supported some massive names including the Who, <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.averagewhiteband.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Average White Band">Average White Band</a>, <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.jgeilsband.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="The J. Geils Band">J Geils Band</a>, Bob Marley, <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.knack.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="The Knack">The Knack</a> and Thin Lizzy. They began by playing lots of soul music covers but in the end they had a substantial amount of self-penned material as well. The band briefly reformed for some live dates in 1993. In case you were wondering they took their name from a well-known brand of cotton swabs. Now enjoy some rather spiffing tunes from the Q-Tips.</p>

<p>Five years ago in 2009 <a class="zem_slink" href="http://ProcolHarum.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Procol Harum">Procol Harum</a>‘s “<a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Whiter_Shade_of_Pale" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="A Whiter Shade of Pale">A Whiter Shade Of Pale</a>” was still the most played song on <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="United Kingdom">UK</a> radio. <a class="zem_slink" href="http://queenonline.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>‘s  “<a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bohemian_Rhapsody" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Bohemian Rhapsody">Bohemian Rhapsody</a>” was at number two in that listing. In a strange coincidence both songs use the word ‘fandango’ in their lyrics. Are their any other songs that use ‘fandango’ in their lyrics?</p>

<span class="date">March 17, 2014</span></h3>
<div class="meta">Filed under: <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/category/trivia/" rel="category tag">Trivia</a> — justwilliam1959 @ 11:43 pm <br/> Tags: <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/brian-may/" rel="tag">Brian May</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/build-your-own-boat/" rel="tag">Build Your Own Boat</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/freddie-mercury/" rel="tag">Freddie Mercury</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/i-want-it-all/" rel="tag">I Want It All</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/john-deacon/" rel="tag">John Deacon</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/khashoggis-ship/" rel="tag">Khashoggis Ship</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/queen/" rel="tag">Queen</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/roger-taylor/" rel="tag">Roger Taylor</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/somebody-to-love/" rel="tag">Somebody To Love</a><br/></div><!-- end META -->
<div class="storycontent">
<div class="pd-rating" id="pd_rating_holder_2548576_post_8154"></div><br/><p><a href="https://justwilliam1959.files.wordpress.com/2014/03/freddie-mercury-of-queen-011.jpg"><img alt="Freddie-Mercury-of-Queen-011" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8156" height="580" src="https://justwilliam1959.files.wordpress.com/2014/03/freddie-mercury-of-queen-011.jpg?w=400&amp;h=580" width="400"/></a>Before they chose the name <a class="zem_slink" href="http://queenonline.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a> (which incidentally was chosen by <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0006198/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a>) <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mr." rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Mr.">Messrs</a> May, Mercury, <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Deacon" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="John Deacon">Deacon</a> and Taylor were at one point considering a very different name. That was a feat achieved in 1960 and 1962. The song was originally by <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.last.fm/music/Hank%2BBallard%2Band%2BThe%2BMidnighters" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Hank Ballard and The Midnighters">Hank Ballard and the Midnighters</a>, although their version only made it to number 28 in the US chart in 1959 and even that was as a <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A-side_and_B-side" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="A-side and B-side">B-Side</a> to “Teardrops On Your Letter”</p>

<span class="date">January 7, 2014</span></h3>
<div class="meta">Filed under: <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/category/trivia/" rel="category tag">Trivia</a> — justwilliam1959 @ 11:30 pm <br/> Tags: <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/abba/" rel="tag">Abba</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/bohemian-rhapsody/" rel="tag">Bohemian Rhapsody</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/freddie-mercury/" rel="tag">Freddie Mercury</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/goodnight-girl/" rel="tag">Goodnight Girl</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/mamma-mia/" rel="tag">Mamma Mia</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/queen/" rel="tag">Queen</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/uk-singles-chart/" rel="tag">UK Singles Chart</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/wet-wet-wet/" rel="tag">Wet Wet Wet</a><br/></div><!-- end META -->
<div class="storycontent">
<div class="pd-rating" id="pd_rating_holder_2548576_post_7981"></div><br/><p><a href="https://justwilliam1959.files.wordpress.com/2014/01/queen-funny-d-queen-31231886-498-361.jpg"><img alt="Queen-Funny-D-queen-31231886-498-361" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7991" height="361" src="https://justwilliam1959.files.wordpress.com/2014/01/queen-funny-d-queen-31231886-498-361.jpg?w=498&amp;h=361" width="498"/></a>Back in 1975 when <a class="zem_slink" href="http://queenonline.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>‘s “Bohemian Rhapsody” was number one in the <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.theofficialcharts.com/singles-chart/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="UK Singles Chart">UK charts</a> for the first time did you notice that it’s lyrics contained the name of the song which succeeded its 9-week run at the top in early 1976? I certainly didn’t notice, but obviously it has the phrase “Mama Mia” in it which is the title of the <a class="zem_slink" href="http://abbasite.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="ABBA">Abba</a> song which followed it to number one. I wonder if that has ever happened since? It certainly didn’t happen when “Bohemian Rhapsody” returned to the <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_UK_Singles_Chart_number_ones" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="List of UK Singles Chart number ones">UK number one</a> spot after the death of <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0006198/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a> in 1991. It was followed by <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.wetwetwet.co.uk" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Wet Wet Wet">Wet Wet Wet</a> with “Goodnight Girl” then.</p>

<span class="date">July 31, 2013</span></h3>
<div class="meta">Filed under: <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/category/news/" rel="category tag">News</a> — justwilliam1959 @ 8:49 pm <br/> Tags: <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/beatles/" rel="tag">Beatles</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/brian-may/" rel="tag">Brian May</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/freddie-mercury/" rel="tag">Freddie Mercury</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/free-as-a-bird/" rel="tag">Free As A Bird</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/guardian/" rel="tag">Guardian</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/jacksons/" rel="tag">Jacksons</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/john-lennon/" rel="tag">John Lennon</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/laura-barton/" rel="tag">Laura Barton</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/mash-up/" rel="tag">mash-up</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/michael-jackson/" rel="tag">Michael Jackson</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/mick-jagger/" rel="tag">Mick Jagger</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/paul-gambaccini/" rel="tag">Paul Gambaccini</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/queen/" rel="tag">Queen</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/roger-taylor/" rel="tag">Roger Taylor</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/smooth-criminal/" rel="tag">Smooth Criminal</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/state-of-shock/" rel="tag">State Of Shock</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/the-show-must-go-on/" rel="tag">The Show Must Go On</a>, <a href="https://justwilliam1959.wordpress.com/tag/william-orbit/" rel="tag">William Orbit</a><br/></div><!-- end META -->
<div class="storycontent">
<div class="pd-rating" id="pd_rating_holder_2548576_post_7166"></div><br/><p><a href="https://justwilliam1959.files.wordpress.com/2013/07/unreleased-mj-and-freddie-mercury-songs-coming-out__opt.jpg"><img alt="unreleased-mj-and-freddie-mercury-songs-coming-out__oPt" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7170" height="305" src="https://justwilliam1959.files.wordpress.com/2013/07/unreleased-mj-and-freddie-mercury-songs-coming-out__opt.jpg?w=409&amp;h=305" width="409"/></a></p>
<p>That well-known badger supporter <a class="zem_slink" href="http://brianmay.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Brian May">Brian May</a> has unearthed some recordings made by <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freddie_Mercury" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a> and <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.sonymusic.co.jp/Music/International/Arch/ES/MichaelJackson/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a> at Jacko’s home in 1983. He has worked on them with fellow Queen member Roger Taylor and <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.williamorbit.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="William Orbit">William Orbit</a>. I guess the issue will be whether or not the three songs will live up to the expectations. was it quality control that kept them from release until now? Another big name duet for Jacko was <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.mickjagger.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Mick Jagger">Mick Jagger</a> who joined the Jacksons on the single “State Of Shock” from the brothers ‘Victory’ album in 1984. Personally I was and still remain underwhelmed by that song. Other elements of posthumous twiddling may have fared well but for me the quality was lacking with the Beatles “<a class="zem_slink" href="http://musicbrainz.org/album/9aee237d-7033-4dfa-b76c-48f269e137a4.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Free as a Bird">Free As A Bird</a>” which was based around a very basic <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.johnlennon.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a> demo recording.But let’s keep fingers crossed that this stuff is actually good.</p>
<p><a href="https://justwilliam1959.files.wordpress.com/2013/07/michaeljacksonfreddiemercuryfreddiemichael.jpg"><img alt="Michael+Jackson++Freddie+Mercury+FreddieMichael" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7171" height="564" src="https://justwilliam1959.files.wordpress.com/2013/07/michaeljacksonfreddiemercuryfreddiemichael.jpg?w=363&amp;h=564" width="363"/></a></p>
<p><a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Gambaccini" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Paul Gambaccini">Paul Gambaccini</a> has suggested that the songs were dubbed the Llama recordings given that Jacko allegedly brought his Llama into the studio when Freddie was there. A cautionary note was sounded by the Guardian’s <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laura_Barton" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Laura Barton">Laura Barton</a> who said “It feels like we’re sucking the artists’ bones dry… sometimes it would be nice to leave them with a little bit of dignity.” I think I can agree with her sentiments there. But I obviously won’t pass judgement until I have heard the songs. The release seems likely to be later this year. So while you wait take a listen to some classic Jacko and <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.amazon.com/Classic-Queen-Guitar-Recorded-Version/dp/079353982X%3FSubscriptionId%3D0G81C5DAZ03ZR9WH9X82%26tag%3Dzemanta-20%26linkCode%3Dxm2%26camp%3D2025%26creative%3D165953%26creativeASIN%3D079353982X" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Classic Queen (Guitar Recorded Version)">classic Queen</a> and a couple of Jacko versus Queen mash-ups!</p>
