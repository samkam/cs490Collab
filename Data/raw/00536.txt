<p><b>Mirwais Ahmadzaï</b> (born 23 October 1960), more commonly known as <b>Mirwais</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> based <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a>. He was born in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> to an <a href="/wiki/Pashtun_people" title="Pashtun people">Afghan</a> father and an <a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">Italian</a> mother. He is a leader in the French style of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Progressive_electronic_music" title="Progressive electronic music">progressive electronic dance music</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Progressive_electronica" title="Progressive electronica">progressive electronica</a>. A former member of the defunct 1980s group <a href="/wiki/Taxi_Girl" title="Taxi Girl">Taxi Girl</a>, he was re-discovered by <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a> in the late 1990s, when he submitted a <a href="/wiki/Demo_(music)" title="Demo (music)">demo</a> to her then <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maverick_Records" title="Maverick Records">Maverick Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirwais_Ahmadza%C3%AF&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxi_Girl_and_Juliette_et_les_independents">Taxi Girl and Juliette et les independents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirwais_Ahmadza%C3%AF&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxi Girl and Juliette et les independents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Known in France for the better part of two decades, Mirwais was a guitarist in the punk group <a href="/wiki/Taxi_Girl" title="Taxi Girl">Taxi Girl</a> for eight years, before forming the <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_music" title="Acoustic music">acoustic</a> outfit Juliette et les Independents with his girlfriend Juliette. Taxi Girl's first <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a>, "(S.O.S Mannequin)", was released in early 1980 on Pathé Marconi/EMI.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"For a teenager, the late 70s were a strange mixture of disco music and punk, and it was okay to like them all," he says. Taxi Girl captured the punk rock power of <a href="/wiki/The_Stooges" title="The Stooges">The Stooges</a>, the techno futurism of <a href="/wiki/Kraftwerk" title="Kraftwerk">Kraftwerk</a> and the disco euphoria of <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Moroder" title="Giorgio Moroder">Giorgio Moroder</a> and molded the diametrically opposed styles into an electrifying new-wave sound that entertained and inspired a generation of listeners bored by the complacency of 80s French pop. Even the current darlings of the French electronic music scene, such as <a href="/wiki/Daft_Punk" title="Daft Punk">Daft Punk</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air" title="Air">Air</a>, continue to namecheck Taxi Girl as a major influence on their artistic directions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirwais_Ahmadza%C3%AF&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1990, Mirwais released his first album, <a href="/wiki/Mirwais_(1st_Album)" title="Mirwais (1st Album)">Mirwais (1st Album)</a> ,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> which contained nine tracks and was performed by <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Mirwais" title="Mirwais">Mirwais</a> in French Language.</p>
<p>In 1998, he signed to the French <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">independent record label</a> <a href="/wiki/Na%C3%AFve_Records" title="Naïve Records">Naïve Records</a> and released the <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a> <a href="/wiki/Disco_science" title="Disco science">Disco science</a>. This single was a success as a club dance hit and appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack" title="Soundtrack">soundtrack</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Guy_Ritchie" title="Guy Ritchie">Guy Ritchie</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Snatch_(film)" title="Snatch (film)">Snatch</a></i>. In August 2000, he released the album <a href="/wiki/Production_(Mirwais_LP)" title="Production (Mirwais LP)">Production (Mirwais LP)</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> which included the single "Paradise (Not For Me)" made in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>. It also included two minor <a href="/wiki/Record_chart" title="Record chart">chart</a> <a href="/wiki/Hit_record" title="Hit record">hits</a> in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>: "Disco Science" (2000, #68) and "Naive Song" (2000, #50).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><br/>
<a href="/wiki/Naive_Song" title="Naive Song">Naive Song</a> scored number 2 on the US Dance Club Charts in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Madonna">Madonna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirwais_Ahmadza%C3%AF&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Madonna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Madonna recruited him for writing and production collaboration on three of her studio albums: <i><a href="/wiki/Music_(Madonna_album)" title="Music (Madonna album)">Music</a></i> (2000), <i><a href="/wiki/American_Life" title="American Life">American Life</a></i> (2003) and <i><a href="/wiki/Confessions_on_a_Dance_Floor" title="Confessions on a Dance Floor">Confessions on a Dance Floor</a></i> (2005). He also wrote co-wrote a song that would later appear on her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greatest_hits" title="Greatest hits">greatest hits</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/Celebration_(Madonna_album)" title="Celebration (Madonna album)">Celebration</a></i> as a previously unreleased track.</p>
<p>Mirwais contributed the following tracks to the Madonna albums:</p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Music_(Madonna_album)" title="Music (Madonna album)">Music</a></i>: "<a href="/wiki/Music_(Madonna_song)" title="Music (Madonna song)">Music</a>", "Impressive Instant", "Nobody's Perfect", "Paradise (Not For Me)", "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Tell_Me_(Madonna_song)" title="Don't Tell Me (Madonna song)">Don't Tell Me</a>", "I Deserve It".</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/American_Life" title="American Life">American Life</a></i>: Co-produced the entire record and co-wrote all songs with Madonna, with the exception of "<a href="/wiki/Nothing_Fails" title="Nothing Fails">Nothing Fails</a>", "X-Static Process" and "Easy Ride" (written by Madonna and other collaborators).</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Confessions_on_a_Dance_Floor" title="Confessions on a Dance Floor">Confessions on a Dance Floor</a></i>: "Future Lovers", "Let It Will Be", bonus track "Fighting Spirit", and the deleted tracks "Super Pop", "Triggering", and "Keep the Trance".</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Celebration_(Madonna_album)" title="Celebration (Madonna album)">Celebration</a></i> (2009): <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> bonus track "It's So Cool", a previously unreleased track from American Life album.</li>
</ul>
<p>The <i>Music</i> album was nominated for three <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Awards</a> including <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Record_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Record of the Year">Record of the Year</a>. "Music" won several MTV Europe awards. He was also involved in the writing and production of the <a href="/wiki/Die_Another_Day_(song)" title="Die Another Day (song)">title track</a> to the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Die_Another_Day" title="Die Another Day">Die Another Day</a></i>, co-written and performed by Madonna. The song was nominated for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a> for <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Original_Song" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song">Best Original Song</a> in 2003.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fischerspooner">Fischerspooner</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirwais_Ahmadza%C3%AF&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Fischerspooner">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mirwais also co-produced two tracks "Cloud" and "Never Win" on <a href="/wiki/Fischerspooner" title="Fischerspooner">Fischerspooner</a>'s second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Odyssey_(Fischerspooner_album)" title="Odyssey (Fischerspooner album)">Odyssey</a></i>, released in 2005.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Y.A.S.">Y.A.S.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirwais_Ahmadza%C3%AF&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Y.A.S.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He worked on a project called <a href="/wiki/Y.A.S." title="Y.A.S.">Y.A.S.</a>. The official website stated: <i>"YAS is the name of Mirwais' new project with the singer and composer <a href="/wiki/Yasmine_Hamdan" title="Yasmine Hamdan">Yasmine Hamdan</a>. The album was released in France on 8 June 2009. All songs are performed by her. It is "Arabic Avant-Garde Music".</i></p>
<p>In an interview on 13 January 2007 in lecourrier.ch, Mirwais said "At the moment I work with Yasmine Hamdan (one half of Lebanon electro-duo <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soapkills" title="Soapkills">Soapkills</a>). The idea is that today, in Western culture, we hear about Arabs everyday - in a bad way, because of terrorism, etc - but we lack of cultural representations coming from those countries that could mix with the western culture, whereas this youth is eager to. I don't want to do world music, but a good western production with a real Arab identity."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Y.A.S.' album, "<a href="/wiki/Arabology" title="Arabology">Arabology</a>" was released in May 2009, with the first single, "Get It Right", following in June that year.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Uffie">Uffie</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirwais_Ahmadza%C3%AF&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Uffie">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After remixing "<a href="/wiki/Pop_the_Glock" title="Pop the Glock">Pop the Glock</a>", Mirwais became one of the producers of <a href="/wiki/Uffie" title="Uffie">Uffie</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Sex_Dreams_and_Denim_Jeans" title="Sex Dreams and Denim Jeans">Sex Dreams and Denim Jeans</a></i>. He composed four songs <a href="/wiki/ADD_SUV" title="ADD SUV">ADD SUV</a>, "Sex Dreams and Denim Jeans", "Illusion of Love" and "Hong Kong Garden"), two of which featured <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a> <a href="/wiki/ADD_SUV" title="ADD SUV">ADD SUV</a> and Mattie Safer ("Illusion of Love").</p>
<p>According to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">The Independent</a></i>, "the record peaks with a cover of Siouxsie’s "Hong Kong Garden" which raises one eyebrow for existing and the other for actually being pretty damn good, leaving you looking like Paul McCartney by the end."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
