<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Heart of the Ocean</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="hatnote">For other uses, see <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Heart_of_the_Ocean_(disambiguation)" title="Heart of the Ocean (disambiguation)">Heart of the Ocean (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p>The <b>Heart of the Ocean</b> (also known as <i><b>Le Cœur de la Mer</b></i>) is the name of a fictional blue diamond featured prominently in the 1997 film <i><a href="/wiki/Titanic_(1997_film)" title="Titanic (1997 film)">Titanic</a></i>. In the story, the diamond was originally owned by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_XVI" title="Louis XVI">Louis XVI</a> and cut into a heart shape after the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>. Caledon Hockley (<a href="/wiki/Billy_Zane" title="Billy Zane">Billy Zane</a>), the wealthy son of a Pittsburgh steel tycoon, purchased the diamond for his fiancée Rose (<a href="/wiki/Kate_Winslet" title="Kate Winslet">Kate Winslet</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heart_of_the_Ocean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the 1943 film <i><a href="/wiki/Titanic_(1943_film)" title="Titanic (1943 film)">Titanic</a></i>, a blue diamond plays an important role in a love affair, as well. A primary plot point in this earlier film is the theft of the diamond, which creates a dramatic break in a romantic relationship.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_film">In the film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heart_of_the_Ocean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In the film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the film, treasure hunter Brock Lovett (<a href="/wiki/Bill_Paxton" title="Bill Paxton">Bill Paxton</a>) searches for a necklace, which he believes lies within the wreck of the <a href="/wiki/RMS_Titanic" title="RMS Titanic">RMS <i>Titanic</i></a>. His hunch seems to be confirmed when his team salvages a drawing, dated April 14, 1912, the day the <i>Titanic</i> struck an iceberg, in which a nude woman is portrayed wearing only the necklace. The necklace was fashioned from a large blue diamond worn by <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVI_of_France" title="Louis XVI of France">Louis XVI</a>. Shortly after his execution in 1793, the diamond disappeared and was recut into a heart-like shape, known as "The Heart of the Ocean". The story of the heart of the ocean is very like the story of the <a href="/wiki/Hope_Diamond" title="Hope Diamond">Hope Diamond</a>, except the Hope Diamond was worn by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_XIV" title="Louis XIV">Louis XIV</a> in a royal necklace rather than a crown. (In the film, Lovett says the <a href="/wiki/Hope_Diamond" title="Hope Diamond">Hope Diamond</a> is worth less than the Heart.)</p>
<p>The woman in the picture, Rose DeWitt Bukater (<a href="/wiki/Gloria_Stuart" title="Gloria Stuart">Gloria Stuart</a> when old and <a href="/wiki/Kate_Winslet" title="Kate Winslet">Kate Winslet</a> when young), contacts Lovett and is flown out to his <a href="/wiki/Salvage_tug" title="Salvage tug">recovery ship</a>, where she relates her story of her voyage. She reveals that, although now known as Rose Dawson Calvert, she is in fact the girl in the drawing. Rose's fiancé, Caledon Hockley (<a href="/wiki/Billy_Zane" title="Billy Zane">Billy Zane</a>), had bought the <i>Cœur de la Mer</i>. Rose wore the necklace when Jack Dawson (<a href="/wiki/Leonardo_DiCaprio" title="Leonardo DiCaprio">Leonardo DiCaprio</a>) drew her—the very drawing that Lovett later salvaged. Rose's perspective of the voyage changes from the past tense to the present, as she relives her memories.</p>
<p>When Rose and Jack return to her suite after having fled Caledon's valet, Spicer Lovejoy (<a href="/wiki/David_Warner_(actor)" title="David Warner (actor)">David Warner</a>), he meets them outside the stateroom door, chastises them, takes hold of Jack's arm, and drops the necklace into Jack's coat pocket, undetected. As they enter the room, they are confronted by Caledon, who claims Jack has stolen the necklace. In a short search of Jack, the diamond is found in the coat he is wearing. Jack's pleas of innocence are thrown in doubt when it is discovered that he has "borrowed" the coat he is wearing to sneak into first class, and he is branded a thief.</p>
<p>Before the <i>Titanic</i> sinks, Caledon puts his top coat on Rose, forgetting the necklace is in its pocket, until later. After the sinking, Rose is still wearing the coat. Before disembarking in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, Rose discovers the necklace.</p>
<p>At the end of the film, Rose walks alone to the stern of the salvage ship and opens her hands to reveal the necklace, which she has always kept a secret. She lets the necklace fall from her hand and into the water above the <i>RMS Titanic'</i>s wreck site.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternative_ending">Alternative ending</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heart_of_the_Ocean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Alternative ending">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Many of the deleted scenes from the film displayed Lovett's obsessive curiosity about the diamond's whereabouts during the course of Rose's story. In one deleted scene, after Cal realizes the diamond is in the coat he gave to Rose, he sends Lovejoy to retrieve it, promising the diamond to Lovejoy if he succeeds. Lovejoy fails, after fighting with Jack.</p>
<p>In the alternative ending, Lovett and Lizzy (<a href="/wiki/Suzy_Amis" title="Suzy Amis">Suzy Amis</a>), Rose's granddaughter, notice Rose walking along the deck toward the ship's stern. They see her stepping onto the railing and run to confront her. She shows them the diamond, dangling it over the rail and threatening to drop it in order to keep them back. When questioned about having the necklace all this time, Rose admits there were many times she thought of selling it, but refrained from doing so in order to live on her own without Cal's money. She allows Lovett to hold it in the palm of his hand, something he has wanted to do since he first searched for the gem. She then flings it overboard.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Original_and_reproductions">Original and reproductions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heart_of_the_Ocean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Original and reproductions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>London-based jewelers <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asprey_%26_Garrard" title="Asprey &amp; Garrard">Asprey &amp; Garrard</a> used <a href="/wiki/Cubic_zirconia" title="Cubic zirconia">cubic zirconias</a> set in white gold<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oocities_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-oocities-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> to create the <a href="/wiki/Edwardian_era" title="Edwardian era">Edwardian</a> style necklace used as a prop in the film.</p>
<p>After the film's success, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asprey_%26_Garrard" title="Asprey &amp; Garrard">Asprey &amp; Garrard</a> were commissioned to create an authentic Heart of the Ocean necklace using the original design. The result was a platinum-set, 171-carat (34.2 g) heart-shaped Ceylon <a href="/wiki/Sapphire" title="Sapphire">sapphire</a> surrounded by 103 diamonds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oocities_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-oocities-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The necklace was valued for up to $20 million and was donated to <a href="/wiki/Sotheby%27s" title="Sotheby's">Sotheby's</a> auction house in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills" title="Beverly Hills">Beverly Hills</a> for an auction benefiting the <a href="/wiki/Diana,_Princess_of_Wales_Memorial_Fund" title="Diana, Princess of Wales Memorial Fund">Diana, Princess of Wales Memorial Fund</a> and Southern California's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aid_For_AIDS" title="Aid For AIDS">Aid For AIDS</a>. It was sold to an unidentified Asprey client<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> for $17 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> under the agreement that <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Celine Dion</a> would wear it two nights later at the <a href="/wiki/70th_Academy_Awards" title="70th Academy Awards">1998 Academy Awards</a> ceremony. Neither of Asprey's creations have ever been made available for public viewing. The original necklace was ultimately acquired by private collector, George Holmes.</p>
<p>When the film was released in 1997, <a href="/wiki/The_J._Peterman_Company" title="The J. Peterman Company">The J. Peterman Company</a> sold less expensive reproductions. Their necklace consisted of 137 Austrian crystals and a detachable, inauthentic "blue diamond" pendant packaged in a navy blue, hinged presentation box.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heart_of_the_Ocean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_film">In film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heart_of_the_Ocean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: In film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>In the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Bruce_Almighty" title="Bruce Almighty">Bruce Almighty</a></i> (2003), Bruce Nolan, aboard the <i><a href="/wiki/Maid_of_the_Mist" title="Maid of the Mist">Maid of the Mist</a></i> at <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Falls" title="Niagara Falls">Niagara Falls</a>, asks a woman he refers to as "<a href="/wiki/Katharine_Hepburn" title="Katharine Hepburn">Katharine Hepburn</a>'s mom", "Tell me, why did you throw the blue 'Heart of the Ocean' jewel over the railing of the Titanic?"</li>
<li>In the animated film <i><a href="/wiki/Shark_Tale" title="Shark Tale">Shark Tale</a></i> (2004), Don Lino throws Sykes into the picture of Rose that Jack drew of her wearing the Blue Heart of the Ocean (The picture, however, shows Rose as clothed rather than nude).</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_music_and_music_videos">In music and music videos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heart_of_the_Ocean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: In music and music videos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a> music video "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oops!..._I_Did_It_Again_(song)" title="Oops!... I Did It Again (song)">Oops!... I Did It Again</a>", an astronaut gives the necklace to Britney, who says, "But I thought the old lady dropped it into the ocean in the end?" The astronaut replies: "Well baby, I went down and got it for you."</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_television">In television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heart_of_the_Ocean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: In television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>In the <i><a href="/wiki/American_Dad!" title="American Dad!">American Dad!</a></i> episode, "The Magnificent Steven", Roger is "drawing [Hayley] nude wearing the Heart of the Ocean".</li>
<li>In the <i><a href="/wiki/Futurama" title="Futurama">Futurama</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/A_Flight_to_Remember" title="A Flight to Remember">A Flight to Remember</a>"—a parody of <i>Titanic</i> book titled <i><a href="/wiki/A_Night_to_Remember_(book)" title="A Night to Remember (book)">A Night to Remember</a></i>—the robot Bender's aristocrat love interest owns an enormous diamond on a chain. After she sacrifices herself to save the rest of the group, the diamond is all Bender has to remind himself of her, but quickly loses interest when Hermes Conrad determines it to be a fake.</li>
<li>The <i><a href="/wiki/NewsRadio" title="NewsRadio">NewsRadio</a></i> episode "Sinking Ship" is a parody of <i>Titanic</i>. Dave gives the necklace to <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Simpson" title="Lisa Simpson">Lisa</a> for her birthday, who says, "It's a bit much."</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">The Simpsons</a></i> episode "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Old_Man_and_the_%27C%27_Student" title="The Old Man and the 'C' Student">The Old Man and the 'C' Student</a>", there is a parody scene on a yacht in which Smithers is drawing Mr. Burns in the nude wearing the necklace, as Jack drew Rose.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/The_Suite_Life_on_Deck" title="The Suite Life on Deck">The Suite Life on Deck</a></i> episode, "The Kidney of the Sea", parodies the <i>Titanic</i> movie. Jack is portrayed to be Zack, Violet is Rose, Ashton is Cal, and Violet's mother is Ruth Bukater. Zack saves Violet from being hit with flying cake, and she invites him to dinner to show her gratitude. At dinner, Ashton gives Violet a necklace called the Kidney of the Sea and later accuses Zack of stealing it. After Zack falls into the hot tub, Violet clutches him from outside and says she will never let go. In the end, Violet tosses the necklace into the ocean.</li>
</ul>
